**Brazil Monetary Policy and Political Risk**
The BCB's rate cut diverges from the hawkish pivot underway globally and reflects the committee's assessment that domestic disinflation has progressed sufficiently to warrant easing. The durability of the cycle depends on how quickly Hormuz-driven oil price relief passes through to domestic fuel and food costs, and whether the external environment stabilises — a materially more uncertain proposition following the Fed's hawkish Wednesday pivot. The Bolsonaro conviction adds legal pressure to a political movement already weakened by Jair Bolsonaro's prior sentencing. How the broader Bolsonarista base responds ahead of the 2026 electoral cycle remains to be seen. Separately, Brazilian investigators suspect Banco Master and related entities made payments — including an apartment and concert tickets — to relatives of Senator Jaques Wagner, a senior Lula ally, deepening governance concerns.

**Regional Political Economy**
Mexico's ruling Morena party moved to annul a local election swept by centrist opposition, raising early questions about institutional responses to potential midterm losses in 2027. In Venezuela, the post-Maduro commercial reopening is accelerating — Brazilian firms dispatched a business delegation to Caracas, while US energy majors including ConocoPhillips, Chevron, and ExxonMobil are re-engaging with Venezuela and Libya, consistent with a broader Trump administration strategy of placing more global oil supply under American commercial influence. The World Bank's revised global growth forecast, cut on the back of Hormuz disruption, carries direct implications for commodity exporters across the region, though lower oil prices also represent a terms-of-trade headwind for producers including Colombia and Ecuador.
