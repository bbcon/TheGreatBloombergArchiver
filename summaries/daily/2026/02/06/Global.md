# Daily Briefing — February 6, 2026

## Global Cross-Currents: Risk Unwind, Energy Geopolitics, and Strategic Materials

**A synchronized retreat from crowded trades dominated global markets this week**, with the unwind extending across US equities, precious metals, base metals, and crypto. The Nasdaq 100 logged its worst three-day decline since April, Bitcoin briefly traded below $61,000 — roughly half its October peak — and silver swung nearly 10% intraday amid thin liquidity. The trigger was less a single catalyst than an accumulation of valuation concerns, headlined by hyperscaler capex disclosures: Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft together now guide to roughly $650 billion in 2026 AI-related spending, with Amazon alone at $200 billion. Whether returns justify outlays remains an open question, and the resulting cash-flow implications appear to be reshaping risk appetite across asset classes. Asian sessions showed tentative stabilization on Friday, though it is too early to characterize the move as a durable bottom.

**The metals complex reflects more than speculative excess.** While Chinese retail flows — channeled into gold, silver, copper and tin amid limited domestic investment alternatives — clearly amplified recent volatility, the underlying drivers are structural: continued central-bank gold accumulation, dollar diversification, and the strategic repricing of copper and other inputs tied to grid, renewables, and AI infrastructure. Shanghai copper futures recorded their highest-ever monthly volumes in January. Separately, the Trump administration is establishing a $12 billion critical-minerals stockpile, an explicit response to last year's demonstration of China's rare-earth leverage.

**In energy, Strait of Hormuz risk is back in focus.** Some VLCC operators are reportedly accelerating transits through the chokepoint amid renewed US-Iran tensions, with Tehran signaling that Friday's talks are unlikely to yield rapid resolution. Concurrently, more than a dozen tankers carrying Russian Urals are repositioning toward Asia, as India appears to retreat from the trade following its US arrangement — a meaningful shift in crude flows that should tighten the discount structure for remaining buyers, principally China.

**Corporate strategy is realigning around the energy transition's slower-than-expected pace.** Stellantis took €22 billion in charges unwinding its EV strategy, joining Ford, GM and Porsche in scaling back electrification timelines — a notable counterpoint to Tesla's reported plans to build domestic solar-cell capacity of up to 10 GW to feed AI power demand.
