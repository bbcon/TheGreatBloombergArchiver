Micron earnings ease investor fears

Wall Street was looking to Micron for reassurance. The company delivered.

The US memory-chip maker released a quarterly sales forecast 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-24/micron-sales-forecast-tops-estimates-on-insatiable-memory-demand?cmpid=062526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260625&utm_content=5922>
 that crushed estimates, signaling its growth run remains strong. The results 
should help rebuild investor confidence following a tech selloff sparked by 
worries over AI.

How good was the report? Very.

 * Revenue will be roughly $50 billion in the fiscal fourth quarter. Analysts 
had estimated $43.2 billion.
 * Profit will be about $31 a share, compared with a projection of $25.31.
 * Third-quarter earnings increased to $25.11 a share. A year ago, they were 
$1.91.
 * Adjusted gross margin more than doubled.
Perhaps most importantly, Micron said it has secured 16 strategic customer 
agreements, which average three years in length. That suggests it can mitigate 
the boom-and-bust cycles that have plagued the memory-chip industry.

We wrote yesterday about how the AI trade is getting crowded, with more and 
more investors finding themselves on the “same bus.” Micron’s earnings report 
suggests investors will be fighting for seats, at least until the next catalyst 
hits. —Charles Riley

Bitcoin’s retreating retail army

For speculative retail traders, Bitcoin is losing some of its luster 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-24/bitcoin-s-retreating-retail-army-exposes-fresh-market-fragility?cmpid=062526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260625&utm_content=5922>
.

The original cryptocurrency fell as much as 5.4% to $59,023 yesterday, the 
lowest since October 2024. It’s down about 50% from a record high set in 
October, even after a slight recovery today.

Analysts say this downturn is different than previous selloffs. One reason is 
that rather than retreating to cash,many investors have been rotating into 
AI-related investments, drawing capital away from digital assets.

“The marginal buyer is no longer a retail investor but an ETF allocator or 
corporate treasury — and increasingly, that same investor is weighing Bitcoin 
against AI,” said Marion Laboure of Deutsche Bank.

“When these participants withdraw or rotate elsewhere, the decline is faster 
and more mechanical than in previous retail-driven cycles,” she added. —
