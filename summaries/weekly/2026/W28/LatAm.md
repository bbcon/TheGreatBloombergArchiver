**USMCA and Mexico Manufacturing Risk**

The Trump administration's decision not to renew the USMCA introduces sustained uncertainty rather than immediate rupture. By declining the 16-year extension option, Washington retains implicit leverage to pursue full termination if demands — including raising regional vehicle content thresholds from 75% to 82% and requiring 50% US-sourced value — are not met. The auto sector, representing nearly 18% of US-Mexico-Canada trade, faces the sharpest near-term exposure. Toyota's announced shift of some truck production from Mexico to Texas was cited as validating the approach. Oxford Economics cautions that the arrangement may suppress cross-border investment irrespective of formal outcomes. A broader replication of that production shift by other multinationals would pose a structural risk to Mexico's automotive export base.

**Brazil Trade Exposure**

Brazil faces a distinctive bilateral trade risk profile. The USTR investigation spans electronic payments infrastructure, ethanol market access, IP enforcement, and deforestation compliance — suggesting a multi-issue negotiation rather than a targeted dispute. Flávio Bolsonaro appeared at the hearing to defend Brazilian commercial interests while simultaneously distancing himself from tariff measures his family previously lobbied for, reflecting polling sensitivity to US trade policy's perceived costs.

**Colombia and Regional Dynamics**

De la Espriella's razor-thin electoral mandate complicates governance of a country facing near-term fiscal stress, structural electricity shortages, and intensifying narco-trafficking security threats. His alignment with Washington may deliver bilateral support but carries conditionality risks. Peru's tenth consecutive hold at 4.25% is consistent with a regional pattern of monetary authorities in hold mode following aggressive tightening cycles, though any sustained fuel-price passthrough from Hormuz disruptions could complicate that stance. Venezuela's oil output has risen approximately 20% since Maduro's arrest, with additional barrels flowing to the US Gulf Coast — a modest positive supply offset for regional energy balances.
