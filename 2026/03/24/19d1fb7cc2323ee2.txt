Europe Shows Early Signs of the Iran War’s Economic Costs
Europe is already seeing economic damage from the war in Iran, new data showed

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17403527&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=03242026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
The war in the Mideast is sending more price and supply shockwaves beyond the 
region and energy markets.

The latest data for Europe, released Tuesday, was among the first to cover the 
period since the US and Israeli attacks on Tehran began Feb. 28. Private-sector 
activity in the euro area rose at theslowest pace 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/euro-zone-activity-gauge-hits-10-month-low-amid-stagflation-fear?cmpid=BBD032426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260324>
 since last May, according to the Composite Purchasing Managers’ Index compiled 
by S&P Global.

“Firms’ costs are rising at the fastest rate for over three years amid the 
surge in energy prices and choking of supply chains resulting from the war,” 
Chris Williamson, chief business economist at S&P Global Market Intelligence, 
said in a statement.

Europe’s PMI revealed the largest decline in future output expectations since 
Russia invaded Ukraine four years ago, according to S&P Global, which said 
input prices increased at the fastest pace since February 2023.

Read More: War Knocks World Economy With Shocks to Growth and Prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/war-knocks-global-economy-with-dual-shock-to-growth-and-prices?cmpid=BBD032426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260324>

Germany saw its reading slip more than anticipated, and France fared worse, 
recording a third straight month below 50 — the dividing line between expansion 
and contraction. Services were the weak point in both cases, with manufacturers 
outperforming.

UK Slump

Meanwhile, British businesses barely grew 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/uk-growth-slows-and-inflation-surges-amid-iran-war-pmi-shows?cmpid=BBD032426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260324>
 this month as the war in Iran sapped demand and triggered the biggest increase 
in costs in more than three years. UK factories, in particular, were hit by the
largest pickup 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/uk-growth-slows-and-inflation-surges-amid-iran-war-pmi-shows?cmpid=BBD032426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260324>
 in inflationary pressures for more than three decades.

Anecdotally, growth headwinds are reaching industries that might’ve seemed 
insulated from the war’s economic fallout, or too far away geographically to 
feel it, according to Bloomberg News reporting. (Read that full storyhere. 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/fallout-from-trump-s-iran-war-ripples-across-the-world-economy?cmpid=BBD032426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260324>
)

 * An Indian film producer postponed release of a new movie for fear of 
missing out on cinema goers across the Gulf region.
 * To conserve fuel, cricket fans in Pakistan were instructed to stay home and 
watch a top tournament on television.
 * Italian farmers worry about squeezed profits from higher input costs like 
diesel.
 * Britain’s hospitality industry is having flashbacks to the soaring power 
rates caused by Russia’s invasion of Ukraine four years ago.
“If the situation continues, we could see input costs increase across food, 
logistics and utilities, presenting potential headwinds of higher costs and a 
further slowdown in demand later this year,” Saxon Moseley, head of leisure and 
hospitality at the consultancy RSM UK, said in a statement last week.
