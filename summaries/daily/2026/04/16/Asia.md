China's first-quarter GDP expanded 5.0% year-on-year, the strongest pace in three quarters, powered by manufacturing and exports. The result exceeded consensus expectations and suggests limited direct economic spillover from the Iran conflict so far. However, the data expose a persistent structural imbalance: industrial output grew 5.7% in March while retail sales rose only 1.7%, falling short of forecasts and signaling that domestic consumption has not meaningfully recovered. The two-speed economy — robust on the supply side, fragile on the demand side — remains a central policy challenge.

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company delivered a first-quarter profit surge of 58% and raised its full-year revenue growth guidance to above 30%, reinforcing its role as the primary proxy for global AI infrastructure investment. TSMC's market capitalization now exceeds $1.7 trillion, and Taiwan's total equity market value has overtaken the UK's to become the seventh largest globally at $4.14 trillion. The Taiex has gained 17% this month, and South Korea's Kospi has rallied approximately 23%, recovering sharply from an 18% two-day decline at the onset of the Iran war. BlackRock has flagged Korean equities as a preferred position given AI-driven earnings momentum.

Japan's Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama, speaking after bilateral discussions with Treasury Secretary Bessent in Washington, warned she was prepared to take "bold action" to support the yen. The statement was widely interpreted as a direct intervention threat; the absence of pushback from Bessent is being read as implicit US tolerance for possible currency-market action.

Macro hedge funds in China suffered notable losses in March. Bridgewater's All Weather Plus strategy fell 5.6% between late February and early April, trimming its first-quarter return to 3.9%. Shanghai Longlife's Macro Hedging No. 1 fund — 2025's top performer with a 153% return — declined 25% in March. The peer-group average loss was 6.3%, the worst on record.
