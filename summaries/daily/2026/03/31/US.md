US equity markets ended March on a tentative positive note, with S&P 500 futures gaining roughly 0.9% as the Wall Street Journal reported that President Trump is now considering ending the US military campaign against Iran without requiring the Strait of Hormuz to fully reopen, opting for diplomatic pressure instead. The report marked a meaningful shift from earlier signals that Washington was prepared to escalate strikes on Iranian energy and water infrastructure. Trump has oscillated repeatedly since hostilities began — alternating between claims that a deal is imminent and warnings of intensified military action — and the situation remains unresolved.

The more significant market development may be occurring in fixed income. US Treasury yields dipped Monday, with Fed Chair Jerome Powell noting that longer-term inflation expectations appear to be in check, and New York Fed President John Williams describing policy as well positioned. Markets appear to be repricing the conflict's growth implications more heavily than its inflation implications — a notable shift from the dynamic that prevailed through most of March. The immediate effect was a brief equity rally, though stocks gave back gains as WTI crude closed above $100 per barrel for the first time since hostilities began.

For US consumers, the conflict's costs are now visible at the pump: average gasoline prices crossed $4 per gallon for the first time since August 2022, carrying obvious political risk for the administration in a midterm election year. The S&P 500 is on track to post its worst monthly decline since September 2022, down approximately 7.8%, though forward earnings estimates for the broad market outside the Magnificent Seven have actually risen through March, tempering pressure on valuations.

On regulatory policy, the administration proposed allowing 401(k) plans to hold private assets, extending retail investor access to private markets. Nike reports earnings after the close, with investors watching for evidence that CEO Elliott Hill's turnaround strategy is gaining traction.
