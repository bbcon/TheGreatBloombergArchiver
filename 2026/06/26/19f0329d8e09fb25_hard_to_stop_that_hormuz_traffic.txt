The compelling need for oil and gas trades to normalize is hard to ignore. 
Hours before Tehran said navigation through the Strait of Hormuz will be 
managed in the spirit of the US-Iran interim peace deal, traffic had resumed
flowing in both directions through the vital chokepoint 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/traffic-flows-through-hormuz-after-shock-container-ship-attack?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 on Friday, despite an earlier shock attack on a container vessel.

Two fully laden tankers are heading out of the Persian Gulf, while four empty, 
inbound, very large crude carriers — bellwethers of demand for energy transport 
— are among the vessels sailing along the Omani coast, ship-tracking data show. 
This southern route is managed by Oman and coordinated by the US.

The strike on the Ever Lovely on Thursday 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/audio/2026-06-26/renewed-tech-rout-ship-struck-in-hormuz-more?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 — the first since an interim US-Iran peace deal was signed — has seen some 
owners and captains pull back, but does not appear to have completely derailed 
a slow return to normality. In a sign of traders’ optimism for a resumption of 
Hormuz traffic despite the attack,Brent crude fell to trade around $74 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-june-26?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 a barrel after climbing on Thursday. Iran’s proclamation later the same day 
should add to confidence.

The decline in oil prices did little to improve sentiment for equity traders. 
Global stocks slumped to a two-week low 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/asia-stocks-set-for-choppy-open-on-tech-volatility-markets-wrap?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 after a slide in Apple’s shares and a report that OpenAI may delay its initial 
public offering triggered anotherselloff in the tech sector 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/apple-price-hikes-spark-asia-tech-selloff-on-memory-cost-concern?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>

Indonesia is dangling unprecedented legal protections for investors in 
President Prabowo Subianto’s sovereign wealth fund, a move analysts warn could 
attract money with questionable origins and further erode the reputation of 
Southeast Asia’s largest economy.Buried in a 207-page financial-sector law 
published this week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/indonesia-opens-door-to-dirty-money-to-fund-prabowo-s-plans?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 is a provision that shields purchases of bonds from the fund, known as 
Danantara, from criminal, civil or tax probes. It also says bond records can’t 
be used for tax assessments or evidence in court proceedings.

Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are preparing to announce hundreds of 
billions of dollars worth in new investments on Monday, according to South 
Korean media reports this week. Samsung Group is set to unveil a1,000 trillion 
won ($646 billion) spending package 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/samsung-and-sk-hynix-prepare-huge-spending-increase-reports-say?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
 over the next decade, Maeil Business Newspaper reported, in what would be the 
largest such plans in the country’s history. Top executives from Samsung and SK 
Hynix, South Korea’s two most valuable companies and the world’s leading memory 
chipmakers, are expected to attend a presidential briefing at the Blue House at 
the start of next week, the report added.

India’s central bank has proposed broader participation in the country’s money 
market to include shadow lenders, companies and other financial institutions, 
in a move aimed atdeepening funding markets and expanding access to short-term 
liquidity 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/rbi-proposes-wider-access-to-indian-money-markets-in-draft-rules?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
. Currently, banks and standalone primary dealers can only participate in 
India’s term money market, with certain prudential limits. In draft proposals 
issued late Thursday, the Reserve Bank of India said non-banking financial 
companies, including mortgage providers would be allowed to trade in the term 
money markets as borrowers and lenders.

A weak monsoon is emerging as India’s next inflation risk, with a developing 
El Niño threatening to curb rainfall and push up food prices just as an easing 
oil relieves broader price pressures. The southwest monsoon, which accounts for 
about 70% of India’s annual rainfall, is vital for the $300 billion farm 
economy and has anoutsized influence on food prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/weak-monsoon-emerges-as-india-s-next-economic-worry-after-oil?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5971>
, rural demand and broader economic output. Reserve Bank of India officials 
have said they are closely monitoring the weather to assess the inflation 
outlook and stand ready to respond if price pressures broaden.
