 Deep Dive: ‘Horrendously High’ Fares 


Flight status in Krakow, Poland, in March. Photographer: NurPhoto/NurPhoto 
Global travel has been upended by the war with Iran, with Asia–Europe routes 
particularly affected. Airfares have soared, leaving travelers facing record 
prices ahead of the Easter rush <https://bloom.bg/46WCo7N>, according to a 
Bloomberg analysis of Google Flights data.

 * Since the start of the conflict, more than 46,000 flights have been 
canceled across the region, and up to 10% of global airline capacity has been 
wiped out—marking the biggest aviation shock since the Covid-19 pandemic. 
 * The price of an economy class round-trip ticket from Sydney to London has 
increased by more than 80% over the past two weeks. 
 * For premium travelers, a Sydney-to-London business class round-trip on 
Cathay Pacific Airways is now selling for as much as $28,000.
