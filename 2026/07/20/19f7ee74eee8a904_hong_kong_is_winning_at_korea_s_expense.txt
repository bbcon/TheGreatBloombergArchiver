Korean stocks are continuing to nosedive as a world-beating rally cools, with 
the Kospi sinking 4.5% on Monday to its lowest level since late April. Hong 
Kong, meanwhile, is becoming a major beneficiary of this rush out of 
chipmakers: the Hang Seng Index rallied 2.4%, the most globally.

So far this month, the Hang Seng is up 10%, while the Kospi has plummeted 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-19/korea-president-s-stock-dream-bumps-into-leveraged-etf-backlash?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 23%. That widening gap is putting the Hong Kong gauge on track for its biggest 
monthly outperformance since the Kospi was launched in 1983. Alibaba, Xiaomi 
and Meituan are among the biggest winners, surging more than 25%. Samsung and 
SK Hynix, meanwhile,are down 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-19/korea-s-ai-heavy-market-now-sets-the-tone-for-global-stocks?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 at least 25%.

That momentum could continue as investor sentiment sours toward chip stocks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/stock-market-today-dow-s-p-live-updates?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 amid worries that the AI spending spree is becoming harder to justify. At the 
same time, Chinese startup Moonshot AI’s new model Kimi K3 has added to the 
pressure,rattling US markets 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/what-is-moonshot-ai-why-china-s-new-model-is-roiling-markets?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 with fresh evidence that China isclosing the gap 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-18/moonshot-s-kimi-upends-conventional-wisdom-on-us-lead-over-china?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 on America’s top AI labs.

Michael Burry, of The Big Short fame, is one investor seeing bargains 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/michael-burry-says-good-time-to-chase-hong-kong-stock-bargains?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 in Hong Kong after takingshort positions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-01/michael-burry-s-short-call-dents-caterpillar-s-ai-fueled-rally?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
 in Nvidia, Applied Materials and the iShares Semiconductor ETF (SOXX).

“It is a particularly good time to look to Hong Kong for cheap stocks that 
should do well as the shine comes off Korea, Japan & the Soxx,” Burry, founder 
of Scion Asset Management, posted on X on Friday. He previously said on July 8 
that he had bought more shares of Chinese e-commerce firm JD.com.

Last month, the Hang Seng Index — which doesn’t include major chipmakers — 
reached its lowest level relative to the MSCI All-Country World Indexin decades 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-29/china-misses-out-on-ai-boom-as-stocks-trail-by-most-since-2001?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>
. That suggests plenty of room for gains as investors seeka safer harbor 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-07-13/korean-stocks-are-most-volatile-since-1998-asian-financial-crisis?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>

exchange is said to be considering extending the hours the market is open. 
Options Hong Kong Exchanges ismulling include scrapping 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-20/hong-kong-mulls-longer-stock-trading-hours-ending-lunch-break?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260720&utm_content=6727>

earlier at 9 a.m., according to people familiar with the matter.

HKEX is also looking at an after-hour session to catch early US activity, 
potentially between 8 p.m. to midnight, but will keep the market-close at 4 
p.m., the people said. The exchange relayed the proposals to brokers and 
trading firms in recent weeks, they said.
