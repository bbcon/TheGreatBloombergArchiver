The most consequential cross-cutting theme Tuesday was the formal activation of Trump's 10% global tariff, a reconstituted trade barrier following the Supreme Court's invalidation of his original IEEPA duties. While the rate is lower than the tariffs it replaced in aggregate, Bloomberg Economics notes it creates a more complex set of winners and losers — with some adversarial nations faring relatively better and US allies generally worse. The White House has signaled intent to raise the rate to 15% via formal executive order, though the timeline is unset, leaving market participants uncertain about the trajectory of the broader trade regime.

On the Iran front, US special envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner were preparing to travel to Geneva for a further round of nuclear talks, even as Trump publicly reserved the right to strike militarily if diplomacy fails. Oil prices steadied near multi-month highs as markets weighed these competing signals — a potential deal implying resumed Iranian supply, military action implying Strait of Hormuz risk. Diamondback Energy separately pushed back against glut concerns, arguing that resilient demand is more durable than consensus forecasts assume.

The Ukraine conflict marked its fourth anniversary with no resolution in sight. Peace talks remain deadlocked, the front line is broadly static, and senior European and NATO officials say there is no indication Putin is prepared to accept terms short of his maximalist demands. The US has been working toward a deal ahead of July 4th Independence Day celebrations, but the self-imposed deadline appears increasingly aspirational. Germany's defense chief publicly criticized Trump's negotiating approach.

On energy, a decade of US LNG exports has materially reshaped global gas markets, with the US now the world's largest LNG exporter. Approximately 25% of global LNG supply now originates in the United States, a share projected to reach one-third by 2030. The flexibility of US cargoes — sold free-on-board and therefore tradeable across global destinations — has progressively eroded Russia's historical ability to leverage gas supply as a geopolitical instrument against Europe. Saudi Arabia's fiscal deficit widened to a five-year high as lower oil prices pressured revenues, forcing a deceleration of some Vision 2030 megaprojects.
