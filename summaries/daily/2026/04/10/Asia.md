The dominant story across the Asia-Pacific region Friday was Xi Jinping's meeting in Beijing with Cheng Li-wun, chairwoman of Taiwan's Kuomintang opposition party and the first KMT leader to visit mainland China in a decade. Xi called for "cooperation and peace," while Cheng echoed the message and expressed hope that the Taiwan Strait would not become a "chessboard for external interference" — language calibrated to appeal to Beijing's longstanding opposition to what it views as foreign meddling. Xi simultaneously reaffirmed China's sovereignty claim over Taiwan, making the diplomatic warmth conditional rather than unconditional. The meeting carries explicit geopolitical signaling: with President Trump due to visit Beijing for a summit with Xi in mid-May, Beijing appears to be demonstrating to Washington that Taipei contains significant constituencies favoring engagement over deterrence. The KMT has stalled a $40 billion Taiwanese defense expansion and a $14 billion US arms deal has not received Trump's approval, reportedly to avoid jeopardizing the planned summit.

On the economic front, China's factory-gate prices exited deflation in March, with PPI rising 0.5% year-on-year after a 0.9% decline in February — an inflection partly attributable to the global energy price surge following the Iran war. Consumer prices, however, continued to cool as holiday-season demand faded. Capital Economics cautioned the PPI upturn remains modest and may not persist. In a separate positive development for Chinese industry, EV and hybrid exports surged 140% year-on-year in March to a record 349,000 units, with BYD, Geely, and Chery leading shipments. Domestic auto sales remain under pressure — BYD's home sales fell more than 40% — underscoring the continued bifurcation between China's export competitiveness and subdued internal demand.

Alibaba disclosed ownership of the "Happy Horse" AI video model, which debuted atop global rankings and displaced ByteDance's Seedance 2.0. Alibaba's cloud unit also led a $293 million funding round for ShengShu Technology, further intensifying competition in AI video generation. In India, the central bank's aggressive currency support effort — the boldest in a decade — risks deterring the foreign investors it has sought to attract, a tension that remains unresolved.
