Deep Dive: The City That Said No to Cars
Pedestrian and cyclists in Place de la Concorde in Paris.Photographer: Cyril 
Marcilhacy/Bloomberg
Paris has been transformed from a car-heavy city into one dominated by bikes, 
pedestrians and green spaces, with cleaner air and water. The shift is driven 
by Mayor Anne Hidalgo’s aggressive policies to cut car use and promote a “
15-minute city 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-paris-transformed-hidalgo/?sref=si897TkM&cmpid=032026_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260320>
.”

 * Major changes like pedestrianized riverbanks, fewer parking spots, slower 
traffic and 1,400 kilometers of bike lanes have helped cut vehicle emissions by 
about 35%.
 * The shift is popular with many residents but controversial among drivers 
and suburban commuters, even as the city ramps up public transport.
 * However, rising housing costs are pushing residents out, meaning the 
benefits are unevenly distributed.
