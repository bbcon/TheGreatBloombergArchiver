Several major Asian markets remained closed Monday for Easter, limiting price discovery, though Nikkei 225 futures indicated modest gains of around 0.5% in early trading. The session's dominant themes were energy market stress and geopolitical uncertainty emanating from Iran.

Saudi Arabia raised the official selling price of its main crude grade to Asia to a record high premium, though the magnitude came in at roughly half what surveyed analysts had anticipated — a reflection of the acute difficulty in gauging near-term demand given war-related volatility. The adjustment nonetheless underscores the structural repricing of energy costs being absorbed by Asian importers, most of whom lack the domestic production buffers of Western economies. Japan is among the most exposed, given its near-total dependence on imported energy.

Japan's Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi marks six months in office this week, a tenure that has delivered meaningful policy shifts — most notably a credible pivot away from deflation and a sustained move higher in JGB yields. Yet the galvanizing effect her election had on Japanese equities has been largely offset by the Iran war shock. The yen continues to weaken despite significantly higher domestic yields, a divergence that appears increasingly unstable and will likely require either policy intervention or a forced adjustment in positioning. Takaichi's political capital remains intact for now, but the energy import burden represents a mounting structural headwind.

The Indian rupee extended what the central bank described as its largest rally in 12 years, following the Reserve Bank of India's reinforced stance against speculative positioning. India's equity markets, however, have fared poorly in the year since Liberation Day tariffs and the onset of the Iran conflict, with the "Chindia" trade — once a consensus long for emerging market allocations — having underperformed sharply. The combination of tariff disruption and energy cost inflation has weighed particularly heavily on both China and India. Whether a durable re-entry point is approaching remains to be seen, likely contingent on clarity around the Iran conflict's trajectory.
