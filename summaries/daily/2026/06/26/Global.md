**Strait of Hormuz: Fragile Normalization**
The attack on the container vessel Ever Lovely — the first since the US-Iran interim peace deal — tested the durability of the nascent ceasefire. Iran subsequently stated that Hormuz navigation would be managed "in the spirit" of the peace agreement, and ship-tracking data confirmed two-directional traffic resumed Friday. Brent's decline following the incident suggests the market interpreted the episode as an isolated provocation rather than a structural breakdown, though the confidence of shipowners and crews remains fragile. Qatar is accelerating LNG production in parallel, reflecting producers' urgency to capitalize on the reopening window. A deterioration in the security environment remains the primary upside risk to oil prices and global inflation.

**Energy Transition and AI Infrastructure**
Texas, the US's largest oil and gas producer, is on a trajectory where solar and battery storage could overtake natural gas in new capacity additions within the next few years. BloombergNEF projects Texas solar capacity more than tripling to 82 gigawatts by 2035 against a bull-case gas addition of only 11 gigawatts. The economics of utility-scale storage are increasingly undermining gas's intermittency advantage. Meanwhile, global EV sales reached 1.65 million in May, with battery-electric vehicles growing 11% year-on-year — the third consecutive month of growth — and China's BEV sales turning positive for the first time in 2026.
