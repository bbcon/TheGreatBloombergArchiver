HSBC reported full-year pretax profit of $29.9 billion, surpassing analyst expectations and sending shares up 5.4% in London trading. The result was underpinned by strong performance in the wealth division, consistent with CEO Georges Elhedery's strategic pivot toward asset and wealth management as part of a broader institutional overhaul. The bank's bonus pool rose 10% to $3.93 billion, the highest in at least a decade, reflecting both the earnings outperformance and Elhedery's deliberate move toward a more performance-differentiated, Wall Street-style compensation structure. HSBC derives the bulk of its earnings from Asia, and its shares reached a record following the results.

Diageo fell 5.8% after the spirits group cut its full-year organic net sales guidance for the second time this fiscal year, now projecting a decline of 2-3%. The company continues to face demand weakness in both the US and China, illustrating how exposure to discretionary consumer spending in two of the world's largest economies creates compounding risk in the current environment. Aston Martin separately announced plans to reduce its workforce by approximately 20%, citing Trump's tariffs as a complicating factor in an already-difficult operational turnaround.

Germany's fourth-quarter GDP data showed a long-anticipated recovery taking hold, driven by domestic spending, though trade remained a drag — a pattern consistent with the broader structural challenge Merz is navigating. Factory orders have shown a distinct improvement in recent months, jumping in December by the most in two years, and business confidence as measured by the Ifo institute rose more than expected this month. Merz's government loosened Germany's constitutional debt brake earlier in his term to fund a substantial defense and infrastructure investment program, and early indicators suggest some economic benefit is beginning to materialize, though the trajectory remains uncertain.

Spain's housing situation deteriorated further into structural dysfunction, with government eviction protections due for another one-year extension. Critics argue the measures have created perverse incentives for tenants to halt rent payments, stranding tens of thousands of property owners in illegally occupied homes with limited legal recourse. A secondary market of specialist firms promising rapid removal of occupants has emerged, charging several thousand euros per engagement. French President Macron accepted the resignation of Louvre president Laurence des Cars following October's high-profile museum heist.
