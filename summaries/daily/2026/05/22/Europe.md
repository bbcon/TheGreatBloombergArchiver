# European Morning Briefing — May 22, 2026

**Macro and inflation dynamics dominate as Iran-war energy shock filters through European economies.** Brent climbed back above $104/bbl, reigniting concerns that the closure of the Strait of Hormuz — if sustained through August — could push crude toward $130 and deliver a stagflationary impulse comparable in scale to past oil-driven downturns, according to Rapidan Energy Group. ECB President Christine Lagarde maintained that medium-term inflation expectations remain anchored at target, though commentary from at least one Governing Council member kept the door open to a further rate hike, and the EU's economic affairs chief said the ECB "will have to respond." Long-end G7 sovereign yields touched two-decade highs this week as investors price in the risk that persistent supply shocks shift 2% targets from ceiling to floor. The tension between Lagarde's anchored-expectations message and bond-market positioning bears watching.

**UK data and politics weighed on sentiment.** Retail sales fell at the sharpest pace in nearly a year, and a closely-watched survey showed consumer confidence among lower earners deteriorating markedly as the energy shock erodes household budgets. Chancellor Rachel Reeves moved to close a corporate tax loophole used by oil and gas producers, projecting roughly £300 million annually to fund cost-of-living measures — a modest sum relative to BP and Shell's combined $3.7 billion in UK tax payments last year, but one that risks compounding the post-2022 windfall-tax drag on North Sea investment. Poland is contemplating a similar windfall levy that could raise 6 billion zloty from Orlen, while France's budget minister flagged TotalEnergies' "exceptional profits" without committing to new taxation.

**On the data calendar**, Germany released final Q1 GDP and France issued business confidence figures; the German Ifo print earlier in the week showed a first improvement in business sentiment since hostilities began, suggesting some resilience.

**In corporate news**, the Estée Lauder–Puig tie-up around Charlotte Tilbury collapsed over compensation terms, sending Puig shares sharply lower. Shell faces Dutch supreme court arguments today from climate activists seeking court-mandated emissions cuts aligned with a 2050 net-zero pathway.
