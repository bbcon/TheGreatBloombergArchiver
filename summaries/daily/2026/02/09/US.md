# US Daily Briefing — February 9, 2026

**Equity market leadership in flux.** Friday's recovery — which lifted the Dow Industrials above 50,000 for the first time — masked a more consequential repositioning underneath. The rotation out of mega-cap software and into chipmakers, equal-weighted indices, small-caps, and value has accelerated, with broader market measures hitting all-time highs even as the S&P 500 and Magnificent Seven sit below recent peaks. The catalyst appears to be Anthropic's release of new AI work-automation tools, which sent the iShares Expanded Tech-Software ETF down 8.7% on the week and prompted reassessments of incumbent software business models. Friday's bounce was thin: volume ran roughly 13% below the five-day average, implied volatility remained elevated, and a 9% rally in Goldman's most-shorted basket suggests short-covering rather than fresh conviction. Goldman's trading desk warned of further selling pressure this week from CTA unwinds. S&P 500 futures were down 0.29% in early trading; 10-year Treasury yields edged up 2bp to 4.23%.

**Fed leadership and institutional questions.** Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent urged the Senate to begin hearings on Kevin Warsh's nomination to chair the Federal Reserve, despite a Republican senator's pledge to block all such nominations pending resolution of a criminal investigation involving Chair Powell. Separately, Warsh floated reviving a Fed-Treasury accord to constrain bond-market intervention — a proposal that has stirred debate across rates markets given its implications for Fed independence and balance-sheet operations.

**China trims Treasury exposure; market reaction muted.** Chinese regulators have verbally instructed large domestic banks to pare US Treasury holdings, citing concentration and volatility concerns. The market response was negligible, reflecting that Chinese official and private holdings have declined steadily for over a decade to roughly $683 billion (about 7% of foreign-held Treasuries) and have been more than absorbed by other foreign buyers.

**Week ahead.** The shutdown-delayed January payrolls report lands Wednesday — including the annual benchmark revision, expected to mark down payrolls growth through March 2025 — followed by CPI on Friday. Fed speakers include Waller, Miran, Bostic, Hammack, Logan, Bowman, and Schmid.
