Deep Dive: Reform Offshoots
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/nigel-farage-s-reform-is-challenged-by-rupert-lowe-s-restore>
Nigel Farage, leader of Reform UK.Photographer: Jason Alden/Bloomberg
Nigel Farage has stoked a crisis in mainstream UK conservatism by hiving off 
voters on its right fringe. After a year leading the polls, others are now 
training that tactic on him.

 * There’s evidence that Reform UK is now leaking support to two new parties 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/nigel-farage-s-reform-is-challenged-by-rupert-lowe-s-restore?cmpid=031626_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 led by colleagues-turned-rivals even further to his right. One of those 
groups, Restore UK, has even secured the endorsement of Elon Musk.
 * Restore was set up by ex-Reform MP Rupert Lowe after a public fallout with 
Farage in early 2025, and formally launched just four weeks ago.
 * “Restore Britain is the party Reform UK pretends to be,” said Conner Haul, 
who left as chairman of a local Reform branch and cited high-ranking Reform 
officials with Muslim backgrounds as reasons to break with Farage.
 * Over December and January, a handful of Reform councilors switched to the 
newly founded Advance UK.
