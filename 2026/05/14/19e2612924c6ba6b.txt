Hello, this is Philip Glamann in Beijing, which is winding down a day of 
pageantry for Donald Trump and Xi Jinping.

Marching bands blared and honor guards paraded. Red carpets were walked and 
youngsters cheered. In short, Xi gave Trumpthe kind of reception he said he 
wanted so badly 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-14/xi-jinping-greets-trump-with-lavish-ceremony-video?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
. Well, without a hug — at least in public.

Besides the style, there was also some substance during the first visit by a 
US president to China since 2017.

First and foremost, the acrimony of the trade wars appears gone. Xi and Trump 
shook hands and were all smiles during a ceremony in a plaza just outside the 
Great Hall of the People.
Chinese leader Xi Jinping and President Donald Trump meet in Beijing on 
Thursday.Photographer: Alex Wong/Getty Images via Getty Images AsiaPac
Later, when the two leaders and their teams sat down before cameras, Xi said 
that he had “always believed that the common interests between China and the US 
outweigh their differences.”

Trump reciprocated the bonhomie, calling Xi “a great leader” and saying 
relations between the two sides would be “better than ever.” He also invited Xi 
to the White House in September.

That doesn’t mean it was all smooth sailing. Xi delivered a sharp message on 
Taiwan 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/trump-meets-xi-in-china-for-summit-as-tensions-over-iran-simmer?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
 when he met with Trump for some two and a half hours. Lest anyone forget that 
the status of the democracy of 23 million people is the reddest of Xi’s red 
lines, he warned about the potential for a conflict if the issue is mismanaged, 
saying that could push “the entire China-US relationship into a highly 
dangerous situation.”
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/sessions/2026-05-14/live-q-amp-a-what-s-next-for-trump-and-xi-after-their-summit-in-beijing>
Bloomberg
The worry by some is that could imperil a truce in the trade war that the two 
men clinched last year in South Korea, one that otherwise appears solidly 
intact.

That’ll be a relief to investors, and it suggests the door remains open to 
deals on things like purchases of semiconductors, airplanes and agricultural 
goods by China and flows of critical rare earth materials for the US.

The White House hinted at that after Xi and Trump met, saying they discussed 
stepping up trading of farm goods and oil.

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/china-reopens-door-to-us-beef-plants-as-xi-and-trump-meet?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
, reviving a trade that Beijing had throttled in response to the trade war.

For Xi, the show he put on for Trump gives him a win back home, too. 
Essentially, he can say to his 1.4 billion people that he has stood up to 
American might that once seemed so threatening. That can only serve to bolster 
the standing of the most powerful leader of China in decades.

There are some early signs that Xi’s message is gaining traction on Chinese 
social media, whereexpressions of confidence (and some amusing memes) 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/chinese-public-greets-trump-in-beijing-with-ai-memes-confidence?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
 have been appearing.

As one person wrote on Weibo: “After all these years, Trump finally figured 
out it is better to cooperate with China.”

Another thing we learned Thursday was that Xi is eager to remind everyone that 
his country is open for business. Hedelivered that message while meeting top 
American executives 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/xi-tells-us-ceos-accompanying-trump-that-china-will-open-up-more?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
 like Tesla’s Elon Musk, Apple CEO Tim Cook, Kelly Ortberg of Boeing and Nvidia 
chief Jensen Huang.

“China’s door to the outside world will only open wider,” Xi said, an apparent 
response to Trump indicating earlier he’d appeal for Beijing to open its 
markets even more.
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-14/trump-xi-meeting-focuses-on-taiwan-issue-video>
Xi’s comments amount to China’s most “direct” warning yet on Taiwan, said Zhu 
Feng, executive dean of Nanjing University’s School of International Studies.

Yet the Chinese leader was bound to raise Taiwan when meeting Trump. American 
arms sales to the democracy — like a massive $11 billion deal from last year 
and a $14 billion package apparently in the works — very much anger China. So 
if this is all Xi has to say about the issue, the summit remains on track.

Xi and Trump also apparently talked about the situation in the Middle East and 
other hotspots like Ukraine and the Korean Peninsula, though fewer details have 
come out. Whether or not the two sidescan make progress on resolving the Iran 
war 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/us-says-china-wants-more-of-its-oil-to-cut-middle-east-reliance?cmpid=BBD051426_CN&utm_campaign=china&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4438>
 is an open question.

The first day of the summit was heavy on messaging, the bulk of it warm. That 
opens the door to the two sides making more concrete progress on day two, 
possibly on things like Boeing jets, flows of rare earths and maybe even 
high-end chips.
