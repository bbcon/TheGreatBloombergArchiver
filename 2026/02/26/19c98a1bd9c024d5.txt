 Deep Dive: Mission Implausible 


 <https://bloom.bg/4b7Z07I> Photographer: Bloomberg Daybreak/Getty Images 
Donald Trump says the US would “easily” win a war with Iran—but the picture on 
the ground looksfar more complicated <https://bloom.bg/4b7Z07I>.

 * Fresh satellite images show Iranian engineers clearing debris at nuclear 
sites damaged during last June’s 12-day conflict <https://bloom.bg/4tTfw2Q> 
with the US and Israel, while reinforcing key sites against future attack. 
 * Any new military campaign would mean targeting a dispersed, partially 
concealed network spread across Iran’s rugged terrain. Still, US air power 
cannot eliminate scientific expertise, stockpiled material or Tehran’s resolve 
to rebuild <https://bloom.bg/4tUSDw1>. 
 * The International Atomic Energy Agency says Iran has amassed enough highly  
enriched uranium <https://bloom.bg/4tXlvDE> for roughly a dozen nuclear 
devices—but its whereabouts haven’t been independently verified in more than 
eight months. 
 * And the risks stretch beyond nuclear sites. Iran’s  allied armed groups 
<https://bloom.bg/3OyFw3r> across Lebanon, Iraq, Syria and Yemen could target 
US forces, while any disruption to the Strait of Hormuz, the artery for about 
20% of global oil flows, could send energy prices surging. 
<https://bloom.bg/4tWzjyk>

 Opinion 



There’s been a lot of bad weather—both actual and economic—this year: tariffs, 
panicking investors and saber-rattling. But none of it is spoiling the 
springtime vibe inEurope’s credit markets <https://bloom.bg/4u0Zqo0>, Marcus 
Ashworth writes. US borrowers are issuing record “reverse Yankee” bonds in 
euros, drawn by yields about 170 basis points lower than in the US. Call it 
de-dollarization if you will, but it’s more likely just progress.
