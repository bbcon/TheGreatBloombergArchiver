Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, Oil 
Europe Team Leader Alaric Nightingale takes stock of the market after a day of 
clashes involving shipping in the Strait of Hormuz and missile attacks on the 
United Arab Emirates.

With the Iran war now in its 10th week, the oil-supply black hole is growing 
and the chances of a swift return to normal are becoming ever-more remote.

US and Iranian forces exchanged fire 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-04/uae-issues-missile-warning-as-rising-tensions-risk-us-iran-truce?cmpid=BBBXT050526_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4134>
 Monday, fraying a ceasefire that has been fragile ever since it was announced 
in early April.

Tehran, meanwhile, published a map showing that the United Arab Emirates’ port 
of Fujairah is now within its “zone of control.” It againattacked an oil 
terminal 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-04/iran-attacks-uae-for-first-time-since-truce-with-missile-strikes?cmpid=BBBXT050526_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4134>
 at the facility.

These events have two effects as far as the oil market is concerned.

First, they represent yet another day when — at the very least — 10 million 
barrels of supply 
<https://iea.blob.core.windows.net/assets/01fe3dd7-21c1-4b16-8c5b-7df1aca6d6ff/Shelteringfromoilshocks.pdf>
 are not reaching global markets via the Strait of Hormuz.

And second, they demonstrate the risks to critical assets that will be needed 
to restore supplies once the conflict is over.
Smoke rises from an energy installation in Fujairah after an earlier attack in 
mid-March.Photographer: AFP via Getty Images
Many of the world’s top traders warned two weeks ago that, because a return to 
normal shipping through Hormuz will take time, the oil market faces aguaranteed 
supply loss 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-21/top-traders-say-billion-barrel-oil-shock-to-echo-long-after-war?cmpid=BBBXT050526_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4134>
 of around a billion barrels.

Since then, another 140 million barrels — at a minimum — have been lost. 

Following Iran’s efforts to widen its area of maritime control, more vessels 
havemoved away 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-05/ships-cluster-further-from-hormuz-strait-as-iran-widens-control?cmpid=BBBXT050526_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4134>
 from Hormuz, a sign that shipowners’ risk aversion is growing.

Yes, President Donald Trump has said the US will begin guiding some neutral 
vessels 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-04/trump-hormuz-plan-leaves-shipowners-guessing-as-attacks-persist?cmpid=BBBXT050526_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4134>
 through the waterway, but so far there’s no visible uptick in commercial 
traffic.

The supply shock has sent global oil prices up more than 50% since the start 
of the war. They’re still far from record highs.

But with every day the conflict drags on, the shortfall gets bigger, 
inventories are eaten away and the point at which prices really start to 
destroy demand draws nearer.

—Alaric Nightingale, Bloomberg News

Chart of the day

Europe’s traditional natural gas stockpiling season has been underway for a 
month and inventories are still well below normal levels. This sets the stage 
for a tense summer, with the region’s gas traders holding off from filling 
tanks at current high prices while policymakers fret about security of supply.

BNEF today

Stocks and bonds exposed to low-carbon activities, including electric 
vehicles, grids and clean power generation and storage, reached a record 
valuation of about $7.4 trillion as of March,BloombergNEF <> data show. 
However, their 32% growth from June 2024 still trails the 35% rise in 
fossil-fuel assets. Consequently, the share of clean energy in the total 
investable universe remained at around 40% over the past two years.
