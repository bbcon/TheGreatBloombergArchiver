Good morning. Silver plunges, erasing a brief rebound. A global tech selloff 
spreads to Asia. And Keir Starmer faces mounting Labour unrest over ties to 
Peter Mandelson.Listen to the day’s top stories <https://bloom.bg/4nmEQLf>.

— Abhishek Shankar and Hari Govind



Silver plunged as much as 17% as  fragile sentiment and thin liquidity 
<https://bloom.bg/4t9vDZI> follow a historic rout. Gold slid as much as 3.5%, 
though analysts expect support from longer-term fundamentals. Chinese 
billionaire trader Bian Ximing, who made almost $3 billion from gold, has built 
thelargest bet against silver <https://bloom.bg/4r4Z79K> on the Shanghai 
Futures Exchange.

Just as speculation is unwinding in precious metals, a  global rout in 
technology stocks <https://bloom.bg/4r2kVmu> snowballed into Asia. An AI-driven 
rout has erased hundreds of billions from stocks and credit 
<https://bloom.bg/45PBidt>, hammering software firms as investors realize AI is 
starting to disrupt entrenched business models. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang said 
the selloff in software stocks over AI disruption fears 
<https://bloom.bg/4qYDnfx> is illogical. Meanhile, in tech earnings: Alphabet  
beat sales estimates <https://bloom.bg/3LVg4Eq> but shares fell in late US 
trading, Arm shares fell on phone jitters <https://bloom.bg/4cbKnkJ>, and 
Qualcomm gave a lackluster revenue forecast <https://bloom.bg/4t9vFAO>.
 <https://bloom.bg/45PBidt> 

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer’s Labour Party is turning against him over his 
ties to Peter Mandelson. Labour MPs describe afebrile, mutinous mood 
<https://bloom.bg/4t9vFRk> within the party, with some privately questioning 
how long he can survive as PM despite rivals holding back from a leadership 
challenge. Meanwhile, UK lawmakersvoted to force disclosure 
<https://bloom.bg/3NXrmZu> of documents on appointment of the longtime 
associate of Jeffrey Epstein as US ambassador.

US President Donald Trump warned Iran to be “very worried” as American 
military forces <https://bloom.bg/4qelOad> amass in the region. Iranian Foreign 
Minister Abbas Araghchi said nuclear talks with the US were scheduled for 
tomorrow morning in Muscat. Oil fell for thefirst time in three days 
<https://bloom.bg/4ral3A8> after Iran confirmed negotiations.

The BOE will almost certainly leave rates on hold <https://bloom.bg/3OnyrSZ> 
and wait for more evidence that inflation is under control. Only one of 32 
economists surveyed expects a cut. The ECB is also likely tokeep borrowing 
costs unchanged <https://bloom.bg/4r0JoZt>.

 Deep Dive: Puzzle Power 


Second Foundation, Prague. Photographer: Milan Jaro 
Second Foundation has grown to dominate Europe’s most volatile power market 
<https://bloom.bg/3McODWH>, thanks to a group of puzzle-loving mathletes in 
Prague.

 * The company is now one of the biggest traders in the European intraday 
market, where gaining even a millisecond can give you an edge 
<https://bloom.bg/4t9vHIW>. 
 * Inspired by science fiction and Asimov’s Foundation, the firm prizes elite 
mathematical thinking and boundless AI. Competitive problem-solving defines the 
culture, with teambuilding that may involve overnight puzzle-deciphering races. 
 * Fast growth. In five years, Second Foundation has spread out to 28 markets, 
at times handling nearly 30% of Europe’s short-term power volumes 
<https://bloom.bg/3NXroAA> and already ranks among the top three short-term 
traders in its US markets. 
 * Next stop. The firm’s next big push is into  battery storage 
<https://bloom.bg/4rilLM9>, with plans for up to 5 gigawatts of projects. It 
already runs assets in Germany and Japan and is expanding into Romania and 
Finland.

 Opinion 



For the first time, cracks in the black market for oil are emerging 
<https://bloom.bg/3NXrqbG>, Javier Blas writes. Millions of barrels of unsold 
Iranian and Russian crude are accumulating in storage. But it’s not just thanks 
to more sanctions and political pressure, the key factor is far more mundane: 
the buyers of sanctioned crude oil have plenty of alternative aboveboard 
barrels available—at reasonable prices.
