Asian equity markets recorded one of their strongest weekly performances since late 2024, with the MSCI Asia gauge rising 5.6%. South Korea's Kospi led globally with a 14% weekly gain, driven by a surge in semiconductor names — SK Hynix rose 30%, Samsung 18% — as easing Middle East tensions and AI optimism redirected investor attention toward regional growth assets. Goldman Sachs raised its Kospi target for the fourth time this year, projecting an additional 20% gain and citing forecast earnings growth of 300% for 2026, a figure the bank describes as the strongest for any Asian market since the 1999 post-crisis recovery. Taiwan's benchmark gained approximately 7% and Japan's Nikkei 225 rose 5.8%. Gains were partially checked on Friday as renewed US-Iran clashes near the Strait of Hormuz reintroduced energy risk.

Toyota issued a notably cautious earnings outlook, projecting ¥3 trillion in operating income for fiscal 2027 against a consensus of ¥4.6 trillion, citing elevated raw material costs stemming from the Iran conflict. The stock fell as much as 3.5%, illustrating how the energy disruption continues to weigh on Japan's industrial exporters even as equity indices broadly recover. The yen remains under pressure despite apparent Bank of Japan interventions that moved the exchange rate from roughly ¥160 to below ¥157 per dollar; Vanguard remains bearish on the currency, arguing that higher oil prices are eroding Japan's terms of trade and that intervention has limited medium-term efficacy.

Asian governments are actively intervening in energy markets to cushion the oil shock. Japan committed $10 billion to assist regional nations with procurement and stockpiling. Australia provided A$30 million to Fiji for fuel purchases and repurposed export financing to support domestic importers, where March import costs for diesel and aviation fuel ran more than 40% above February levels. Separately, a Bangkok-based company — OBON — has been identified in US prosecutorial filings as the suspected conduit for smuggling Super Micro servers containing Nvidia chips to China, with Alibaba named as a potential end customer.

China's labor market showed continued deterioration in early-career cohorts, with the jobless rate for workers aged 25-29 rising to 7.7% in March, the highest since data revisions two years ago. Chinese courts have begun siding with workers displaced by AI automation, signaling that authorities are attempting to manage social tensions arising from rapid AI adoption — even as Beijing pursues AI development as a strategic priority.
