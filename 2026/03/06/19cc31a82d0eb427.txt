Russia Is the First Clear Winner From War in Iran
Indian refiners are snapping up Moscow’s oil after Middle East flows have 
bottled up in Persian Gulf

Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, oil 
strategist Julian Lee looks at how Moscow is winning from the Middle East war.

Russia has emerged as the first clear winner of the war in Iran after 
Washington agreed to let refiners in India resume purchasing Moscow’s oil.

The US Treasury Department issued a temporary waiver 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/us-issues-license-to-allow-some-russian-oil-sales-to-india-mme5qix4?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 allowing Indian processors to buy Russian barrels loaded onto tankers before 
March 5. The waiver runs until April 4, but could be extended.

The move has been made necessary because Iran has effectively closed 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/hormuz-tracker-traffic-comes-to-a-near-halt-amid-signal-fog?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 the Strait of Hormuz, the narrow neck of water through which most of the 
Middle East’s oil reaches international waters, following last weekend’s 
strikes by the US and Israel.

While Hormuz isn’t physically blocked and Iran’s leadership has said it has no 
intention <bbg://news/stories/TB852JKGZAIW> of halting traffic, actual and 
threatened attacks on ships using the waterway mean that owners aren’t allowing 
their vessels to pass through. That’s effectivelybottled up 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/strait-of-hormuz-how-iran-conflict-is-disrupting-oil-and-gas-shipping?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 about 20% of the world’s oil supplies in the Persian Gulf, boosting prices to 
the highest since 2024 and raising concerns about shortages.

But for Russia, the closure has proved an unexpected opportunity.

Since late 2025 its oil exports have been under more pressure than at any time 
since the Kremlin ordered the full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022.

India emerged as the biggest buyer of Russia’s seaborne oil after European 
buyers shunned Moscow’s barrels. But last year, New Delhi came under increasing 
pressure to reduce, or even halt the trade, with US President Donald Trump
doubling tariffs 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-06/trump-hits-india-with-additional-25-tariff-over-russia-oil-buys?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 on India.

That led to a 50% drop in India’s purchases of Moscow’s crude, with deliveries 
dropping to just 1.1 million barrels a day in February.

Russian barrels were left at sea, boosting the amount on the water by 65% to 
140 million barrels. It also forced Moscow to offerdeeper discounts 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/russia-and-iran-slashing-prices-to-china-as-oil-piles-up-at-sea?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 to shift those barrels into China.

Now that pressure has evaporated overnight.

Indian buyers are snapping up 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/millions-of-barrels-of-russian-oil-are-already-close-to-india?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
 cargoes that were stranded at sea andpaying a premium 
<bbg://news/stories/TBGKW3T96OSQ> to get them, boosting the Kremlin’s war chest 
just when the sanctions were starting to bite.

—Julian Lee, Bloomberg News

Chart of the day

Commercial vessel traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains largely 
suspended, with sparse transits occurring in recent days, ship-tracking data 
compiled by Bloomberg show. In the 24 hours leading up to the Joint Maritime 
Information Center’s latest report, there were no oil shipments going through 
and only two commercial transits — bringing traffic to anear-complete halt 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/strait-of-hormuz-shipping-traffic-at-near-total-halt-jmic-says?cmpid=BBBXT030626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260306>
.

BNEF today

The Trump administration threatened to cut off all trade with Spain, including 
LNG, for denying it access to military bases in the Iran war. The South 
European country could face higher gas procurement costs if the US follows 
through, according toBloombergNEF <bbg://news/stories/TBFY4DT96OSG>. Volumes 
could be rebalanced through swaps within Europe rather than disappear outright, 
but Spain would still be exposed to higher marginal costs.
