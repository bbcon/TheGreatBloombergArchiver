Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter John Ainger explores America’s struggle to secure enough power for its 
sprawling, energy-hungry data centers.

It’s crunch time for the operator of America’s biggest power grid.

PJM Interconnection LLC’s latest auction to procure electricity once again 
failed to attract enough supply commitments to guarantee reliable grid 
performance in the coming years.

The shortfall comes against a backdrop of booming demand 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-12/biggest-us-grid-may-be-too-large-to-function-regulator-says?cmpid=BBBXT071626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6572>
 from massive data centers, and raises the stakes for an emergency process 
expected to kick off in September.

That mechanism is aimed at getting AI hubs to cover the costs 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-15/trump-to-direct-key-us-grid-operator-to-hold-emergency-auction?cmpid=BBBXT071626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6572>
 of securing their future energy needs, plugging a projected shortage before 
the situation spirals out of control.

The problem is that demand from data centers is surging faster 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2025-ai-data-centers-electricity-prices/?cmpid=BBBXT071626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6572>
 than new generation is being built, and politicians and consumer advocates are 
loath to see residential customers shoulder the burden of expansion costs.
An Amazon Web Services data center in Sterling, Virginia.Photographer: 
Bloomberg
PJM plans a two-step process 
<https://www.pjm.com/-/media/DotCom/committees-groups/cifp-rbp/2026/20260416/20260416-item-05---pjm-reliability-backstop-procurement-proposal---paper.pdf>
: bilateral contracting, followed by a special auction to clear any remaining 
capacity needs. Success is crucial to avoid a supply gap that PJM’s own 
projections suggest could reach the equivalent of 60 nuclear reactors in the 
next decade.

This week’s regular auction ended more than 6 gigawatts short of what’ll be 
needed in the year beginning June 2028, even as total supply coststied a record 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/largest-us-grid-misses-power-supply-goal-amid-rampant-ai-surge?cmpid=BBBXT071626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6572>
. A PJM spokesperson said it’s confident it can meet the challenge.

But time is running out. If the backstop procedure is to start in September, 
PJM must submit plans to the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission by the end of 
this month. As things currently stand, it’s not even clear yet how the process 
will work.

PJM serves one out of every five Americans — as well as Northern Virginia’s 
famous Data Center Alley — and is under intense pressure tokeep costs down 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/data-centers-drive-76-rise-in-power-bills-on-largest-us-grid?cmpid=BBBXT071626_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6572>
 for consumers. FERC has scheduled a conference on July 23 to discuss the 
grid’s future.

PJM’s “progress has been uneven and uncertain, and it’s unclear to us if these 
efforts will be sufficient to satisfy the concerns of politicians,” Scotiabank 
analysts led by Andrew Weisel wrote in a note. The “outlook remains clear as 
mud.”

—John Ainger, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

Solar project owners are adding batteries in markets saturated with renewable 
energy, aiming to protect revenue at times when the grid is flooded with cheap 
power. A record 87 gigawatts of solar capacity built last year was co-located 
with energy storage, according to BloombergNEF. That’s a quarter of all the 
solar plants installed.
