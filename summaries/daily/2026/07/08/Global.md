**Oil and the Strait of Hormuz**
The escalation represents the most direct challenge yet to the interim ceasefire signed June 17. Trump's declaration that the agreement is "over" came alongside US Central Command strikes targeting more than 80 sites in retaliation for Iranian attacks on commercial shipping. Roughly 63 million barrels of Iranian crude are now stranded across Persian Gulf and Asian waters, with most vessels showing no declared destination. Strait traffic, while not fully closed, remains materially below pre-conflict levels. Iran's parliament speaker issued a pointed warning—"the era of bullying and extortion is over"—while Tehran's display of regime continuity around the Khamenei funeral proceedings signals no near-term willingness to capitulate. Market-implied US one-year inflation swaps, at 2.23%, remain below the 3.67% consumers expect, a divergence that may narrow if energy prices remain elevated.

**Gold and Momentum Reversal**
Gold has benefited structurally from geopolitical fragmentation, with central bank accumulation accelerating and new clearing infrastructure being established in Hong Kong and Singapore. Simultaneously, global equity momentum strategies are suffering a significant reversal: quant funds are caught between collapsing semiconductor winners and a sharp recovery in prior losers across the US, Japan, and Europe. The trigger is less clear-cut than prior momentum shocks, suggesting the unwind may have further to run before stabilizing.
