Tech rout

Tech losses are stacking up around the world as the AI rally slams into 
reverse.

Here’s the latest:

 * Nasdaq futures are sharply lower 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-22/asian-stocks-set-for-gains-as-oil-holds-losses-markets-wrap?cmpid=062326_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260623&utm_content=5825>
.
 * Intel and Micron Technology lead a broader decline among chipmakers.
 * SpaceX is poised to fall 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-23/spacex-rout-set-to-push-market-value-below-2-trillion?cmpid=062326_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260623&utm_content=5825>
 below its first-day opening price of $150.
 * South Korea’s Kospi Index plunged 10% from a record high.
 * SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics both slid 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-23/korean-stocks-fall-more-than-4-from-record-high-on-tech-selloff?cmpid=062326_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260623&utm_content=5825>
 over 12% in Seoul.
 * Chinese equities in Hong Kong entered a bear market 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-23/china-stocks-in-hong-kong-eye-bear-market-on-spending-woes?cmpid=062326_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260623&utm_content=5825>
. 
Investors appear to be focused on two main risks. One is the colossal amount 
of spending on AI by hyperscalers such as Alphabet. The other is the prospect 
of higher interest rates this year in the US.

“Since the start of the Iran war, tech has been the only sector where 
institutions have increased holdings, so some volatility and profit-taking 
should not come as a surprise,” said Marija Veitmane of State Street Markets.

The big question is whether the tech selloff has staying power or amounts to 
just another speedbump.So far, there appears to be little appetite among 
investors to buy the dip in any of the assets caught up in risk-off moves.

Attention will soon shift to Micron Technology’s quarterly results on 
Wednesday. The stock has been this year’s standout performer in the 
Philadelphia Semiconductor Index, rallying more than 300% since January. —
Charles Riley

In Elon they trust

Credit markets are placing an unusual amount of trust in Elon Musk 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-23/-a-lot-of-leeway-spacex-s-high-grade-debt-brings-out-skeptics?cmpid=062326_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260623&utm_content=5825>
 and his vision for a business built around reusable rockets, a globe-spanning 
satellite network, AI and even data centers in space.

Consider: When Moody’s Ratings first evaluated Nvidia almost a decade ago, it 
settled on a Baa1 investment-grade rating. The chipmaker had a relatively light 
debt load and more than $1 billion of free cash flow after 16 years as a public 
company.

Last week, Moody’s gave SpaceX the same Baa1 rating even though the company 
has a limited public financial record, “sustained negative free cash flow” and 
years of heavy capital spending still to come.

In equities, buyers often pay for big stories in hopes of explosive gains. Yet 
it’s far rarer in investment-grade credit, which is built around steady cash 
flows, manageable leverage and dependable, albeit more modest, returns.

“It appears that the agencies are giving them a lot of leeway and a lot of 
positivity for events that are going to happen in the near- or middle-term,″ 
said Sal Naro of Coherence Credit Strategies.

Enthusiasm will be tested shortly. Bond investors are lining up to provide 
SpaceX with about $20 billion of financing, and maybe more, as soon as today. —
