Affordability Angst

Monday was a bad day for Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent’s favorite 
bond-marketmetric 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2025-02-07/trump-presidency-bonds-not-stocks-emerge-as-key-metric-for-economy-team?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
, with10-year Treasuries 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/us-treasuries-extend-fall-as-iran-war-fuels-global-bond-rout?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 at one pointsliding 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/treasuries-swing-to-losses-as-reports-of-talks-damp-haven-demand?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 the most since the tariff-driven turmoil of last April.

At issue: Fears that the Iran conflict-driven surge in oil costs will add to 
price pressures in aneconomy 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/jamie-dimon-says-exuberant-market-doesn-t-match-fine-economy?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 with inflation still a percentage point away from the Fed’s target. 
Ironically, the Monday selloff hit one year to the day since the Treasury chief 
touted plans for an “affordability czar 
<https://www.cbsnews.com/news/scott-bessent-treasury-secretary-face-the-nation-03-02-2025/>
” to address cost-of-living concerns that had help usher Trump back to the 
White House.

Ian Lyngen, head of rates strategy at BMO Capital Markets, highlighted that 
the risk is “anotherpotential tax 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/trump-s-iran-war-adds-new-potential-shock-for-us-economy-voters?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 on the consumer who is already struggling with affordability on multiple 
fronts.”

While Trump declared victory 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-19/trump-says-he-won-on-affordability-singaling-messaging-shift?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 on affordability last month, polls suggest Americansremain concerned <> about 
inflation. Now, the Middle East conflict threatens to drive up the price of 
energy products including gasoline — one of the few major categories to see an 
outrightdecline <https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_01132026.htm> 
last year. And the administration’s opponents are also hardly prepared to 
concede.

The Democratic National Committee Monday publicized how Bessent had failed to 
follow up on his “affordability czar.” DNC Chair Ken Martin asked, “What ever 
happened” with that plan. “This broken promise is just another example of how 
this administration has betrayed everyday Americans after promising to ‘lower 
costs on Day One,’” Martin said in astatement 
<https://democrats.org/news/one-year-later-trump-vance-admins-affordability-czar-is-nowhere-to-be-found/>
.

Bessent argues it was the Biden administration that created the inflation 
problem with excess spending — a view shared by some economists. But the 
Treasury had no response to a request for comment on why there wasn’t a 
follow-through on the affordability czar, whom the secretary had said would 
focus on “the five or eight areas where this administration can make a big 
difference for working class Americans.”

“We are going to have an Affordability Council. We are laser focused on this,” 
Bessent had said on CBS’sFace the Nation. But a year on, investors and voters 
are signaling angst.

Need-to-Know Research

This section last Thursday took a look at one potential overhaul 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-02-26/waymofication-ai-s-promise-and-peril-spurs-debate-at-economics-conference?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 of Fed rate projections if and when Warsh becomes chair. South Korea’s central 
bank has provided another template to consider.

The Bank of Korea’s system 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-26/bank-of-korea-to-release-6-month-rate-guidance-to-boost-comms?cmpid=BBD030326_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 involves each policymaker submitting not one but three dots for each time 
horizon: A base case, plus upside and downside risk scenarios. A very confident 
official can put all three dots at the same rate level. This addition of 
confidence levels around median projections “should help anchor market 
expectations over the medium term,” Nomura economist Jeong-Woo Park wrote in a 
note. The projections cover a rolling six-month horizon.
Source: Bank of KoreaSource: Bank of Korea
“Based on probability distribution” as it is, the Korean dot-plot “can 
indicate the BOK’s confidence in its rate projections,” Park said. His 
colleague Rob Subbaraman added, “With Kevin Warsh likely to soon chair the Fed 
and wanting to shake up transparency and communication, it will be interesting 
to see if some of these BOK innovations make their way” to Fed guidance.
