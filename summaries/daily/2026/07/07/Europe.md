**France: Fiscal and Political Pressure Mount**
The French government's "public finances alert committee" convened Tuesday against a backdrop of deteriorating budget indicators. Budget Minister David Amiel described the situation in unusually stark terms, warning that all fiscal indicators are "flashing red." The central government balance through May has worsened year-on-year, with risks compounding from local governments and social security. OAT spreads over Bunds remain elevated relative to pre-2024 levels, reflecting persistent political risk premium that has built steadily since Macron's snap election decision. A budget must be presented to parliament in October, in what is likely to be a contentious process given the three-way parliamentary impasse.

**Le Pen Ruling and 2027 Elections**
The appeals court decision on Le Pen's five-year ban from office carries significant market implications, though perhaps less than commonly assumed: polling consistently shows the Rassemblement National leading France's first-round presidential race regardless of whether Le Pen or Jordan Bardella leads the ticket. Markets appear to have been gradually pricing in elevated political risk rather than awaiting a discrete event, which may limit the immediate market reaction to the ruling. The 2027 election nonetheless represents a genuine policy discontinuity risk given the fiscal trajectory.
