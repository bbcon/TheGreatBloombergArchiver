Asia

China's tightening of oversight over US capital flows into its technology sector warrants attention, though details from the source material are limited. The move appears consistent with Beijing's broader effort to assert regulatory control over strategic industries and manage the composition of foreign investment in sensitive areas. Separately, Treasurer Bessent's signaling that Gulf and Asian nations are seeking dollar swap lines reflects continued demand for dollar liquidity mechanisms among emerging-market and commodity-exporting economies — a dynamic that, if formalized, would reinforce dollar primacy even as questions mount about US fiscal sustainability. India's Maldives are reported to be experiencing a financial-offshoring boom, suggesting capital continues to seek jurisdictions with favorable regulatory frameworks in the region. China's domestic solar industry faces capacity rationalization pressures, with Beijing reportedly moving to curb excess production as the sector grapples with structural overcapacity — a tension between industrial policy ambitions and near-term profitability that has broad implications for global clean-energy supply chains. Chinese companies meanwhile continue to dominate the EV battery supply chain globally, with competitive pressure from alternative chemistries remaining a medium-term rather than immediate threat to their market position.

Global

Two cross-cutting themes dominate this period. The first is the ongoing economic fallout from the US-Israel conflict with Iran, which is generating inflationary spillovers across multiple regions. Energy supply disruptions have raised fuel costs globally, including in remote Amazonian communities dependent on diesel, while central banks from Uruguay to elsewhere in the emerging-market world are navigating a more complex inflation environment as a result. Colombia is hosting fossil-fuel exit talks even as the energy crisis deepens — a genuine tension between energy security imperatives and decarbonization commitments that has no easy resolution. The second theme is a structural shift in emerging-market macro resilience. The experience of Latin American economies during the current episode illustrates how central bank credibility, built through proactive inflation-fighting during the pandemic cycle, has materially altered the transmission of external shocks. Currencies have proven more stable than historical precedent would suggest, and policymakers have retained space for counter-cyclical responses — a meaningful regime change relative to prior decades. The IMF has flagged that this dynamic partly reflects a relative deterioration in advanced-economy risk perception, with US Treasuries losing some of their traditional premium amid concerns over fiscal trajectories and questions about Federal Reserve institutional independence. The battery and energy storage theme carries genuine macro significance: Brazil's program to electrify isolated Amazon communities via solar and lithium storage represents a convergence of falling technology costs, federal subsidy rationalization, and emissions reduction, with the government projecting $171 million in subsidy savings and avoidance of 800,000 metric tons of greenhouse gas emissions by 2036. The Iran war's upward pressure on diesel prices has, counterintuitively, accelerated the economics of this transition.
