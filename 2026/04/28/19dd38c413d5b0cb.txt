There is little doubt which stock market is winning this year: South Korea. 
The Kospi benchmark has jumped almost 60%, the most among major indexes, and 
total market value of listed companieshas now eclipsed 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/korea-passes-uk-to-become-world-s-eighth-largest-stock-market?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 that of the UK.

The justification for the rally is AI, and demand for high-bandwidth memory 
and other chips used in data centers which is boosting earnings for Korean 
memory makers. Samsung Electronics, which dominates global supply along with SK 
Hynix and Micron Technology, posted a755% increase 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-06/samsung-profit-up-eight-fold-after-ai-chip-sales-defy-war-fears?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 in profit in the March quarter.

BlackRock in April said South Korea was a key driver 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-16/blackrock-strategist-definitely-likes-korean-stocks-on-ai?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 for its upgrade of emerging-market stocks to overweight, with the nation as 
part of the tech supply chain ecosystem showing “incredible momentum.”

Despite the rally in shares of Samsung and SK Hynix, the stocks are still 
relatively cheap 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-21/memory-stock-valuations-spark-debate-over-supercycle-potential?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 compared to rival firms. The two firms trade at less than 6 times forward 
estimates versus about 21 times for Taiwan’s TSMC. Samsung shares have tripled 
while SK Hynix’s quadrupled since the end of August as chip prices skyrocketed.

Yet the size of the gains means risks are growing for the broader market if 
the rally reverses — as wasbriefly but clearly 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-03-04/asia-s-markets-bear-the-brunt-of-iran-war-as-korean-stocks-crash?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 seen when the US war with Iran began. Samsung and SK Hynix together now 
account for 42% of the Kospi’s total market capitalization, compared with 28% 
two years ago.

Employees are pushing for a greater share of the profit windfall from AI. Last 
weekend thousands of Samsung employeesheld a protest 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/samsung-rally-draws-30-000-to-demand-greater-share-of-ai-profits?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 demanding higher wages. The workers’ union has threatened an 18-day strike 
starting May 21.

With demand for their products expected to grow, the frenzy for Samsung and SK 
Hynix shares is likely to continue, but so is the market’s dependence on the 
two firms to continue rallying.

—Richard Frost

Government bond yields are set to stay higher for longer as the Iran war keeps 
inflation elevated, according to BlackRock Investment Institute.

Inflation pressures were already percolating before the latest conflict in the 
Middle East, BlackRock strategists wrote in a report. The oil shock from the 
war will onlycompound those risks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/blackrock-says-higher-government-bond-yields-are-here-to-stay?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
, piling even more pressure on central banks to keep monetary policy tight to 
rein in prices.

“We think higher yields are here to stay – and that long-term government bonds 
are no longer effective diversifiers against equity declines,” they wrote.

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un confirmed a policy that requires soldiers to 
commit suicide 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-28/kim-jong-un-confirms-soldier-self-blasting-policy-in-ukraine?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260428&utm_content=3918>
 on the battlefield to avoid capture, while fighting Russia’s war against 
Ukraine.

Speaking at an event to inaugurate a memorial in Pyongyang for North Koreans 
who have died in Ukraine, Kim twice mentioned soldiers who had “self-blasted,” 
according to the official Korean Central News Agency.

“Heroes who unhesitatingly opted for self-blasting, suicide attack, in order 
to defend the great honor” were praised by Kim in the speech. “They did not 
expect any compensation, though they performed distinguished feats,” he added. 
“They died a heroic death.”
