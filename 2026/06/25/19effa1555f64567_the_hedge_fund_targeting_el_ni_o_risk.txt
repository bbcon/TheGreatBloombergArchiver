Hedge fund Moreton Capital Partners is targeting $500 million for a

вАЬWe think itвАЩs going to be a dramatic reshaping of the global food situation,вАЭ

A burnt ear of corn during a heat wave in MexicoPhotographer: Hector

El Ni√±o, confirmed by scientists earlier this month, is characterized by a 
warming of the Pacific Ocean that alters global weather patterns, which can 
damage crops. The phenomenon could be one of the strongest on record and 
already top rice exporter India has been hit by a delayed start to the monsoon. 
The World Bank warned that El Ni√±o could push food prices above current 
expectations, just as farmers contend with the impact of the Iran conflict on 
the fertilizer and fuel costs.

вАЬWe all know itвАЩs here, but the scale and the severity of this, I mean, thatвАЩs 
kind of the part that concerns us for a lot of these crops,вАЭ Finemore said. 
вАЬThis could be the worst El Ni√±o on record and if that plays out, that has 
incredible implications for food inflation.вАЭ

The MCP Special Opportunities Fund is aiming to raise the funds by the end of 
September and will seek various trading strategies from cross-commodity 
relative-value trades to long and short positions in futures and swaps. The 
fund will target large institutional investors, including insurers, endowments, 
and pensions that have significant weather-risk exposure.

Moreton also sees opportunities in commodities such as soybeans, sugar, coffee 
and vegetable oils, along with water. There may even be opportunities beyond 
agriculture, like the Shanghai aluminum contract, if there is dryness in China 
that puts pressure on hydropower, a source of energy for smelting, Finemore 
said.

вАЬWe will kick off trading almost immediately in early July,вАЭ Finemore said. 
вАЬWeвАЩve been focused on the Iranian war situation. The next event will be El
