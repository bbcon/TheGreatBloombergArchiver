UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer faced the most acute political pressure of his tenure as he delivered a speech aimed at resetting his government following Labour's sweeping defeat in local elections. Deputy Prime Minister Angela Rayner publicly called for an urgent change in direction, and Reform UK's Nigel Farage — who polled as the biggest winner from last week's voting — is consolidating his position as a serious political force, though his path to power remains uncertain given his divisiveness and the prospect of opponents coalescing against him. Labour's predicament reflects a broader public frustration with the government's economic management against the backdrop of Iran-war-induced inflation pressures.

On the economic front, German industrial production unexpectedly fell in March for a second consecutive month, reinforcing concerns about the eurozone's largest economy amid rising energy costs. A Bloomberg survey of economists showed the ECB is expected to hike rates twice in 2026 as inflation accelerates, a marked shift in the policy outlook. Separately, demand for affordable electric vehicles is rising sharply across Europe: inquiries for EVs priced below €30,000 on Germany's Mobile.de surged 87% since early March, with Renault's Zoe among the leading models. This reflects a meaningful consumer behavioral response to the fuel price shock. British employers meanwhile continued shifting toward temporary hiring in April, with permanent-role hiring falling at its fastest pace in three months, extending a downturn that began in 2022, according to the Recruitment and Employment Confederation. US troop drawdowns from Europe remain a live concern among European defense planners, with allies anticipating further announcements potentially including reductions in Italy and the cancellation of plans to station long-range missiles in Germany.
