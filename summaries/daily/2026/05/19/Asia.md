# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — May 19, 2026

**Putin arrives in Beijing as energy leverage shifts.** Vladimir Putin landed in Beijing today for his first foreign trip of the year, accompanied by an unusually heavyweight delegation including five deputy prime ministers, central bank Governor Elvira Nabiullina, and the heads of major state enterprises. Moscow is hoping that Middle East-driven energy turmoil — with Brent near $110 and the Strait of Hormuz constrained — will soften Beijing's position on pricing for the long-stalled Power of Siberia 2 pipeline. The optics, however, favor Xi. Putin arrives weakened by a record Ukrainian drone strike on Moscow over the weekend and fresh from Xi's own summit with Donald Trump, at which Xi reportedly suggested Putin may come to regret the invasion. With Russian gas largely shut out of Europe and the economy heavily dependent on Chinese demand, Beijing has room to play hardball, though both sides will likely emphasize their "no-limits" partnership.

**Asia equity divergence widens sharply.** Korea's Kospi fell 3.3% today, paring some of an extraordinary 73% year-to-date gain that has been driven almost entirely by Samsung and SK Hynix — together accounting for roughly two-thirds of the index's advance. Retail margin debt has climbed to a record ₩36.5 trillion ($24bn), concentrating risk further. The Jakarta Composite, meanwhile, slumped 3.5%, extending its position as 2026's worst-performing major benchmark (-26%). Pressures include MSCI's warning of a possible frontier-market downgrade, a widening fiscal deficit, and speculation that Jakarta will centralize commodity exports to defend the rupiah. Indonesia subsequently confirmed plans to tighten control over coal and palm oil exports.

**Japan growth surprises, but bond market signals strain.** Q1 GDP expanded an annualized 2.1%, accelerating from a revised 0.8%, on stronger consumption and trade. However, PM Takaichi's supplementary fiscal package pushed super-long JGB yields to fresh record highs, prompting Finance Minister Katayama to flag markets as a policy risk. The global bond selloff — with US 30-year yields at 5.14% — threatens to compound funding pressures across weaker Asian economies and could weigh on the AI-driven equity rally.

**Other notable items.** President Marcos reiterated that the Philippines would likely be drawn into any Taiwan conflict, a stance that will irritate Beijing. Indian carriers including Air India and IndiGo have asked state refiners to defer jet fuel price hikes until the Iran conflict subsides. Australia secured supplementary jet fuel from China and urea from Brunei, while the RBA flagged elevated risk of inflation expectations becoming unanchored.
