Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter Jack Ryan looks at how the conflict in the Middle East has affected 
gold markets.

The Iran war 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-27/iran-and-israel-keep-up-attacks-after-trump-extends-deadline?cmpid=BBBXT032726_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
 is threatening to put a key driver of gold’s record-breaking rally into 
reverse.

Turkey has offloaded $8 billion of gold 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/turkey-s-8-billion-gold-drawdown-since-iran-war-hits-bullion?cmpid=BBBXT032726_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
 reserves this month in a bid to protect its currency from soaring energy costs 
and higher demand for dollars. The country’s central bank sold and swapped 
about 60 tons of bullion over two weeks in March.

That’s bigger than the outflow triggered by investors’ flight from gold-backed 
exchange traded funds over the same period,, which was fueled by a dash for 
cash amid a broader meltdown in financial markets, rising bond yields and a 
resurgent dollar.

If more monetary institutions follow Turkey’s example, it would slow the 
overall pace of purchases and call into question long-term assumptions that 
central banks are loath to sell gold.

“The narrative of central banks as perpetual one-directional buyers is being 
challenged,” said Nicky Shiels, head of metals strategy at MKS PAMP SA, a 
trader and refiner.

Since the global financial crisis, central banks have been net buyers of gold. 
The pace of purchases picked up in late 2022 after the freezing of Russia’s 
foreign exchange reserves, which highlighted the benefits of diversifying from 
dollar assets.

The gold price has more than doubled since then, surging above $5,000 an ounce 
earlier this year.

The price <> is already down about 18% from that peak and the war in the 
Middle East could lead to more bullion sales elsewhere.

Some countries that have been accumulating gold are energy importers, so a 
steeper oil and gas bill means fewer dollars retained to be recycled into 
reserves of the precious metal.

Beyond that, Persian Gulf states have seen their much-needed flow of 
petrodollars crimped by the closure of the Strait of Hormuz to most energy 
exports, although they do have sizable holdings of other assets in addition to 
bullion.

Unlike in the market for US Treasuries, there’s no overarching authority in 
gold. That means countries can’t be held to ransom with the threat of their 
assets being frozen, but also means there’s no Federal Reserve to step in as 
buyer of last resort to support prices.

Instead, gold bulls will be hoping China’s central bank 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-07/china-s-pboc-extends-gold-buying-as-middle-east-tension-simmers?cmpid=BBBXT032726_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
 will pick up the slack. But in a crisis where emerging economies collectively 
rush to the market to raise dollars, a downward spiral of selling becomes all 
the more likely.

—Jack Ryan, Bloomberg News

When Europe’s gas stockpiling season begins in April, governments are keen to 
make an early start refilling severely depleted storage tanks. But the gap 
betweensummer and winter 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-27/europe-s-gas-dilemma-gets-more-acute-as-storage-season-starts?cmpid=BBBXT032726_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
 gas prices is telling traders to wait. If they lock in costly supplies right 
away to secure fuel for next winter, the transaction would lose them money. So 
for now, traders are largely staying on the sidelines.

BNEF today

If Qatar’s LNG exports are halted for two months from March 2, gas storage 
across Northwest Europe, Italy, Spain and Austria is projected to reach a still 
comfortable level of around 85% by the end of September, according to 
BloombergNEF. A three-month halt scenario would result in lower storage levels 
at 82% full.
