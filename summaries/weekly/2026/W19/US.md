**Equity Market Resilience**
US equity markets sustained record highs through much of the week, with the S&P 500 approaching 7,400 by Wednesday despite an increasingly bifurcated underlying economy. The advance remains narrowly concentrated: factor analysis reveals that quality stocks have persistently underperformed since Liberation Day, while momentum and liquidity factors continue to dominate. Goldman Sachs strategists flagged "yellow flags" around the thinness of the rally, and the composition — AMD surging roughly 17% on raised guidance, Super Micro gaining 19%, Pinterest beating expectations, while Cloudflare fell as much as 17% on weak guidance and Trade Desk sank 15% — illustrates that selective execution rather than broad re-rating is driving the index higher.

**AI Capital Expenditure Cycle**
The AI investment thesis remains the primary structural support. First-quarter data show real spending on information-processing equipment rising 64% and software up 46%, even as all other capex declined 17%. Alphabet raised nearly $17 billion in debt on Tuesday to fund data center buildout, and S&P 500 companies announced $665 billion in authorized buybacks in the first four months of the year — the highest pace on record — providing a structural floor beneath indices. Nvidia's 9% decline over six sessions and its status as the worst performer in the Philadelphia semiconductor index year-to-date suggests competitive chip architectures from Alphabet, Amazon, and Intel are registering in investor sentiment, even if market share loss remains limited in practice.

**K-Shaped Consumption and the Energy Tax**
The energy shock's asymmetric incidence is becoming well-documented. US gasoline prices topped $4.50 per gallon for the first time since July 2022 by Wednesday. New York Fed research shows households earning under $40,000 reduced real gasoline consumption 7% even as nominal spending rose 12%, while higher-income cohorts barely adjusted. Kraft Heinz's CEO warned that lower-income shoppers are "literally running out of money at the end of the month." Spirit Airlines' cessation of operations offered a pointed illustration of the shock's asymmetric impact on businesses serving price-sensitive consumers. Pantheon Macro estimates the energy shock may translate into roughly a 1% annualized decline in real after-tax income in Q2, with Bank of America characterizing the full-year "gas tax" at approximately 0.5% of GDP.

**Monetary Policy and Fiscal Tensions**
The Federal Reserve left rates unchanged, with internal divisions deepening. Two-year inflation swaps crossed 3% — their highest since 2022 — and 30-year Treasury yields briefly breached 5% on Thursday. Fed Governor Collins aligned herself with recent dissenting easing language, suggesting the FOMC has not resolved its internal debate. The self-reinforcing dynamic between the administration's military posture in the Gulf and its publicly stated desire for lower interest rates represents a material policy inconsistency. On fiscal matters, the CBO projects federal debt reaching 108% of GDP by 2030. Jeffrey Gundlach disclosed he is repositioning for an extreme restructuring scenario, joining Apollo's Marc Rowan and Bridgewater's Ray Dalio in unusually direct warnings, though five-year CDS-implied default probabilities remain below 1%. A federal trade court additionally declared Trump's 10% global tariffs unlawful, and the administration began processing refunds for $166 billion in previously vacated tariff collections.

**Corporate Developments**
GameStop proposed a $56 billion all-cash-and-stock acquisition of eBay on Monday, offering a roughly 20% premium to Friday's close — a striking bid from a company with a $12 billion market capitalization. Amazon's launch of Amazon Supply Chain Services, packaging its freight, trucking, air, ocean, and last-mile capabilities into a single commercial offering, weighed on FedEx, UPS, and Expeditors, though Bloomberg Intelligence analysts characterize the near-term competitive threat as incremental. The April nonfarm payrolls report, expected Friday to show approximately 62,000–65,000 jobs added, is the week's remaining data event of consequence.
