US equity futures advanced on Wednesday, with S&P 500 futures up roughly 0.5% and Nasdaq 100 futures gaining 0.6%, after President Trump announced an indefinite extension of the Iran ceasefire. The move reinforced what markets have come to call the TACO trade — the wager that Trump will consistently seek an off-ramp rather than permit escalation — and confirmed the pattern that has defined post-selloff behavior since the pandemic: once traders decide risk is back on, there is no retest. As Bloomberg's Longview Economics notes, the historical retest rate for major corrections stood at 87% from 1978 through 2020; it has been zero since. Whether that reflects structural changes in retail participation, tighter hedge fund risk controls, or simply extrapolation of a short behavioral sample remains genuinely uncertain.

The session also brought Kevin Warsh's long-awaited Senate Banking Committee confirmation hearing. The Fed chair nominee pledged independence repeatedly but declined to offer any near-term guidance on the interest rate path, leaving his views largely opaque. More substantively, Warsh signaled comfort with the administration's "economic statecraft" agenda, describing a Fed role in supporting dollar stability through Treasury Secretary Bessent and Secretary of State Rubio's framework. His comments on dollar swap lines were notably restrained — he framed the Fed's contribution as delivering stable prices rather than directly expanding liquidity facilities — but the alignment with the Bessent-Rubio posture on dollar supremacy is worth monitoring. The nomination remains complicated by Senator Thom Tillis's hold, tied to the Justice Department's probe of the Fed.

On the tariff front, Trump signaled that companies seeking refunds from the Supreme Court-ordered repayment of struck-down IEEPA tariffs — a process potentially totaling more than $160 billion — might face implicit political consequences. "If they don't do that, I'll remember them," the president said of firms that forgo claims. The comment adds a chilling dimension to what CBP framed as a routine administrative process. Tesla reports quarterly earnings after the close, with Wall Street focused less on the headline figures — expected to beat lowered estimates — than on concrete progress toward robotaxi commercialization and AI/robotics initiatives that underpin the stock's 183x forward earnings multiple.
