# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — February 11, 2026

**Regional equities extend outperformance.** The MSCI Asia Pacific Index rose 1% to a fresh record, widening its lead over US and European benchmarks as a softening dollar and rising conviction in Fed easing drew flows toward tech-heavy markets including Taiwan and South Korea. The regional gauge is now up 39% since the start of 2025. A weak US December retail sales print and a cooling employment cost index have shifted rate-cut expectations back toward two reductions this year under Fed chair-designate Kevin Warsh, supporting risk appetite across the region ahead of the delayed US payrolls release.

**China — disinflation easing, but recovery uneven.** January factory-gate deflation moderated more than expected, though the improvement leaned heavily on the global metals rally rather than a durable pickup in domestic demand. Morgan Stanley's Robin Xing cautioned there is "no decisive reflation yet." Consumption fragility was underscored by the abrupt closure of all Shanghai outlets of premium chain Xiaonanguo days before Lunar New Year. Separately, SMIC warned that the breakneck pace of AI chip spending may be pulling forward years of demand, raising the prospect of underutilized data center capacity — a notable caution from inside the supply chain. Beijing's reported guidance to domestic banks to limit Treasury purchases briefly weighed on US bonds but appears consistent with a decade-long, gradual reduction in holdings rather than a fresh inflection.

**India — trade-deal ambiguity, capital markets maturing.** The White House revised its US-India trade fact sheet, removing references to pulses and softening language on Indian commitments to American goods, compounding confusion already flagged by Indian farmer groups. Separately, Goldman Sachs vaulted to fourth in Indian equity underwriting and fifth in M&A league tables in 2025, overtaking Morgan Stanley in stock sales for the first time in a decade.

**Commodities and policy.** Indonesia cut the production quota at Weda Bay — the world's largest nickel mine — to 12 million tons from 42 million, an aggressive supply curb aimed at lifting prices. The RBA's Deputy Governor Hauser reiterated inflation remains "too high," reinforcing the recent hawkish tilt. Singapore's PM Wong is preparing a budget centered on AI adaptation and trade fragmentation.
