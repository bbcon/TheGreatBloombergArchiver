Asia-Pacific markets opened Tuesday to a deteriorating security picture that undermined Monday's brief optimism. Overnight, Iran launched missile and drone strikes on the Israeli cities of Eilat, Dimona, and Tel Aviv, as well as US bases across the Middle East. Saudi Arabia intercepted a drone in its eastern region, Kuwait reported power line disruptions following an Iranian attack, and sirens sounded in Bahrain. Counter-strikes attributed to the US-Israeli alliance damaged a gas pressure-regulation facility and struck a pipeline supplying the Khorramshahr power plant in southwestern Iran. S&P 500 futures fell 0.4% in choppy trade; Brent crude rose approximately 2% to $102 per barrel.

The conflict's economic footprint in the region is widening rapidly. South Korea's Prime Minister Kim Min-seok cancelled a planned visit to China to manage domestic energy and economic disruption, with Seoul announcing vehicle-usage restrictions in the public sector — a step that may extend to private motorists. South Korean government bond yields rose in tandem with a broader global move higher in short-duration debt spanning Canada, Brazil, and the UK. Meanwhile, the Iran crisis is opening an unexpected commercial opportunity for South Korea's defense industry, with Middle Eastern countries increasingly attracted to its lower-cost air-defense systems.

Aviation stress is intensifying across Southeast Asia. Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. warned that grounding planes due to jet fuel shortages remains a "distinct possibility," while Vietnam Airlines has suspended select domestic routes and VietJet has reduced flight frequencies. Qantas subsidiary Jetstar has cut roughly 17.5% of intra-New Zealand services in May as demand softens under higher fares. In a separate development, Japan's Finance Ministry is reported to have made inquiries with market participants about potential intervention in crude oil futures markets, reflecting Tokyo's growing anxiety over energy costs and yen pressure.

In corporate news, Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group is reportedly developing contingency plans for a potential takeover of Jefferies Financial, with a small internal team monitoring the US bank's declining share price for an opportunistic entry. Nintendo reduced Switch 2 production guidance by more than 30%, citing weaker-than-expected holiday demand in the US and rising memory chip costs, with the reduced output rate expected to persist through April.
