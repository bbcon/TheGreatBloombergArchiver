**Climate Stress and Energy Infrastructure**
The current heatwave, driven by El Niño, is exposing meaningful vulnerabilities in European energy infrastructure. France's nuclear curtailments are particularly significant given the system's reliance on nuclear baseload; the combination of heat-reduced output and surging cooling demand is compressing supply margins continent-wide. The UK's grid warning, issued in summer — historically a period of lower demand risk — underscores how climate-related shocks are broadening the seasonal risk window for energy system operators. The Network for Greening the Financial System published guidance this week warning central bankers that more frequent physical shocks will generate persistent monetary policy trade-offs, as heatwaves and floods can "push output and inflation in opposite directions simultaneously."

**Fiscal Framework and Politics**
The European Commission's decision to grant modest additional budget flexibility to indebted sovereigns, including Italy, has not provoked market disruption, which the EU frames as a validation of its post-pandemic fiscal surveillance framework. On UK politics, Andy Burnham's path to the Labour leadership appears to be clearing after Darren Jones withdrew from consideration; Burnham's northern-English political base is seen as potentially helpful in stemming voter losses to Reform UK.
