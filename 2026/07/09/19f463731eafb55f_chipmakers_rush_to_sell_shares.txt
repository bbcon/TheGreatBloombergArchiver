For chipmakers, it’s becoming a race to sell shares as a boom in the first 
half of the year starts to deflate.

Korea’s SK Hynix is preparing to price its US offering 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/sk-hynix-us-offering-is-more-than-seven-times-oversubscribed?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 Thursday, which could raise around $24.5 billion, making it among the largest 
ever debuts in the US by a foreign company. Demand is high — with the listing 
being more than seven times oversubscribed — despite the Seoul-traded shares 
falling 32% from their June peak.

China’s CXMT — which Apple is seeking 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-01/apple-seeks-to-buy-chinese-made-memory-chips-with-lobbying-push?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 to buy chips from amid a global shortage — plans to raise at least 29.5 
billion yuan ($4.3 billion) in the domestic market and will next week open 
investor subscriptionsfor its IPO 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/memory-chipmaker-cxmt-to-launch-4-3-billion-china-ipo-next-week?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
. A gauge of Chinese tech stocks fell for past six straight days before 
rebounding Thursday.

Firms have come to market as they took advantage of frenzied investor demand 
for AI-related tech — and to meet heavy capex needs. SpaceX was the apex, 
raising $75 billion last month in the largest listing of all time. Its stock 
has fallen 27% since its peak, despitea rash 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-07/spacex-shares-win-early-bullish-calls-from-wall-street-brokers?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 of bullish calls by analysts. Share sales in Hong Kong surged to afive-year 
high 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-28/ai-fever-powers-hk-share-sales-through-hurdles-to-five-year-high?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 in the first half of the year, buoyed by Chinese tech offerings. In Taiwan, 
companies have raised a record $4.8 billion via convertible bonds, exceeding 
every full-year amount.

At least one canny investor is tapping out. Bain Capital has sold its entire 
stake 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/bain-exits-kioxia-after-landmark-chip-deal-yields-record-returns?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 in Japanese chipmaker Kioxia, Bain Managing Partner David Gross said in an 
interview on Bloomberg Television. And short sellers are circling. Michael 
Burry ofThe Big Short fame said he’d shorted shares of US chipmaker Micron 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2026-07-05/michael-burry-is-right-about-memory-chipmakers?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
, saying that it “defines cyclical like no other.”

As the saying goes, timing is everything.

Observable movements in the world’s most vital energy conduit largely occurred 
along an Iran-approved route nearer to the waterway’s north, while the 
US-supported Omani corridor was quiet, ship-tracking data show.

The slowdown comes after a spate of Iranian attacks on vessels that had 
prompted the US strikes, while President Donald Trump also said the ceasefire 
with Iran was over. Some 14commodity carriers

interim peace deal in mid-June.

The Taiwan dollar fell to its weakest level since April 2025, erasing gains 
from last year’s historic rally as dollar strength and record dividend payouts 
weighed on sentiment.

The currency dropped as much as 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/taiwan-dollar-hits-14-month-low-on-greenback-gains-dividends?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 0.6% to 32.210 per US dollar on Thursday. The move wiped out the sharp gains 
from May last year, when the currency soared by the most since the 1980s on 
easing trade tensions and upbeat tech earnings.

China is facing torrential rain and flooding this weekend from a massive 
typhoon tracking toward the country’s east coast, following recent severe 
storms that led to deaths and crop damage.

Typhoon Bavi is forecast to cross the coast of Fujian province on Saturday 
night, packing top sustained winds of up to 162 kilometers (101 miles) per 
hour, the China Meteorological Administration forecast. That would make the 
system equivalent to a Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson Scale.

Bavi is a “gargantuan typhoon 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/-gargantuan-typhoon-bavi-threatens-more-floods-in-rain-soaked-china?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
, with a wind field size ranking in the top 3% of all western Pacific typhoons 
of the past decade,” the US Joint Typhoon Warning Center said. The huge storm 
is currently around 1,000 kilometers southeast of Japan’s Okinawa island, 
packing winds of 204 kilometers per hour.

Hundreds of snakes have escaped from breeding farms amid heavy flooding in 
China’s Guangxi region.

Authorities have been on high alert after snake farms were flooded, sending 
large numbers of reptiles, includingvenomous cobras 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/hundreds-of-snakes-on-the-loose-after-severe-flooding-in-china?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
, into nearby villages and farmland, according to state broadcaster CCTV. Teams 
have been deployed to capture the escaped snakes, and temporary medical clinics 
have been set up to ensure rapid treatment for bite victims. A woman in 
Hengzhou, a city in Guangxi, has died after being bitten by a snake, local 
media reported.

Ex-Banker Convicted in Deadly Car Crash Seeks Ferrari Records

An ex-Deutsche Bank banker imprisoned over a fatal car crash in Hong Kong is 
seeking to clear his name in a UK court by forcing Ferrari to hand over 
internal documents he claims could prove the vehicle was unsafe.

Wall Street banks were largely absent from a $5.8 billion fundraising spree by 
Chinese AI stars in Hong Kong this week.

AI model maker Zhipu led with a $4 billion share placement Thursday, the 
second-biggest equity offering in Hong Kong this year. Zhipu’s IPO was only six 
months ago, and it had rallied about 1,500% through Wednesday. The shares rose 
a further 22% at one stage Thursday morning.

International banks didn’t have a role 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/wall-street-banks-miss-multibillion-dollar-ai-deals-in-hong-kong?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260709&utm_content=6377>
 in Zhipu’s share sale, which only had league-table topper China International 
Capital Corp. on board. Meanwhile, CICC and Citic Securities were the only 
banks involved in Shanghai Biren Technology’s $901 million share placement 
launched Friday. Wall Street banks face a challenge working on these 
transactions as AI is at the heart of the rivalry between Washington and 
Beijing.
