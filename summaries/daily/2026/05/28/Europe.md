**UK Labour Market**
The UK youth unemployment deterioration is drawing serious policy attention. The government-commissioned review attributes the crisis to a confluence of factors: surging employer national insurance contributions reducing hiring incentives, worsening youth mental health outcomes, and AI-driven displacement of entry-level roles. The structural nature of these drivers suggests the problem is unlikely to self-correct through the economic cycle alone, raising questions about the adequacy of existing active labour market programmes.

**UK Cost Pressures**
The food and drink sector's pricing intentions are a concrete illustration of how the Hormuz disruption is transmitting into European consumer prices. With over 80% of surveyed manufacturers planning to raise prices and roughly a third considering restructuring or job cuts, the sector is signalling both a demand-side pass-through and supply-side adjustment that the Bank of England will need to weigh carefully against an already fragile growth backdrop.

**Hungary Post-Orbán**
The Champions League final in Budapest this weekend arrives as an inadvertent audit of Orbán's governing legacy. The football infrastructure programme — built through tax subsidies, cronyism, and state-directed capital — now faces scrutiny as Hungary's new government confronts its fiscal cost. The episode illustrates a broader pattern across central European political economies, where cultural patronage and economic statecraft became difficult to disentangle during the illiberal turn of the 2010s.
