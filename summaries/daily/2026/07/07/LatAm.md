**Brazil Trade Exposure**
Brazil faces a distinctive bilateral trade risk. The USTR investigation, now in its second day of public hearings, spans a broad set of grievances — electronic payments infrastructure, ethanol market access, IP enforcement, and deforestation compliance — suggesting Washington is treating Brazil as a multi-issue trade partner requiring structural concessions rather than a single-issue negotiation. Flávio Bolsonaro appeared at the hearing to defend Brazilian commercial interests, underscoring the domestic political salience of the proceedings. The outcome remains uncertain but carries meaningful implications for agricultural and digital-sector trade flows.

**Regional Investment Climate**
Mexico's April investment data illustrates the challenge facing the Sheinbaum administration: while the headline rebound is a positive, its dependence on government outlays rather than private capital formation suggests limited durability. Sheinbaum has pursued state-led energy development strategies while simultaneously lobbying business leaders for capacity-expanding investment — a tension that remains unresolved. Cuba's complete electricity grid collapse, driven by crumbling infrastructure and a de facto US oil embargo, represents an extreme case of the region's energy vulnerability but carries limited direct macro contagion for larger economies.
