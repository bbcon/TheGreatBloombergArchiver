European equity markets edged lower, with the Stoxx Europe 600 down 0.8% even as technology-driven gains in the US provided some offset. The ECB is expected to hold policy steady until June, with Germany's business outlook deteriorating more than expected and the war-driven energy crisis complicating any straightforward easing trajectory. Forward electricity prices for the summer in major EU markets remain elevated year-on-year despite strong solar output and improved nuclear availability, as high natural gas costs keep a floor under power prices.

Europe's airline sector faces a particularly acute challenge. Jet fuel equivalent to $196 per barrel has prompted Lufthansa to cancel 20,000 short-haul flights, KLM to eliminate 80 return services from Amsterdam, and EasyJet to warn of a potential first-half loss. Persian Gulf refineries that normally supply roughly 20% of EU jet fuel remain effectively cut off behind the Strait of Hormuz, and Asian refineries — constrained by reduced crude supply — have their own product shortages. India's Reliance has prioritized domestic LPG output over jet fuel exports, removing one alternative source. Shell's Pernis refinery in the Netherlands is maximizing jet fuel production, and Nigeria's Dangote refinery has raised shipments to Europe to record levels, but these measures appear insufficient to offset summer demand.

The EU finalized disbursement of its €90 billion loan to Ukraine after Hungary lifted its veto, and European leaders signaled preliminary conditions are in place to begin Ukraine's formal EU membership process, though a committed timeline remains absent. Europe's ban on Russian LNG imports took effect Saturday, a poorly timed development given current energy market stress. SAP reported strong cloud revenue growth, gaining 7.3% in Frankfurt, while Siemens Energy rose 4.9% after raising its annual outlook on demand for gas turbines and grid infrastructure.
