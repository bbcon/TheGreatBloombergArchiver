Two UK parliamentary by-elections in Gorton and Denton drew significant attention as a test of Prime Minister Keir Starmer's Labour government. What might otherwise have been a safe Labour hold appears to have become a three-way contest among Labour, Reform UK, and the Greens — a result that, if confirmed, would expose the breadth of political dissatisfaction with the governing party from both its right and left flanks. With November's general election still distant, the immediate consequence is reputational rather than structural, but the signal for Labour's governing coalition is uncomfortable.

European credit markets presented a more constructive picture. US borrowers have been issuing record volumes of "reverse Yankee" bonds denominated in euros, attracted by yields approximately 170 basis points below comparable US-denominated issuance. The spread differential reflects both the ECB's more accommodative stance relative to the Fed and residual demand for euro-denominated paper, and represents a tangible capital flow toward European debt markets. The ECB itself reported another operating loss, though President Christine Lagarde noted the institution is monitoring for productivity gains from AI while remaining attentive to any associated job displacement.

Italy's industry minister escalated pressure on the EU's Emissions Trading System, calling it "ineffective and harmful" and urging the European Commission to suspend the program pending a thorough overhaul. The intervention follows an 8% decline in European carbon prices after Chancellor Merz indicated openness to revising or delaying the program, and aligns with French President Macron's acknowledgment that the ETS "is not functioning well for some countries." The Commission is due next week to unveil a "Made in Europe" industrial act, with the latest draft setting strict criteria favoring European firms in public procurement while requiring some foreign competitors to share technology and hire locally — a meaningful shift from the free-trade framework that has governed EU industrial policy for decades.
