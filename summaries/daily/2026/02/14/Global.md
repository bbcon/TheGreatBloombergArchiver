**Global Macro Daily — February 14, 2026**

**The Electricity Constraint Tightens.** The IEA's latest annual power report frames the coming half-decade as the "Age of Electricity," forecasting global electricity demand growth roughly 50% faster than the prior decade and more than double the pace of overall energy demand. Critically, electricity consumption is now expected to outpace GDP growth — a structural break from the historical relationship that carries meaningful implications for capex cycles, utility regulation, and inflation dynamics. Goldman Sachs economists Manuel Abecasis and Hongcen Wei project data centers will account for 40% of incremental US power demand through 2030, with electricity-price inflation holding near 6% through 2027. That print, if realized, would complicate the Fed's disinflation narrative and sits awkwardly against the cost-of-living politics shaping November's US midterms.

**Divergent National Responses.** China continues to add generating capacity at a pace that materially exceeds the US build-out, reinforcing what Elon Musk and others have flagged as a structural disadvantage in the AI infrastructure race. The Trump administration's response — pushing hyperscalers to internalize power-generation capex alongside data-center investment — addresses the optics but, per the Goldman analysis, the inflation outcome hinges critically on cost allocation between commercial and residential ratepayers. In Europe, Chancellor Merz signaled openness this week to reworking the EU's flagship carbon-market framework if it continues to weigh on industrial competitiveness, a notable softening reflecting persistent concerns over German deindustrialization. Europe's lag in the AI buildout, while a competitive liability, has incidentally reduced strain on its grid — a modest offset to an otherwise unfavorable position. The substitution of US LNG for Russian gas, meanwhile, has traded one form of geopolitical energy dependence for another, with reliability concerns around Washington now part of the European calculus.
