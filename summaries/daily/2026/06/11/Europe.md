**ECB Policy Pivot**

The ECB's anticipated 25-basis-point hike represents a meaningful shift after more than two years on hold. Policymakers concluded that persistent oil-price elevation — now exceeding three months — combined with evidence that price pressures are broadening beyond energy, made further inaction untenable. Market expectations are building for additional tightening this year, reflecting the view that a single move will be insufficient if the Iran conflict and Hormuz disruption continue. This puts the ECB in an awkward position: tightening into a growth environment already pressured by elevated energy costs risks amplifying the economic drag from the war.

**UK and Corporate Developments**

Chancellor Reeves' move to address double-taxation on US LLC members signals a deliberate strategy to attract capital from the United States amid bilateral trade and investment repositioning. The UK housing market showed tentative signs of stabilization per agent surveys, though the macro backdrop of elevated rates and persistently high services inflation remains challenging. On the corporate side, Frasers Group's Hugo Boss bid continues Mike Ashley's acquisition strategy in European fashion; at current terms the offer implies a modest premium that markets have received constructively.
