In Pope Leo XIV, Donald Trump has met his match 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/pope-leo-vows-to-continue-iran-war-critiques-after-trump-attacks?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
.

The mild-mannered Midwestern, known affectionately as Father Bob in his native 
Chicago, was an unlikely foe and, possibly, a reluctant one.

Unlike his combative predecessor Francis, who was overtly political in 
discussing climate change and migration in a way that was relished by 
progressives, this was not a pontiffitching for a fight 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/trump-attacks-pope-for-iran-war-immigration-agenda-criticism?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
. Indeed, he was dubbed the “silent pope” in Rome.

That’s changed.

On Saturday evening, as peace talks between the US and Iran were headed to an 
impasse, Leo denounced the “delusion of omnipotence.”
US Vice President JD Vance with Pope Leo.Photographer: Vatican 
Media/Corbis/Getty Images
“Enough of the idolatry of self and money!” he said at a prayer service. 
“Enough of the display of power! Enough of war!” He wasn’t yet naming names, 
but in denouncing Trump’s intervention in the Middle East, Leo found his voice.

This is not a match of equals.

Iran’s asymmetric warfare, which military supremacy cannot vanquish, has 
frustrated Trump.

Leo’s moral pedigree is equally vexing. Trump’s Truth Social putdowns aren’t 
effective against the spiritual leader ofmore than 1.4 billion Catholics 
worldwide 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-14/trump-s-dispute-with-pope-adds-frisson-to-historic-algeria-visit?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
 — even when the pope occupies a mere 49 hectares of admittedly prime land.

Indeed, they might be counterproductive. For Italy’s Giorgia Meloni, it 
provided the pretext she neededto break with Trump 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-14/trump-slams-meloni-deepening-fallout-from-his-pope-attack?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
. His increasingly erratic behavior has prompted more foreign leaders to 
distance themselves from a president they once rushed to appease.

In the US, there are upcoming midterms to consider. The first American pope is 
respected by many conservative voters whom Trump can’t afford to alienate. 
Further outrage at Trump’s post depicting himself as Jesus Christ prompteda 
rare retreat 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/trump-deletes-social-media-post-seemingly-depicting-him-as-jesus?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
 as he took it down.

Both Vice President JD Vance and Secretary of State Marco Rubio are Catholics 
and in the line of Republican succession.

In a Fox News interview, Vance sided with his boss 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-15/vance-says-pope-should-be-careful-with-words-after-iran-critique?cmpid=BBD041526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260415>
. Time will tell if that hurts him. —Flavia Krause-Jackson 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ACWlz_feWqg/flavia-krausejackson>
