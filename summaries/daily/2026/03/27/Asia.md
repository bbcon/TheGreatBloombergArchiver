China's economic and geopolitical positioning was the dominant theme across Asia this week. The White House confirmed a Trump-Xi summit for May 14-15 in Beijing, providing the clearest diplomatic signal yet that the US sees the Iran conflict winding down — though this outcome remains far from assured. Beijing, characteristically, has not confirmed the dates, preserving flexibility should conditions shift. China simultaneously launched retaliatory trade investigations into US supply chain and renewable energy practices, staking out negotiating leverage ahead of the summit rather than signaling a détente.

The economic read on China is mixed. Container throughput at Chinese ports remained firm in the first three weeks of March, up over 6% year-on-year, suggesting trade disruption from the war has not yet materialized at scale. However, ground-level conditions in export hubs like Yiwu are deteriorating, with manufacturers facing rising input costs for oil-linked materials and Middle Eastern buyers pulling back on orders over shipping uncertainty. A factory manager in Jiangsu described the environment as the most difficult in over a decade, and the risk of demand-driven overcapacity translating into domestic price wars appears real if the conflict persists.

The energy dislocation is reshaping asset prices regionally. BYD's Hong Kong-listed shares gained 12% in March, on track for their best month in over a year, as surging fuel prices drive consumers toward electric vehicles across Southeast Asia. Queues at EV dealerships in the Philippines and Indonesia have been reported. Japan, however, faces a more complex dynamic: rising oil prices combined with yen weakness are stoking stagflation concerns, prompting the Finance Minister to flag potential currency intervention. Japanese super-long bond yields also rose amid the uncertainty. In the Philippines, President Marcos is reportedly considering setting aside South China Sea territorial disputes with Beijing to explore cooperative oil exploration — a striking illustration of how energy stress is reshaping regional geopolitical alignments.
