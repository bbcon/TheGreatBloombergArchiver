Bitcoin down

For Bitcoin, the worst may be yet to come.

The largest cryptocurrency recovered some ground after slumping 16% in the 
seven days through Sunday, its steepest weekly fall since the bankruptcy of Sam 
Bankman-FriedтАЩs FTX triggered a 23% rout in November 2022.

Analysts are warning that the tokenтАЩs modest rebound may prove short-lived 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-09/bitcoin-s-worst-week-since-ftx-crash-may-signal-more-pain-ahead?cmpid=061026_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260610&utm_content=5350>
 as structural frailties are exposed. Investors are fleeing Bitcoin ETFs, 
technical indicators have weakened and interest-rate expectations have shifted.

тАЬI believe there is further downside,тАЭ said Griffin Ardern of Primal Fund. тАЬWe 
are still some way off a proper bottom.тАЭ

The selloff last week was partly attributable to Michael SaylorтАЩs 
Bitcoin-buying company Strategy divesting a tiny portion of its holdings. It 
bought on Monday, but confidence may not be easily restored.

The shift in interest-rate expectations is also part of the problem, as the 
prospect of higher borrowing costs draws capital away from speculative assets 
like crypto. The US-Iran war and strong US jobs data have seen markets move 
from expecting rate cuts to pricing in the chance of increases.
