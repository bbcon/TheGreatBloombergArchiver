The record-breaking advance in US stocks has many of the hallmarks of an 
unloved rally

The S&P 500 is up almost 9% in April, closing above 7,000 for the first time

Investors see plenty of red flags:

 * Only about half of S&P 500 members traded above their 50-day averages when 
the index set a record last week. ThatтАЩs well below the levels seen when the 
benchmark last hit an all-time peak in January.
 * Trading volume in S&P 500 stocks this month has been 11% below the 
six-month average, while it was 9.6% above that level in March. To JC OтАЩHara at 
Roth Capital Partners, that indicates investors had more conviction in the 
declines than they do in the recovery.
 * A measure of investor sentiment compiled by Barclays has declined even as 
stocks have surged. There have only been five other instances in the past 20 
years where the indicator slipped during a sharp recovery.
