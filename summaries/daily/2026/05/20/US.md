# US Daily Briefing — May 20, 2026

**Bonds Reprice the Long End; Equities Diverge.** The 30-year Treasury yield rose to 5.19% on Tuesday, the highest since June 2007, as persistent oil-driven inflation pressures from the Hormuz closure continued to push the term premium higher. Bloomberg's gauge of average global sovereign yields is now at its highest since July 2008. The selloff is increasingly bifurcating the equity market: large-cap AI names remain bid, while options activity in the Russell 2000 shows the first meaningful pickup in hedging demand in six weeks, reflecting concern that smaller, rate-sensitive companies will absorb the brunt of tighter financial conditions. Futures markets are now pricing roughly a three-in-four probability of a Fed hike by December — a notable shift for a committee nominally still in a cutting cycle. Barclays' Ajay Rajadhyaksha argued that, absent a reopening of Hormuz, only weaker macro data is likely to arrest the move in yields.

**Fed Posture and the Housing Channel.** Fed official Anna Paulson reiterated a preference for holding rates steady, with FOMC minutes due later in the session. TD Securities now expects 30-year mortgage rates to end the year near 6.15%, leaving housing affordability at levels not sustained since the 1980s and pointing to continued stagnation in existing sales and new construction. HSBC's base case now contemplates a 2026 Fed hike as a tail risk that can no longer be dismissed.

**Nvidia and the AI Capex Question.** Nvidia reports after the close, with consensus looking for 79% revenue growth to $78.9 billion. Hyperscaler capex projections for 2026 and 2027 have been revised to $812 billion and $968 billion respectively — up 89% year-to-date among the top four buyers. Equity holders have rewarded the spending; credit markets have not, with hyperscaler IG spreads now 25% wider than the broader index, having been 15% tighter a year ago. The Shiller CAPE stands at 39.6, a level exceeded only during the late stages of the dot-com bubble.

**Trade and Corporate.** The EU finalized the text of its US trade deal, clearing a procedural hurdle ahead of the July 4 tariff deadline; the pact caps EU export tariffs at 15% in exchange for removal of EU duties on US industrial goods. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are reported to lead the SpaceX IPO, with the filing potentially landing today. The Pentagon confirmed a permanent reduction of roughly 4,000 US troops from Europe.
