The collapse of Market Financial Solutions in London has left Wall Street banks
scrambling to recoup <https://bloom.bg/46ZyGu2> billions. Barclays has about 
£600 million tied up in the lender and Atlas roughly £400 million. Jefferies’ 
exposure is about £100 million, a person familiar said.

The Greens won a by-election in Manchester <https://bloom.bg/3ZZhQrw> and 
Reform UK received the second-highest number of votes, underscoring the threats 
from the left and right to UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer. Local plumber Hannah 
Spencer secured 40.7% of the vote in Gorton and Denton, Reform’s Matt Goodwin 
got 28.7% and Labour’s Angeliki Stogia received 25.4%.

Donald Trump has put the fate of two of the world’s thorniest issues 
<https://bloom.bg/4ryeXtI>—the war in Ukraine and a standoff with Iran—in the 
hands of son-in-law Jared Kushner and friend Steve Witkoff. The pair’s ability 
to “speak with authority” on what the president wants is positive, said Kurt 
Volker, who was Trump’s first term special envoy for Ukraine. “On the negative 
side, they don’t have a deep understanding of the issues and the sensitivities.”

Jack Dorsey’s Block is set to cut 4,000 employees—nearly half its workforce 
<https://bloom.bg/4rIHHjA>—as the American financial technology firm bets on AI 
to lift productivity. The company, parent of Square and Cash App, surged as 
much as 27% in extended trading.

 Deep Dive: Russia’s Pain 


Russia's President Vladimir Putin in the Nizhny Novgorod Oblast in September. 
Photographer: Mikhail Metzel/Getty Images 
As Russia’s war economy strains under growing pressure from sanctions 
<https://bloom.bg/4rFpa7T> and slumping revenue, even firms in regions that 
have benefited from high military spending are feeling the pain. They’re now 
turning to officials for help.

 * In a letter to a top official earlier this month, companies in Nizhny 
Novgorod—an industrial city located east of Moscow—highlighted sharp falls in 
investment, profit, orders and production over the past year. 
 * The businesses urged officials to raise the concerns with President 
Vladimir Putin’s government in Moscow, appealing for renewed investments, the 
return of preferential loan rates and faster settlement of invoices. 
 * Giant state enterprises, such as the United Shipbuilding Corporation and 
Roscosmos, were among the main culprits of overdue payments, they said.
