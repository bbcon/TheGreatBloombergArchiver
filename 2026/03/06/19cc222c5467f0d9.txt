 Deep Dive: Childless City 


The City of London. Photographer: Jose Sarmento Matos/Bloomberg 
London risks becoming a childless city as young families flock away from the 
expensive capital <https://bloom.bg/4rkkRxN>, prompting some schools to shut 
and fertility rates to crash, according to the London Assembly.

 * The city’s child population is collapsing faster than anywhere in Britain, 
a report by the group said. 
 * In the decade through 2023 the number of residents under 10 years of age 
fell by 99,100 as families moved out and fertility rates crashed—even while the 
capital’s overall population increased by 506,000. 
 * The city’s fertility rate has fallen below 1.2 births per woman. That 
compares with an England and Wales average of 1.41—well below the 2.1 level 
needed, in economists’ terms, for the population to maintain itself without 
immigration.

 Opinion 



The term “energy crisis” is being used too loosely: while the attack on Iran 
is bad news for the global economy, it hasn’t produced anything like the 
extreme, broad and long-lastingprice shock of 2021-22 <https://bloom.bg/4cwDnPQ>
, Javier Blas writes. Oil and LNG prices have risen, but most power markets 
remain far below past peaks, and this doesn’t yet resemble a true crisis.
