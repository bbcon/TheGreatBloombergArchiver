**Global Daily Briefing — January 30, 2026**

**Trump Names Warsh to Succeed Powell at the Fed.** President Trump selected Kevin Warsh as his nominee to chair the Federal Reserve following the May expiration of Jerome Powell's term. The choice is notable for its tension with prior expectations: Trump has consistently pressed for lower rates, yet Warsh is broadly viewed on Wall Street as a relative hawk, having criticized accommodative policy during and after his prior tenure as a Fed governor. Warsh has publicly aligned himself with the President's monetary policy views, though institutional constraints and FOMC composition suggest any pivot would be gradual rather than immediate. Markets responded with a risk-off tone: US equities declined, gold and silver sold off sharply amid broader volatility in precious metals, and the reaction was compounded by a hotter-than-expected PPI print. The juxtaposition — a hawkish nominee against a White House demanding easier policy — leaves the trajectory of US monetary policy unusually difficult to forecast, with implications for global rate differentials, dollar positioning, and the relative attractiveness of duration outside the US. Senate confirmation is expected given the Republican majority, but the substantive question of how Warsh balances political pressure against the Fed's institutional preferences remains open.

**Geopolitical Risk Premium Persists.** Renewed friction over Trump's demand for US control of Greenland has reactivated the "Sell America" trade that surfaced periodically through 2025, contributing to defensive positioning across portfolios. Gold's run to record levels earlier in the week reflected this safe-haven bid, though today's sharp reversal in metals illustrates how crowded the trade had become. Among wealth managers surveyed by Bloomberg, the response to the current environment has tilted toward value equities and selective international exposure, with Japanese equities cited favorably — consistent with a view that US exceptionalism is being repriced at the margin.
