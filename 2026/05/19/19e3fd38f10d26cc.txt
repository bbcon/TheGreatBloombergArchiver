Here’s why bonds are selling off

Bonds are buckling around the world, propelling borrowing costs to multi-year 
highs. The causes are rooted in geopolitical, demographic and technology trends 
that are hitting most everywhere, all at once.

Here are some of the reasons investors are selling sovereign debt 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/reasons-why-investors-are-selling-government-bonds-from-uk-to-us?cmpid=051926_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4594>
:

 * War: Inflation was already stubbornly high, and it’s poised to surge anew 
after the Middle East war pushed Brent crude prices to $110 a barrel.
 * AI: Tech companies are spending big on the technology, driving up chip 
prices while making electronics, cars and other products more expensive.
 * Debt: Politicians are promising to spend more or tax less and pay the cost 
by issuing debt, prompting investors to demand higher yields.
 * Rates: Some investors are betting that interest rates will need to go up 
and stay higher because central banks are sleeping on inflation risks.
 * Economy: The US has shown continued economic momentum that could push 
prices higher, with data pointing to a strong job market.
 * The big picture: Long-term trends such as aging populations and the green 
transition may be pushing inflation risks and yields higher.
The selloff is handing investors losses in almost every major bond market, 
though some are starting to beenticed by US yields at multi-year highs 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/us-yields-flirting-with-2007-highs-entice-and-divide-investors?cmpid=051926_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4594>

‘Bull capitulation’

For contrarians, they’re ominous signs: Fund managers recently piled into 
stocks at the fastest pace on record 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/fund-managers-boost-stock-allocations-by-a-record-in-bofa-poll?cmpid=051926_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4594>
, and their cash levels have seen the biggest monthly drop since February 2024.

The findings, from Bank of America’s latest survey of professional investors, 
are a sign that a market pullback may be looming.The thinking goes that when 
there’s that much optimism, all the would-be bulls are already fully invested, 
leaving little fuel for further gains.

Bank of America strategist Michael Hartnett reiterated his view from last week 
that “bull capitulation” in the equity market is almost complete. He has 
identified early June as a period when stocks will be “ripe for profit-taking,” 
with bond yields set to “determine degree of pullback.” —Levin Stamm 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AW6xnzP2dmo/levin-stamm>
