Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter Grant Smith looks at the divisions between Europe and the US at the 
IEA summit in Paris.

President Donald Trump <https://www.bloomberg.com/profile/person/1252249>’s 
shake-up of major institutions from NATO to the United Nations has reached the 
world of energy.

At the normally sedate gathering of the International Energy Agency’s member 
nations in Paris this week, US Energy Secretary Chris Wrightassailed the 
organization 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-18/us-renews-threat-to-withdraw-from-iea-over-climate-advocacy?cmpid=BBBXT022026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260220>
 over its policy direction.

The European-led fight against climate change has squandered $10 trillion in a 
decade, Wright said. Emissions policies have enfeebled the continent’s economic 
growth, shrunk its access to energy and left its people more vulnerable to the 
predations of Russia, he said.

If the IEA doesn’t ditch dreams of achieving net zero carbon emissions and 
getting energy exclusively from renewable sources, Washington will head for the 
exit, Wright warned.
Chris Wright, US energy secretary, in Washington on Jan. 29.Photographer: 
Aaron Schwartz/CNP/Bloomberg
The IEA was co-created by the US in the 1970s, with the aim of protecting 
major industrialized consumers from supply shocks following the Arab oil 
embargo. It advises its members on energy policy and helps to coordinate the 
release of fuel stockpiles during emergencies.

This decades-long cooperation is now deeply fractured, with the IEA failing to 
agree 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-19/iea-meeting-ends-without-unity-as-us-pushes-to-scrap-net-zero?cmpid=BBBXT022026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260220>
 on the usual joint ministerial statement at the two-day conference. Instead, 
it released a summary of the discussions compiled by this year’s chairing 
nation, the Netherlands.

Behind closed doors, some officials said the American tone was more emollient. 
IEA members could at least agree to cooperate on critical minerals and — with 
geopolitical risks escalating in the Middle East — the need for security of 
energy supplies.

Wright publicly praised IEA Executive Director Fatih Birol for showing some 
flexibility, after the agencysoftened its predictions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2025-11-12/the-iea-isn-t-so-sure-about-peak-oil-anymore-energy-daily?cmpid=BBBXT022026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260220>
 last year for an imminent plateau in oil consumption.

Whether the schism has further ramifications has yet to be resolved.

Republican lawmakers have sought to choke off funding for the agency, fanning 
speculation about Birol’s tenure as executive director. But so far no major 
changes have come to pass.

One test will be whether this year’s long-term demand outlook from the agency 
includes any explicit scenarios for a net zero pathway. Another will be the 
next round of UN climate talks, scheduled for November in the Ethiopian capital 
of Addis Ababa.

For all his harsh words, Wright said the US wanted to reform rather than 
abandon the IEA.

—Grant Smith, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

gas vehicles are outstripping battery cars, according to BloombergNEF. Indian 
customers bought nearly 957,000 CNG vehicles in 2025, up 24% from the year 
before.
