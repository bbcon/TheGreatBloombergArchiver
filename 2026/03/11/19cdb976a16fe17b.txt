Good morning. The International Energy Agency reportedly proposes a massive 
release of oil reserves. Tube commuters should brace for more disruptions later 
this month. And British wildlife is replacing Winston Churchill and Jane Austen 
on banknotes.Listen to the day’s top stories <https://bloom.bg/4nmEQLf>.

— Victoria Batchelor



The International Energy Agency proposed the largest ever release 
<https://bloom.bg/4s4bgvZ> of oil reserves, according to the Wall Street 
Journal, as governments seek to contain a spike in energy prices driven by the 
war in the Middle East. Countries are expected to make a decision today. Stocks 
extended their gains and oil held below $90 a barrel after the reportboosted 
market confidence <https://bloom.bg/4umKsJ9>.

European Central Bank President Christine Lagarde said the central bank will 
ensure the conflict in Iran doesn’t inflict thesame inflation pain on the euro 
zone <https://bloom.bg/4s4gJmN> as Russia’s invasion of Ukraine did.

AI roundup: China moved to restrict state firms and government agencies from 
running OpenClaw AI apps <https://bloom.bg/4syeCHr> on office computers, people 
familiar said.Oracle shares surged <https://bloom.bg/47p2IYk> in extended

German automakers are feeling the heat in China, with Porsche’s sales slumping 
<https://bloom.bg/4sf2FGQ> there as local consumers increasingly turn to a new 
breed of whisper-quiet, home-grown electric cars. Mercedes-Benz, meanwhile,
unveiled a luxury electric van <https://bloom.bg/40lcYx1> aimed at bolstering 
sales in the nation, where fast-moving local manufacturers are encroaching on 
its premium stronghold.

London commuters will face disruption with more strikes on the city’s 
underground <https://bloom.bg/4s4YAoU> train network planned from later this 
month. Industrial action will take place across 12 days on the Tube in 
opposition to Transport for London’s plan to allow drivers to volunteer to work 
a four-day week, the RMT said.

The football club that bans talk about winning <https://bloom.bg/4b2yfBI> is 
winning. A lot.

 * Bodo Glimt has made it to the last 16 of the Champions League. The team 
from the Norwegian Arctic fishing port has beaten giants Inter Milan, Atletico 
Madrid and Manchester City already this season. 
 * And tonight, it will host Sporting Lisbon,  the club that produced 
Cristiano Ronaldo <https://bloom.bg/3N91Q3l>, at its 8,200-capacity stadium 
more than 2,000 miles north of the Portuguese capital. 
 * This match is more than another lucrative chapter in Bodo’s 
David-against-Goliath tale. 
 * It’s a chance to showcase a transformation underpinned by a fighter pilot 
turned mental-health coach who says that winning isn’t the point.
