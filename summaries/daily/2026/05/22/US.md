# US Daily Briefing — May 22, 2026

**Markets: AI Concentration Sustains the Rally as Bond Markets Reprice Inflation Risk**

US equity futures pointed modestly higher overnight, with the S&P 500 on track for an eighth consecutive weekly gain — the longest streak since 2023. The breadth picture, however, remains notably narrow: an equal-weighted version of the S&P 500 is unchanged since late February, while the cap-weighted benchmark has risen 8.2% since the start of the US-Israel war against Iran. Ten technology names — led by Nvidia, Amazon, Apple, Alphabet and Broadcom — account for the entirety of the index's gain over that period. Workday rose roughly 11% premarket on results that eased concerns about AI-driven disruption to enterprise software, while Take-Two and Zoom also gained. US-listed shares of Futu and Up Fintech fell more than 35% on China's cross-border brokerage crackdown.

Behind the equity advance, the longer end of the Treasury curve continues to signal discomfort. G7 long yields touched a two-decade high this week as investors demand greater compensation for inflation risk tied to the Strait of Hormuz disruption. Pimco's Dan Ivascyn noted that prior energy shocks have historically preceded recessions, a view that, if it gains traction, points to lower equities, wider credit spreads and eventually lower yields. Rapidan warned that a Hormuz disruption persisting through August could push Brent toward $130 and produce a demand shock comparable to 2008.

**Fed: Warsh Sworn in as Chair**

Kevin Warsh takes the Fed chair today, with markets focused less on imminent rate action than on potential changes to the communications framework. ABN Amro analysts suggest the SEP and press conference cadence could be scaled back within 6–12 months, and that Warsh may commission an external review of the Fed's framework — a step pointedly not taken after the post-Covid inflation overshoot.

**Fiscal and Political**

Senate Republicans rebuffed Trump's $1.8 billion request for a "weaponization" account to compensate political allies, a notable intra-party setback. Separately, Trump reversed course and announced 5,000 additional US troops for Poland.

US markets are closed Monday for Memorial Day.
