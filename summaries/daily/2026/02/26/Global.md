The dominant global risk theme remains the US-Iran nuclear standoff. A third round of Geneva talks began on February 26, with President Trump's March 1-6 diplomatic window — and his threat of military action — framing the negotiations. Trump's assertion that the US would "easily" prevail in any armed confrontation sits in tension with satellite imagery showing Iranian engineers actively reinforcing and concealing nuclear and missile sites damaged during last June's conflict, hardening tunnels and burying facilities against future strikes. The IAEA reports Iran has accumulated enough highly enriched uranium for roughly a dozen nuclear devices, though its precise whereabouts have not been independently verified in over eight months. Critically, US air power can destroy physical infrastructure but cannot eliminate scientific knowledge, stockpiled material, or political will — a constraint that military planners and energy markets are pricing accordingly.

Oil supertanker rates have surged to levels not seen since early 2020, with very large crude carrier day-rates on the Middle East-to-China benchmark route exceeding $200,000 — a rise of more than 600% year-to-date. Two factors are driving the spike: elevated volumes of oil at sea, partly reflecting sanctioned Russian crude struggling to find buyers alongside increased Saudi exports, and a highly concentrated market position being built by South Korean shipowner Sinokor Group, which is acquiring enough tonnage to potentially control roughly 40% of the global VLCC fleet. The resulting cost pressure is already affecting crude price differentials, with West African barrels being offered at deeper discounts to Asian buyers. Any Strait of Hormuz disruption — through which approximately 20% of global oil flows — would layer a supply shock onto an already tight tanker market.

Global debt reached $348 trillion at end-2025 per the Institute of International Finance, a fresh record driven by a $29 trillion annual increase — the fastest accumulation since the pandemic. The figure underscores the fiscal vulnerability of sovereign borrowers in an environment of persistently elevated interest rates. Russia's war in Ukraine entered its fifth year with the battlefield broadly stalemated, Putin facing growing economic strain, and US diplomacy increasingly focused on arranging a leaders-level summit between Trump and Zelenskyy, with Geneva talks between US and Ukrainian officials on February 26 representing the next procedural step.
