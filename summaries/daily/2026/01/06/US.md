**US Daily Briefing — January 6, 2026**

**Wall Street's 2026 Outlook: Growth With Caveats**

The consensus emerging from sell-side and asset manager outlooks for 2026 — synthesized across more than 60 institutions including Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan Wealth Management, and the BlackRock Investment Institute — points to modest optimism tempered by an unusual set of macro tensions. The dominant theme is that continued AI-related capital expenditure, combined with supportive government policy, is extending the cycle at a stage when growth would typically be moderating. This dynamic underpins constructive equity views, though forecasters broadly caution that valuations leave limited margin for execution missteps in the AI complex.

The corollary, however, is that inflation is unlikely to return cleanly to target. A sustained capex impulse, fiscal support, and tight labor conditions are expected to keep price pressures sticky through the year, which in turn implies that the Federal Reserve may have less scope to deliver the easing currently embedded in front-end pricing. This sets up a recurring source of volatility: if growth and inflation data continue to surprise to the upside, market expectations for policy accommodation will likely need to be repriced. The asymmetry favors caution on duration positioning.

On the dollar, the consensus leans toward continued depreciation, reflecting a combination of narrowing rate differentials, fiscal trajectory concerns, and ongoing reserve diversification. The view is not uniform, and the path will depend heavily on the relative pace of Fed easing versus other G10 central banks. Private assets, meanwhile, are expected to continue gaining share of institutional allocations, though questions around liquidity, valuation marks, and vintage performance remain unresolved.

**Assessment**

The setup for 2026 contains genuine internal tensions — AI-driven growth optimism sitting alongside sticky inflation and a softer dollar call. Investors should expect these narratives to compete for primacy depending on the data flow, with the first major test arriving with January CPI and the next FOMC meeting. Otherwise, US-specific newsflow today is light.
