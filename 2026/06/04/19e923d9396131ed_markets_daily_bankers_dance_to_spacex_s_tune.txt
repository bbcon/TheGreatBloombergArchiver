Gilt buyers wanted

Britain could use a few loyal, long-term buyers for its government bonds 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-04/uk-urged-to-tap-2-trillion-pot-and-bring-gilts-to-the-masses?cmpid=060426_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260604&utm_content=5173>
. Executives at some of the UK’s biggest financial institutions think they may 
be hiding in plain sight: ordinary savers.

Their argument is straightforward. UK households are sitting on £2 trillion in 
deposits.Government debt offers annual returns of 4% to 5.5%, compared to the 
1.7% average rate on basic savings accounts. Japan and Italy use cartoons and 
catchy jingles to sell bonds to consumers, and proponents say, so too can the 
UK.

Brits may be difficult to convince. The gilts market is on track for its third 
loss in five years. The retail crowd owns less than 4% of outstanding bonds, 
Bank of England analysts have found.

Proponents say low participation rates make households an obvious target for 
the government, which borrows to cover the gap between its spending and tax 
revenue. That’s especially true as traditional domestic buyers such as pension 
funds pull back from the market, leaving the UK increasingly reliant on hedge 
funds and overseas investors.

“There is an untapped opportunity for the government,” said Michael Smith at 
Winterflood Securities. “In five to 10 years time, gilts could be as normal to 
invest in as just leaving your money in the bank.” —Alice Atkins 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AUJRzAw42ww/alice-atkins?cmpid=060426_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260604&utm_content=5173>

Word from Wall Street

“All great technology changes produce bubbles. Nobody can get it exactly 
right. You have to either spend a ton of money to capture your market share and 
don’t worry about whether it’s too much or not, or you don’t spend enough money 
and you lose your market share.”

Ray Dalio

Founder, Bridgewater Associates
