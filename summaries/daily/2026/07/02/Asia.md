**Semiconductor Selloff**
The proximate trigger for Asia's equity rout was Meta Platforms' announcement of a cloud infrastructure business that would sell AI computing capacity externally, raising concerns about overinvestment and potential overcapacity across the AI stack. The selloff was amplified by a structural market dynamic: the CSOP SK Hynix 2x leveraged ETF in Hong Kong has expanded to $13 billion — the largest of its kind globally — and analysts argue its scale is now mechanically magnifying intraday swings in both SK Hynix and the broader Kospi. South Korea's index had doubled in six months, leaving it acutely vulnerable to any sentiment reversal.

**China Equities and Capital Flows**
Chinese equities extended their decline, with the tech-heavy ChiNext falling 5.7% on Thursday. The offshore Hang Seng China Enterprises Index entered technical bear market territory last week after falling more than 20% from October highs. Analysts at Gavekal Research attribute part of the pressure to Beijing's late-May crackdown on Hong Kong-based online brokers, interpreted as an effort to limit capital outflows as domestic interest rates grind lower. Despite the selloff, BNY data indicate institutional investors continue to accumulate Chinese equities on valuation grounds, though new inflows appear insufficient to offset mark-to-market losses on existing holdings.
