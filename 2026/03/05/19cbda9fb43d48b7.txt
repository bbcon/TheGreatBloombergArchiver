The nation’s latest growth target suggests its economy is running out of steam.

China’s meteoric economic rise is running out of steam. That has implications 
for governments the world over, not least Beijing.

For a country long obsessed with setting and meeting targets, the growth goal 
just announced 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/china-softens-gdp-goal-to-range-of-4-5-to-5-as-growth-slows?cmpid=BBD030526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260305>
 is modest.

The target range of 4.5% to 5% is the lowest since 1991, when China accounted 
for less than 2% of global economic output. Now it’s closer to 10%.

It’s a reality that will hang over Xi Jinping’s talks with Donald Trump this 
month.

For decades, booming exports and high investment fueled rapid expansion, but 
also left the economy lopsided with strong supply and weak domestic demand.

Now the world’s second-largest economy is slowing and must find new drivers of 
growth, a task Premier Li Qiang described today as “formidable.”

The humble growth goal is perhaps reasonable given geopolitical tensions 
fueled by Trump, from trade conflicts to real war against Iran which add to 
volatility despite Beijing’s appeals for calm.

Net exports accounted for roughly a third of China’s 5% growth last year, the 
largest contribution in almost three decades. That’s a bone of contention for 
those countries being flooded with Chinese goods displaced from the US as a 
result of Trump’s tariffs.

For Xi, the slowdown shows the limits to the Communist Party’s power. 
Officials can’tforce people to spend 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/china-keeps-budget-deficit-target-at-record-high-to-spur-economy?cmpid=BBD030526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260305>
 and have few good options for reversing the property crash.

As the purse strings tighten and growth stalls, even military spending is being
pared back 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/china-lifts-defense-spending-by-7-after-big-surge-by-trump?cmpid=BBD030526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260305>
 in relative terms, in contrast to the budgets of others including neighbor 
Japan. With the US becoming ever more unpredictable comes a greater need for 
self-reliance in everything from tech to energy.

There is perhaps one silver lining. Xi’s willingness to grow slower takes the 
pressure off as new US tariffs loom. Going into his meeting with Trump, that 
gives him an even stronger hand. — Nectar Gan

The US-Israeli war on Iran entered a sixth day with the Islamic Republic 
pledging toescalate its retaliation

military and intelligence assets, while Arab states across the Persian Gulf 
reported interceptions of Iranian missiles and drones overnight and into today.

Satellite imagery shows that while Iran’s nuclear facilities have largely 
avoided attack, air strikes have damaged sites including hospitals, police 
stations, a court and densely populated areas of northern Tehran. Trump and his 
team offered a series of shifting objectives at the start of the campaign, and 
thelack of clarity 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-iran-map-trump-israel-strikes/?cmpid=BBD030526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260305>

achieve.

strategy of fighting the onslaught by overwhelming the Persian Gulf with drones 
and missiles to cause maximum chaos, exhaust the region’s defensive
