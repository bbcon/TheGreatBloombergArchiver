The second data point to remember is $288.4 billion.

ThatтАЩs the value of foreign direct investment the US received last year, 
according to data releasedlast week 
<https://apps.bea.gov/iTable/?reqid=62&step=6&isuri=1&tablelist=65&product=1&_gl=1*18jqux*_ga*MTgwMjQzNTcyNy4xNzYyOTYwMjk2*_ga_J4698JNNFT*czE3NzQ4Mjk4ODYkbzE3JGcxJHQxNzc0ODMxMDI4JGo0NyRsMCRoMA>
 by the Bureau of Economic Analysis. Which matters because the argument that 
Trump and his supporters make is that his tariffs are spurring a surge in 
investment into the US by companies eager to build new factories to avoid his 
tariffs. FDI data captures the actual flow of long-term investments foreigners 
make in an economy such as factories and other projects as well as the income 
they reinvest.

Modest FDI Decline

What happened in 2025 was actually a modest decline in flows from what the US 
received in 2024. In fact it was lower than any year in President Joe BidenтАЩs 
term and down significantly from the more than $480 billion in direct 
investment the US attracted in 2016, the year Trump was first elected.

ThereтАЩs also been an interesting change 
<https://globalbusiness.org/foreign-direct-investment-in-the-united-states-preliminary-4th-quarter-full-year-2025/>
 in the composition of that FDI since 2016, according to Jonathan Samford, 
president and CEO of the Global Business Alliance, which represents big foreign 
companies investing in the US.

In 2016 less than 20% of the FDI invested in the US was in the form of 
reinvested earnings, Samford says. In 2025 the equivalent figure was 70%. Which 
means less of the FDI flowing into the US came from new investors in 2025 than 
it did the year Trump was first elected. Though the more positive way to look 
at it perhaps is that existing investors were willing to reinvest their 
earnings last year.

There is one big caveat to the FDI data. The BEAтАЩs FDI data captures actual 
flows of capital. It doesnтАЩt reflect the many promises of future investment 
Trump elicited from other countries and companies in 2025.

To date, the White House lists <https://www.whitehouse.gov/investments/> $10.5 
trillion in investment promises from both domestic and foreign companies since 
Trump returned to office. Those may still come. Many are multi-year 
commitments. TheyтАЩre just not showing up in the actual economic data just yet.
