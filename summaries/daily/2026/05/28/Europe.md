**UK Labor Market and Cost Pressures**
The UK's youth unemployment surge is emerging as a structural concern rather than a cyclical one. The government-commissioned review cited a "whole-system failure" spanning entry-level job creation, mental health provision, and educational outcomes, with AI-driven reductions in graduate and junior roles compounding existing weaknesses. Separately, the Food and Drink Federation's survey showing more than 80% of producers planning price increases is a forward-looking inflation signal that the Bank of England will need to weigh carefully — particularly given that much of the cost pressure traces to the Middle East conflict and elevated energy prices rather than domestic demand.

**Political Economy: Hungary**
With the Champions League final taking place in Budapest this weekend, the occasion spotlights the political legacy of Viktor Orbán, who was ousted in Hungary's election last month. Football served as a vehicle for patronage and subsidy under Orbán's 16-year tenure, with clubs, academies, and stadiums channeling public funds toward political allies. The incoming government now faces the fiscal and institutional cost of unwinding a system built around a single leader — a transition with broader implications for Hungary's relationship with EU institutions and the structural integrity of its public finances.

**Monetary Policy**
The ECB made no new policy announcements in this period. However, the combination of sticky food and energy inflation driven by the Hormuz disruption, and softening demand in core eurozone economies, maintains the central bank in a difficult calibration exercise between supporting growth and containing embedded price expectations.
