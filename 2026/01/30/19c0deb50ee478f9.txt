Squirreling away cash looks pretty tempting right now.



 Geopolitical tensions are shaking up financial markets: President Donald 
Trump’s demand for US control over Greenland brought back the“Sell America” 
trade 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-20/wall-street-s-calm-shattered-by-greenland-and-japan-shocks>
 last week, and concerns about mega-cap tech have many investors on edge. 
Gold’s safe-haven appeal is alsofueling record prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-27/gold-price-record-how-debasement-trade-safe-haven-demand-is-fueling-rally>
. 


While the wealth managers we surveyed 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/features/how-to-invest-100k-q1-2026/> for the latest 
Where to Invest don’t recommend stockpiling cash, a risk-off mood did lead to a 
focus on value stocks for two of the four experts. Japanese stocks got a nod, 
as did companies that excel in anticipating consumer behavior. When asked how 
they’d spend $100,000 on a personal passion, responses ranged from a family 
trip to Antarctica to buying a thoroughbred horse.
