**China: Stalling Reflation**
June inflation data present a nuanced picture. While the base effect from weak 2025 kept the year-on-year PPI in positive territory, the month-on-month decline in factory prices signals that China's reflation momentum is fading. Core CPI at 1.0% points to softening domestic demand even as the trade surplus widens. A growing number of analysts estimate Q2 GDP growth may have slipped below the lower bound of the official 4.5%–5.0% target. The People's Bank of China's monetary policy committee flagged growing concern over divergence between external and domestic growth. The next meaningful policy window is the Politburo meeting in late July.

**Chipmaker Capital Markets**
A wave of semiconductor share issuance reflects both strong investor appetite and insider urgency to monetize gains before the cycle turns. Alongside SK Hynix's landmark US offering, China's CXMT — a memory chipmaker that Apple is reportedly seeking to source from — plans to raise at least 29.5 billion yuan via a domestic IPO. Bain Capital sold its entire Kioxia stake, logging returns exceeding 4,500% from the Japanese chipmaker's debut. Short interest is building: Michael Burry disclosed a short position in US chipmaker Micron, describing it as defining "cyclical like no other."
