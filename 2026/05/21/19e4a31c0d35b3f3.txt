Two of AsiaтАЩs top trading powers are showing resilience amid war in the Middle

In South Korea, exports adjusted for working-day differences climbed about 53% 
from a year earlier from May 1-20, according to customs office data released 
Thursday.Chip exports

computer-related products rose 306%.

One threat to that trend was labor unrest set to start as soon as today at

Meanwhile, JapanтАЩs trade balance unexpectedly stayed in surplus for a third 
consecutive month onsolid shipments abroad 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/japan-maintains-trade-surplus-as-energy-imports-plunge?cmpid=BBD052126_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4546>

month from a year earlier, while imports gained nearly 10%.

Japanese chip exports were up 44%. By country, exports to Europe climbed about 
27%, China advanced 16% and those to the US rose 10%.
