The US-Iran nuclear negotiations in Geneva concluded on a cautiously constructive note, with both sides describing the second round of talks as positive. Iran's delegation is expected to return with detailed proposals within two weeks, after which a third round would address outstanding sticking points. The White House, however, was explicit that military strikes remain on the table, with Vice President Vance noting that Iran has not yet acknowledged core US red lines. President Trump is deploying a second aircraft carrier to the region, maintaining coercive leverage even as diplomacy proceeds. The dynamic reflects a deliberate two-track strategy, though the timeline for resolution is compressed: Trump faces midterm elections and has signaled limited tolerance for protracted negotiations. Supreme Leader Khamenei retains the capacity to threaten regional escalation, and oil markets remain sensitive to any deterioration — traders are already pricing a risk premium given the proximity to the Strait of Hormuz.

On energy and trade, the divergence in US tariff outcomes sharpened. Port of Los Angeles export volumes fell 8% year-on-year in January, with soybean shipments to China down 80% and South American competitors — Argentina and Brazil — having locked in multi-month supply contracts that effectively displace US exporters for at least another agricultural cycle. Against that, Japan's $36 billion investment commitment represents a tangible win for the administration's bilateral deal framework. The US also renewed threats to withdraw from the International Energy Agency unless the organization reduces its climate advocacy focus. Meanwhile, Ethiopia's EV adoption offers a case study in policy-driven energy transition: a fossil fuel import ban and tariff cuts on electric vehicles have pushed EV penetration from below 1% to over 6% in under two years, primarily through inexpensive Chinese models, driven by fiscal necessity rather than climate ambition — a dynamic likely to repeat across other fuel-importing emerging economies.
