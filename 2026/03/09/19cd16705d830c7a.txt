Good morning. Oil surges as Iran picks Khamenei’s son as supreme leader, but 
comes off a tad after the Financial Times reported about talks on a possible 
coordinated release of reserves. Asian shares slide in a volatile session. And 
the latest search for Malaysia Airlines’ MH370 comes up empty.Listen to the 
day’s top stories <https://bloom.bg/4nmEQLf>.

— Nasteho Said 



Crude oil surged as much as 29%, the biggest intraday move since April 2020 
<https://bloom.bg/3Nho9DM>, as more Gulf producers curbed production and Iran 
named the son of the late Ayatollah Ali Khamenei as its new supreme leader. 
Gains were pared after the FT said the Group of Seven finance ministers will 
discuss apossible joint release of oil <https://bloom.bg/4uiH55V> from 
reserves, in coordination with the International Energy Agency. President 
Donald Trump downplayed the oil spike a “small price to pay,” with neither side 
showing any sign of deescalating a war now entering its 10th day.

Iran named Mojtaba Khamenei as its supreme leader <https://bloom.bg/4bxFNwo>—a 
man likely to continue his father's hardline approach to rule, and resist the 
US and Israel's military campaign. Trump is “not happy” with the appointment, 
according to Fox News, and had previously denounced Khamenei as an 
“unacceptable” choice.

 Deep Dive: Why the AI Boom Makes Cars More Expensive 


 <https://bloom.bg/3OXIte7> SK Hynix's 12-layer HBM4 memory chips on display. 
Photographer: SeongJoon Cho/Bloomberg 
AI demand is triggering a historic memory-chip shortage. That will make meeting
exponential demand for chips <https://bloom.bg/3OXIte7> expensive—and maybe 
even impossible.

 * Tech giants are buying up memory chips like never before, and paying a 
premium for multiyear contracts. 
 * That’s prompted chipmakers to allocate more of their production to these 
higher-margin orders, leaving fewer memory chips available for things like 
consumer devices and cars. 
 * Prices have surged almost 700% in some cases.
