# US Morning Briefing — February 13, 2026

**Markets: AI disruption trade broadens into a cross-sector repricing.** US equities extended losses, with the S&P 500 down 1.6% on Thursday and futures off another 0.3% overnight, as fears that newly released models from Google and Anthropic threaten incumbents spread well beyond software. Commercial real estate stocks slid sharply on concerns AI-driven productivity gains will erode office demand, while insurance brokers, wealth managers, private credit firms and logistics names also came under pressure. The episode that crystallized the mood: Algorhythm Holdings, a $5 million micro-cap former karaoke importer, touted an AI freight platform — sending the Russell 3000 Trucking Index down 6.6%, with C.H. Robinson off 15% and Landstar 16%. Treasury volatility, by contrast, sits at a five-year low, suggesting the rotation remains an equity-market phenomenon rather than a broader risk-off event.

**Inflation print in focus; Fed policy debate widens.** January CPI lands today against a backdrop of bond-market conviction that the Fed will resume cuts later this year. Some desks are pushing back: Citi recommends five-year, five-year inflation forwards near 2.5%, while JPMorgan suggests tactically shorting 2-year Treasuries on resilient growth. Separately, Judy Shelton — whose 2020 Fed nomination failed in the Senate — has re-emerged in policy discussions, meeting privately with Treasury Secretary Bessent on central bank reform, including a supply-side rate framework and redeployment of the $2.9 trillion in bank reserves parked at the Fed. Implementation would require buy-in well beyond the administration.

**Trade and corporates.** The administration is working to narrow the 50% steel and aluminum tariffs, which have proven difficult for importers to calculate and remain a sticking point in EU negotiations. Washington also finalized a Taiwan trade deal featuring $44 billion in US LNG and crude purchases plus $15 billion in aircraft orders. In single names, Goldman Sachs general counsel Kathy Ruemmler will depart this summer following renewed scrutiny of her Epstein ties; Pinterest fell 20% premarket on a weak sales outlook, DraftKings declined 14%, while Applied Materials, Roku, Rivian and Airbnb rallied on results.
