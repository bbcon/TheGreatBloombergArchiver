**Corporate Earnings**
Micron Technology delivered what Wall Street needed. Revenue guidance for the fiscal fourth quarter came in at approximately $50 billion, roughly 16% above the $43.2 billion analyst consensus, while EPS guidance of ~$31 per share exceeded projections of $25.31. Twelve-month trailing earnings have quadrupled in two quarters, and the company disclosed 16 multi-year strategic customer agreements averaging three years in length — a structural development that may reduce the cyclicality historically associated with memory-chip demand. Qualcomm also guided to more than $15 billion in AI data-center component sales by 2029, lifting semiconductor names broadly in premarket trading.

**Monetary Policy and Markets**
The unwinding of the debasement trade accelerated. Gold broke below $4,000 per ounce — down approximately 29% from its January peak — while the DXY dollar index closed at a 14-month high. Fed Chair Warsh's explicit prioritization of price stability at his maiden press conference has anchored rate expectations, with market-implied projections shifting toward an extended hold. Inflation analysts caution that price pressures now appear to extend meaningfully beyond energy, with Vincent Deluard of StoneX estimating a 3.5–4% CPI plateau even as oil retreats. Apollo's Torsten Slok raises a counterintuitive scenario: lower energy prices acting as a demand stimulus in an already-tight labor market, potentially forcing rate increases rather than cuts.
