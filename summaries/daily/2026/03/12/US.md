US February CPI data arrived broadly benign, with core inflation holding at 2.5% year-on-year — the lowest since the 2021 inflation breakout — as shelter costs rose just 0.1% from January, the slowest pace in five years, and used-car prices continued declining. The data would ordinarily support a more accommodative Federal Reserve posture, but it is being overshadowed by a more concerning divergence: the core PCE is on track to print at approximately 3.1% for January, which would represent the widest gap between the two inflation gauges in decades. Economists at Barclays, Morgan Stanley, and Bank of America project core PCE goods prices advancing at least 0.8% in February alone. The implication, as Citigroup noted, is that PCE data should keep Fed officials cautious and on hold, even as the Trump administration cites CPI prints to argue rates should be cut.

Fiscal pressures are mounting independently of the monetary debate. The 30-year Treasury yield approached 4.90%, its highest level in a month, as investors price in the cost of military operations and potential household energy subsidies. The US budget deficit narrowed in February but at a slower pace than the prior month as tariff revenue continued to decelerate from late-2025 peaks. Morgan Stanley and Cliffwater LLC separately capped investor withdrawals from multi-billion-dollar private credit funds after redemption requests materially exceeded permitted limits — a reminder that liquidity mismatches in private markets become visible precisely when macro stress is elevated.

On trade policy, the US Trade Representative's office filed sweeping Section 301 investigations against more than a dozen economies — including China, the EU, Mexico, India, and Taiwan — marking the first formal step to reconstruct a tariff wall following last month's Supreme Court ruling that struck down prior levies estimated at $170 billion. A public hearing is set for May 5th. The move risks complicating a planned Trump-Xi summit and further straining USMCA renegotiation efforts with Mexico.
