**Mexico: Trade Asymmetry as Negotiating Pressure**
The tariff disparity complaint underscores the structural tensions within the USMCA framework as it approaches its 2026 review. Mexican President Sheinbaum has simultaneously pursued domestic incentives — tax breaks and regulatory relief — to protect the automotive sector, but the gap with Asian competitor tariff rates represents a genuine competitiveness concern for an industry that accounts for a significant share of Mexican manufacturing employment. The outcome of the Greer discussions will be closely watched as a signal of US willingness to address asymmetries versus using tariff leverage as broader negotiating currency.

**Venezuela and Regional Political Economy**
Venezuela's military seizure of the Las Claritas mining region reflects President Maduro's attempt to formalize control over informal gold operations as a precondition for attracting foreign capital. The strategy carries execution risk: criminal organizations have long dominated the area, and the displacement of unlicensed workers creates humanitarian and security complications. Cuba's prospective receipt of the largest US fuel shipment since the Cold War era — from a Florida-based trading firm — signals a quiet, transactional softening of energy embargo enforcement under current conditions.
