European equities outperformed on the day, with the Stoxx Europe 600 gaining 0.5% to 628, near the all-time high of 630 reached Wednesday. Strategists are already signaling caution, however: the median of 17 Bloomberg survey forecasts suggests European stocks will finish 2026 little changed from current levels, as eight consecutive months of gains and valuations at five-year highs leave limited room for further multiple expansion. Luxury names were a notable bright spot, with Moncler rallying 13% on results described by Barclays as significantly ahead of estimates, pulling LVMH and Hermès higher.

The euro area flash PMI surprised to the upside, with manufacturers recording their best performance since 2022, driven by an unexpected improvement in Germany. Q4 negotiated wages rose 3%, a data point the ECB will monitor carefully as it balances disinflation progress against residual services-sector price pressures. Separately, the UK recorded its largest budget surplus on record, boosted by surging capital gains tax receipts and lower debt servicing costs — a brief fiscal respite for a government navigating difficult spending decisions ahead.

The arrest of Prince Andrew on suspicion of misconduct in public office — on his 66th birthday — marks a significant escalation in the broader Epstein fallout, with the investigation set to scrutinize years of alleged ties to the convicted sex offender. The development adds pressure to the monarchy at a moment when public confidence in institutions is already fragile. Separately, Primark faces structural headwinds as sales flatlined at £9.5 billion and the company remains without a permanent CEO; parent AB Foods is weighing a potential spin-off, though analysts caution this would require performance improvement first. On climate policy, UK Energy Secretary Ed Miliband defended his clean energy agreement with California Governor Newsom despite criticism from President Trump, framing it as consistent with British national interest.
