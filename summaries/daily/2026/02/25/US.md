Donald Trump's State of the Union address, delivered Tuesday evening, offered a defiant defense of his economic record while introducing limited new policy substance. The president declared the US is "back bigger, better, richer and stronger than ever before" at a moment when public skepticism of his economic stewardship is rising and consumer sentiment has softened. Notably, Trump made no direct mention of China — the first time in two decades a sitting president has omitted the country from the annual address, a striking rhetorical choice given the centrality of trade policy to his agenda. He was also measurably milder in his criticism of the Supreme Court justices who recently struck down many of his signature tariffs, with several present in the chamber.

On the policy margin, Trump proposed new tax-advantaged retirement savings accounts for workers without employer-sponsored plans, with a government match of up to $1,000 annually — a modest fiscal gesture unlikely to move macro aggregates materially.

In equity markets, US futures were marginally higher as investors awaited Nvidia's quarterly results after the close, the most consequential single earnings event in the current AI-valuation debate. S&P 500 fourth-quarter earnings growth came in at approximately 13%, roughly six percentage points above expectations, and yet the index has declined 1.7% over the six-week earnings window — tied for its worst earnings-season performance in ten quarters — suggesting valuation and policy uncertainty are dominating fundamental signals. Workday fell 9.2% in premarket trading on a subscription revenue miss, HP dropped 5% on a disappointing profit outlook citing tariffs and rising memory-chip costs, while Cava gained 9% on above-consensus 2026 guidance. First Solar slid 14% on a sales shortfall.

The Warner Bros. Discovery-Paramount media consolidation dynamics evolved further, with Warner Bros. signaling a new $31-per-share Paramount Skydance offer could produce a superior outcome to its existing Netflix arrangement, without withdrawing that recommendation. Stripe is separately reported to be exploring an acquisition of some or all of PayPal, a combination that would reshape the payments landscape significantly if pursued.

Bitcoin's price structure remained technically fragile, with Glassnode data showing 19 of the first 22 days in February recorded net realized losses. Approximately 45% of circulating supply sits underwater relative to acquisition cost, creating a persistent ceiling on rallies as distressed holders sell into any recovery.
