The Trump-Xi summit in Beijing dominated regional attention, delivering a day of carefully choreographed pageantry alongside sharper-edged substance. Xi greeted Trump with a full state welcome at the Great Hall of the People, and both leaders struck cooperative tones in public remarks. Trump called Xi "a great leader" and invited him to the White House in September. Xi declared that common interests "outweigh differences" and pledged China's door would "open wider," delivering the message directly to a delegation of US executives representing roughly $20 trillion in market capitalization, including Elon Musk, Tim Cook, Jensen Huang, and Kelly Ortberg.

Beneath the cordiality, Xi delivered a pointed warning that mishandling Taiwan could push the bilateral relationship into a "highly dangerous situation," with Chinese state media publishing the remarks while the two leaders were still in the room. The Taiwan message appeared calibrated to establish a firm boundary without derailing trade discussions. On the economic side, China renewed import licenses for hundreds of US beef plants as an early gesture, while Treasury Secretary Bessent outlined discussions on a potential board of investment to allow Chinese capital into non-sensitive US industries, and suggested possible tariff relief on roughly $30 billion of non-critical trade. The question of whether the summit produces durable progress on semiconductors, rare earths, and agricultural goods will crystallize on day two.

Elsewhere across the region, Asian central banks are facing rising strain. The Philippines, India, and Indonesia are burning foreign-exchange reserves to defend weakening currencies as oil import costs mount, with shrinking import cover pushing policymakers toward rate hikes. A Bank of Japan board member called for tightening "as early as possible," citing inflation risks from the Iran war, sending the yen higher and lifting June hike expectations. South Korea's equity market continued to exhibit speculative characteristics, with retail borrowing at record levels and a YouTuber with 1.3 million subscribers describing the mood as "nearly maniacal" — even as underlying chip earnings at Samsung and SK Hynix remain robust.
