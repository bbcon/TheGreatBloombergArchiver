Daily Brief

Friday, July 17, 2026

US

  • Netflix fell nearly 10% after guiding for slowing sales growth, adding to a broad tech selloff in which Nasdaq 100 futures declined 1.8% — a momentum-driven rotation rather than a single macro catalyst.
  • US CPI declined in June for the first time in six years, beating consensus to the dovish side; jobless claims ticked down and retail sales rose modestly, together painting a tepid but marginally constructive picture of the economy.
  • Fed Chair Kevin Warsh reintroduced M2 money supply as a policy dashboard indicator in the semiannual Monetary Policy Report, signaling a broader deliberative framework under his leadership.
  • US corporate insiders are selling shares at a near-record pace, a potential warning signal that those with the most granular knowledge of business conditions are reducing exposure at elevated valuations.

Macro Data

June CPI declined on a year-over-year basis for the first time since 2020, a print that came in below consensus and provided some near-term relief on the inflation front. Jobless claims edged lower and retail sales posted a modest gain, together suggesting the consumer remains functional if unexcited. Bloomberg Economics analysis argues that AI's contribution to GDP is likely understated by official data — potentially more than double the reported figure when adjusted for quality improvements in computing power — which, if accurate, implies 2025 growth of 2.1% was more AI-driven than the official 0.53 percentage-point attribution suggests.

Monetary Policy

Fed Chair Warsh used his Senate testimony and the semiannual Monetary Policy Report to rehabilitate M2 as one input in a broader "mosaic" of inflation indicators, stopping short of endorsing monetarism outright. The Fed's report noted that M2 growth has returned toward its 2010s average, with pandemic-era excess balances largely unwound, though the metric has recently been re-accelerating — a dynamic that former Fed board member Steven Miran argues could eventually warrant tighter policy if the trend continues.

Markets

Nasdaq 100 futures fell 1.8% and the semiconductor ETF dropped 4.4% in premarket trading as investors rotated out of AI-adjacent positions. Netflix's sharp decline after weak forward guidance, combined with Intuitive Surgical falling 10% on slowing da Vinci procedure growth, reinforced a broader pattern: earnings season is beginning to test the assumption that AI-related capex will translate into commensurate revenue. Hoisington Investment Management, a three-decade Treasury bull, has cut its portfolio duration to under one year, citing structural fiscal deficits and an interest-rate regime likely less benign than the 1990–2020 period. ---

Asia

  • Japan's Nikkei 225 fell 4.2%, entering technical correction territory, as memory chipmaker Kioxia extended its decline to 52% from its June peak — the sharpest illustration of the regional AI valuation unwind.
  • China's Q2 GDP grew 4.3% YoY, missing the government's target range and below consensus, with domestic demand components uniformly weak while exports reached a record $412 billion in June.
  • China's GDP deflator turned positive for the first time in three years, but the shift appears driven by oil-shock cost inflation rather than demand revival — a distinction economists are characterizing as "bad inflation."
  • President Xi Jinping made his first personal appearance at the World AI Conference in Shanghai, using the platform to position China as an open, collaborative alternative to US technology governance, while signing 29 nations into a China-led AI cooperation organization.

China Macro

The Q2 GDP miss at 4.3% — below the government's approximate 5% target — underscores the structural imbalance in China's economy. Fixed-asset investment recorded what data describe as a historic contraction, and consumer spending barely expanded. The positive GDP deflator reading is technically a milestone after three years of economy-wide deflation, but it reflects higher raw material costs from the Iran-linked oil shock rather than restored consumer pricing power, widening margins between upstream commodity producers and downstream manufacturers. Exports remain the primary growth engine, with semiconductor shipments rising at triple-digit rates for a second consecutive month on higher prices. Goldman Sachs estimates roughly 1.8 trillion yuan in undeployed government bond issuance quota remains available as a fiscal buffer.

AI Geopolitics and Markets

Xi's appearance at the World AI Conference — an elevation from prior years when he sent a deputy — signals Beijing's intent to shape global AI governance commercially and diplomatically. The launch of the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization, with 29 member nations including Brazil and Indonesia, formalizes that ambition. Chinese startup Moonshot's Kimi K3 model, described as rivaling top OpenAI and Anthropic offerings, contributed to the regional equity selloff by prompting investors to question US AI capex assumptions. TSMC fell 7.3% despite reporting earnings that beat estimates, and Taiwan's Taiex shed 6.5% — its largest single-day decline since April 2025. DeepSeek is reportedly preparing for an IPO as soon as this year. ---

Europe

  • Andy Burnham is set to enter Downing Street as the UK's next Prime Minister, with early reports indicating he will announce new North Sea oil and gas drilling and public control of Thames Water among his first acts.
  • The ECB is expected to pause at its next meeting before delivering a final rate hike in September, according to economist consensus, as the inflation outlook remains unsettled.
  • The Stoxx Europe 600 fell 0.8% as the global chip selloff spread to European semiconductor names, with ASML down 3.8%, ASM International and BE Semiconductor each falling 5.0%.

UK Political Transition

Burnham's arrival at Downing Street follows a period of sustained political instability — six prime ministers since 2016, none completing a full term. The IMF has warned against raising the top income tax rate as Burnham's team shapes its fiscal agenda, with chancellor candidate Shabana Mahmood described as entering with a relatively open policy slate. The political context matters for markets: UK gilt and sterling pricing will be sensitive to early signals on the fiscal framework, particularly given ongoing uncertainty around the growth outlook. Burnham's reported willingness to expand North Sea production represents a pragmatic departure from his party's prior energy stance, though the market impact will depend on the scale and speed of licensing decisions.

Corporate and Markets

Burberry fell 5.4% in London after sales growth disappointed investors seeking firmer evidence of a sustained turnaround. The broader Stoxx 600 decline reflected contagion from the semiconductor selloff rather than any Europe-specific macro catalyst. The ECB's expected September rate hike — if delivered — would represent a divergence from the Fed's current posture and could exert modest upward pressure on the euro, though the trajectory remains contingent on incoming inflation data. ---

LatAm

  • US 25% tariffs on Brazilian goods have introduced a significant political economy dynamic ahead of Brazil's October presidential election, with President Lula using the measure to cast opponent Flávio Bolsonaro as complicit in economic punishment of Brazil.
  • Chile's Senate approved the president's wide-ranging economic reform bill, clearing a key legislative hurdle aimed at reviving the country's sluggish growth.

Brazil Political Economy

The imposition of 25% US tariffs on Brazilian exports arrives three months before the presidential election, creating an asymmetric political dynamic: rather than weakening Lula as Trump and his regional allies may have intended, the measure has handed Lula a sovereignty narrative he has used successfully before. His Workers' Party revived the "TariFlávio" slogan to link Senator Flávio Bolsonaro — his principal electoral opponent — to the tariff decision, exploiting the Bolsonaro family's sustained cultivation of Trump ties. Whether the tariffs cause sufficient economic damage to offset Lula's nationalist framing remains to be seen, but the near-term political calculus appears to favor the incumbent. The episode illustrates a broader pattern in which Trump's Latin America policy has taken on an ideological character that diverges from the transactional flexibility — "TACO" — observed elsewhere.

Regional Policy

Chile's Senate passage of the economic reform package removes a significant legislative obstacle for President Boric's economic agenda, though implementation and market confidence effects will take time to materialize. No major macro data releases for Brazil, Mexico, Colombia, or Argentina were reported in the coverage period. ---

Global

  • US forces conducted a sixth consecutive night of strikes against Iran targeting coastal surveillance, air defense, and maritime assets, with Strait of Hormuz shipping traffic declining materially and oil prices heading for their largest weekly gain since April.
  • The global chip selloff — triggered in part by Chinese AI startup Moonshot's Kimi K3 breakthrough and broader valuation concerns — spread across US, Japanese, Taiwanese, and European equity markets simultaneously, marking the worst day for tech-heavy indices since the April 2025 tariff shock.
  • China signed 29 nations into a new World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization, formalizing a geopolitical contest over AI governance that now has institutional architecture on both sides.

Hormuz and Oil Markets

The Strait of Hormuz has not been fully closed, but shipping flows have declined markedly from levels seen before US-Iran hostilities intensified. Dark transits — tankers moving without tracking signals — have become the primary mechanism for continuing Iranian oil exports, and these are increasingly being targeted. Crude timespreads are in heavy backwardation and refined product premiums are at historic highs, signaling acute near-term supply anxiety. Insurance premiums for Hormuz transits have surged and some crews are refusing passage. The market's central question is how long the US will sustain military pressure if oil prices continue to rise — a political constraint Trump's administration has not yet resolved publicly. An unusual options strategy involving $1-wide put spreads on roughly 400 million barrels has emerged as the preferred hedge against abrupt US policy reversals.

AI Governance Fracture

Xi's World AI Conference appearance and the formation of the AI Cooperation Organization with 29 member nations crystallizes a bifurcating global AI order, with China promoting open-source, low-cost models as an alternative to US proprietary platforms. The simultaneous selloff in AI-linked equities globally — triggered partly by Moonshot's competitive model release — illustrates that the commercial and geopolitical dimensions of AI competition are now directly interacting with capital markets. The "DeepSeek moment" parallel cited by multiple market participants suggests investors may be re-rating AI capex return assumptions more fundamentally than a single-session selloff implies.
