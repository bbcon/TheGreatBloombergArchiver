Officials in Brussels and several member states are skeptical that the 
European Union has the political will or guts to take decisive trade action on 
China, should current diplomatic efforts fail.

Member states are aligned on the severe stakes posed by China’s dominant 
economic position but leaders can’t yet agree on what tangible steps to take to 
fix a trade deficit that runs at about a billion euros a day, Bloomberg News
reports today 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-02/eu-flinches-in-china-trade-reset-fearing-painful-retaliation?cmpid=BBD070226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260702&utm_content=5938>
.

Several capitals want to promptly give the European Commission, the bloc’s 
executive arm, a mandate to develop and deploy new instruments to counter 
Beijing, while others have a more wait-and-see approach and believe differences 
can be resolved through dialog. A small number feel the battle is already lost 
given how deeply dependent the bloc has become on China in sensitive sectors.

Read More: Chinese Carmakers Exceed 10% of Europe Sales on Hybrid Surge 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/one-in-10-new-cars-sold-in-europe-in-may-was-chinese?cmpid=BBD070226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260702&utm_content=5938>

The EU’s problem isn’t a lack of tools, according to one senior official — it 
has the instruments that would hand it ample flexibility to act if there was 
the will to do so. Another official told Bloomberg that not enough member 
states are willing to absorb the pain that would follow any inevitable Chinese 
retaliation.

For now, the EU and China agreed to set up consultations and working groups to 
tackle their trade disagreements following a meeting on Monday. They set an 
October deadline to make progress.

Beijing has so far been reluctant to focus talks on moderating its exports but 
is open to exploring how to facilitate more EU exports. The EU is keen to 
tackle both as well other issues such as navigating China’s export controls and 
barriers that curb access to its market.

Explainer: Why Rare Earths Are China’s Trump Card in Trade War 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/how-xi-is-using-rare-earths-against-trump-in-the-us-china-trade-war?cmpid=BBD070226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260702&utm_content=5938>

Unsurprisingly, Beijing is pursuing a transactional approach to negotiations 
and wants the EU to reciprocate any changes it makes. Briefing national envoys 
after the meeting, the EU’s trade chief acknowledged that not all issues would 
be solved by the October deadline, but said he hoped there will be a clearer 
understanding on how to rebalance trade flows.
