# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — February 5, 2026

**Global tech rout reaches Asia; software, chip names lead declines.** The selloff that erased close to $1 trillion from an iShares software ETF over seven days extended into Asian trading, with Samsung Electronics tumbling and a Hang Seng gauge of Chinese technology shares approaching bear-market territory. The proximate catalyst — concern that AI tools may disrupt incumbent software business models — has now broadened to chipmakers, with AMD down 17% post-earnings and Qualcomm and Arm guiding cautiously, citing potential memory-component shortages. Against this, Baidu announced its first-ever dividend alongside a buyback of up to $5 billion, a notable shift in capital-return posture from China's large-cap internet names. Alphabet's capex guidance of up to $185 billion (versus $119.5 billion consensus) will be read in Asia as supportive for memory and AI infrastructure suppliers, though the broader rotation away from momentum names complicates that read.

**Japan: Takaichi looks set to consolidate her mandate.** Polling ahead of Sunday's lower-house election suggests Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's LDP-led coalition will comfortably clear the 233 seats needed for a majority. The yen has whipsawed on her comments regarding a weaker currency, which she has partially walked back. More structurally, the Topix rally appears increasingly underpinned by genuine governance reform — rising dividends, accelerating buybacks, and a re-rating of long-discounted sectors such as non-ferrous metals (Sumitomo Electric has tripled since the US election). The 30-year JGB yield surge is best characterized as catch-up to G7 peers rather than incipient fiscal crisis, though Takaichi's expansionary instincts warrant continued monitoring.

**China policy and geopolitics.** More than a dozen Chinese provinces have lowered 2026 growth targets, and consensus is coalescing around a national target of 4.5%–5% at next month's NPC — the first downward revision in three years. Citi now treats its previously baseline stimulus package (≈1 trillion yuan fiscal, 20bp rate cut, 50bp RRR cut) as a ceiling rather than a baseline. Separately, Beijing has instructed state firms to halt new project discussions in Panama and is intensifying customs inspections on Panamanian goods, retaliating against the voiding of a Hong Kong company's port contract. Trump and Xi spoke by phone, covering trade and Taiwan; tone appears constructive, though substance remains opaque.

**India and Australia.** India's exports to the US will face an 18% tariff rate once a joint statement is finalized.
