Asian equity markets slipped modestly on Thursday as ceasefire optimism faded and oil prices partially rebounded, with WTI futures recovering toward $98-99 a barrel. The China CSI 300 fell 0.6%, while broader Asian sentiment remains fragile given the region's acute energy import dependence.

China occupies a structurally ambiguous position in the current environment. While Bruegel warns that Beijing's reliance on Iranian crude represents a "severe test" for its energy strategy, Deutsche Bank's Jacky Tang argues China is the war's principal economic beneficiary given its status as the world's dominant clean-technology producer. As Japan, South Korea, and India accelerate energy diversification, the equipment required — solar panels, batteries, and associated infrastructure — will largely originate from Chinese manufacturers. Beijing is also weighing a substantial bailout for its state-run airlines, with options including subsidies, tax breaks, cheap state-backed loans, and potentially mergers, as elevated fuel costs push the "Big Three" carriers deeper into losses. Two fully laden Chinese supertankers are positioned at the mouth of the Strait of Hormuz, potentially among the first vessels to test the fragile transit arrangement.

Japan's consumer confidence fell in March by the most since the early stages of the Covid pandemic, compounding concerns about a security vacuum in Asia as US military assets remain concentrated in the Persian Gulf. Seven & i Holdings delayed the planned US listing of its convenience store operations, sending shares down 4.6%, citing insufficient progress in turning around the business. Fast Retailing, by contrast, raised its outlook on strong Japan sales and global demand.

In India, foreign investors have sold $17.8 billion in equities over 23 consecutive sessions — a record outflow pace — as higher crude prices fan inflation fears and squeeze margins in an import-dependent economy. State elections are running concurrently, functioning as a de facto referendum on Prime Minister Modi's handling of the energy crunch, with fuel shortages visible on the campaign trail. Sri Lanka, meanwhile, is nearing a $700 million IMF disbursement after clearing a program review, with growth and reserves tracking ahead of plan despite ongoing vulnerability to oil price volatility. TPG is exploring a sale or IPO of Asia OneHealthcare, with a potential valuation of up to $7.5 billion.
