**Fed Independence**
The Supreme Court's 5-4 ruling that President Trump cannot remove Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook without due process represents a significant, if largely anticipated, affirmation of central bank independence. Markets barely reacted, for several reasons: the outcome had been telegraphed in oral arguments, inflation and Iran war fallout have rendered near-term rate cuts implausible regardless of FOMC composition, and the window to install a pro-administration majority on the rate-setting committee has effectively closed. The more consequential element of the ruling may be the companion decision granting the president broad authority to dismiss officials at the SEC, CFTC, and other independent agencies — a development with direct implications for crypto regulation and financial oversight.

**Markets and Deals**
US equities are closing out the strongest quarter in six years, with S&P 500 futures at 7,507. Deal activity has proven more resilient than feared amid the Iran conflict: PwC estimates full-year global deal value will exceed $4 trillion, with AI transactions and mega-IPOs — including SpaceX's record $86 billion offering — providing much of the impetus. IPO volumes remain well below the euphoric 2021 peak of 250 deals, which Goldman's Ben Snider argues limits bubble comparisons, though valuations remain elevated by historical standards. Strategy Inc.'s decision to allow Bitcoin sales introduces uncertainty into crypto markets by removing a key source of structural demand.
