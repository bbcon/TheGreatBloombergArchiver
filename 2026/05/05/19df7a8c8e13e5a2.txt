Corporate America’s vote of confidence

As some investors wonder if a torrid rise in US equities is running out of 
steam,one of the market’s most important buyers 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-04/-ultimate-dip-buyers-of-stocks-remain-strong-as-buybacks-surge?cmpid=050526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4121>
 is standing firm.

Corporate America announced plans to buy back $665 billion worth of shares in 
S&P 500 companies in the four months through April, the most ever to start a 
year, according to data from Birinyi Associates.Just last week, Apple announced 
the year’s biggest repurchase as it seeks to reassure investors during its 
leadership transition.

Authorized repurchases are estimated to reach $1.55 trillion in 2026, a figure 
that would eclipse last year’s record. That’s a crucial vote of confidence in 
corporate fundamentals at a time when valuation multiples appear rich and 
uncertainty lingers around the long-term impact of rising oil prices.

“We hear a lot about individual investors being the dip-buyer, but the 
ultimate dip-buyers are the corporations themselves,” said Jeff Rubin of 
Birinyi Associates. —Alexandra Semenova 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AT8YbvmLmbw/alexandra-semenova> and Natalia 
Kniazhevich <https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AUdOpcrZfmo/natalia-kniazhevich>

Getting a piece of the AI action

Crypto platforms are offering trades tied to the most valuable private AI 
companies 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-05/ai-s-hottest-private-companies-have-booming-crypto-shadow-market?cmpid=050526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260505&utm_content=4121>
 on earth — ones ordinary investors have almost no other way to access.

The result is a new frontier in the financialization of private markets: 
crypto infrastructure, once the domain of digital token speculation, being 
redeployed to give traders a way to bet on Anthropic and OpenAI — in real time, 
24 hours a day, with leverage.

The trades on two venues riding that shift, Ventuals and PreStocks, don’t 
confer real ownership:

 * Traders on Ventuals are speculating on valuations via perpetual futures — 
derivatives with no asset backing, no expiration date and no anchor to any 
actual transaction.
 * On PreStocks, tokens are minted one-to-one against special-purpose vehicle 
exposure intended to track real shares on secondary markets.Anthropic has 
explicitly warned those structures may carry no legal value and that the 
company treats them as void.
Still, the two have seen their trading activity — measured by open interest 
and market value combined — surge more than threefold from the start of the 
year to last month. —Ryan Weeks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AWv6qGVTGCA/ryan-weeks> and Sidhartha Shukla 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AVqtJtvz_s8/sidhartha-shukla>
