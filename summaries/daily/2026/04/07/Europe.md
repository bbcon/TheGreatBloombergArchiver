European equity markets showed modest gains on Tuesday, with the Stoxx Europe 600 rising 0.7%, though the region faces a more challenging medium-term backdrop than the headline number suggests. Earnings estimate revisions have turned negative for European equities even as US estimates hold in positive territory—a reversal from the earlier-year narrative in which European stocks were outperforming on the back of fiscal stimulus expectations and ECB easing. The European Central Bank's next policy move now appears likely to be a rate increase rather than a cut, potentially as early as this month, as energy-driven inflation reasserts itself. Britain and France are identified by BloombergNEF as the most exposed European markets to diesel and kerosene supply disruption, given their reliance on refined product imports from Kuwait and Saudi Arabia respectively. Several Italian airports have already issued limited fuel supply advisories.

ASML declined 4.7% in Amsterdam after US lawmakers introduced legislation to tighten chip tool export restrictions to China, a move with significant implications for one of Europe's most strategically important technology companies. Universal Music Group shares surged 11% in Amsterdam after Bill Ackman proposed a deal valuing the company at approximately $65 billion. H&M remains under pressure, with the retailer's CEO facing investor skepticism over a turnaround strategy squeezed between Inditex at the premium end and Shein and Primark at the value end—a credibility problem compounded by broader consumer confidence concerns as the conflict sustains elevated fuel costs. Hungary's election on Sunday carries outsize geopolitical weight for the EU: polls suggest Viktor Orbán's grip may be loosening, with JD Vance campaigning alongside him in Budapest and the outcome carrying direct implications for EU Ukraine policy and the bloc's Russia sanctions architecture.
