Asia is navigating the Hormuz disruption without a unified regional framework, with each major economy improvising its own response. India has been negotiating safe passage arrangements directly with Tehran, while China has floated a diplomatic peace proposal. Japan is reportedly engaging with a 35-country coalition led by London to restore navigation. Trump's instruction for nations to "take the lead" has deepened this fragmentation rather than catalyzing coordination, leaving a quarter of global oil trade in limbo and no multilateral mechanism in sight.

China's policy response is revealing in its prioritization of energy security over market efficiency. Beijing has instructed independent refiners — the so-called "teapot" sector — to maintain 2025 output levels or risk losing future import quotas, even as margins turn negative and access to discounted sanctioned crude dries up. The directive makes commercial losses a cost absorbed by smaller private processors, while the state ensures downstream fuel availability. Meanwhile, the People's Bank of China withdrew cash from its financial system for the first time in approximately a year, a cautious move that keeps policy options open without signaling a pivot in either direction. Separately, Zhongji Innolight — an Nvidia optical components supplier — has confidentially filed for a Hong Kong IPO targeting roughly $3 billion, underscoring the continued momentum in AI-adjacent hardware even as broader sentiment remains pressured.

Japan's bond auction underperformed on Thursday, adding to the global fixed-income selloff following Trump's address. On a structural note, Japan's labor market is generating what may be its most sustained wage cycle in decades: the Shunto spring negotiations secured average increases exceeding 5% for a third consecutive year, while base pay recorded its largest year-on-year gain in more than 33 years. Notably, total employment is rising despite population contraction, as female participation, immigration liberalization — a legacy of Abenomics — and extended working careers among older cohorts collectively expand the labor supply. South Korea is contending with inflation nudging above the 2% target and President Lee Jae Myung urging energy conservation while deploying a $17 billion support package. India's rupee staged its largest single-day gain in over a decade after the RBI banned key offshore derivatives and forced the unwind of tens of billions in speculative positions, though the structural pressure from elevated energy import costs persists.
