The central development across global markets remains the unresolved status of the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately 20% of global oil supply ordinarily flows. A chaotic weekend saw Iranian foreign minister declare the strait "completely open" on Friday, only for Tehran to reverse course within 24 hours after the US refused to lift its own blockade of Iran-linked vessels. The US Navy then fired upon and boarded an Iranian-flagged cargo ship in the Gulf of Oman — the first such seizure since the blockade began — prompting Iranian authorities to reimpose controls. Brent crude surged as much as 7.9% to near $95 per barrel in Asian trading, while European gas rose as much as 11%.

The ceasefire is due to expire Tuesday, and the prospects for US-Iran talks in Islamabad appear uncertain. Vice President Vance, special envoy Witkoff, and Jared Kushner were scheduled to depart for Pakistan Monday evening, but Iran's Foreign Ministry stated no decision had been made on attendance, with a spokesman citing a lack of "seriousness" on the US side. The gap between the two parties' stated positions remains wide: the US claims Iran has agreed to surrender enriched uranium and end its nuclear program; Iranian officials deny both.

Polymarket bettors place only approximately a one-in-five probability on tanker traffic returning to normal by the end of April. The economic effects are beginning to propagate more broadly: tankers face 3.5-day delays at the Panama Canal as diverted traffic surges, Pakistan is experiencing severe power and gas outages, Colombia is reckoning with energy demand disruptions, and Singapore is scrambling to secure alternative LNG supplies. The war is simultaneously accelerating the energy storage boom, with battery installations expected to jump roughly a third this year as economies seek to reduce fossil fuel dependence. China's clean tech export surge — with solar cells up 80% year-on-year in March — reflects both this structural demand shift and Beijing's ability to capitalize on a crisis not of its making.
