Good morning. The US House voted to end Donald Trump’s tariffs on Canada as 
six Republicans crossed the aisle. Read about an alleged multi-million euro 
insider trade. And Jim Ratcliffe says the UK is “colonized” by immigrants.
Listen to the day’s top stories <https://bloom.bg/4nmEQLf>.

— Abhishek Shanker and Hari Govind



US President Donald Trump’s tariff agenda faced its  biggest setback 
<https://bloom.bg/4rLmzsA> as the Republican-led House passed a bill targeting 
levies on Canadian imports. Though unlikely to survive a veto, the vote exposed 
GOP divisions and rising election-year anxiety over affordability and Trump’s 
weakening political grip.
Friedrich Merz (left) and Emmanuel Macron. Photographer: Ludovic 
Marin/AFP/Getty Images 
French President Emmanuel Macron renewed his “Made in Europe 
<https://bloom.bg/4qxwdxY>” push before a key EU meeting, urging priority for 
local content in strategic sectors. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz pushed 
back, warning against excessive protectionism and underscoring divisions over 
how to strengthen Europe’s competitiveness. UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves urged
inclusion of partners <https://bloom.bg/40b3F2l> like the UK, Norway and 
Canada. Europe mustaccelerate reforms and scale up efforts 
<https://bloom.bg/4qvR3h0> to strengthen its economy, European Central Bank 
Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel said.

Trump told Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu he will pursue Iran talks 
and prefers a deal <https://bloom.bg/4qxXZdA>, despite the country’s concerns 
about Washington’s diplomatic push. Crude oil edged higher as traders remain 
concerned about apotential escalation <https://bloom.bg/4071fBS> in tensions 
between the US and Iran. Middle East tensions aren’t rattling markets yet—but 
if oil flows get hit,inflation and central banks will feel it fast 
<https://bloom.bg/3MzRJUR>.

Russia has moved to fully block WhatsApp its popular encrypted messaging 
service <https://bloom.bg/404pQqX> in the country as part of an effort to drive 
adoption of a new, state-sponsored app. Authorities are also moving to restrict 
Telegram as Moscow tightens control over foreign messaging platforms.

London house prices are expected to rise for the first time in over a year, a 
RICS survey showed, signalingtentative improvement <https://bloom.bg/401svlk>. 
While UK buyer enquiries picked up, overall activity remains subdued after tax 
uncertainty and a prolonged downturn.

 Deep Dive: The Paris Wiretaps 


Photographer: Illustration by Stella Murphy 
The trial of two traders in Paris offers a rare and detailed view 
<https://bloom.bg/4rJ6weJ> into the alleged mechanics of what prosecutors claim 
is a multi-million-euro insider trade.

 * The story is based on transcripts and additional conversations, as well as 
the original audio recordings presented in court this month. 
 * Listening in were cops from the brigade financière de Paris after  some 
suspicious trades <https://bloom.bg/3MEY6pV> attracted the attention of 
France’s equivalent of the US Security and Exchange Commission. 
 * Criminal insider trading cases are extremely rare in France, with only a 
handful occurring in the past decade. What makes the Airgas deal case 
<https://bloom.bg/3ZsHdBL> exceptional even among those are the phone 
recordings. 
 * The Paris criminal court said it would read out its ruling on April 13.
