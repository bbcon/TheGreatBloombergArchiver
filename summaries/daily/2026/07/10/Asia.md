**China Geopolitics and Property**

China's test of a submarine-launched intercontinental ballistic missile — likely a JL-3 variant with an estimated range of approximately 10,000 kilometers — represents the clearest demonstration yet of its nuclear triad development. While neighbors registered alarm, the primary signal appears directed at the US: Beijing is demonstrating, or approaching, a credible second-strike capability from sea-based platforms widely regarded as harder to neutralize than land-based systems. Separately, China's property market recovery remains narrow. Small, older apartments in major cities such as Shanghai have seen modest price appreciation — up 2.4% from November lows through April — but the trend is confined to prime urban locations and does little to address the broader glut of developer inventory. A broader market recovery appears distant.

**Japan Pension Policy and Yen**

Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama's call for pension funds to shift toward domestic assets produced an immediate yen rally, though structural headwinds persist. The yen has declined approximately 32% against the dollar over the past five years, and government intervention totaling $72.6 billion earlier this year failed to arrest its slide. Global positioning remains heavily against the currency — hedge funds are the most net-short yen since 2007. Whether a policy signal, absent compulsory reallocation mandates, can sustainably reverse capital outflows from a country holding nearly $5 trillion in overseas assets remains uncertain.
