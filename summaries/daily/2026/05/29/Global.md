**Oil Markets and the Hormuz Truce**
The tentative 60-day US-Iran ceasefire extension is the dominant macro event of the period, and its impact on energy markets is stark: Brent crude has fallen approximately 19% in May, its steepest monthly decline since 2020, as traders price in the potential resumption of flows through the Strait of Hormuz. The reversal matters globally — elevated energy prices since the conflict began have been a primary driver of inflation reacceleration across the G7, and a sustained reduction in oil prices would reduce pressure on central banks currently contemplating hikes rather than cuts. However, Trump has not yet formally agreed to the terms, and the deal remains tentative; premature dismissal of Hormuz risk appears unwarranted. Meanwhile, the US oil system shows early strain from export demand that has helped plug the Hormuz supply gap, with American crude shipments falling to a near two-month low.

**AI Capital Flows**
The scale of capital mobilizing around artificial intelligence infrastructure is approaching macro-relevant magnitudes. Anthropic's $65 billion fundraise at a $965 billion valuation — eclipsing OpenAI for the first time — was accompanied by a $36 billion private credit facility assembled by Apollo and Blackstone to finance AI chip procurement, effectively transforming advanced semiconductors into a financeable asset class. Taiwan's tech firms are separately borrowing a record $14.5 billion. These flows are compressing risk premia in private credit, concentrating equity gains in AI-adjacent equities globally, and creating feedback loops between chip demand, energy infrastructure, and sovereign growth forecasts.

**Geopolitics and Maritime Risk**
The Pentagon's 121-page Indo-Pacific command report to Congress strikes a notably harder tone on China than the broader Trump administration, warning that the PLA is targeting Taiwan readiness by 2027 and requesting billions for asymmetric sea-denial capabilities. The Hormuz conflict has sharpened Washington's focus on maritime chokepoint control, with the Taiwan Strait now explicitly framed through the same lens. Separately, Russia's drone strike into Romanian territory — a NATO member — represents a qualitative escalation on Europe's eastern flank, while the China-Canada diplomatic rapprochement and Beijing's intensifying South China Sea operations reflect a global order in active realignment.
