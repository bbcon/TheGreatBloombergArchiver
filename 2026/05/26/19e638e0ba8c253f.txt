The Mideast ceasefire is still holding and efforts to reach a deal continue to 
gather pace, but the pathway there is looking treacherous.

US jets hit 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/trump-touts-iran-progress-while-hormuz-strike-reports-show-risk?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4833>
 missile launch sites in Iran and boats trying to place mines as part of 
defensive moves, according to US Central Command.

The strikes came after Trump earlier said negotiations between the US and Iran 
over an agreement to extend their ceasefire and reopen the strait were “
proceeding nicely 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/us-iran-edge-closer-to-deal-but-still-need-to-negotiate-points?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4833>
.” US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said talks with Iran could take several 
days, adding that Trump would either agree to a good deal or make no deal at 
all.

Trump is under pressure from Iran hawks including Republican Senator Lindsey 
Graham, who argue the emerging accord concedes too much to Tehran. The 
president later called for Saudi Arabia, Qatar and othercountries to join 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/trump-pushes-saudis-to-recognize-israel-via-abraham-accords-as-part-of-iran-deal?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4833>
 the Abraham Accords and recognize Israel, which could be a way for him to 
appease critics within the Republican Party. Yet Saudi Arabia and Qatar have 
said they will not normalize ties with Israel without progress toward 
Palestinian statehood.
Donald Trump at the White House on Monday.Photographer: Aaron 
Schwartz/CNP/Bloomberg via Consolidated News Photos
Adding complexity, Iran’s Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei said US military 
bases in the Middle East will no longer be safe after the war.

“The clock cannot be turned back; the nations and lands of the region will no 
longer be a shield for American bases,” he said in a written statement marking 
the Islamic Hajj pilgrimage in Saudi Arabia, which he said had an important 
role in “narrating the victory” of the war against the US-Israeli alliance.

Meanwhile, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said Israel would intensify 
attacks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/israel-will-intensify-lebanon-strikes-amid-us-iran-deal-talks?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4833>
 on Hezbollah after striking sites in southern Lebanon. Iran has demanded an 
end to hostilities against Hezbollah in Lebanon as part of any peace agreement 
with the US.

Still, negotiations appear to be progressing. An Iranian delegation led by 
Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf traveled to Doha for consultations 
with senior Qatari officials. The Iranian central bank governor, Abdolnaser 
Hemmati, was part of the group and was set to discuss the release of frozen 
Iranian funds, the Fars news agency reported. Al Arabiya reported that 
Pakistan’s army chief Asim Munir is headed to Doha.
