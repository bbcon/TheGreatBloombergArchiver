Keir Starmer faces a potentially defining political test as former senior civil servant Olly Robbins testifies before the House of Commons regarding the prime minister's handling of Peter Mandelson's appointment as US ambassador. The central question — whether Starmer was aware of Mandelson's vetting failure or pressured the Foreign Office to proceed regardless — goes to the heart of his credibility on standards in public life. Starmer has already survived the first day of parliamentary scrutiny, but Robbins's testimony carries greater evidentiary weight. UK businesses stepped up job cuts in March, a sign that the Iran war is generating fresh caution in the labour market, adding to the economic headwinds facing the government.

In European financial markets, the UniCredit-Commerzbank takeover battle has entered a decisive phase. UniCredit CEO Andrea Orcel and Commerzbank counterpart Bettina Orlopp met last month but failed to find common ground despite Orcel having accumulated just under 30% of Commerzbank and tabling a €35 billion offer. The German government continues to back Orlopp's resistance, though questions about the sustainability of that defense are sharpening as Commerzbank's profit tailwinds from higher rates fade. ECB President Christine Lagarde noted that the Iran war's "double uncertainty" — simultaneously threatening energy supply and inflation — is complicating monetary policy deliberations, effectively arguing for a data-dependent approach that delays further rate action. German investor optimism fell to a three-year low, reflecting broader eurozone anxiety. Associated British Foods announced plans to spin off budget clothing chain Primark after more than three decades as part of the conglomerate, while Ukraine reported the Druzhba pipeline would resume oil flows Tuesday, potentially paving the way for a reconstruction loan.
