Eight car-carrying ships operated by BYD allow the Chinese electric-vehicle 
producer to deliver to Africa, Europe, Latin America and the Middle East — 
markets that don’t have tariff barriers as prohibitive as the US.

BYD’s in-house fleet gives it more control over its supply chain and helps it 
navigate challenges such as geopolitical volatility and bad weather, according 
to Bloomberg News reporting Wednesday. (Click here 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/china-s-byd-builds-up-shipping-fleet-to-export-cars-amid-war-weather-threats?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 to read the full story.)

As the Iran conflict and the closed Strait of Hormuz roil trade routes and 
send shipping rates soaring, BYD’s car carriers are coming in handy as the 
company bets on exports to pull it out of a yearlong earnings slump.

BYD’s overseas sales soared in April 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/byd-exports-jump-as-soaring-fuel-prices-spur-global-ev-demand?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 as surging gasoline prices in the wake of the Iran war spurred renewed 
interest in EVs, helping offset softer domestic demand. It’s just one way China 
projectsexport strength 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-09/chinese-export-growth-rebounds-as-war-fails-to-curb-trade?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 that shows few signs of weakening,posing risks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/china-s-policies-threaten-650-billion-in-g-7-us-chamber-says?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 to industries in developed economies.

Read More: BYD Talks to Stellantis, Others About Taking Europe Plants 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/byd-in-talks-with-stellantis-others-about-taking-europe-plants?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>

Born out of necessity in 2022 in the disruptions of the Covid pandemic, when 
BYD realized it couldn’t count on chartered vessels, the company’s seaborne 
reach helps it transport about 300,000 cars worth billions of dollars a year.

According to BYD, its ships have succeeded in “converting systemic 
geopolitical risk into a precisely calibrated operational certainty.”

That’s particularly clear around the Arabian Peninsula, where BYD vessels save 
time and money plyingcontested waters 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/putin-arrives-in-china-for-xi-talks-with-gas-project-on-agenda?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 where other commercial cargo carriers see too much risk.

Trump’s Tariffs

Meanwhile, manufacturers 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/european-automakers-press-brussels-for-further-co2-reprieve?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 in the European Union especially have beenstruggling 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/vw-pledges-more-cost-cuts-after-first-quarter-returns-decline?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 with uneven EV demand and intensifying competition from BYD and other Chinese 
rivals. Car sales in the region haven’t bounced back to levels seen before the 
pandemic, leaving many factories underused.

Now EU officials are exploring 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/eu-plans-tougher-trade-measures-to-rebuff-chinese-export-surge?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 whether the bloc needs new measures to protect its economy against an influx 
of Chinese goods.

“The EU has launched a scatter of product-specific trade defenses and a 
piecemeal buy-European industrial policy,” the Centre for European Reform said 
ina report 
<https://www.cer.eu/publications/archive/policy-brief/2026/china-shock-20-cost-germanys-complacency>
, which is titled “China Shock 2.0: The Cost of Germany’s Complacency” and was 
posted Wednesday. “But China’s trade surplus with the EU is still growing at 
around 30 percent annually, showing these efforts are too slow and too narrow.”

Read More: Ford’s CATL Deal Shows Way for Trump, Xi to Deepen Ties 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-05-12/can-ford-catl-s-3-billion-venture-be-template-for-chinese-investment-in-us?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>

Also causing headwinds are US President Donald Trump’s import taxes on 
foreign-made goods including cars.

Early on Wednesday, after months of delay, EU officials finished the text of 
its US trade deal announced last July,clearing a major hurdle 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/eu-expedites-start-of-us-trade-deal-in-bow-to-trump-demand?cmpid=BBD052026_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260520&utm_content=4547>
 to ratifying the pact before Trump’s threatened July 4 deadline to increase 
tariffs on European automobiles to 25% from 15%.
