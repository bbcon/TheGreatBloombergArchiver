WTO Meets Amid ‘Worst Industrial Crisis’ in Memory
The World Trade Organization faces a ‘defining moment’ in talks kicking off 
today amid energy crisis

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17436226&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=03262026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
The World Trade Organization kicks off its 14th biennial ministerial conference
 <https://www.wto.org/english/thewto_e/minist_e/mc14_e/mc14_e.htm> Thursday 
confronting a brewing economic crisis that’s sparking fresh concerns about the 
resilience of the global trading system.

“From a business perspective, we believe this could yet become the worst 
industrial crisis in living memory,” John Denton, secretary-general of the 
International Chamber of Commerce, said in a statement this week. “The test of 
the system is not rhetorical. It is practical. Can it respond at the speed and 
scale of the disruption facing the real economy?”

It’s too soon to know the answer, but the Mideast war now in its fourth week 
showsfew signs 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/israel-and-iran-keep-up-strikes-with-talks-to-end-war-uncertain?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
 of abating soon. The energysupply shock 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-25/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-26?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
 threatens to tank economies in Asia and Europe especially, forcing governments 
to use export controls and subsidies to cushion the impact.

Read More: Asia Braces for Worst-Case Oil Scenario as Iran War Drags On 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-25/asia-braces-for-worst-case-energy-scenarios-as-iran-war-drags-on?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>

Denton said “we are already seeing major companies invoke force majeure on 
their supply contracts and cut output as shortages ripple through energy, 
chemicals and other critical supply chains.” Fertilizer strains could create 
“food security risks” later this year, he said.

.
For Director-General Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala and trade ministers gathering in 
Cameroon’s capital of Yaoundé through Sunday, MC14 already had a full agenda 
including long-needed institutional reform and deciding on rules for customs 
duties ondigital services 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/us-presses-wto-to-keep-the-global-internet-tariff-free-forever?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
.

‘Boring’ Speeches

Chris Bryant, the UK minister for trade, arrived sounding intent on avoiding 
the dull lineup of lectures that wasted everyone’s time at past gatherings.

Read More: Global Trade to Slow Amid Forces of Energy Surge and AI 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/global-trade-to-slow-amid-opposing-forces-of-energy-surge-and-ai?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>

“So often, these big international congregations of ministers could end up 
just being one minister reading out a very boring monotonous speech, 
immediately followed by another minister reading out a very boring monotonous 
speech,” Bryant said in apost on X 
<https://x.com/RhonddaBryant/status/2036765216596172934>. “We want to stop that 
— we want to have genuine, genuine debate.”

That’s a worthy goal, but a tougher one to achieve with WTO’s multilateral 
approach to international commerce and low tariffs increasingly under threat.

As Bloomberg reported earlier today 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/germany-is-drafting-plan-to-hit-us-companies-in-next-trump-clash?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
, officials in Berlin have started mapping vulnerabilities in US supply chains 
to identify points where Germany and its European Union partners could apply 
leverage against President Donald Trump.

Transatlantic Deal

Today’s trade developments in Europe weren’t all divisive for the 
transatlantic relationship. As the Cameroon ministerial began, EU lawmakers
finally approved 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/eu-lawmakers-approve-us-trade-deal-after-several-delays?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
 a trade deal with the US, clearing a key obstacle for the long-delayed 
agreement despite lingering uncertainty about Trump’s import taxes.

Given all the turmoil facing the WTO’s 166 members, it’s hard to handicap the 
odds of MC14 success as talks unfold through the weekend. In almost a year 
since Trump’s “Liberation Day” imposed his now-illegal reciprocal tariffs on 
the world, nations largely absorbed his tariffs rather than retaliate.

Read More: War Hits Global Economy With US Inflation Seen at 4.2% 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/war-hits-global-economy-with-us-inflation-seen-by-oecd-at-4-2?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>

For its part, Washington submitted a 7,000-word outline 
<https://ustr.gov/sites/default/files/files/Issue_Areas/Trade%20Organizations/US%20Further%20Perspectives%20on%20WTO%20Reform%20-%20March%202026.pdf>
 of WTO reforms ranging from finding pathways for plurilateral agreements to 
rethinking the “unconditional” principle of most-favored nation treatment in 
trading relations.

“We intend to be constructive and cooperative as members prepare for a new and 
more member-driven phase of the reform effort,” the US said in its plan.

Many economies like the EU are pushing for WTO reform while adhering to the 
rules-based system andsigning trade deals 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/europe-australia-agree-to-trade-deal-to-boost-ties-defy-us?cmpid=BBD032626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260326>
 designed to lower barriers rather than raise them to fix imbalances.

EU trade chief Maros Sefcovic is set to meet US Trade Representative Jamieson 
Greer on Friday at the event. Speaking to reporters in Brussels earlier this 
week, Sefcovic said, “we see it as a defining moment for the World Trade 
Organization.”
