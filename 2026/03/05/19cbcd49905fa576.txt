Good morning. Oil jumps as Donald Trump praises military operation. China 
suspends exports of diesel and gasoline. And Elon Musk denies swaying Twitter’s

Oil continued a multi-day rally as Donald Trump declared that the US is doing “
very well <https://bloom.bg/3OR1I91>” in its joint military rampage against 
Iran, even as key details remain murky. China asked its largest oil refiners to 
suspend exports <https://bloom.bg/4spjvm8> of diesel and gasoline due to flow 
disruptions, people familiar said, prompting heating oil, gasoline and gasoil 
to jump.

Asian equities advanced for the first time since America and Israel struck 
Iran, showingsigns of recovery in markets <https://bloom.bg/4soAipt> roiled by 
the sudden attack. Haven asset gold received fresh demand, while US and 
European futures edged lower. Check out ourMarkets Today live blog 
<https://bloom.bg/4gqxcM0> for all the latest news and analysis relevant to UK 
assets.

In the latest slew of job cuts, Morgan Stanley is axing about 3% of its 
workforce <https://bloom.bg/4soeytI>, with the global cull set to impact about 
2,500 employees across all business lines, a person familiar said. Individual 
performance is among reasons for the dismissals.

Private credit woes persist as investors place bearish wagers 
<https://bloom.bg/40bP3A6> against the embattled Blue Owl Capital, as short 
sellers reckon the firm’s sinking share price has more room to fall. That’s as 
Goldman Sachs head David Solomon keepsa watchful eye on the lender industry 
<https://bloom.bg/47sKhlx> for “frothiness.”

 Deep Dive: Prized Possessions 


Talei’s car collection, which includes a Porsche Carrera GT, a Ford GT and 
multiple Ferraris.Source: Ben Talei 
The wealthy are turning garages, wine cellars and closets into gallery-like 
spaces to display prized possessions like supercars and designer handbags.

 * Beverly Hills surgeon Ben Talei created a glass-walled showroom for his car 
collection, while other homeowners in West London built custom wine rooms with 
specialized lighting. 
 * These bespoke spaces, which gained popularity during the pandemic, serve as 
personal museums that showcase owners' passions and investments to visitors.
