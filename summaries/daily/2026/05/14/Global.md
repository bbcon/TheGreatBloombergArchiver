The Strait of Hormuz remained the most consequential single geographic flashpoint in global markets. US efforts to resolve the Iran war were set back after a commercial vessel was apparently seized by unauthorized personnel near the UAE, injecting fresh uncertainty over who effectively controls the waterway. A Japanese supertanker emerged in the Gulf of Oman after what appears to have been a covert transit, while two India-bound LNG cargoes also appeared to cross the strait — highlighting that flows are occurring, but under conditions of elevated risk rather than normalized passage.

The energy disruption is rippling into global price data with increasing clarity. US PPI accelerated to 6% year-on-year, led by freight costs and energy, while US jet fuel exports surged to a record 455,000 barrels per day as airlines in Europe and Asia scrambled for supply. OPEC+ members are targeting a completion of quota restoration by end-September, though whether that alleviates tightness depends substantially on Hormuz conditions. WTI futures held near $101 per barrel.

The summit's energy subtext was notable. Xi reportedly opposes any toll regime for Hormuz transit and expressed interest in purchasing more US oil to reduce Gulf dependency — an alignment of interests that could, at the margins, create an opening for US LNG as China's Qatari supply has been disrupted. The UN climate chief, speaking at Tsinghua University during Trump's visit, praised China's clean energy leadership explicitly as a counter-model to US withdrawal from Paris Agreement commitments.

Big Tech's global debt issuance added a separate cross-regional theme: Alphabet has raised nearly $60 billion over four months in dollars, euros, pounds, Swiss francs, Canadian dollars, and yen, with banks warning the scale of AI-driven corporate borrowing risks crowding out local issuers in foreign-currency bond markets. Investors snagged 5% yields on 30-year Treasuries for the first time in nearly two decades, marking a notable level for long-duration pricing globally.
