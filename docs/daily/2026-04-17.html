Daily Brief

Friday, April 17, 2026

US

US equity markets continued their strong recovery from the war-related selloff, with the S&P 500 on track for a third consecutive week of gains exceeding 3% and closing above 7,000 points for the first time since January. The Nasdaq 100 extended its winning streak to 12 consecutive sessions. The rally has been underpinned by Iran peace optimism, robust earnings broadly, and renewed appetite for risk among retail investors. A basket of shares favored by individual investors is on course for its best month relative to mutual fund favorites since 2020, with the Roundhill Meme Stock ETF up 54% since late March — a reflection of sentiment rather than fundamentals, though the two are not entirely separable in the current environment. Netflix provided the most notable earnings disappointment of the week, with shares falling approximately 10% in premarket trading after the streamer's earnings forecast missed estimates. Co-founder and chairman Reed Hastings simultaneously announced his departure from the board after 29 years. Alcoa also disappointed despite elevated metal prices, with operational disruptions and higher costs weighing on results. QVC, the television shopping network, filed for bankruptcy as part of a plan to restructure more than $5 billion in debt — a casualty of secular shifts in retail rather than the macro environment. On fiscal and energy policy, states including California, Colorado, New York and New Jersey are accelerating large-scale renewable energy procurement to qualify for federal investment tax credits that expire on July 4. The deadline — tied to the 250th anniversary of US independence — is concentrating deployment activity, with Southern California Edison alone targeting 6,000 megawatts of additional capacity. The Trump administration, meanwhile, rejected CenterPoint Energy's request to retire a 60-year-old Indiana coal plant, a decision consistent with the administration's posture toward fossil fuel infrastructure. Former Treasury Secretary Henry Paulson, speaking on Bloomberg Television, warned that a collapse in Treasury demand remains a tail risk and urged authorities to develop contingency plans — a sobering note amid an otherwise ebullient market backdrop.

Asia

China's first-quarter GDP expanded 5.0% year-on-year, beating expectations, offering a measure of reassurance to policymakers navigating simultaneous headwinds from US tariffs and the Iran war. However, the Canton Fair in Guangzhou provided a more granular and cautionary picture. Middle Eastern buyers — who had increasingly substituted for absent American and European customers since the pandemic and the onset of Trump's tariffs — are now conspicuously absent due to the conflict and flight disruption. Exporters described this year as potentially the worst in memory, facing the sequential blows of tariffs and war simultaneously. High-tech exports surged nearly 30% in the first quarter, offering a partial offset, but the durability of that momentum against deteriorating external conditions remains uncertain. Xi Jinping had his most diplomatically active week in nearly two years, receiving at least five high-profile delegations including Spain's prime minister, Abu Dhabi's crown prince, Vietnam's president, and Russia's foreign minister. Xi framed China as a source of stability against what he described as a "crumbling" global order, proposing a four-point Middle East peace plan and vowing to deepen ties with Moscow. His positioning is deliberate — building on the 2023 Saudi-Iran rapprochement — but Beijing's concrete contributions to conflict resolution have so far been limited, and state media pushed back against suggestions that China nudged Tehran toward negotiations. A Trump-Xi summit in roughly a month's time looms as the next test. The yuan's strength has prompted the People's Bank of China to use its daily reference rate to temper further appreciation, as war-driven capital flows fuel bullish sentiment. On trade infrastructure, yuan-denominated crude oil transactions reached a single-day record of 1.22 trillion yuan through CIPS. Separately, Australia announced a multibillion-dollar agreement to procure 11 advanced naval frigates from Japan — the first three vessels covered by an A$15–20 billion contract — a transaction that advances both Australian defense modernization and Japan's ambitions in global arms markets. LNG supply disruptions are reshaping long-term energy strategy across the region. Asian LNG flows have fallen to their lowest since 2020, and with prices still 50% above pre-war levels, Vietnam is reconsidering gas-fired power in favor of renewables, Pakistan is reviving domestic gas production, and China — buffered by domestic gas, pipeline imports, and record coal output — has seen LNG imports fall 25% without facing a supply deficit.

Europe

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer faces a deepening political crisis after it emerged that Peter Mandelson, his ambassador to Washington, was granted security clearance despite objections from vetting officials — a decision complicated by Mandelson's past associations with Jeffrey Epstein. Starmer responded by dismissing the Foreign Office's top civil servant, Olly Robbins, but the episode has rekindled questions about his judgment and grip on power, with calls for his resignation growing. The episode arrives at a moment when Starmer was attempting to position the UK as a constructive international actor, co-hosting with France a summit on establishing a naval force to ensure freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz. European divergences over the Hormuz mission are becoming apparent, with France and the UK convening the discussion but member states approaching the operational question from differing strategic perspectives. The London Metals Index reached a record high, driven largely by aluminum, as the Iran war disrupts supply chains and industrial metals respond to both genuine scarcity and safe-haven demand. Alstom fell 36% in Paris after withdrawing its free cash flow guidance for the full year, citing slow progress on key projects — the sharpest single-day decline for the French rail manufacturer since 2023. On monetary policy, a Bloomberg survey points to an ECB rate hike in June as 2026 inflation runs above earlier projections. ECB policymaker Madis Müller called for vigilance on inflation risks, and President Christine Lagarde used the IMF-World Bank meetings in Washington to warn of the risks posed by Anthropic's new Mythos AI model — a somewhat unusual intervention that nonetheless reflects genuine institutional anxiety about cyber vulnerabilities at a moment of elevated geopolitical stress. Bloomberg Opinion editors cautioned that European central bankers should be careful not to amplify the energy shock's economic damage through premature tightening. In France, three major mobile operators entered exclusive talks to acquire Patrick Drahi's SFR in a transaction valued at approximately $24 billion, a deal that would reshape the country's telecoms landscape if completed.

Global

The dominant macro narrative remains the trajectory of the US-Iran conflict and its implications for energy markets and global growth. Trump expressed optimism about a permanent ceasefire, claiming Iran had conceded on nuclear ambitions and Hormuz access — assertions Tehran has not publicly confirmed. Talks may resume this weekend, though Gulf Arab and European officials believe a durable agreement is more likely six months away. The ceasefire in Lebanon, announced this week, offers modest positive signal, but the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, with only a single Pakistan-flagged tanker completing an exit with crude cargo since the US blockade began — underscoring the gap between diplomatic tone and operational reality. Oil responded to the peace signals, with Brent falling toward $98 per barrel and WTI near $93, paring weekly gains. Price volatility has cooled markedly from the record $38 intraday range seen in mid-March, with Brent trading in approximately a $10 band this week. Industrial metals tell a different story — the London Metals Index reached a record high on aluminum supply concerns, with the six-metal LME index up roughly 12% over four weeks. The broader structural damage from the Hormuz disruption is becoming clearer. Panama Canal congestion has reached its worst since the 2023-2024 drought, with vessels facing 3.5-day waits and one ship paying $4 million to jump the queue. LNG's credibility as a long-term energy solution for emerging Asia has been materially set back — a second price shock in four years following Russia's invasion of Ukraine is accelerating diversification toward renewables, domestic gas, and coal, with implications for Big Oil's growth strategy in developing markets. Anthropic's Mythos AI model — described as highly capable of identifying system vulnerabilities and orchestrating cyberattacks — emerged as an unexpected focal point at the IMF-World Bank spring meetings in Washington. Central bank governors from the ECB, Bank of England, and New York Fed all raised the issue publicly within days of each other, reflecting institutional alarm at a moment when international coordination capacity is already strained. The dollar's safe-haven rally appears to be fading, with Wall Street banks including Deutsche Bank and Wells Fargo recommending short positions as ceasefire prospects reduce haven demand.
