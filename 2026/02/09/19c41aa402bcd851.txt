Less than a month ago, bond markets had a meltdown over Japanese Prime 
Minister Sanae Takaichi’s plan to cut taxes on food. After her gamble to call a 
surprise winter election secured thebiggest victory <https://bloom.bg/3O2BIaq> 
for a single party since World War II, investors seem willing to give her the
benefit of the doubt <https://bloom.bg/4tmO90Q>.

Japan’s equity benchmark surged almost 4% to a fresh record high 
<https://bloom.bg/3OgTAON> on Monday while the yen and government bonds stayed 
noticeably calmer <https://bloom.bg/4r47274> than many had feared following 
weeks of volatility fueled by concerns about fiscal sustainability. While 
longer-tenor Japanese bond yields initially jumped, they quickly pulled back, 
easing concerns of a disorderly selloff that fueled January’s bond rout.

Reassurances from policymakers helped steady sentiment. Finance Minister 
Satsuki Katayama emphasized that a proposed consumption tax cut would be 
limited to two years, apply only to food, and would not be funded through 
additional debt issuance. Meanwhile, Takaichi stressed that she would conduct a 
responsible and proactive fiscal policy.

Now she has a clear mandate to lead, the victory will show us the true Takaichi
 <https://bloom.bg/3OgTC9n>, argues Bloomberg columnist Gearoid Reidy. One 
major change she might now pursue could be the first-ever revision of the 
US-imposed pacifist constitution to recognize the nation’s military. With a 
sufficient majority in the lower house, and enough parties that say they want 
revision in the upper, it’s an idea whose time might soon come.

That would prove extremely unpopular in Beijing and will likely only push her 
closer to Washington.—Samson Ellis



 What You Need to Know Today 



Thailand’s stocks and currency rose as a convincing win by the ruling 
Bhumjaithai Party in Sunday’s election raised expectations forpolicy certainty 
<https://bloom.bg/3OgTCGp> and stronger reform momentum. The benchmark Stock 
Exchange of Thailand Index advanced as much as 4% in Monday trading. The baht 
strengthened more than 1% versus the dollar.

Stecon Group shares jumped as much as 22%, leading a rally 
<https://bloom.bg/3Ms5Awp> in companies tied to victorious Prime Minister 
Anutin Charnvirakul. A solid victory for Anutin and likely coalition partners 
clears out risks of sustained political deadlock or turmoil. It also ensures 
policy continuity, sustaining some handouts promoted by his party and easing 
the way for a new budget.




A Hong Kong court sentenced former media mogul Jimmy Lai to 20 years in prison
, theheaviest penalty <https://bloom.bg/46y1Obt> ever meted out under Beijing’s 
imposed national security law. Three judges hand-picked by the government 
ordered the 78-year-old pro-democracy advocate to effectively spend the rest of 
his life behind bars for two counts of conspiring to collude with foreign 
forces and a separate sedition conviction. The landmark case has prompted 
intense international scrutiny and will test Western governments’ willingness 
to press for the British citizen’s release even as they seek to stabilize 
relations with China.
Effective life sentence: Jimmy Lai Photographer: Anthony Wallace/Getty Images 



Chinese regulators have advised financial institutions to rein in their 
holdings of US Treasuries, citing concerns over concentration risks and market 
volatility, according to people familiar with the matter. Officials urged banks 
to limit purchases of US government bonds and instructed those with high 
exposure to pare down their positions, the people said. The directive doesn’t 
apply to China’s state holdings of US Treasuries. Communicated verbally to some 
of the nation’s biggest banks in recent weeks, the guidance reflectsgrowing 
wariness <https://bloom.bg/3OgTDtX> among officials that large holdings of US 
government debt may expose banks to sharp swings.




The People’s Bank of China is boosting the supply of money available to banks 
to ensure they can meet thesurge in demand <https://bloom.bg/4rFQpi9> for cash 
during the Lunar New Year holiday. The central bank injected $86.4 billion via 
a 14-day repurchase agreements late last week, ending a two-month hiatus for 
such operations.

Industrial Securities forecasts the PBOC to add over $500 billion via similar 
tools before the holidays kick off on Sunday. Keeping the financial plumbing 
well-greased is essential to ward off a seasonal cash crunch and maintain 
economic momentum against mounting headwinds.




Lower lending margins and higher tax expenses weighed on DBS’s fourth-quarter 
profit. Net income for Southeast Asia’s largest lender dropped 10% to $1.85 
billion,well below <https://bloom.bg/4aCyvqO> analyst estimates. Shares fell as 
much as 1.9%. The success of the bank’s wealth-management unit helped cushion 
earnings during a decline in interest rates both at home and in the US. With 
heightened geopolitical concerns in the region, the business has also benefited 
from the rich looking for a safe haven to put their money.




Gold climbed above $5,000 an ounce, as dip-buyers returned to the market after 
an exceptionally volatile week for precious metals. Bullion rose as much as 
1.7% in Asian trading on Monday,recovering some more ground 
<https://bloom.bg/3OeDoxr> after a historic rout at the end of last month. The 
metal has recovered around half of the losses sustained since it plunged from 
an all-time high hit on Jan. 29. Silver also advanced.

Precious metals had been on a record-breaking ascent, driven by heightened 
geopolitical risks, the debasement trade and concerns about the Federal 
Reserve’s independence. US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent cited “unruly” 
trading in China as a reason behind last week’s wild price swings.




Seattle’s “Dark Side” defense carried the Seahawks to a  29-13 victory 
<https://bloom.bg/3OgTFC5> over the New England Patriots to claim the 
franchise’s second Super Bowl. Quarterback Sam Darnold threw a touchdown pass 
to AJ Barner, Kenneth Walker III ran for 135 yards and Jason Myers set a Super 
Bowl record by making all five of his field-goal tries. Walker became the first 
running back to win the MVP award since Hall of Famer Terrell Davis did it with 
Denver 28 years ago.

Meanwhile, Bad Bunny headlined a visually stunning halftime performance that 
also featured appearances by Lady Gaga and Ricky Martin. The Grammy-winning 
Puerto Rican artist sang entirely in Spanish. The backlash to his performance — 
from people upset that someone who performs in Spanish is headlining the most 
American of sporting events — is confusing <https://bloom.bg/4qYQYnc>, Lucas 
Shaw points out in the latest edition of our Screentime newsletter. He is not a 
niche artist, or a coastal artist. His music videos on YouTube over the last 
year generated 1.56 billion views — compared to 158 million for Kid Rock, who 
is performing the alternate halftime show.

 For Your Commute 



American activist investor Steven Wood’s one-man mission to overhaul the 
governance and culture of Swatch Group AG just became a lot harder.

The Swiss watchmaker and its plain-talking, cigar-smoking CEO Nick Hayek may 
have spent most of the past two years disappointing shareholders with tumbling 
sales and shrinking profits, but last month they surprised with an upbeat 
outlook and bullish predictions of a recovery this year. The market reaction 
was dramatic. The company’s shares — the most shorted in Europe — had their 
best day ever, and are up 15% this year.

It was a timely riposte from Hayek, the son of Swatch’s founder, as Wood’s 
push to get on the board enters its second year. The CEO says he’s not troubled 
by the campaign, but recent statements suggest the company isn’t taking any 
chances. Days after the updated forecasts, it nominated its own new board 
member, which it said would strengthen governance. The implied message from 
Hayek to shareholders: Trust him anddon’t side with the outsider 
<https://bloom.bg/3OgTRBj>.
