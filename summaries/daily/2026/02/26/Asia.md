China's trade posture hardened ahead of a planned Trump-Xi summit, with Beijing warning against further tariff increases even as Washington reaffirmed its commitment to maintaining elevated levies on Chinese goods. Trump's State of the Union address notably omitted any direct mention of China — the first such omission by a US president in two decades — leaving the bilateral relationship in an ambiguous posture: confrontational on trade, yet diplomatically active at the leadership level. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz's concurrent visit to Beijing with a delegation of German business leaders underscores how other major economies continue to engage China commercially even as Western political rhetoric remains cautious.

Japan's corporate governance reform narrative advanced with Reuters reporting that Toyota is preparing to unwind approximately ¥3 trillion ($19 billion) in cross-shareholdings with banks and financial institutions, potentially as early as this year. The move would represent a meaningful step in Japan's multi-year effort to improve capital efficiency and shareholder returns. Separately, the Bank of Japan's most hawkish board member renewed his call for higher interest rates, a signal consistent with the yen outperforming G10 peers following reports that sustain rate hike expectations. South Korea's central bank held its benchmark rate at 2.5%, upgrading both growth and inflation forecasts while publishing new dot-plot projections.

India presented a more nuanced picture. Prominent investor Raamdeo Agrawal cautioned that the post-pandemic equity rally has largely bypassed the broader population, with financial asset ownership concentrated in just 5% to 7% of households, raising questions about the sustainability of consumer demand. Indian regulators simultaneously tightened bank lending to brokers and raised margin requirements on derivatives in an explicit effort to reduce systemic risk, pressuring exchange and brokerage stocks. On the corporate side, Apple is in discussions with major Indian lenders and card networks for a potential mid-2026 Apple Pay launch integrating with the Unified Payments Interface — a strategically significant move given UPI's dominance. In Hong Kong, Jimmy Lai's 2022 fraud conviction was overturned by the Court of Appeal, a narrow legal reprieve that leaves his 20-year national security sentence intact.
