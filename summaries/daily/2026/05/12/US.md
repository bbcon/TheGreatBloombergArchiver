The dominant US market theme on May 12 centers on the recalibration of Federal Reserve expectations as Kevin Warsh's nomination clears an initial Senate vote. Rather than delivering the rate-cutting cycle that investors had initially priced around his appointment, bond market positioning is shifting toward tighter-for-longer policy. Bank of America strategists have explicitly flagged the possibility of rate increases as underpriced, recommending short positions in two-year Treasuries. The pivot in sentiment reflects two forces: April's solid employment report and war-driven inflation pressures. Economists surveyed by Bloomberg expect Tuesday's CPI print to show annual inflation of 3.7%, the highest reading since 2023 — a figure that would materially constrain Warsh's room to maneuver regardless of his stated preferences. The US 10-year yield held near 4.43%.

Equity futures softened, with S&P 500 futures down 0.3% and the Nasdaq 100 off 0.7%, following back-to-back record highs in the prior session. The pullback appears driven by oil price uncertainty and the inflation print rather than a fundamental reassessment of the tech rally, though Michael Burry publicly warned that Nasdaq valuations have reached unsustainable levels following a "parabolic" surge. At the individual stock level, ZoomInfo plunged 32% after cutting its profit outlook and announcing layoffs, while Hims & Hers fell 17% on a profit miss.

On the fiscal and policy front, the Trump administration nominated former Navy SEAL Cameron Hamilton to lead FEMA ahead of the Atlantic hurricane season, which begins June 1. Hamilton's record is notably ambiguous — he previously authored a memo titled "Abolishing FEMA" before publicly contradicting that position before Congress. Roughly 5,000 staffers have departed the agency since January, raising questions about operational capacity precisely as wildfire and hurricane seasons converge. The administration separately asked a federal trade court to pause a ruling that declared the president's 10% global tariffs unlawful while appeals proceed.
