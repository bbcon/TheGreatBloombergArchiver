**UK Political Transition**
Burnham's arrival at Downing Street follows a period of sustained political instability — six prime ministers since 2016, none completing a full term. The IMF has warned against raising the top income tax rate as Burnham's team shapes its fiscal agenda, with chancellor candidate Shabana Mahmood described as entering with a relatively open policy slate. The political context matters for markets: UK gilt and sterling pricing will be sensitive to early signals on the fiscal framework, particularly given ongoing uncertainty around the growth outlook. Burnham's reported willingness to expand North Sea production represents a pragmatic departure from his party's prior energy stance, though the market impact will depend on the scale and speed of licensing decisions.

**Corporate and Markets**
Burberry fell 5.4% in London after sales growth disappointed investors seeking firmer evidence of a sustained turnaround. The broader Stoxx 600 decline reflected contagion from the semiconductor selloff rather than any Europe-specific macro catalyst. The ECB's expected September rate hike — if delivered — would represent a divergence from the Fed's current posture and could exert modest upward pressure on the euro, though the trajectory remains contingent on incoming inflation data.
