US equity markets continued their strong recovery from the war-related selloff, with the S&P 500 on track for a third consecutive week of gains exceeding 3% and closing above 7,000 points for the first time since January. The Nasdaq 100 extended its winning streak to 12 consecutive sessions. The rally has been underpinned by Iran peace optimism, robust earnings broadly, and renewed appetite for risk among retail investors. A basket of shares favored by individual investors is on course for its best month relative to mutual fund favorites since 2020, with the Roundhill Meme Stock ETF up 54% since late March — a reflection of sentiment rather than fundamentals, though the two are not entirely separable in the current environment.

Netflix provided the most notable earnings disappointment of the week, with shares falling approximately 10% in premarket trading after the streamer's earnings forecast missed estimates. Co-founder and chairman Reed Hastings simultaneously announced his departure from the board after 29 years. Alcoa also disappointed despite elevated metal prices, with operational disruptions and higher costs weighing on results. QVC, the television shopping network, filed for bankruptcy as part of a plan to restructure more than $5 billion in debt — a casualty of secular shifts in retail rather than the macro environment.

On fiscal and energy policy, states including California, Colorado, New York and New Jersey are accelerating large-scale renewable energy procurement to qualify for federal investment tax credits that expire on July 4. The deadline — tied to the 250th anniversary of US independence — is concentrating deployment activity, with Southern California Edison alone targeting 6,000 megawatts of additional capacity. The Trump administration, meanwhile, rejected CenterPoint Energy's request to retire a 60-year-old Indiana coal plant, a decision consistent with the administration's posture toward fossil fuel infrastructure.

Former Treasury Secretary Henry Paulson, speaking on Bloomberg Television, warned that a collapse in Treasury demand remains a tail risk and urged authorities to develop contingency plans — a sobering note amid an otherwise ebullient market backdrop.
