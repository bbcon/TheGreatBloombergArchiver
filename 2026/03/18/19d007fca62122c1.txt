‘The next ChatGPT’

The latest twist in the stock market’s AI frenzy is taking place in China, 
where investors and tech fans are obsessing over OpenClaw, an agent that uses 
large language models to do things like hail a ride and book restaurants.

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang triggered a huge rally today 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/china-s-openclaw-stocks-rise-as-nvidia-calls-it-the-next-chatgpt?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
 with his comment that OpenClaw is “definitely the next ChatGPT,” describing it 
as a foundational shift that expands what individuals can do with AI.

LLM providers MiniMax and Zhipu, which have offered tokens that power the 
agents, gained 20% and 19%, respectively today in Hong Kong. Major Chinese 
cloud computing providers also are rushing to offer OpenClaw to their 
customers, and their stocks have risen accordingly.

Tencent, in particular, has introduced several products in the past week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/tencent-s-sales-rise-13-in-boost-for-broader-ai-ambitions?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
 aimed at tapping the enthusiasm, and it’s working to integrate its own AI 
agent into its WeChat app. Tencent — which since 2025 had lagged Alibaba in 
stock market gains — has climbed about 4.7% since it launched its agentic AI 
services, setting the stock up for itsbest monthly performance against Alibaba 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/tencent-seizes-momentum-in-china-agentic-ai-race-against-alibaba?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
 in two years. —Charlie Zhu, Jing Jin, Zheping Huang, Luz Ding, Pei Li and

Korea discount narrows

The wild rally in South Korean stocks, which had been derailed by the war in 
Iran,got a fresh impetus today 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/korea-to-ban-double-listing-of-affiliates-to-boost-market-value?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
.

The Kospi index surged 5% after authorities moved to restrict publicly traded 
companies from listing certain subsidiaries, curbing a practice blamed for 
diluting shareholder value and helping to create a long-running “Korea 
discount.”

The move is also part of President Lee Jae Myung’s broader push to modernize 
governance 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/korea-seeks-fuel-from-shareholder-meets-after-138-gain-stalls?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
 and restore confidence in capital markets. Such measures helped fuel the 
world’s best stock rally last year, along with the AI frenzy that generated 
huge demand for the chips and memory made by Korea’s tech companies.

One of those firms, Samsung Electronics, said it’s considering a shift toward 
multi-year contracts for memory chips 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/samsung-weighs-multi-year-deals-to-ease-memory-chip-crunch-fears?cmpid=031826_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260318>
, a much longer timeframe than is typical that may help stabilize supply and 
ease concerns about a shortage. The stock surged 7%. —Sangmi Cha 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AVmRVLkjSjE/sangmi-cha>
