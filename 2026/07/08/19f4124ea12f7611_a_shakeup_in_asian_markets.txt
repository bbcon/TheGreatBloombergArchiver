Asian markets are seeing a sharp reversal of this year’s trends in July. A 
gauge of Chinese stocks traded in Hong Kong has surged 7% to top global peers, 
after falling behind all but Indonesia in the first six months. Korea’s Kospi — 
which blew the competition away before this month —is languishing 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/korean-stocks-extend-drop-from-peak-to-20-as-ai-jitters-spread?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 at the bottom of the league tables with a 15% loss.

There is little to suggest a change in fundamentals: investors favored Korean 
and Taiwanese chipmakers because of their earnings growth. Samsung on Tuesday 
reported a19-fold increase 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-06/samsung-scores-profit-beat-due-to-runaway-demand-for-ai-memory?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 in quarterly profit. Chinese tech giants, like Tencent and Alibaba, had 
plunged as weak domestic demandweighed on 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/tencent-revenue-miss-heightens-pressure-for-ai-payoff?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 their results.

But the divergence had become so marked that by the end of June, the MSCI 
China Index traded atthe lowest level 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-29/china-misses-out-on-ai-boom-as-stocks-trail-by-most-since-2001?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 relative to MSCI’s world index since the immediate aftermath of the Sept. 11, 
2001 attacks. This gap, perhaps, meant timefor a shift 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-07-02/chip-stocks-losing-their-shine-spells-trouble-for-south-korea?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
, especially as concern grew overextreme volatility 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-07-01/how-a-13-billion-leveraged-etf-drives-volatility-in-a-key-ai-memory-stock?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 in the Kospi.

There are some potential favorable headwinds. Reuters reported 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-07/chinese-ai-startup-deepseek-developing-own-ai-chip-reuters-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 that China’s DeepSeek is developing its own chip to help power AI systems, 
while The Information said Zhipu is considering designing its own AI chip due 
to increasing demand and US export controls. Alibaba jumped 13% on Wednesday 
after reports of apre-earnings update 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/alibaba-s-shares-jump-most-in-ten-months-as-earnings-hopes-grow?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 that the company conducted for analysts.

The reality is the rally in Korean stocks in particular had become shaky. 
Daily moves of more than 5% in either direction suggested increasing 
nervousness. The Kospi has now plunged 20% from last month’s high, taking it 
into a technical bear market. While China has tepid domestic demand and 
recurring regulatory risk, at least it isreassuringly 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/chinese-stocks-in-hk-jump-most-in-14-months-as-tech-rallies?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6334>
 cheap.
