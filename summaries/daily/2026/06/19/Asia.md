• Japan's inflation held steady in May, supported partly by government energy subsidies, leaving the Bank of Japan's policy path unchanged; BoJ Governor departed hospital, removing a near-term uncertainty.
• Asian equity markets retreated 0.5% from an all-time high as Iran-deal optimism gave way to concern about the durability of the US-Iran interim agreement; markets in China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan were closed for holidays.
• Fujikura surged 16% in Tokyo after raising profit guidance above consensus, citing AI-driven fiber-optic demand.
• MSCI's latest Indonesia review signaled a low probability of a frontier-market downgrade, offering relief to a market that has been the world's worst performer.

**Macro and Central Bank**
Japan's inflation print came in steady, with energy subsidies dampening the headline rate and reducing near-term pressure on the Bank of Japan to accelerate policy normalization. The BoJ governor's discharge from hospital removes a tail risk around leadership continuity, though it does not materially alter the policy trajectory. Separately, Indian software stocks declined following Accenture's warning on growth, illustrating how US corporate guidance is transmitting directly into Asian IT sector valuations. Thailand's finance ministry reported capital inflows at the expense of Indonesia, attributing the rotation to relative fiscal strength — a pattern consistent with broader EM differentiation in a higher-for-longer dollar environment.

**Markets and Corporate**
The dollar's sharp appreciation following the Fed's hawkish pivot is the dominant external variable for Asian asset markets. A stronger dollar raises the cost of dollar-denominated debt service across the region and reduces the attractiveness of EM local-currency bonds. Fujikura's guidance upgrade — driven by AI hyperscaler demand for fiber optics — is a reminder that AI infrastructure buildout continues to generate discrete earnings upgrades in the supply chain. The ASML-China chip machine controversy (addressed in the Global section) carries direct implications for technology-sector investment in the region.
