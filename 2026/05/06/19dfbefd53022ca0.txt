Keir Starmer.Photographer: Jose Sarmento Matos/Bloomberg
In the central London neighborhood of Hackney, Mayor Caroline Woodley is 
finding the Labour Party that’s dominated local government here for over 50 
years to be an unusually hard sell. All over London,voters are turning elsewhere
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-06/uk-local-elections-starmer-s-labour-risks-losing-in-london-to-polanski-s-greens?cmpid=050626_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260506&utm_content=4191>
.

 * Since Prime Minister Keir Starmer led the party to a landslide win less 
than two years ago, he’s been battered by a succession of policy fumbles. An 
early decision to cut pensioners’ fuel payments was followed by grilling over 
his judgment in appointing Peter Mandelson as US ambassador.
 * In Thursday’s local elections, Labour is on course to win its lowest 
proportion of the capital’s voters in more than a century, as Zack Polanski’s 
Greens and Nigel Farage’s Reform chip away at their base.
 * What plays out in London could also end up being existential for Starmer’s 
premiership, with several factions of his party ruminating challenges in the 
event ofpoor results
