US equity markets are heading into the month-end under pressure, with S&P 500 futures off 0.35% as February draws to a close. The benchmark is on course for its largest monthly decline since April, down roughly 0.4% on the month, a striking contrast to European and emerging-market outperformance. The underperformance reflects the twin concerns that have defined February: stretched valuations in AI-adjacent equities and growing recognition that AI's disruptive potential may compress margins and pricing power across the economy.

Among individual names, Block surged as much as 27% in extended trading after Jack Dorsey's fintech announced it would cut approximately 4,000 employees — nearly half its workforce — framing the reduction as an AI-driven productivity bet. Dell gained 9.8% on an outlook for AI server sales that exceeded estimates, underscoring that infrastructure demand remains robust even as software sentiment wavers. CoreWeave tumbled 11% after reporting a larger-than-expected loss alongside elevated capital expenditure guidance. Duolingo fell 24% in premarket after a below-consensus full-year outlook triggered downgrades from Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, and Evercore ISI.

The most notable market development in February may ultimately be the Treasury rally. US government bonds returned 1.5% for the month, with long-dated securities gaining 4% — their best monthly performance in roughly a year. The flight-to-quality bid reflects elevated geopolitical uncertainty and AI-related equity volatility, and appears to confirm that the $30 trillion Treasury market retains its role as the primary haven asset despite periodic debate about US fiscal sustainability.

On the trade policy front, reporting reveals a $112 billion gap between Chinese export data and US import data for 2025 — suggesting that up to a quarter of goods shipped from China may have circumvented existing tariffs through fraud, shell-company routing, and customs undervaluation. This has material implications: compliant US manufacturers face a structural pricing disadvantage, and the revenue and reshoring rationale underlying the tariff regime is partially undermined.
