**China: Domestic Demand Deterioration**
May's data confirmed a deepening bifurcation in China's economy. Retail sales contracted 0.6% year-on-year — the first negative reading since late 2022 — with auto purchases plunging 16%. Fixed-asset investment shrank 4.1% in the January-May period, the worst since 2020, with private capital expenditure falling 7.1% and manufacturing investment declining for the first time in six years. Against this, industrial production accelerated to 4.5% growth, driven by high-tech manufacturing expanding 15% in value-added terms. Some analysts estimate April GDP growth near 4%, below the government's 4.5%–5.0% full-year target, raising questions about whether Beijing will need to deploy meaningful demand-side stimulus.

**Bank of Japan**
The BOJ's 25-basis-point hike to 1.0% was accompanied by a commitment to hold bond purchases steady at approximately ¥2 trillion monthly from April 2027. A Bloomberg survey of BOJ watchers expects roughly two hikes per year; former BOJ chief economist Hideo Hayakawa sees the next move potentially as early as October. The Reserve Bank of Australia held at 4.35% — its first pause this year — warning inflation remains elevated while acknowledging tightening is beginning to weigh on activity.
