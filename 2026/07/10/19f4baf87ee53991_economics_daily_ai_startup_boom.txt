Need-to-Know Research

AI is hailed as a transformational general purpose technology (GPT) in the 
vein of railways, electricity and the internet. One thing about those: history 
shows while GPT тАЬvastly increased productivity in the economy, many of them 
were also associated with significant overinvestment and massive capital 
overhang.тАЭ

So wrote Federal Reserve board economist Geng Li in a new paper 
<https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/do-major-technology-advancements-lead-to-overinvestment-20260706.html>
 this week. HeтАЩs crafted a model looking at the impact of new technology 
lifting productivity, not just from capital plowed into the new technology 
itself, but more broadly across the economy.

One pattern that can unfold is, as early results come in and beat 
expectations, investors turn increasingly optimistic тАФ opting to plow more 
capital in. Eventually, so much can get invested that a swath of it ends up 
being unproductive, Li wrote.

тАЬAs implied by the model, in order to drive a sustained investment boom, the 
technology advancement has to be sufficiently large тАФ enough to repeatedly beat 
investor expectations, and the same criterion applies to AI,тАЭ Li said. WeтАЩre 
not there yet, Li indicated. If AI generates a sizable productivity surge, then 
itтАЩs possible the US will see another lasting investment boom, he said. That 
would then raise the potential for тАЬa significant overinvestment and capital 
overhang.тАЭ
