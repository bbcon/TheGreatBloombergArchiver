# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — May 20, 2026

**Xi anchors Beijing diplomacy as Putin visit underscores axis.** President Xi Jinping welcomed Vladimir Putin to Beijing with full state honors, the Russian leader's 25th visit, as the two convened against the backdrop of escalating tensions over Iran and the ongoing Ukraine conflict. Xi explicitly cautioned Washington against resuming strikes on Iran, calling a "comprehensive ceasefire" imperative—remarks that followed President Trump's renewed threats of military action. Talks centered on the long-discussed Power of Siberia 2 pipeline and deepening energy ties, with Russia now reliant on China for over 90% of sanctioned technology imports. The visit caps a year in which Xi has hosted leaders from the UK, Canada, South Korea, and Vietnam, positioning Beijing as an alternative diplomatic hub amid Washington's more transactional posture.

**Samsung labor breakdown threatens chip supply.** Negotiations between Samsung Electronics and its largest union collapsed, with a general strike set for Thursday after management rejected a government-mediated proposal. Shares fell as much as 4.4% before paring losses. The dispute reflects broader political tensions over AI-era wealth distribution: Samsung's semiconductor arm posted a 48-fold quarterly profit increase and is projected to generate nearly $200 billion in operating profit this year. President Lee Jae Myung's government has intervened urging direct talks, though the underlying question—how AI windfalls are shared—remains unresolved and increasingly politicized.

**Indonesia tightens monetary and commodity controls.** Bank Indonesia raised rates 50bp to 5.25%, double consensus expectations, in a defensive move against rupiah weakness amid Iran-driven volatility. Separately, President Prabowo announced a state-owned entity to centralize exports of palm oil, thermal coal, and nickel—a sweeping reassertion of state control echoing earlier authoritarian-era practices and likely to unsettle foreign commodity buyers.

**Other developments.** Indian rupee weakness continues, with funds positioned for a move toward 100/USD as New Delhi prepares vessels to transit the Strait of Hormuz pending government approval. Taiwan's President Lai used his second-anniversary address to cast Taipei as a stabilizing actor, comments aimed at reassuring Trump following last week's Xi summit. Japanese 30-year yields approach 3%, though Pimco has turned constructive on the long end.
