European equity markets are finding limited upside as the war-driven energy shock intersects with deteriorating growth prospects. Euro-area consumer inflation expectations jumped across the board in March, complicating the ECB's policy calculus at a moment when the region is simultaneously absorbing higher energy costs, weak demand, and growing recession risk. Analysts note that European stocks' brief period of outperformance relative to the US has faded, with stronger US growth and technology-driven market gains pulling capital away from the region.

UK politics remains turbulent. Prime Minister Keir Starmer faces a parliamentary vote today on whether to open a formal investigation into his assurances that due process was followed in appointing Peter Mandelson as ambassador to Washington. While Starmer's allies expect to win the vote, the prolonged controversy — which has run alongside several hours of committee hearings on Mandelson's security vetting — constitutes a costly distraction less than two weeks before local elections seen as a meaningful test of his leadership. King Charles III has arrived in Washington for a state visit, a trip freighted with uncertainty about the durability of the UK-US special relationship under the current US administration.

On corporate earnings, BP reported adjusted net income of $3.2 billion in the first quarter, more than double the year-earlier figure and ahead of the $2.64 billion analyst consensus, as surging oil prices and market volatility drove strong oil trading results. Barclays' trading division, by contrast, failed to match the outsized revenues generated by Wall Street peers during the same period of elevated volatility. In European M&A, EssilorLuxottica heir Leonardo Maria Del Vecchio agreed to buy out two siblings' stakes in family holding company Delfin for approximately €10 billion. UK Lords separately called for a substantially larger fiscal headroom, adding to Starmer's domestic policy pressures.
