Memory stocks are booming

They’re the hottest stocks in the market right now, and they’re still among 
the cheapest shares going. That tells you a lot aboutthe nature of the 
memory-chip industry 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-21/memory-stock-valuations-spark-debate-over-supercycle-potential?cmpid=042226_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260422>
.

Samsung shares have tripled while SK Hynix’s have quadrupled since the end of 
August as chip prices skyrocketed thanks to ravenous demand from AI. The 
companies and rivals such as Micron Technology are riding the surging sales to 
record profits, yet their stocks still trade at well under 10 times earnings, a 
fraction of the valuation of other top chip companies.

Bulls argue that AI is driving unprecedented demand in a so-called supercycle, 
so this time is different —the lumpy nature of memory earnings, which have 
historically tracked volatile cycles in the broader economy, is a thing of the 
past. Skeptics say the lower valuations are a message: The cycle isn’t dead, 
just dormant.

“We are, in a way, in a new paradigm for memory,” said Jorry Noeddekaer at 
Polar Capital. However, “this idea that you will never ever see cyclicality in 
memory — we are not buying that.” —Youkyung Lee 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ATNSmOYIcQc/yoojung-lee>

Tesla’s stock rut

Tesla is likely to report blowout earnings this afternoon. The trouble is the 
actual numbers might be overlooked as Wall Street seeks evidence that Elon 
Musk’s AI and robotics venturesjustify the stock’s sky-high valuation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-22/tesla-s-cooling-ai-hype-makes-investors-yawn-at-blowout-earnings?cmpid=042226_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260422>
.

Tesla’s ambitions for AI and robotics are what pushed the shares to an 
all-time high in December. And questions about those goals are what have sent 
the stock reeling 21% since then, the worst performance among the Magnificent 
Seven.

At 183 times estimated earnings, Tesla is by far the most expensive stock 
among the seven. As a result, investing pros say quarterly numbers that beat 
lowered expectations aren’t likely to move the stock.

Rather, Tesla needs one of two things to drag its shares out of their rut: 
Concrete signs of progress on its robotaxi plans or a shiny new object from 
Musk’s playbook that moves the goalposts for the company and resets the timer 
to show results.

“When a stock trades on a long-term story, patience doesn’t disappear 
overnight,” said Haris Khurshid at Karobaar Capital. “The existing base is 
still holding on, but it’s getting harder to attract new buyers without clearer 
results.” —Jordan Fitzgerald 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AWWN3K7Po-w/jordan-fitzgerald>
