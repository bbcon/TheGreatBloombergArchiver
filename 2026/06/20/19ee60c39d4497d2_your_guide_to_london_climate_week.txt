London climate week cheatsheet

By Bloomberg Green

Monday June 22

Climate Innovation Forum 
<https://londonclimateactionweek.org/event/lcaw-opening-event-climate-innovation-forum/>

The opening event of London Climate Action Week is a chance to hear from 
United Nations climate leaders, COP presidents past and future, as well as 
senior government officials. Speakers include Simon Stiell, executive secretary 
of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change, and Andr├й Corr├кa do Lago,

The eight-legged giant of British energy supply is hosting a buzzy,

<https://events.climateaction.org/climate-innovation-forum/agenda/>

Murat Kurum, the COP31 president-designate, will lay out priorities for the

<https://www.tickettailor.com/events/medialeadersltd/2240645>

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Time: 9:00 a.m.

<https://www.unepfi.org/grt2026/agenda/>

Financial supervisors and central banks, especially in Europe, are pushing

Bank and Sabine Mauderer of the Deutsche Bundesbank, will speak at the UN

Time: 10:00 a.m.

Global Renewables Alliance, are bringing together experts to talk about how to 
speed the deployment of electricity technologies around the world.

Time: 12:30 p.m.

E3G Convene Reception <https://www.e3g.org/>

One of the weekтАЩs best networking events, bringing together investors, 
diplomats, policymakers, climate advocates and business leaders for informal

Time: 5:30 p.m.

T├╝rkiye Energy Transition Investment Forum <https://luma.com/j1vfmq2k>

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Climate and Development Finance Forum 
<https://londonclimateactionweek.org/event/climate-and-development-finance-forum-unlocking-finance-at-scale-for-country-led-transformation/>

Hosted by E3G, British International Investment, EBRD and the Country

Time: 8:30 a.m.

A scientific update on what exactly is going on with global climate change

<https://events.barclays.com/ereg/newreg.php?eventid=882191&eb=&ebs=*/encrypted_id/>

With the AI boom causing a spike in power needs, how can the UK meet that 
demand without compromising on security and affordability? These questions will

Time: 3:30 p.m.

The Missing Middle of Climate Finance 
<https://hub.wri.org/events/2026/6/wri-london-climate-action-week-2026>

Investors, development-finance institutions and policymakers explore why so

the gap between ambition and investment. Hosted by the World Resources 
Institute.

Time: 10:30 a.m.

Golden Square тАУ A Day of Ocean Discovery 
<https://londonclimateactionweek.org/main-programme/?utm_source=chatgpt.com>

A public festival in Soho featuring ocean-themed activities, exhibits and 
hands-on experiences designed to connect visitors with marine conservation and 
climate action. Hosted by Kanaloa, 10% for the Ocean and Blue Marine Foundation.

Time: 11:00 a.m.

Thursday June 25

London Climate Resilience Finance Summit 
<https://www.climateresilience.finance/>

Government leaders, investors, development-finance institutions and 
civil-society organisations will explore how to scale investment in climate 
adaptation and resilience, particularly in vulnerable economies. The summit 
focuses on closing the adaptation-finance gap and developing investable 
resilience projects.

Time: 9:00 a.m.

Friday June 26

Indigenous Women on the Front Line <https://luma.com/t7s8w00y>

Female indigenous leaders from four countries across three continents will 
speak at this event hosted by Greenpeace, to argue that bureaucracy is stopping 
them from receiving climate finance and more direct funding is needed.

Time: 9:30 a.m.
