Euro-area inflation jumped to 2.5% in March, the steepest monthly acceleration since March 2022, when the Russian invasion of Ukraine drove a comparable energy shock. The reading came in below the consensus estimate of 2.6%, providing modest relief, and — importantly — core and services inflation actually softened relative to February. The divergence suggests that the energy pass-through is the primary driver rather than broadening underlying price pressure. Poland's inflation rose to 3%, while Tokyo's gauge cooled, illustrating uneven spillovers across economies.

The ECB faces a genuinely difficult calibration. Bank of Italy Governor Fabio Panetta emphasized the need to prevent a wage-price spiral while keeping policy proportionate to the mandate. Bloomberg Economics' assessment is that April rate hike odds remain low given that the data slightly undershot projections, providing cover for the doves on the Governing Council. That said, the ECB's credibility was damaged by its slow response to the 2022 shock, and policymakers appear determined to signal vigilance even if action is not yet warranted.

European equity markets have fallen 7.6% in March — the worst monthly performance in nearly four years — with LVMH, SAP, and Novo Nordisk collectively accounting for more than half of the €420 billion in market capitalization erased this quarter. Energy supply disruption is weighing on diesel availability, as tankers divert away from the Persian Gulf. On the corporate side, Unilever announced advanced talks to sell most of its food business to McCormick, with a final announcement potentially imminent. In the UK, house prices unexpectedly rose according to a major mortgage lender, suggesting consumer demand held up at the onset of the conflict, though the UK's structural rigidities — regulated energy pricing and inflation-linked wages — leave it comparatively exposed to energy shocks relative to euro-area peers.

A former Deutsche Bank manager, Michele Faissola, filed suit in London for at least £473 million, alleging wrongful blame for the Monte Paschi accounting scandal.
