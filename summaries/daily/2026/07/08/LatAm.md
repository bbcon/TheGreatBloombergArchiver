**USMCA and Trade Uncertainty**
The Trump administration's decision not to renew the USMCA introduces sustained uncertainty rather than immediate rupture. By declining to formally select the 16-year extension option, Washington retains implicit leverage to pursue full termination if its demands—including raising regional vehicle content thresholds from 75% to 82% and requiring 50% US-sourced value—are not met. The auto sector, representing nearly 18% of US trade with its neighbors, faces the sharpest near-term exposure. Toyota's announced shift of some truck production from Mexico to Texas was cited by the administration as validating its approach. Oxford Economics cautions the outcome may suppress cross-border investment irrespective of which party formally bears the political cost.

**Colombia Transition Risk**
The mutual suspension of transition meetings between Colombia's incoming and outgoing governments represents an unusual breakdown in institutional process. The handover, already expected to be the most contested in decades, now carries elevated policy continuity risk across fiscal, energy, and security portfolios.
