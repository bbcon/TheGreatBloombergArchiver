Europe is absorbing the Iran war's economic consequences at a moment of pre-existing fragility. The bloc had only recently begun shaking off the growth drag of the Ukraine conflict, and energy-driven inflation now threatens to reinstate the stagflationary pressure that defined 2022-23. European economies are revising output expectations downward while simultaneously bracing for a renewed inflationary impulse, a combination that complicates ECB policy sequencing significantly. UK retail sales declined for the first time in three months, with consumers pulling back even ahead of the more recent escalation in oil prices.

In London, the housing crisis deepened. Ground broken on residential homes fell to just 5,547 units last year — down more than 75% from a decade earlier and the lowest in at least fifteen years — while London's population has grown by over half a million in the same period. The collapse of a contractor in Ealing has left over 100 affordable homes unbuilt, illustrative of a broader pattern of cancellations and indefinitely paused projects. The supply-demand gap appears set to widen further.

On the corporate side, Pernod Ricard is in preliminary discussions to acquire Brown-Forman, owner of Jack Daniel's, with Brown-Forman's Class B shares rising as much as 21% on the news while Pernod fell 5.7% in Paris. The potential deal reflects consolidation pressure across the global spirits industry amid a downturn in demand. The UK's flood insurance program came under scrutiny for channeling subsidies from lower-income northern households to wealthy southern property owners through a structurally flawed council tax calculation dating to 1991 — a distributional distortion the program's own CEO acknowledges cannot be corrected independently.

UK Prime Minister Starmer signaled publicly that he will not be pressured by Trump, as the Iran war continues to strain the bilateral relationship. Trump's criticism of NATO's refusal to support US military operations — warning he would "remember" the alliance's response — adds a further layer of institutional tension across the Atlantic.
