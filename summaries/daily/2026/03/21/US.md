Federal Reserve communications remained a focal point this week as policymakers navigate the tension between still-elevated inflation and mounting signs of fiscal drag. No major policy decisions were announced, though the broader backdrop of persistent data-center-driven energy demand and Iran-war energy price pass-throughs continues to complicate the Fed's path to its 2% target. The interplay between energy prices and shelter inflation appears to be extending the timeline for any easing cycle, though the precise magnitude remains difficult to quantify.

On the fiscal and policy front, New York Governor Kathy Hochul introduced a proposal to revise the state's Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act, seeking to push its 40% emissions reduction target back from 2030 to 2040. Hochul cited affordability pressures — specifically, rising energy bills linked to data-center demand growth and Iran-war-related fuel price increases — as the rationale for delay. The proposal also includes a provision to defer implementation of a cap-and-invest program to 2030. The political optics are notable: a Democratic governor in a reliably blue state moving to weaken climate legislation at the same moment the Trump administration is rolling back federal emissions rules. Whether the state legislature accepts the revisions ahead of the April 1 budget deadline remains uncertain. Critics, including Earthjustice and several state senators, argue the changes primarily benefit fossil-fuel incumbents rather than consumers.

Separately, the robotaxi sector drew attention following Uber's announcement of an investment of up to $1.25 billion in Rivian's autonomous vehicle program. The deal reflects continued capital deployment into the AV space, though analysts have flagged the potential congestion externalities of large-scale AV deployment — particularly deadheading miles — as an underappreciated urban policy challenge. The absence of road-pricing frameworks in most US cities may amplify rather than resolve traffic dynamics as the technology scales.
