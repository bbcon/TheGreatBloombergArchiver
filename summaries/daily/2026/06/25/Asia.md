**Semiconductors and AI**
The Micron results reverberated most forcefully in Asia. Korea's Kospi staged a sharp recovery after two sessions of volatility driven by SK Hynix's pivot toward lower-margin DRAM chips. The $29 billion SK Hynix US equity issuance — the scale of which would ordinarily pressure shares — was absorbed positively in the context of Micron's demand signal. Japan's Kioxia separately announced plans for US depositary receipts in spring 2027 alongside a stock split, seeking access to deeper capital markets. Kioxia shares have risen approximately 900% this year, making it Japan's most valuable listed company.

**China and Geopolitics**
Anthropic's accusation that Alibaba deployed thousands of fraudulent accounts to access its Claude AI model illicitly represents, according to the company, the most extensive attempt by a Chinese firm to circumvent US AI export controls. Baidu and Xiaomi fell more than 3% in sympathy, reflecting broader investor concern about Chinese firms' competitive position in frontier AI. Separately, the US declined to send senior officials to the APEC meeting in Macau, citing what it described as China's discriminatory visa restrictions on American diplomats — a signal of continued friction in bilateral relations.
