**ECB Policy**
The contrast with the ECB's 2022 Sintra moment is notable. Four years ago, with rates still negative and Spanish inflation approaching 10%, policymakers were plainly behind the curve. Today, having led G7 central banks with a rate hike following the Iran war's energy shock, the ECB convenes at Sintra from a position of relative confidence. Inflation, while above target, is smaller in magnitude than the 2022 shock, and the economy appears able to absorb tightening. Chief Economist Philip Lane declined to pre-commit to further moves, emphasizing a meeting-by-meeting approach as policymakers assess how four months of elevated energy costs are still feeding through to prices. Belgian governor Wunsch — typically hawkish — acknowledged another hike is possible but less urgent than markets had previously priced.

**Spain Solar Oversupply**
Spain's solar sector illustrates the investment risk inherent in rapid renewable buildout. Overcapacity has driven wholesale prices deeply negative during peak hours, with 2026 already surpassing the annual record for negative-price hours. Asset values are falling sharply, several projects are for sale at steep discounts, and short sellers have opened positions in listed solar names. For consumers, the oversupply has delivered some of Europe's lowest power prices — roughly half German levels — but the episode highlights grid management challenges that are also causing the UK to likely miss its 2030 clean electricity target by five years.
