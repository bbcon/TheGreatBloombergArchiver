**ECB Policy**
The ECB's decision to hold was fully anticipated by markets, with Bloomberg Economics characterizing the September meeting as the probable venue for a final rate increase in this tightening cycle, contingent on commodity prices remaining elevated. The Stoxx Europe 600 fell 0.8%, reflecting broader risk-off sentiment tied to rising energy costs and earnings disappointments rather than any policy surprise.

**UK Politics and Paris Finance**
New Prime Minister Andy Burnham marked his first week in office with a package of cost-of-living measures, including tax reductions on pubs and live-music venues. Separately, a court ruling that Marine Le Pen may contest next year's French presidential election has unsettled London-to-Paris banking relocations: many financiers who moved following Brexit are now weighing further relocation to Dubai or Milan as French tax incentives begin to expire, representing a meaningful if slow-moving risk to Paris's post-Brexit financial hub ambitions.
