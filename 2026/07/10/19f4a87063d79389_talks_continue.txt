Deep Dive: Targeting Illegal Casinos

A trade body for European gambling companies has filed a complaint to the 
Lithuanian banking regulatorseeking an investigation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-10/egba-seeks-probe-of-walletto-over-unlicensed-gambling-payments?cmpid=071026_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260710&utm_content=6411>
 of a payment company that it alleges props up illegal online casinos.

 * The European Gaming and Betting Association’s complaint, filed 
confidentially last month and seen by Bloomberg News, alleges the electronic 
money institution Walletto provides payment services to unlicensed gambling 
websites in violation of European Union and national anti-money laundering 
regulations.
 * Targeting a payment company represents a new strategy by Europe’s gambling 
industry as it struggles to curb the online black market, which has grown on 
the continent to almost $24 billion annually, according to estimates from 
research firm H2 Gambling Capital.
 * Attention is now shifting to companies that enable unlicensed casinos to 
function after regulators, under pressure from legitimate operators, had 
limited success in shutting down such sites directly.
