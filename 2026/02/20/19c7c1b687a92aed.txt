The US Supreme Court on Friday ruled against President Donald Trump’s use of 
emergency powers to impose so-called reciprocal tariffs.

The court’s 6-3 decision invalidates Trump’s “Liberation Day” tariffs and 
duties imposed on goods from dozens of countries, and could cut the US average 
effective tariff rate by more than half.

The justices did not address the extent to which importers are entitled to 
refunds, leaving it to a lower court to sort out those issues, which could 
total as much as $170 billion if fully allowed, according to Bloomberg 
Economics.

White House officials have said tariffs will remain in its economic arsenal, 
under different legal authorities.

“We’re thrilled. It’s a feeling of tremendous relief that we don’t have to 
manage the uncertainty of not knowing what the tariff rates will be.”

Eva St. Clair

Co-founder of e-commerce apparel company Princess Awesome

Princess Awesome is one of thousands of small companies that have sued to 
receive potential tariff refunds, unlike many larger companies.
