European sovereign bond markets came under significant pressure this week, with UK 10-year gilt yields rising to their highest level since 2008 following the Bank of England's Thursday statement that it "stands ready to act" against rising inflation. The BoE voted unanimously to hold rates, but the hawkish shift in tone — Governor Andrew Bailey explicitly noting that the prior expectation of scope to cut has changed — drove a sharp repricing. UK gilt volatility is now a focal point for global fixed income investors, compounded by February borrowing data that came in above forecast due to a surge in debt-interest costs.

The European Central Bank's posture has shifted similarly. Governing Council member Joachim Nagel told Bloomberg Friday that a rate hike as early as April is conceivable if energy-driven inflation risks materialize, and the ECB has dropped language describing policy as being in a "good place." Money markets are now pricing two quarter-point ECB hikes this year. The policy pivot echoes the post-Ukraine inflation episode of 2022, which Nagel explicitly referenced. Bloomberg Economics estimates March inflation across advanced economies is running approximately 0.4 percentage points above pre-conflict projections.

At the EU Brussels summit, leaders called for a moratorium on strikes against energy infrastructure, with Italian Prime Minister Meloni described as particularly animated about the severity of the energy situation. European officials are simultaneously navigating fatigue from US-related crises, and there are signs that governments are reconsidering their posture toward China as a more predictable trade partner — a strategic reorientation that is still nascent but appears to be gaining momentum.

In corporate news, Unilever confirmed it has received an offer from McCormick for its food division, valued by Barclays at approximately €28-31 billion. The deal, if completed, would represent the largest restructuring in Unilever's near-century history, pivoting the group toward beauty and personal care.
