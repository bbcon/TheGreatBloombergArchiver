European markets bore a heavier share of the global selloff than US counterparts, consistent with the region's greater energy import dependence. The Stoxx Europe 600 fell 2.3% to its lowest since September, now down 11% from its February peak — technically in correction territory. UK gilts are on track for their worst monthly performance since the Truss-era crisis of autumn 2022, with two-year yields climbing to their highest since February 2024 at approximately 4.7%. Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey attended an emergency cabinet meeting convened by Prime Minister Keir Starmer, underscoring the severity of the dual fiscal-monetary stress the UK faces.

The ECB signaled it remains on alert for inflation pass-through from elevated energy prices, with Vice President Luis de Guindos warning of war-related price fallout. ECB officials Lane, Cipollone, and Escriva are scheduled to speak Monday. Business PMI data due Tuesday is widely expected to show synchronized deterioration across the eurozone's manufacturing and services sectors — the first comprehensive economic health check since the conflict began.

European political developments were notable on several fronts. In France, Socialist Emmanuel Grégoire won the Paris mayoral race, extending the left's hold on the capital, while Marine Le Pen's National Rally fell short in its bid to take control of major cities — a result that may temper expectations for the RN ahead of next year's presidential election. In Germany, Chancellor Merz's CDU dislodged the Social Democrats from power in Rhineland-Palatinate after 35 years, though the result adds complications for SPD within the governing coalition. Slovenia's Prime Minister Golob secured a narrow victory over nationalist Janša in that country's closest election on record, with coalition negotiations likely to be protracted. On the corporate side, Italy's Poste Italiane made a €12.5 billion offer for full control of Telecom Italia, with Poste shares falling 7% and Telecom Italia rising 4.2%.
