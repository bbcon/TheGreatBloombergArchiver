Thursday, June 04, 2026

US

  • ISM services activity beat expectations, with input costs rising at the fastest pace in nearly four years — a significant inflation signal echoing the 2021 price surge warning.
  • The Fed's Beige Book described prices increasing "at a moderate to strong pace," with energy-related costs from the Middle East conflict driving broad inflationary pressure across shipping, groceries, and packaging.
  • US petroleum inventories including strategic reserves have fallen for 10 consecutive weeks to their lowest level since 2004, intensifying medium-term oil price risks.
  • Broadcom shares fell approximately 14% in premarket trading after issuing AI chip revenue guidance of $16 billion for the current quarter, below Wall Street expectations.

Economic Data and Inflation Signals

The ISM services survey showed activity expanding even as input costs accelerated to the fastest pace in nearly four years — a combination that complicates the Federal Reserve's policy calculus. The parallel Beige Book reinforced this picture: activity remains broadly firm, with the Oxford Economics word-count index of Fed district conditions indicating resilience, but price pressures are broadening. Energy costs tied to the US-Iran conflict were cited as the primary driver, with spillovers into freight, packaging, fertilizers, and groceries. Non-labor input costs are rising faster than selling prices, generating margin compression particularly among consumer-facing firms. UCLA's Anderson School projects core CPI peaking at 4.5% by year-end, up from May's 3.8%, with the oil shock having now displaced tariffs as the leading economic risk.

Logistics and Supply Chains

The Logistics Managers' Index transport price gauge rose to 96, the highest reading in the report's 10-year history, with capacity contracting and utilization elevated. Overall logistics costs reached their highest level since March 2022. Regional Fed contacts reported fuel surcharges of up to 50% on domestic freight, compressing retailer margins. The report noted that supply-driven inflation is harder for the Fed to counter with rate increases than demand-driven price pressures.

Markets and Corporate News

Equity markets pulled back — S&P 500 futures were off 0.4%, with Nasdaq 100 futures down 1.2% — as Broadcom's guidance miss weighed on AI-linked hardware names broadly. Vanguard's VOO S&P 500 ETF became the world's first trillion-dollar exchange-traded fund. SpaceX launched investor briefings ahead of a $75 billion IPO scheduled for June 12, with Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JPMorgan competing aggressively for the lead role despite razor-thin fee arrangements. ---

Asia

  • The Bank of Japan is set to consider a 25 basis point rate increase at its June meeting, with officials seeing the possibility of a further hike later in 2026 — a significant shift for a central bank that spent decades battling deflation.
  • The yen weakened back through 160 per dollar despite hawkish BOJ signaling and prior Ministry of Finance intervention warnings, highlighting persistent structural pressures on the currency.
  • South Korea's KOSPI fell 1.8%, with Broadcom's guidance miss weighing on tech-heavy indexes in Seoul and Taiwan.

Monetary Policy and Currency Stress

The BOJ's apparent readiness to raise its benchmark rate toward 1% marks a remarkable inflection for Japanese monetary policy. Yet yen weakness persists near 160 per dollar — a level that previously triggered intervention in April — suggesting markets remain skeptical that rate increases alone can resolve Japan's structural imbalances. Brookings' Robin Brooks attributes the weakness to Japan's debt overhang; others point to growth remaining too fragile to sustain meaningfully higher rates. The yen's depreciation against the Chinese yuan is also deepening bilateral tensions. Across the broader region, currency guardians in Seoul and Jakarta stepped up interventions as dollar strength, rising oil prices, and expectations of higher US rates pressured regional exchange rates. India is reportedly weighing capital inflow measures to support the rupee without tightening borrowing costs.

Corporate and Political Developments

TSMC's CEO stated AI chip supply will remain constrained for years despite ongoing capacity expansion, confirming a structural bottleneck in AI infrastructure buildout. Indonesia faces compounding headwinds: the rupiah is at record lows, an MSCI investability review is pending, and a corruption investigation has reached President Prabowo Subianto's flagship $15 billion free-meals program. South Korea's ruling Democratic Party won broadly in local elections, further pressuring the opposition People Power Party following former President Yoon's impeachment. ---

Europe

  • SoftBank announced plans to invest up to €75 billion in AI data centers in France, reinforcing Paris's emerging position as Europe's primary AI infrastructure hub.
  • Germany, France, and the UK are working with Kyiv on a framework to engage Russia in Ukraine peace negotiations, citing a perceived shift in battlefield momentum toward Zelenskyy.
  • UK financial institutions are exploring retail savings deposits — totaling £2 trillion — as a potential long-term buyer base for gilts, where retail ownership currently stands below 4%.

France's AI Positioning

SoftBank's commitment builds on a deliberate strategy by President Macron, who personally lobbied founder Masayoshi Son, and extends France's early-mover advantage in AI policy dating to its 2018 national AI strategy. France's combination of technical talent and substantial nuclear power capacity has made it an attractive destination for data center investment relative to European peers. The announcement is a meaningful near-term legacy achievement for Macron, who faces term limits next year and a nationalist right polling well ahead of the 2027 presidential election. Execution risks remain: SoftBank's financing capacity is uncertain, and public opposition to data center land use is rising across Europe.

Gilt Market and Fiscal Dynamics

UK financial institutions are advocating for a retail gilt distribution push, citing the gap between the 4%–5.5% yield on government bonds and the 1.7% average savings rate. Proponents argue household savings represent an underleveraged source of long-duration domestic demand at a time when pension funds are reducing gilt exposure, increasing the UK's reliance on hedge funds and overseas buyers. The gilts market is on track for its third annual loss in five years, which may complicate efforts to build retail investor confidence. ---

Political Economy

His characterization of US pressure as politically motivated may complicate the Sheinbaum administration's diplomatic positioning, particularly as the new tariff framework exempts USMCA-compliant goods, giving Mexico a degree of insulation from the broader forced-labor levy. The exemption mirrors an earlier Liberation Day carve-out and suggests the bilateral trade relationship retains structural anchors despite rhetorical friction. Global

  • US petroleum inventories including strategic reserves fell for a 10th consecutive week to their lowest since 2004, raising the risk of a price shock if summer demand or hurricane disruptions reduce the buffer further.
  • A fragile Israel-Lebanon ceasefire was announced contingent on Hezbollah withdrawal from southern Lebanon, pushing oil modestly lower, but Iran stated there has been "no tangible progress" in broader peace talks with Washington.
  • Washington's new tariff framework — framed around forced labor — raises the effective US tariff rate by approximately 0.5 percentage points, covering economies representing 99% of US imports in 2024.
  • The Iran war is accelerating structural energy transitions in Asia and Europe, with more than 20 nations fast-tracking renewables deployment in response to fossil fuel supply disruptions.

Oil Markets and Hormuz

Brent crude remains just below $100 per barrel, with December futures turning upward even as near-term prices dipped on ceasefire news. The underlying supply picture is deteriorating: US diesel stocks are near 2003 lows and risk falling to a critical 20-day supply threshold by August, according to Goldman Sachs. Vitol confirmed that gasoline inventories globally are well below seasonal norms. With hurricane season approaching and the strategic reserve buffer largely depleted, the margin for error in global oil markets is narrow. The ceasefire announcement provided only modest relief given Iran's explicit statement that broader negotiations have stalled.

Energy Transition Acceleration

The Iran war is functioning as a structural catalyst for clean energy deployment across Asia and Europe, analogous to the role Russia's Ukraine invasion played for European gas diversification. The Philippines fast-tracked more than 30 renewable projects; Pakistan's LNG-fired generation fell 80% year-on-year in April. China's exports of solar panels, EVs, and related electrotech reached record levels in March and April. The IEA identifies more than 20 nations making irreversible structural shifts in their energy mix. In parallel, soaring US power demand from AI data centers is straining PJM Interconnection — the nation's largest grid operator — with federal officials now openly discussing a potential breakup if governance reforms fail.

Monetary Policy Divergence

The global monetary picture is increasingly asymmetric. The BOJ appears set to hike toward 1%, Australia's central bank is seeing early evidence its hikes are transmitting, and the Fed faces mounting pressure from energy-driven inflation — with FOMC communications under new Chairman Kevin Warsh potentially becoming more concise. Subdued readings in Switzerland, Sweden, and the Czech Republic support holds in those economies. The dollar index reached its highest level in nearly a month on tariff announcements and geopolitical uncertainty, reinforcing the dollar's safe-haven status despite the US being the proximate source of both trade and geopolitical disruption.
