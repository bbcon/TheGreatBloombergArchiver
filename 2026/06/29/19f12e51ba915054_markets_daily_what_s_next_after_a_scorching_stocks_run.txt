Focus on risks

The US stock market has been on a wild ride. On March 30, the S&P 500 was down 
more than 7% for the year as war gripped the Middle East. Since then, it has 
reversed direction and is now up more than 7%.

As investors look ahead to the second half of 2026, they are grappling with a 
series of risks

and US elections that could shift the balance of power in Congress.
