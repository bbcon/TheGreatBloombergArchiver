An emboldened Israel has emerged, though more isolated on the international 
stage following its deadly responses to Oct. 7. As American voters turn away, 
Israel can no longer take US support for granted as it has become accustomed to 
do.

Oil-producing nations from Saudi Arabia to the United Arab Emirates have been 
caught in the crosshairs of the changing order and responded in opposing ways. 
The UAE bound itself closer to Israel, while the Saudis are questioning the 
role of the US as a protector and building stronger defense ties with the likes 
of Turkey and Pakistan.

Only a year ago, both were lavishing gifts on Donald Trump during his Gulf 
tour. Now the Saudi-UAE relationship has broken down, with the Emiratis 
quitting the OPEC+ oil cartel.
