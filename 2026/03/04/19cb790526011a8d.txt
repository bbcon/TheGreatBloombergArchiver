Good morning. Oil prices rise as Donald Trump’s plan to escort and insure 
tankers in the Strait of Hormuz fails to bring calm. Blackstone executives chip 
in $150 million for fund redemption payments. And FIFA is in high spirits about 
the World Cup despite chaos in Iran.Listen to the day’s top stories 
<https://bloom.bg/4nmEQLf>.

— Nasteho Said



Brent extended gains as fresh attacks flared in the Middle East and traders 
weighed Donald Trump’s plan toinsure and escort <https://bloom.bg/4b8mfxp> oil 
tankers and other vessels through the Strait of Hormuz. The shipping industry 
sees it as only apartial solution <https://bloom.bg/4b4khxU> to a historic 
crisis, while US Navy escorts would risk being “sitting ducks” for Iran, ING 
said. Meanwhile, Iran’s Revolutionary Guards said they have “complete control” 
of the strait, AFP reported.

Asian stocks plunged the  most in a year <https://bloom.bg/4snk7sA>, led by 
the biggestSouth Korean crash <https://bloom.bg/4lqOo7L> since the global 
financial crisis in 2008. Thai markets <https://bloom.bg/4l5VWwh> also 
plummeted. US futures dropped, while European contracts were mixed. Gold and 
the dollar gained. Check out ourMarkets Today live blog 
<https://bloom.bg/4gqxcM0> for all the latest news and analysis relevant to UK 
assets.

The US president said he’ll “cut off all trade with Spain” after the country 
deniedaccess to its military bases <https://bloom.bg/4aOYBqQ> for his bombing 
campaign against Iran. Madrid said Washington must respect international law.

Chancellor of the Exchequer Rachel Reeves said the UK shouldn’t sacrifice its 
principles for morefavorable trading terms <https://bloom.bg/4cZ6wTG> with the 
US, as she stood by the government’s decision to withhold British backup for 
the American-Israeli assault on Iran.
