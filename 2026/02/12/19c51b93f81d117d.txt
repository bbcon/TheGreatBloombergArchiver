China Tourism

The latest data dose on Chinese consumer spending was — as has been the case 
for some time now — another downer.Retail sales 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-19/china-s-economy-grows-5-in-2025-as-export-boom-helps-meet-goal?cmpid=BBD021226_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260212>
 in December were at their weakest since the Covid reopening.

Subdued domestic demand has for years now been linked to the nation’s 
continuing real-estate bust. But there’s one industry that, nevertheless, is 
seeing a notable rebound: Tourism. Some of this is thanks to a return of 
foreign visitors, bolstered by Beijing’s expanding visa-free access push.

China’s inbound tourism is on track this year to surpass 2019 levels, 
according to Bloomberg Industries, with easier access stoking demand. The UK 
justjoined the club 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-29/china-to-allow-britons-visa-free-travel-for-visits-under-30-days?cmpid=BBD021226_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260212>
 of nations whose citizens don’t need business or tourist visas for short 
stays, bring it into line with the likes of Australia, France, Germany, Italy, 
and Japan. Canada also won that status last month.

Top tourism hubs including Beijing, Jiangsu in central China and Guangdong in 
the south have each saw a jump of about 40% in overseas visitors last year, 
resulting indouble-digit growth 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-01/-shop-in-china-travel-boom-risks-complicating-pboc-s-yuan-math?cmpid=BBD021226_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260212>
 in travel-related inflows to the yuan.

Not all nationalities are pouring in. Bloomberg Industries notes that while 
Russian arrivals have soared, tourism from the US has contracted. Read their 
full note on the industry on the Bloomberg Terminalhere 
<https://blinks.bloomberg.com/screens/DOCV%20RES%20T9WLE9KGIFTG>.

But domestic spending is also on the rise. Morgan Stanley analysts see the 
industry’s share of China’s GDP climbing by 1.9 percentage point by 2030, to 
around 6.7%. Behind that call: a base case for an 11% average growth rate for 
tourism revenue over the 2026-30 period. That would take it to 12 trillion yuan 
($1.7 trillion), with inbound visitors accounting for 16% of that.

“Travel is emerging as a powerful answer to China’s call for people’s 
wellbeing and happiness, echoing policymakers’ push to boost service 
consumption, improve employment, and further open the country to the world,” 
Morgan Stanley research analysts including Qianlei Fan wrote in a note this 
month.
Visitors at the Badaling section of the Great Wall in Beijing.Photographer: 
Andrea Verdelli/Bloomberg
The team noted that, among the “indelible memories 
<https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202512/31/content_WS695522f3c6d00ca5f9a085fe.html>
” President Xi Jinping cited in his year-end 2025 speech was a thriving in the 
tourist industry. The sector can serve as an effective “anchor” for encouraging 
domestic demand “because of its long and interconnected service supply chain – 
from transport and hospitality to retail, entertainment, and local services. 
This allows demand to diffuse across a wider set of industries,” Fan and her 
colleagues wrote.

Bottom line: China is “turning up the joy dial,” they said.

Need-to-Know Research

Concerns about crime, the future of their children and career prospects have 
driven more than 1 million rich and well-educated South Africans to emigrate,
according to a new report 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-11/crime-job-prospects-drive-rich-skilled-south-africans-abroad?cmpid=BBD021226_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260212>
.

The study is based on a poll of 1,500 South Africans living in 73 countries by 
the University of Cape Town’s Liberty Institute of Strategic Marketing. It 
reveals a diaspora that is predominantly university-educated and includes a 
“substantial concentration of high-value individuals,” the report said.
Source: UCT Liberty Institute
About 60% of those canvassed live in households earning more than $100,000 and 
a further 20% more than $200,000, it said. More are set to join them.

“Among younger Black students in particular, the intention to seek 
opportunities overseas has surged — rising from 39% in 2021 to 90% in 2024 — 
driven largely by limited local job prospects and the belief that international 
markets offer better career pathways,” the report said.
