# US Daily Briefing — February 17, 2026

**Markets digest soft equity tape despite supportive macro.** US technology shares were positioned for further declines as trading resumed after the Presidents' Day holiday, with Nasdaq 100 futures down 0.75%. The S&P 500's 14% gain since election day represents the weakest start to a presidential term in decades on a comparable basis, and the rotation underway — consumer discretionary lagging consumer staples — suggests a market reassessing AI infrastructure risks rather than embracing macro tailwinds. That weakness has come despite Q4 corporate earnings growth of 14.4% (the strongest in four years), Treasury yields running below Election Day 2024 levels, and inflation swaps little changed. Some strategists frame the AI-driven selloffs in adjacent sectors — logistics declined nearly 7% Thursday on disruption fears before partially retracing — as offering selective entry points, though the broader signal of markets shrugging off favorable data warrants caution.

**Inflation trajectory remains constructive heading into Friday's PCE.** January CPI data showed continued disinflation across the Fed's preferred underlying measures: the trimmed mean and median CPI sit at their lowest since 2021, supercore services continues to decline, and the Atlanta Fed's sticky-price gauge fell to 3%. The market is pricing roughly two cuts in the back half of 2026, with little expectation of action at the March meeting. Friday's PCE release — delayed since November owing to last year's shutdown — carries unusual weight; PCE had been stuck near 3% and risen above core CPI. A failure to resume its descent would complicate the easing path. One caveat: new car prices posted their largest January jump in years, masked by falling used car prices, suggesting tariff pass-through to consumers may only now be materializing.

**Private equity strains.** With roughly $7 trillion in AUM, holding periods at a 20-year high of 4.4 years, and LPs increasingly vocal about distribution shortfalls, the asset class faces mounting pressure. A pickup in IPOs and strategic deals through 2026 appears plausible but unlikely to clear the backlog quickly.
