**US-Iran Diplomacy: Constructive Tone, Unresolved Substance**

The first round of US-Iran nuclear negotiations in Oman concluded on a constructive note last weekend, with President Trump describing the talks as "very good" and Iranian President Pezeshkian calling them "a step forward." A second round was scheduled for this week. Brent crude opened lower Monday in response but remained up more than 10% year-to-date, suggesting traders were unwilling to fully discount the geopolitical premium. As SEB's Bjarne Schieldrop noted, the Iranian risk premium cannot be fully defused as long as US warships remain positioned as they are. Meaningful divergence persists between the two sides: Iran insists negotiations remain confined to its nuclear program, while Secretary of State Rubio has signaled Washington wants a broader framework covering ballistic missiles and proxy financing. Netanyahu traveled to Washington on Wednesday to press for tighter constraints on Tehran, explicitly signaling Israeli discomfort with the diplomatic opening. By Thursday, Brent was trading around $70 and heading for back-to-back weekly declines as the glut narrative reasserted itself alongside risk-off sentiment. At the London oil trading summit, Pimco's Greg Sharenow captured the prevailing mood: "The uncertainty band is wider than we would have thought six or 10 weeks ago."

**Energy Transition: Diverging National Trajectories**

The IEA's annual power report, released Friday, framed the coming half-decade as the "Age of Electricity," projecting global electricity demand growth roughly 50% faster than the prior decade and decoupling from GDP in a manner that marks a structural break. Goldman Sachs economists projected data centers will account for 40% of incremental US power demand through 2030, with electricity-price inflation near 6% through 2027. China continues to add generating capacity at a pace well beyond the US build-out, potentially shaping where the marginal data center is sited — though US export controls on advanced chips continue to constrain China's position in the stack. The US EPA moved this week to rescind the 2009 endangerment finding underpinning federal greenhouse gas authority, while EU carbon prices fell sharply after Chancellor Merz indicated openness to revising the bloc's emissions market — representing notable simultaneous softening of climate commitment from both Washington and Berlin. Against this, initial CREA analysis suggested China's CO2 emissions fell 0.3% in 2025, the first non-pandemic annual decline, as renewables absorbed all incremental power demand. The policy divergence between the two largest emitters appears increasingly structural.

**Monetary Policy Divergence Widens**

The week underscored how country-specific dynamics are reasserting themselves as post-Covid synchronization fades. Norway's January CPI surprised sharply higher on Tuesday, pushing 10-year yields to levels not seen since early 2009 and pricing a March Norges Bank hike — even as eurozone yields drifted lower on the same session. The RBA's Deputy Governor Hauser reiterated Australian inflation remains "too high." A hawkish BOJ member signaled conditions for the next rate hike could align by spring, while the PBOC's growing focus on the overnight rate fueled speculation of a formal shift in its policy target. Russia's central bank delivered an unexpected rate cut. In the US, the stronger-than-expected Wednesday payrolls print pushed the next anticipated Fed cut to July, though two cuts remain priced for the full year — an assumption that may face tests if inflation proves sticky. The MOVE index continues its steady decline from last April's Liberation Day shock, a notable disconnect given the breadth of geopolitical uncertainty. Political pressure on G7 central banks remained a consistent undercurrent: Trump's 15% GDP growth claim for Warsh, the Villeroy succession in France, and the BOJ's implicit political constraints all illustrate how the institutional framework for monetary independence is under simultaneous stress across major economies.
