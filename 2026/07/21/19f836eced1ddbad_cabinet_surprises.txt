Europe’s status as the planet’s fastest-warming continent is becoming a 
serious drag on its economic prospects 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-21/heat-morphs-into-a-major-economic-shock-for-an-unprepared-europe?cmpid=072126_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260721&utm_content=6774>
. The region, where temperatures are rising at a pace that’s roughly twice the 
global average, can no longer afford to ignore the economic impact of global 
warming, according to economists interviewed by Bloomberg.

 * They say governments will need to invest billions of euros in the years 
ahead to protect citizens from increasingly dangerous heat waves that are 
already cutting productivity, hurting industry and resulting in significant 
numbers of deaths.
 * This summer’s heat waves should be a “wake-up call for all European 
policymakers,” said Carsten Brzeski, global head of macro at ING Group. The 
temperature extremes have “really gripped all of Europe.”
 * The European Commission this year estimated that EU member states should be 
spending close to €70 billion each year through 2050 to adapt to the fallout 
from rising temperatures.
