**ECB Policy and Inflation**

The ECB faces a shifting calculus. Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel's statement that the bank can no longer look through war-driven inflationary pressures represents a meaningful rhetorical shift, suggesting the dovish case for further easing has weakened. Euro-area CPI due Tuesday will be the next data point to watch. Separately, the EU is considering a temporary freeze on its Russian oil price cap — currently $60 — ahead of a July review, with the ceiling likely to rise to at least $65 given prevailing market prices. This would limit Russia's windfall while acknowledging that the cap's original calibration is no longer consistent with current market realities.

**UK Political Economy**

UK political risk is rising. Andy Burnham, widely seen as a potential successor to Prime Minister Keir Starmer, has left open the possibility of an early general election — a statement that raises constitutional and market uncertainty. Meanwhile, the Starmer government faces pressure over leaked diplomatic exchanges involving former US ambassador Peter Mandelson. UK house prices fell at their fastest pace in nearly a year, compounding the political headwinds. EasyJet's board rebuffed a takeover approach from Castlelake as "highly opportunistic," though the airline's share weakness reflects genuine fundamental stress from elevated fuel costs.
