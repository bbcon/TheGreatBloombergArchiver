**Strait of Hormuz**
The US completed a seven-hour wave of strikes against targets near the strait and along Iran's coast immediately before resuming its naval blockade. Only a handful of vessels transited the waterway on Wednesday, including a US-sanctioned Iranian oil tanker and several commercial carriers. The IMO Secretary General described the passage as remaining unsafe. Two Iranian tankers were signaling Pakistan as their destination, suggesting they were seeking holding positions. Asian refiners are accelerating purchases of US crude as Persian Gulf supply becomes effectively inaccessible near-term; South Korea is absorbing record volumes of Canadian crude, a substitution that analysts say may outlast the conflict itself.

**Energy and Monetary Policy Divergence**
The tension between the June US CPI print — which offered temporary relief on the inflation outlook — and resurging oil prices creates an analytically uncomfortable juxtaposition. One month of benign data is unlikely to be sustained if Brent remains above $85. US natural gas, meanwhile, is decoupling from heat-driven power demand as solar and battery storage account for 91% of new US generation installed in Q1, with gas now supplying less than 40% of US power — four to five percentage points below a year ago. Globally, the Ebola outbreak in the DRC is accelerating, with the WHO warning true case counts may be two to four times the official figure.
