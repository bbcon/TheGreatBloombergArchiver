DeepSeek, the Hangzhou-based AI startup that triggered a significant market repricing in early 2025, unveiled preview versions of its V4 flagship model on Friday, introducing the V4 Flash and V4 Pro series. The company cited advances in coding benchmarks, reasoning, agentic capabilities, and a 1 million-token context window. Chinese chipmaker stocks rallied on expectations that the new model will support domestic semiconductor demand, a dynamic consistent with Beijing's broader push toward AI self-sufficiency. The announcement arrives roughly a year after DeepSeek's R1 model contributed to a sharp selloff in US technology stocks.

Chinese regulators separately announced plans to restrict technology companies from accepting US capital without government approval, framed as a response to Meta's acquisition of startup Manus. The move reinforces a pattern of Beijing tightening control over strategic technology flows in both directions. On climate policy, the State Council published rules holding provincial officials directly accountable for meeting Xi Jinping's emissions targets, including carbon peaking before 2030 and clean energy consumption benchmarks. Authorities rated unsatisfactory face formal disciplinary processes; clean energy equities advanced on the news, with Huadian Energy gaining as much as 10%.

TSMC shares rose as much as 5.1% to a record after Taiwan's Financial Supervisory Commission moved to relax a rule capping single-stock fund holdings at 10% of net asset value, a change JPMorgan estimated could draw more than $6 billion in inflows to the chipmaker. Tim Cook's announced departure as Apple CEO generated notable discussion on Chinese social media, reflecting his significant role in building China's technology supply chain and managing Sino-American commercial tensions. Indonesia's sovereign wealth fund Danantara confirmed it is in early-stage talks to acquire France's Eramet's 38.7% stake in PT Weda Bay Nickel, the world's largest nickel mine, as President Prabowo moves to consolidate natural resource assets under state control.
