Fast-Track Appointment

Speculation is well under way about who will succeed 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-22/knot-is-most-likely-heir-to-lagarde-at-ecb-survey-shows?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
 Christine Lagarde as ECB president at the end of next year. Recent events in 
France have added a timing factor to the debate.

Francois Villeroy de Galhau’s surprise resignation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-09/villeroy-quits-bank-of-france-letting-macron-pick-next-governor?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
 last week as national central-bank chief — for personal reasons, he insists — 
leaves thechoice 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-09/macron-gifted-a-chance-to-start-making-france-more-le-pen-proof?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
 of hisappointment 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-10/villeroy-exit-sparks-race-for-pivotal-bank-of-france-chief-role?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
 to outgoing French President Emmanuel Macron, rather than a possible far-right 
successor such as Marine Le Pen or her protege, Jordan Bardella.

Their National Rally party is riding high in the polls ahead of elections in 
April 2027, causing alarm across the old continent. So chatter is intensifying, 
what if the ECB — just like the Bank of France — could be madefar-right-proof 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-16/le-pen-s-shadow-is-falling-on-ecb-job-succession-to-lagarde?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
, too?

The opportunity exists for European governments, and it reaches beyond just 
Lagarde‘s job. That’s because Chief Economist Philip Lane is set to leave five 
months before her, in May next year, whileIsabel Schnabel 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-08/ecb-s-schnabel-says-she-d-be-ready-to-succeed-lagarde-if-asked?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
’s term on the six-strong Executive Board expires two months later, in December 
2027.

It’s a coincidence that makes an all-at-once, package appointment of 
successors more likely.

“I think that would be clearly Macron’s preference,” said Shahin Vallee, a 
senior associate fellow at the German Council on Foreign Relations, who advised 
Macron when he was France’s economy minister. “It’s not clear if other leaders 
will play along, but if Macron can have his way, he would make Lane’s 
appointment the moment for a broader deal.”

That means the nomination of Lagarde’s successor may be less than 12 months 
away. Or closer still, if the Frenchwoman, who says she’s “not a quitter 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-09/christine-lagarde-ecb-chief-on-inflation-trump-and-central-bank-independence?cmpid=BBD021626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260216>
,” decides she really doesn’t want to leave things to chance.

Need-to-Know Research

A geographical analysis shows the slowdown in January’s CPI was evident even 
in major metros that usually have higher inflation than the country as a whole, 
according toAlex Tanzi 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ADENOlzHbyY/alexandre-tanzi> of Bloomberg 
Economics. For the 12 large US cities tracked by the Bureau of Labor Statistics 
in January, average inflation was 2.16% — below the US average.

Annual inflation is now below 1% in the US Census Bureau’s West South Central 
division, which includes Arkansas, Louisiana, Oklahoma, and Texas. The Dallas 
metro area is now in deflation for the first time since the pandemic, while 
other major metros such as Chicago, Boston, and Minneapolis all saw annual 
inflation below 2% in January. In New York City, inflation has slowed from a 
peak of 6.7% in June 2022 to 2.9%.
