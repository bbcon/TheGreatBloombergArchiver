# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing
**May 21, 2026**

**AI optimism drives historic Asian tech rally.** Asian equities staged a powerful advance following Nvidia's earnings and SpaceX's IPO filing, with the gains concentrated in semiconductor and hardware supply chains where the region holds structural advantages. South Korea's Kospi surged 8.4%, while Taiwan's Taiex rose 3.4%. Samsung climbed 8% after reaching an eleventh-hour deal with its union — averting a potentially crippling strike at the world's largest memory-chip maker — that could distribute roughly ₩40 trillion ($26.6 billion) in AI-linked bonuses, averaging around $340,000 per chip employee. SK Hynix jumped nearly 8%, lifting its market capitalization to $910 billion. Samsung and TSMC have together added approximately $1.2 trillion in market value year-to-date. South Korean export data for May 1-20 reinforced the narrative, with working-day-adjusted shipments up 53% year-on-year and chip exports surging 202%. Japan posted a third consecutive trade surplus as semiconductor exports rose 44%, partly offset by a 64% collapse in crude imports reflecting Hormuz disruptions.

**Indonesia's policy turn unsettles markets.** Bank Indonesia delivered a surprise 50bp hike as the rupiah traded near record lows and the Jakarta Composite extended its year-to-date decline to roughly 26% — the world's worst-performing major equity market. The move followed President Prabowo's announcement of a new Danantara-controlled state entity to oversee exports of palm oil, coal, and ferro-alloys (collectively over $65 billion in 2025 shipments), a step that reportedly blindsided senior officials. While Jakarta frames the overhaul as targeting under-invoicing, investors read it as deepening economic nationalism. The hike may provide only temporary relief absent a shift in policy direction.

**Regional pressures mount elsewhere.** The Reserve Bank of India is reportedly weighing all options — including a rate hike, expanded FX swaps, and overseas dollar issuance — after the rupee touched nearly 97/USD. Trump's renewed suggestion of a direct call with Taiwan's Lai Ching-te introduces fresh tail risk to recently stabilized US-China ties. A BOJ official signaled support for a near-term hike, with June increasingly in play.
