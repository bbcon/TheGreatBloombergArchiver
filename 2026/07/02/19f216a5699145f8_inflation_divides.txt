Deep Dive: JPMorgan’s Europe Bet
Jamie Dimon in Paris, France.Photographer: Nathan Laine/Bloomberg
JPMorgan Chase’s engineers in Britain have spent years building software the 
firm hopes will be the foundations of a Europe-wide retail banking empire. Now, 
the Wall Street giant has decided Chase’s international effort — anchored by 
its UK arm — is apriority for the business 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-02/jpmorgan-wants-to-challenge-revolut-monzo-in-europe-bank-expansion?cmpid=070226_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260702&utm_content=6148>
.

 * Chase’s launch in the UK had much initial hype. By some metrics, Chase UK 
has come out ahead of newer rivals despite being one of the younger entrants: 
it had almost £30 billion of assets as of the end of last year.
 * Still, Chase’s rollout has been complicated by slow product launches, 
curtailed customer rewards, layoffs and regulatory hurdles, according to people 
familiar.
 * The consumer bank is said to be looking to expand deeper into Europe 
following its launch in Germany. In the UK, it wants users to invest with 
JPMorgan, and investors to save with Chase. It also wants better technology.
 * “I’m jealous,” said Jamie Dimon, JPMorgan’s CEO, about Revolut. “We’ve 
learned a lot by watching some of these folks. And we’ve got to get faster and 
better sometimes.”
