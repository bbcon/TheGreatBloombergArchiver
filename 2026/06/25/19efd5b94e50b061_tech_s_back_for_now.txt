Deep Dive: Dangote’s $40 Billion Nigeria IPO
A view of the compound of the Lekki Free trade zone, which houses Dangote 
Refinery, in June.Photographer: Victor Adewale/Bloomberg
Nigerian tycoon Femi Otedola is preparing to invest $100 million. Hundreds of 
miles away, security guard Wuroyda Danke plans to spend $150 borrowed from his 
grandmother. In Texas, Galahard Woghiren is lining up a $3,000 investment. All 
three have the same goal:buying a stake 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-06-25/dangote-nigeria-refinery-ipo-draws-tycoons-students-and-first-time-investors?cmpid=062526_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260625&utm_content=5919>
 in Aliko Dangote’s refinery business.

 * Dangote Petroleum’s plan to raise as much as $2 billion in Africa’s biggest 
initial public offering has sparked an investor frenzy across Nigeria. A rare 
public market debut for an industrial asset of this scale on the continent will 
be a once-in-a-generation test of market depth.
 * While global equity markets have been captivated by listings of artificial 
intelligence startups, investors in Africa are betting on something far more 
tangible: energy security.
 * The enthusiasm surrounding the Dangote Refinery reflects the strategic 
importance of the facility. Built at a cost of about $20 billion, the refinery 
reached full capacity weeks before the Middle East conflict intensified 
concerns about global fuel supplies.
