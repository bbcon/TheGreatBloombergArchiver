**Business Formation and AI**

The US is experiencing a second wave of entrepreneurial activity driven by artificial intelligence, following the post-pandemic surge that plateaued in 2021. Business formations in AI-adjacent professional services are projected to rise 24% over the next year, according to independent economists. The dominant form appears to be single-person "solopreneurs" rather than employer firms, leaving open the question of whether the wave will generate net employment or merely redistribute it. A Federal Reserve Board paper released this week cautioned that transformational technologies historically generate overinvestment cycles, and that AI would need to "repeatedly beat investor expectations" to sustain a durable investment boom — a threshold not yet met.

**Markets and Bank Earnings**

Equity futures drifted lower ahead of the weekend, with S&P 500 futures off 0.1–0.2%, as geopolitical risk from the US-Iran situation remained in focus. Investors are positioned for a consequential week ahead: five of the six largest US banks report Tuesday, with consensus expecting second-best-ever equity trading revenues. The KBW Bank Index's 13% year-to-date outperformance of the broader market, if sustained, would mark its third consecutive year of beating the S&P 500. Goldman Sachs separately banned employees from trading on prediction markets — excluding sports and entertainment — citing regulatory and insider-trading risks raised by the rapid growth of event-betting platforms.
