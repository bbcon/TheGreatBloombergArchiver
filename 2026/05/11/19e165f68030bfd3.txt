China’s sharp move away from deflation last month is a welcome development for 
Beijing, although it comes on the back of higher energy costs caused by the 
Iran war.

Producer prices rose 2.8% from a year earlier, the fastest since 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/china-s-factory-inflation-soars-near-4-year-high-after-cost-jolt?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 July 2022 and higher than all estimates in a Bloomberg survey of economists, 
whose median was 1.8%. Consumer inflation unexpectedly climbed to 1.2%.

“China has officially exited deflation,” said Carlos Casanova, senior Asia 
economist at Union Bancaire Privee in Hong Kong.

China had been trapped in a deflationary spiral since late 2022, as a 
manufacturing glut and sluggish domestic demand led to intense price wars. 
Price increases in mining and processing of non-ferrous metals, as well as 
crude oil, contributed to last month’s jump in factory inflation.

Economic damage from the conflict in the Middle East will likely feature 
prominently intalks this week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/china-confirms-x-trump-summit-that-was-delayed-by-iran-war?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 between Donald Trump and Xi Jinping.

Trump is expected to press Xi over Beijing’s approach to Iran, senior US 
officials said. While China has been adding its voice to global pressure to 
stop the conflict, revenue that it provides to Iran as well as potential 
weapons exports would be among the topics discussed, one of the officials said.

The US leader and Iran have rejected 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-10/trump-rejects-latest-iran-peace-offer-as-totally-unacceptable?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 each other’s latest peace proposals to end the war as the two sides struggle 
to maintain a fragile ceasefire.

In final preparations for the first US presidential trip to China in nearly a 
decade, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent will meet his Chinese counterpart He 
Lifeng for last-minute talks in Seoul on Wednesday, both sides confirmed.

—Richard Frost

What You Need to Know Today

Rising prices in China are fueling gains in the yuan, which has climbed to a 
more than three-year high. That strength is likely to continue over the coming 
year, according to Goldman Sachs, which said the currency is more than20% 
undervalued 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/goldman-says-yuan-20-undervalued-boosts-currency-forecasts?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 against the US dollar. Goldman analysts now see the yuan at 6.5 in a year’s 
time, up from 6.8 currently.

Other Wall Street banks have also predicted gains, with JPMorgan Asset 
Management saying that a “productive” summit between Trump and Xi may be a 
catalyst that pushes the yuan to 6.50.

Separately, Goldman economists said South Korea and Taiwan’s AI-fueled chip 
booms 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/goldman-sees-rates-pressure-as-ai-fuels-k-shaped-korea-taiwan?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 are set to swell both economies’ current-account surpluses to fresh records 
and pressure their central banks to raise interest rates later this year.

The oil market is in “a race against time” as the factors that combined to 
restrain price rises from the Iran war mayno longer hold 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/oil-market-in-race-against-time-on-hormuz-morgan-stanley-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 if the Strait of Hormuz stays closed into June, according to Morgan Stanley.

Despite the loss of almost 1 billion barrels, futures have failed to top 
levels seen in 2022 as the market entered the crisis with buffers, and 
investors kept expecting the strait to reopen, Morgan Stanley analysts said. In 
addition, higher crude exports from the US, coupled with slowing imports from 
China, helped to shield the market from the shock, they said.Brent crude 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-10/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-may-11?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 was up more than 2% to above $103 a barrel.

Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi appealed to citizens to cut fuel use and 
limit travel as rising oil prices from the Iran warthreaten to widen 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-10/modi-urges-indians-to-conserve-fuel-curb-costly-oil-imports?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 the nation’s import bill and strain foreign-exchange reserves.

“It is time for us to use petrol, diesel and gas with great care,” Modi said 
on Sunday while inaugurating projects in Hyderabad in southern India. The 
country, the world’s third-largest oil importer, has been among the hardest hit 
with the rupee falling to a new record low in recent weeks. To conserve energy, 
Modi said measures from the Covid pandemic such as working from home and online 
meetings could be adopted.

Australia’s upcoming budget will take aim at soaring home prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-09/australia-to-tackle-unacceptable-housing-market-chalmers-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 and seek to make it easier for people to gain a foothold in the housing 
market, Treasurer Jim Chalmers said.

The government is mulling changes to long-debated tax concessions — including 
negative gearing and capital gains tax — which have been widely viewed as 
favoring property investors, according to recent press reports. The backdrop is 
a deepeninghousing crisis 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-08/australia-to-try-to-thread-the-needle-in-budget-tested-by-war?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260511&utm_content=4336>
 in Australia, where soaring home prices and a dearth of supply have locked 
many younger buyers out of the market.
