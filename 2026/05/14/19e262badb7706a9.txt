The leaders of the US and China are wrapping up the first full day of talks in 
Beijing, with expressions of hope from both sides about putting their economic 
relationship back on an even keel and the trade hostilities of the past year 
behind them.

Out of the gate, President Xi Jinping warned Donald Trump 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/trump-meets-xi-in-china-for-summit-as-tensions-over-iran-simmer?cmpid=BBD051426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4354>
 of a potential conflict if the Taiwan issue is mismanaged, in blunt remarks 
that punctuated an otherwise cordial start to the first trip to China by a 
sitting US president in nearly a decade.

 * Follow all the developments in Beijing on Bloomberg’s live blog here 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/live-blog/2026-05-13/trump-xi-us-china-summit?cmpid=BBD051426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4354>
.
China offered what appeared to be the summit’s first concession on Thursday: 
Beijing hasrenewed import licenses 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/china-reopens-door-to-us-beef-plants-as-xi-and-trump-meet?cmpid=BBD051426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4354>
 for hundreds of US beef plants, reviving trade in a meat whose cost has surged 
for American consumers. A pound of US ground beef cost $6.90 in April, up 27% 
since Trump’s second term began, according to Federal Reservedata 
<https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/APU0000703112>.

Speaking on CNBC on Thursday, US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said 
Washington and Beijing are discussing a “board of investment” that would 
potentially allow Chinese companies to invest in the US in non-sensitive 
industries.

“There are plenty of things that the Chinese could invest in,” he said in a 
taped interview 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-14/bessent-says-us-china-are-discussing-a-board-of-investment?cmpid=BBD051426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260514&utm_content=4354>
 broadcast Thursday, declining to confirm a suggestion that Beijing has plans 
for $1 trillion or more.

A previously reported Board of Trade is also under negotiation, Bessent said. 
One idea is to remove tariffs on about $30 billion worth of trade “for 
non-critical areas and areas that we’re not trying to reshore,” he said.

according to Bureau of Labor Statistics data. A core measure of wholesale 
inflation that excludes food and energy increased 5.2% from April 2025 — the 
biggest advance in more than three years.
