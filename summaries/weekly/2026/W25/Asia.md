• China's May retail sales fell 0.6% YoY — the first contraction since the Covid reopening in late 2022, badly missing consensus — while fixed-asset investment contracted 4.1% in January–May, worse than forecast.
• The Bank of Japan raised its benchmark rate 25 basis points to 1.0% Tuesday, the highest level since 1995, in a widely anticipated move; the Nikkei hit a new record while the yen remained above 160 per dollar.
• Bank Indonesia raised its BI-Rate 25 basis points to 5.75% — its third hike in approximately one month — and the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas raised 25 basis points to 4.75%; Taiwan held for a ninth consecutive quarter.
• The MSCI China index fell 1.5% by Thursday and is approximately 20% below its October peak, approaching bear market territory, as weak consumer data and disappointing Alibaba and Tencent earnings weighed on sentiment.

**China: Domestic Demand Deterioration**
Tuesday's data confirmed a deepening bifurcation in China's economy. Retail sales contracted 0.6% YoY in May, with auto purchases plunging 16% and home appliance sales declining by double digits as government trade-in subsidies were reduced to 250 billion yuan from 300 billion in 2025. Even catering expenditure — historically resilient — rose at its slowest pace since late 2022. Fixed-asset investment shrank 4.1% in January–May, the worst since 2020, with private capex down 7.1% and manufacturing investment contracting for the first time in six years. Industrial production accelerated to 4.5% growth, driven by high-tech manufacturing expanding 15% in value-added terms, illustrating a demand-side rather than supply-side failure. Some analysts estimate April GDP growth near 4%, below the 4.5%–5.0% full-year target. PBOC Governor Pan Gongsheng separately signaled at the Lujiazui Forum a potential shift toward an overnight policy rate as the primary benchmark — a structural evolution that would meaningfully increase the central bank's influence over short-term funding costs.

**Bank of Japan**
The BOJ's Tuesday hike to 1.0% was accompanied by a commitment to hold bond purchases at approximately ¥2 trillion monthly through April 2027, when quantitative easing ends entirely. Statement language softened slightly — replacing "real interest rates are at significantly low levels" with "financial conditions are accommodative" — suggesting the tightening path remains intact but is communicated with less urgency. Futures markets assign roughly 50/50 odds to a further hike as early as October. The yen's failure to strengthen above 160 per dollar illustrates the limits of the BOJ's capacity to move the currency unilaterally. The Reserve Bank of Australia held at 4.35% — its first pause this year — warning inflation remains elevated while acknowledging tightening is beginning to weigh on activity.

**Southeast Asia and Regional Markets**
Indonesia's three consecutive hikes reflect urgency in defending the rupiah amid dollar strength amplified by the Fed's hawkish pivot. India's monsoon deficit, running approximately 40% below normal as of mid-June per the India Meteorological Department, introduces additional risk to agricultural output and rural incomes. The KOSPI gained 5.2% Monday on Hormuz relief, lifted by SK Hynix and Samsung, reflecting the region's acute sensitivity to energy import costs. South Korea's financial regulator opened an inquiry into Mirae Asset Securities after the firm failed to secure any SpaceX IPO allocation for clients despite serving as underwriter, with retail demand exhausting a $300 million tranche in under one minute.
