Here’s what we learned from the filing:

 * The listing is targeted to raise as much as $75 billion.
 * Elon Musk is all but certain to be the world’s first trillionaire

 * For Musk to get his full performance bonus

 * SpaceX plans to offer shares to retail investors through Charles Schwab,

 * The company had $18.7 billion in revenue in 2025, up from $14 billion the 
previous year. It swung from a profit of $791 million in 2024 to a loss of 
$4.94 billion last year.
 * Its Starlink satellite internet business accounted for about two-thirds of 
sales in the first quarter. The space segment is losing money.

 * The stock will trade on Nasdaq under the ticker SPCX. 
What’s driving investor enthusiasm? Musk’s company is playing a huge role in 
the stock market’s speculative frenzy for all things space-related, and the IPO 
is likely to only add to the mania.

Nvidia’s next chapter

Nvidia just reported 85% sales growth and touted a world of so-called physical 
AI, as humanoids and automated vehicles become more widespread. Yet the stock 
is only marginally higher and Nasdaq 100 futures are down.

So investors are yawning at another blowout quarter from the world’s biggest 
company? It depends where you look. This iswhat happened in Asia overnight 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/nvidia-s-huang-spurs-asia-tech-rally-with-ai-robots-hype?cmpid=052126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4711>
:

 * South Korea’s Kospi index soared 8.4%, helped along by a last-minute deal 
between Samsung and its union to avert a strike. Employees of the company’s 
semiconductor division will getan average bonus of $340,000 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/samsung-chip-workers-to-get-average-340-000-bonus-in-ai-boom?cmpid=052126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4711>
.
 * The Taiex Index in Taiwan, home to Nvidia suppliers TSMC and Hon Hai 
Precision, rallied 3.4%.
 * Memory-chip maker (and Nvidia supplier) SK Hynix jumped almost 8% in Seoul, 
pushing its market value to $910 billion.
 * A raft of other stocks — robotics-related companies like LG Electronics and 
Hyundai Mobis in South Korea, Nvidia supplier Ibiden in Japan — also surged on 
AI optimism.
The divergence between the US and Asian stock reaction comes down to this: 
Investors are looking at where the chokepoint is in the AI buildout, and it’s 
in memory and components. To play that, you go to the beneficiaries of all the 
capital spending, and they’re in Asia. Markets there also had a sharp pullback 
in recent weeks, emboldening dip buyers.

And as Hebe Chen at Vantage Global Prime notes, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang’s 
comments have “given investors a more concrete next chapter for the trade — 
moving the story beyond data centers and chips into robotics, automation and a 
hardware supply chain where Asia holds the keys.” —Phil Serafino
