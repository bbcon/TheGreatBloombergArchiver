European equity markets declined on Monday, with the Stoxx Europe 600 falling 1.45%, though the regional picture was uneven. The UK's FTSE 100 outperformed materially, down less than 1%, benefiting from its structural tilt toward energy, commodities, aerospace and defense, and defensive sectors — a composition Citigroup described as an effective geopolitical hedge, upgrading UK equities to overweight from underweight. UK bases, including Diego Garcia and RAF Fairford, were granted to the US for destroying Iranian missile depots following discussions with Prime Minister Keir Starmer, though Britain did not join the strikes directly. A suspected drone attack on the UK's Cyprus base occurred hours after Starmer's announcement, underlining the costs of British association with the campaign.

The UK's fiscal backdrop shifted notably ahead of Chancellor Rachel Reeves' Spring Statement scheduled for Tuesday. Tax receipts came in approximately £22 billion ahead of Office for Budget Responsibility forecasts, driven by stronger-than-expected self-assessed income tax and VAT revenues. The outperformance raises the prospect of reduced gilt issuance and relieves some pressure on UK yields, which remain the highest in the G7. Reeves' fiscal headroom against the rule requiring current spending to be covered by taxation by decade's end will be closely scrutinized, though the statement was broadly anticipated to be a measured affair before this weekend's events added an external complication.

European natural gas futures surged as much as 52% on Monday — the largest intraday gain since the 2022 energy crisis — as Qatar shut down the world's largest LNG export facility following an Iranian drone attack. Goldman Sachs estimates a month-long Hormuz shipping halt could cause European gas prices to more than double. Italian fiscal credibility received a modest setback after data showed it failed to meet the EU's 3% deficit ceiling last year. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz is due in Washington on Tuesday, where clarity on tariff policy for EU goods will be a central agenda item, alongside the broader geopolitical situation.
