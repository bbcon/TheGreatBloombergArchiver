# US Daily Briefing — February 6, 2026

**Markets: Risk Unwind Extends, With Tentative Stabilization**

US equity futures pointed to a modest recovery Friday morning, with S&P 500 futures up 0.55% and Nasdaq 100 futures gaining 0.71%, following a sharp three-day decline that marked the Nasdaq 100's worst stretch since April. The selloff has been broad rather than catalyzed by a single trigger, reflecting an unwind of crowded positioning across megacap tech, precious metals, and crypto. Bitcoin recovered toward $66,000 after touching $60,033 in Asian trading — still roughly half its October peak. Software stocks have been particularly weak, with traders coining the term "SaaSpocalypse" as investors reassess the threat AI poses to incumbent business models. The 10-year Treasury yield was little changed at 4.20%.

**The AI Capex Question Intensifies**

Amazon shares tumbled approximately 8% in premarket trading after the company guided to $200 billion in 2026 AI-related capital expenditure. Aggregate planned spending from Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft now approaches $650 billion for 2026 — a scale of investment with few historical analogs outside the late-1990s telecom buildout. The disclosures are sharpening investor scrutiny of return timelines, and Blackstone-backed Liftoff Mobile postponed its IPO citing the deteriorating tape. Whether this represents healthy repricing or the early stages of a more material correction remains to be seen.

**Fed Politics**

Senate Republicans have raised concerns about the Justice Department's investigation into Fed Chair Jerome Powell, while former Vice Chair Richard Clarida weighed in publicly on Kevin Warsh, whom President Trump has tapped to lead the Fed. The succession dynamic, combined with ongoing questions about institutional independence, has been one factor cited in this year's earlier bid for gold and dollar-diversification trades.

**Corporates and Trade**

Stellantis took $26 billion in charges tied to walking back its EV strategy, with shares down as much as 24% in Milan. Molina Healthcare plunged 33% premarket after guiding 2026 profit to less than half consensus, dragging Centene and Oscar Health lower. French customs data showed US tariffs weighing on European exports: Q4 spirits shipments to the US fell 47%, wine 39%, and cosmetics 25%. Separately, the administration set aside $12 billion to seed a critical-minerals stockpile aimed at reducing reliance on Chinese supply.
