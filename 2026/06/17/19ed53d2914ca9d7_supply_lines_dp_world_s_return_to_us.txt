DP World is getting closer 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/dp-world-seeks-return-to-us-container-operations-at-texas-port?cmpid=BBD061726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260617&utm_content=5492>
 to operating a US container port for the first time in two decades.

The Dubai-based port and logistics giant announced Tuesday that it entered 
into an exclusive negotiation for a long-term lease to operate a terminal at 
the Port of Corpus Christi in Texas. DP World would design and build the new 
facility.

If finalized, the project would mark a significant expansion into the world’s 
largest economy for DP World, whichexited <> its US port operations business in 
2006 on security concerns raised by some American lawmakers citing Sept. 11 
terrorists’ links to the United Arab Emirates.

Related: Hormuz Halt Rewires Trade to Turn Desert Roads Into Vital Links 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/iran-s-strait-of-hormuz-closure-turns-desert-roads-into-vital-trade-routes?cmpid=BBD061726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260617&utm_content=5492>

State-owned DP World has existing logistics operations in states including 
Pennsylvania and North Carolina, and currently uses Vancouver as its main entry 
point for cargo into the US, via rail to Chicago and elsewhere.
Source: DP World
Corpus Christi is one of the most significant US ports ranked by overall 
tonnage, largely because of its handling of energy, chemicals, agricultural 
commodities and bulk cargo.

‘New Pathways’

“The US Gulf Coast is one of the nation’s most important trade and economic 
corridors,” Brian Enright, CEO of DP World in the Americas, said in a press 
release. “The Port of Corpus Christi presents a significant opportunity to 
expand container capacity, strengthen supply chain connectivity, and create new 
pathways for American businesses to access global markets.”

Related: Texas Braces for More Rain, Floods as Gulf Storm Strengthens 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/texas-flood-threat-persists-as-gulf-system-nears-storm-status?cmpid=BBD061726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260617&utm_content=5492>

In the statement, Kent Britton, CEO of the south Texas port, credited a 
shipping channel improvement in 2025 with unlocking the potential for expanded 
trade, saying it meets “evolving demands of the global marketplace while 
spurring additional cargo diversification.”

“DP World’s global expertise in terminal operations and integrated logistics 
makes them a strong partner as we explore the development of container services 
at the port,” he said.
