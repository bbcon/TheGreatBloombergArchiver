Global goods trade buffeted for three months by supply disruptions faces a 
possible demand jolt that keeps boosting seaborne freight costs before the 
usual restocking race in the third quarter.

тАЬEarly peak season is possible as shippers pull orders forward amid 
geopolitical and fuel-access concerns,тАЭ SEKO Logistics said in anadvisory 
<https://www.sekologistics.com/en/resource-hub/news/client-advisory-asia-pacific-capacity-constraints-through-late-june/>
 this week. тАЬSpace is critically tight out of China and Southeast Asia, with 
most vessels fully booked into late June.тАЭ

The Iran war, the blocked Strait of Hormuz and lingering safety concerns 
around the Red Sea tightened container shipping capacity during whatтАЩs usually 
a seasonal lull in demand.

But orders are reportedly picking up a few months before they typically do on 
the cargo calendar, especially from European importers buying goods from Asia 
and worrying about how the Mideast turmoil will play out in the second half.

These тАЬearly peak season trendsтАЭ are also expected to emerge on transpacific 
routes connecting Asia with the Americas, according to Drewry, a maritime 
research consultancy.

The combination of supply strains, higher fuel expenses and buoyant demand 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-05-13/container-shipping-demand?cmpid=BBD052726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260527&utm_content=4836>
 is already raising spot rates for 40-foot containers.

DrewryтАЩs world composite index recently hit 
<https://www.drewry.co.uk/supply-chain-advisors/supply-chain-expertise/world-container-index-assessed-by-drewry>
 $2,712 per FEU, the highest since last July, with the rate for shipments to 
northern Europe from Asia jumping 30% in the past three weeks. Asia to Los 
Angeles, by comparison, rose about 16% over the same period.

According to booking platform Freightos, cargo owners could be frontloading 
orders to get ahead of carriersтАЩ planned surcharges in June and July tied to 
higher fuel costs and the uptick in seasonal demand. These range from $600 to 
$1,000 per container, Freightos said in a research note Tuesday.

Hapag-Lloyd, for instance, issued an advisory 
<https://www.hapag-lloyd.com/en/services-information/news/2026/05/shipping-from-asia-to-north-europe---the-mediterranean--a-peak-s.html>
 Wednesday saying itтАЩll start collecting a peak season surcharge of $1,000 per 
40-foot unit from Asia to Europe starting June 8. CMA CGM issued a similarnotice
 
<https://www.cma-cgm.com/news/5442/pss-from-asia-to-north-europe?utm_source=otf&utm_medium=5442&utm_term=Prices%20&%20Surcharges&utm_content=&utm_campaign=newsletter>
 last week for Asia shipments to northern Europe, with an effective date of 
June 1.
