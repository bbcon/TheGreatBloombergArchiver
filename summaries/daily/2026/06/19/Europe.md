• Andy Burnham won the Makerfield by-election with 24,927 votes versus Reform UK's 16,000, securing a parliamentary seat and opening a formal path to challenge Prime Minister Starmer; UK gilts fell on the result.
• Goldman Sachs characterized sterling as the most overvalued currency in the G10.
• The ECB's Chief Economist Philip Lane defended the institution's decision to raise rates this month, signaling the tightening cycle remains active.
• The Bundesbank published research estimating that demographic aging has suppressed German annual growth by approximately 0.4 percentage points per year over the past decade, rising to 0.6 percentage points in coming years.

**UK Political Economy**
Burnham's decisive victory — he outpolled all other parties combined — materially raises the probability of a Labour leadership contest. Starmer has publicly committed to remaining in post, meaning a formal challenge would require at least 81 parliamentary supporters to trigger a confidence vote. For markets, the political uncertainty arrives alongside already-strained UK fiscal dynamics. Goldman's characterization of sterling as the most overvalued G10 currency reflects both the currency's prior resilience and the risk that political instability could accelerate a reassessment. UK gilt weakness on the day is a preliminary signal of investor unease, though the move remains contained.

**Continental Europe**
The ECB's Lane defended this month's rate increase, reinforcing that the institution is not yet prepared to pivot despite slowing growth dynamics. Germany faces a compounding challenge: the Bundesbank's demographic findings suggest structural headwinds to growth are intensifying precisely as the country contends with a new US pharmaceutical tariff probe, with Trade Representative Greer criticizing German drug pricing reform as detrimental to US pharmaceutical investment recovery. EU leaders separately sought to calibrate their China trade posture, acknowledging deepening imbalances while seeking to avoid escalation — a tension that remains unresolved.
