**China: Macro Pressure and Strategic Investment**
China's May CPI of 1.2% YoY, fractionally below consensus, underscores a demand environment where firms are absorbing higher raw material costs — partly driven by the Iran war's energy price shock — rather than passing them through. The resulting margin compression is a meaningful headwind for corporate earnings. Separately, Beijing's directive to state banks to reduce interbank lending reflects official concern that excessive liquidity is fueling asset bubbles, particularly in government bonds, even as the broader economy remains soft.

**AI Ambitions and Market Distortions**
Beijing's reported $295 billion data center plan, funded via sovereign debt and state investment vehicles, reflects the leadership's determination to remain competitive with the US in AI infrastructure, despite access restrictions on leading-edge Nvidia GPUs and ASML equipment. Meanwhile, India is preparing to allow its fiscal deficit to widen to approximately 4.8% of GDP against a 4.3% target — the first miss since the pandemic — as surging energy import costs inflate subsidy bills. In Indonesia, Bank Indonesia delivered a surprise rate increase to defend the rupiah, the worst-performing Asian currency this year, while student protests over the cost of living added political pressure on President Prabowo's government.
