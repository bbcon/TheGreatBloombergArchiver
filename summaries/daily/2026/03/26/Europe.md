Europe is absorbing a simultaneous growth and inflation shock from the Iran war, with Italy now preparing to cut its 2026 GDP forecast to as low as 0.5%. UK consumer confidence fell to its weakest reading since the British Retail Consortium's tracker began in March 2024, as households anticipate an inflation surge. The Stoxx Europe 600 fell 1.0%, tracking the broader deterioration in risk sentiment. Airfare disruption adds a further consumer cost: ticket prices on major Asia-Europe routes have risen as much as 560% this month and are likely to remain elevated through summer, according to Alton Aviation. British Airways is offering pilots financial incentives to reduce fuel consumption, reflecting the pressure on airline economics.

Germany is advancing contingency planning against potential US coercion, with officials mapping vulnerabilities in American supply chains — spanning Big Tech, AI infrastructure, and pharmaceutical flows — that the EU could exploit in a future confrontation with the Trump administration. The exercise reflects a broader shift in Berlin's strategic posture, treating economic leverage as genuinely bilateral rather than asymmetric. Separately, EU lawmakers approved a long-delayed trade deal with the United States, a constructive development at the margins of the WTO's 14th ministerial conference opening in Yaoundé, Cameroon.

In the UK, the Green Party under new leader Zack Polanski has moved ahead of both Labour and the Conservatives in some polls, a development worth monitoring as energy costs continue to drive voter behavior. On the pharmaceutical side, Novo Nordisk's market capitalization has declined by nearly $500 billion over 21 months, with patent expirations in India and China — markets representing roughly 40% of the world's population — enabling generic GLP-1 drugs at prices as low as $14. Henkel reached a deal to acquire hair-care brand Olaplex for $1.4 billion, with Olaplex shares surging nearly 50%.
