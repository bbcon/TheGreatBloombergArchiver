Rising fears about artificial intelligence on Wall Street keep pummeling 
shares of companies at risk of being caught on the wrong side of it all 
<https://bloom.bg/4anSAQm>. Tuesday’s selloff — spurred by a tax strategy tool 
<https://bloom.bg/3ZYSSZ8> — was the deepest slide for Charles Schwab and LPL 
Financial since last April’s trade-war meltdown.

Bitcoin whales are back. The token's largest holders are returning to the 
market even as most other investors stayed on the sidelines.Whale wallets 
accumulated about 53,000 coins <https://bloom.bg/40105I2> in the past week, 
their biggest buying spree since November, after weeks of heavy selling. Those 
kinds of purchases helped steady prices.

Keir Starmer shored up his position as UK prime minister at the end of a  
dramatic day in Westminster <https://bloom.bg/4asgEBI> when he secured the 
public support of every cabinet minister, including potential rivals Wes 
Streeting and Ed Miliband. The reprieve is likely only to betemporary 
<https://bloom.bg/402tf9L> as any potential challengers wait for an opportunity 
to launch a bid to replace him. Labour insiders tip that Angela Rayner's 
position has been strengthened.

The European Parliament is moving closer to approving a US trade deal 
<https://bloom.bg/3MCuKsa> after lawmakers agreed to make some changes ahead of 
a vote later this month. They agreed to include a sunset clause in the deal — 
set to expire in 2028 — giving the US six months to lower the current 50% 
tariff on products using steel and aluminum to 15%. The EU may reimpose levies 
if Washington fails to do so.

 Deep Dive: Muscling In 


Skyscrapers on the skyline in Warsaw, Poland on April 2, 2024. Photographer: 
Damian Lemanski/Bloomberg 
Poland is flexing its muscle as its companies make more and more acquisitions 
<https://bloom.bg/4tvuls4> in Western Europe, particularly in Germany. Data 
compiled by Bloomberg show that companies from Poland announced 22 acquisitions 
in Western Europe last year, the highest number on record, and another four are 
in the works this year.

 * The tally also points to a growing number of takeovers in Germany, with 
nine deals spanning industries such as automotive, IT and food production. 
 * “It was unimaginable for me that the Polish economy could catch up with the 
German one in terms of expertise, service quality or scale of operations,” said 
Jacek Swiderski, founder of Wirtualna Polska.
