Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell's final FOMC press conference headlines a consequential afternoon for markets, with the rate decision arriving concurrently with first-quarter results from Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft, and Amazon — the combined weight of these events representing a significant near-term test for equity valuations. Ahead of the decision, concern over whether AI capital spending is translating into returns briefly dragged the Nasdaq 100 down 1% after a report suggested OpenAI missed internal targets for user acquisition and sales, though the company pushed back on that characterization. Tech sector earnings are nonetheless expected to have grown roughly 41% in the first quarter — the strongest gain among all S&P 500 sectors — providing some fundamental underpinning to recent market resilience.

On fiscal policy, key congressional Republicans appear poised to break with the White House over a proposed 44% increase to the Pentagon budget, a rare act of intra-party defiance that may signal the administration's softening grip as midterm elections approach. Separately, Republican lawmakers are also pushing to lower taxes on capital gains. Both dynamics complicate the fiscal arithmetic at a moment when 30-year Treasury yields are pressing toward 5%, with bond traders pricing the inflationary consequences of sustained elevated oil prices.

Corporate news was broadly constructive. NXP Semiconductors surged 16% premarket on an upbeat revenue forecast, suggesting the auto semiconductor cycle may be turning. Seagate rallied 18% on strong AI-related storage demand. Starbucks gained after raising its sales outlook. On the downside, Robinhood fell nearly 10% after revenue missed estimates and management flagged an additional $100 million investment requirement for its "Trump account" initiative. Brown-Forman and Pernod Ricard abruptly ended merger talks, removing a potential transatlantic deal from the table. Meanwhile, Kearney's annual Reshoring Index remained in negative territory for 2025, suggesting that while tariffs have redirected some sourcing away from China, a meaningful revival of domestic manufacturing capacity remains years away.
