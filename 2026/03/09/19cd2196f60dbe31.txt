 Flight from risk 



Optimism for a quick resolution of the conflict in the Middle East is rapidly 
ebbing in financial markets <https://bloom.bg/4ujVLBL>.

What just days ago was a tentative wait-and-see trade has flipped into 
something far more decisive.Investors are pricing in a deeper and 
longer-lasting supply shock — one that could squeeze growth while reigniting 
inflation.

The selling means that about $6 trillion of market value has now been erased 
from global stocks since the war in Iran began.

The shift gathered pace after Donald Trump warned of a widening war in Iran 
<https://bloom.bg/4rdJNXv> and said $100 crude was “a very small price to pay” 
for “safety and peace,” undercutting hopes the conflict would be relatively 
contained.

Some notable moves overnight:

 * Brent crude smashed through $100 a barrel before paring the advance, though 
it’s still on course for the biggest daily gain in dollar terms since futures 
began trading in 1988. European natural gas futures jumped as much as 30% 
<https://bloom.bg/4lsmMin>. 
 * Some of the year’s best-performing stock markets are plunging: Both  
Japan’s Nikkei 225 <https://bloom.bg/4rqzmA5> and the MSCI Emerging Markets 
Index are down 10% from their record highs, Wall Street’s definition of a 
correction. South Korea’s Kospi index sank 6% <https://bloom.bg/40UjpHq>. 
 *  Global bond markets are tumbling <https://bloom.bg/4rq8EaT> as the oil 
price shock prompts investors to price in higher inflation and a deteriorating 
economic growth outlook. 
 * Traders now see  two quarter-point hikes by the ECB 
<https://bloom.bg/4ldOK16> this year compared with one on Friday, and a 70% 
chance of a BOE hike by year-end versus bets on cuts last week. 
 * The dollar strengthened, adding to last week’s surge, as investors  sought 
the currency as a haven <https://bloom.bg/4rgO3Wb>. 
The price action bore the hallmarks of capitulation rather than caution.

“The pendulum is swinging toward panic,” said Danny Wong of Areca Capital. 
“There is a stampede to sell or pare all kind of risk assets.” —Abhishek Vishnoi

 The week ahead 



While the war in Iran has upended the inflation outlook, a pair of reports 
this week will give a look at the prewar trend. The CPI on Wednesday is 
projected to show a core measure rose just 0.2% in February. Data on Friday 
will likely paint a picture of more stubborn inflation. Economists see the 
Fed’s favored core personal consumption expenditures price index up 0.4% again 
in January. Clickhere for the full rundown on the week ahead 
<https://bloom.bg/40SsEId>.
