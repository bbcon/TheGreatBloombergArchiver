King Charles III’s mother, Queen Elizabeth II, lived to the ripe old age of 
96, giving the UK public a lot of time to consider how her son would perform as 
monarch.

A common concern, for many years, was that Charles would be too political for 
the throne. A man of strong opinions, he championed environmental causes and 
intervened in areas such as architecture.

Yet as king, Charles has become a highly-disciplined monarch adept at speaking 
only when necessary, a master of subtlety.

Today, as he arrives in Washington to meet with Donald Trump, he’ll require 
those skillsturned up to the max 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-26/king-charles-us-visit-uk-monarch-tries-to-stop-another-split-with-america?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
, with UK-US relations at their lowest ebb in decades and the president having 
justescaped 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-26/another-attack-at-hinckley-hilton-raises-new-security-concerns?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
 anotherattempted shooting 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-26/alleged-shooter-at-white-house-press-event-in-custody?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
.
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-04-27/what-we-know-about-the-gunman-in-dc-gala-attack-video>
Bloomberg’s Laura Davison reports on the gunman who stormed the White House 
Correspondents’ Dinner.
Charles’ four-day visit includes a bilateral meeting with Trump, a White House 
banquet and a speech to a joint session of Congress. The president is a fan of 
the king — a “fantastic man” — and has suggested thepomp and ceremony 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/trump-tells-bbc-that-king-charles-s-visit-may-improve-us-uk-ties?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
 may be a chance to mend relations.

The king is supposed to be strictly apolitical, but is likely to try to nudge 
his fellow head of state in a more amiable direction.

When Trump visited Britain last year, Charles gave a pretty obvious nod to 
White House policy when he said that “we and our allies stand together in 
support of Ukraine” during a set-piece speech to a room full of the US 
administration’s most senior officials.

Otherwise, Charles will be looking for the visit to go off without any further 
drama following theweekend shooting 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-26/trump-says-suspect-in-custody-officer-shot-but-doing-great?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
.

Then Buckingham Palace will hope its work is done with regard to Trump; After 
all, two meetings, either side of the Atlantic, goes beyond the call of duty.— 
Julian Harris <https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AV1oOPlc7hY/julian-harris>

And Finally

Most evenings, 78-year-old retiree Mohamed Ismail would sit at his local ’ahwa,
 one of the small, no-frills coffee shops that are the cornerstone of social 
life in Cairo, smoking shisha and playing chess with friends often until the 
wee hours. Then came the Iran war and a surge in energy prices, forcing 
businesses to close earlier to curb electricity use,dimming the lights 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-04-27/cairo-relishes-return-to-late-nights-after-month-of-eerie-calm?cmpid=BBD042726_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260427&utm_content=3835>
 on Cairo’s nocturnal activity. Authorities have just announced that the 
month-long measures are easing, and Ismail is among the Cairenes rejoicing that 
the dark, eerie calm over the largest city in the Middle East and Africa is 
being lifted.
