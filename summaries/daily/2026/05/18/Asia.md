# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — May 18, 2026

**China growth disappoints across the board.** April data delivered a uniformly weak reading that has reopened the debate over Beijing's stimulus restraint. Retail sales rose just 0.2% year-on-year — the softest print since the post-Covid reopening of December 2022 — with auto sales down 15%, jewelry off 21%, and previously subsidy-supported categories such as appliances and furniture declining at double-digit rates. Industrial production grew 4.1%, the weakest in nearly three years, while fixed-asset investment unexpectedly contracted 1.6% year-to-date. No economist surveyed by Bloomberg had anticipated a downside surprise of this breadth. The figures, following last week's contraction in new loan demand, suggest the booming export channel is no longer offsetting domestic weakness. They also complicate the post-summit narrative: while Beijing agreed to purchase at least $17 billion of US agricultural products annually through 2028 and to establish new trade and investment boards, the agreements remain vague, and the trade board's mandate appears to cover only around $30 billion in flows. The "placeholder summit" framing leaves meaningful scope for friction when Trump's tariff replacement architecture is unveiled this summer.

**Japan's fiscal pivot and JGB stress.** Prime Minister Takaichi instructed the finance ministry to compile a supplementary budget after weeks of insisting none was needed, citing commodity-driven inflation pressures from the Iran conflict. The 30-year JGB yield surged as much as 20bp to a post-1999 high before paring gains, contributing to a broader Asian bond selloff that extended to Indonesia and India, both heavily energy-import dependent. HSBC's Frederic Neumann noted that with Gulf flows unlikely to be restored quickly, regional inflation pressures will continue building.

**Elsewhere.** Thailand is preparing a 200 billion baht ($6.1 billion) cost-of-living package. Taiwan's President Lai invoked the island's semiconductor centrality as questions linger over US commitments post-summit. Samsung shares rose on resumed union talks, and Kospi gained modestly on AI sentiment. Malaysia's Anwar raised the prospect of a snap election amid coalition strains. Putin's upcoming Beijing visit revives Power of Siberia 2 talks, though pricing leverage remains with Xi.
