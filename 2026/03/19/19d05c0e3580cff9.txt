In Washington, the Iran war is stirring trouble 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/trump-seeks-end-to-attacks-on-energy-sites-as-gas-fields-burn?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 — calls for an exit ramp, charges of poor planning and delusional 
expectations, ahigh-level resignation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/top-counterterrorism-official-resigns-in-protest-of-iran-war?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 and a majority of the public opposed. President Donald Trump’s Republican 
Party is worried as midterm elections loom.

Things look very different in Israel.

Polls show overwhelming support for the military operation that Prime Minister 
Benjamin Netanyahu has been advocating for four decades to eliminate what is 
widely viewed in the Jewish state as an existential threat.

The war has shelved challenges to the ruling coalition, including a demand 
from ultra-Orthodox parties for a law exempting their young men from military 
conscription, controversy over domestic legislation and theprime minister’s 
corruption trial 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-30/netanyahu-submits-formal-request-for-pardon-to-israeli-president?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
.
Netanyahu and Trump at Mar-a-Lago, Florida, on Dec. 29.Photographer: Joe 
Raedle/Getty Images North America
Elections are due by the end of October and, despite having presided over the 
catastrophe of the 2023 Hamas attack, Netanyahu remains the top choice for 
premier in all polls, although his coalition falls below the needed majority, 
as does the opposition.

Part of this is that the government has enunciated three goals from day one: 
eliminate Iran’s nuclear-weapons program, eradicate its ballistic-missile 
program and destroy its ability to fund andarm proxy regional militias 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/israeli-army-advances-in-south-lebanon-after-hezbollah-flare-up?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 that seek Israel’s destruction.

Every day of the past three weeks, Israeli tacticians and leaders say, has 
brought those goals closer in both Iranand Lebanon 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/israel-expands-war-on-hezbollah-in-lebanon-as-crisis-grows?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
. So the now-routine ducking into and out of bomb shelters by millions is 
accepted as a necessary sacrifice to these widely embraced ends.

Israel’s intelligence services have so penetrated Iran’s inner sanctums that 
assassinations of top Iranians are daily occurrences. Whether this will lead, 
as Netanyahu hopes, to a popular Iranian uprisingis far from clear 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/netanyahu-says-no-guarantee-of-iran-regime-change-after-war?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
.

But Israel’s view is that it will come eventually. And the longer its military 
gets to degrade the Iranian threat, the better Israel will be in the long term.

No one here is seeking an exit ramp. — Ethan Bronner 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ASGGjEWbfUs/ethan-bronner>

US efforts to forge a critical-minerals partnership with Brazil are stalling 
amidpolitical tensions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/us-snubbed-by-lula-government-at-brazil-critical-minerals-summit?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 with President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva’s government. While both sides want 
to deepen ties over rare-earth reserves — the US seeking to bypass China, and 
Brazil keen for the investment — a bill setting out policy for critical 
minerals is stuck in Congress in Brasilia, and the Trump administration is 
ruffling feathers with Lula.

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi faces challenges ahead of a meeting with Trump 
today as she tries to nudge Japan away

war in Iran, with 82% of respondents in a survey saying they don’t support the 
conflict, and the nation has deep sensitivities over getting involved in any 
military activity abroad.

Chart of the Day

Anutin Charnvirakul won a parliamentary vote today for a new term as 
Thailand’s prime minister, clearing the way for the conservative politician to 
form a cabinet. He leads a 16-party coalition that represents thestrongest 
majority 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/thai-leader-anutin-tightens-grip-on-power-after-winning-new-term?cmpid=BBD031926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 for a governing alliance in more than a decade and should reassure investors 
after years of political upheaval that saw multiple prime ministers ousted by 
courts or coups.

And Finally

An estimated 40,000 seafarers are stuck on board ships either side of the 
Strait of Hormuz, which has been almost entirely shut since the US and Israel 
began bombing Iran in late February. Half of those, according to the 
International Maritime Employers’ Council, are effectively trapped in the Gulf, 
left to face the constant threat of attacks and having to navigate in a fog of 
electronic warfare, often blind to the position of other vessels. Read our Big
