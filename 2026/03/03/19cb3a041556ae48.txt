Container Shipping Turns Worries on Supply Crunch From Iran War Congestion
Container shipping lines fear another global disruption if Mideast war drags on

An annual conference we’ve dubbed “Davos on the Docks” kicked off this week in 
Long Beach, California, amid worries that a war in the Mideast causinglogistics 
snarls 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/iran-strikes-snarl-e-commerce-delivery-times-to-middle-east?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 and anenergy price spike 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-3?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 will last long enough to become a global economic problem.

S&P Global’s TPM26 agenda was supposed to center around how the container 
shipping industry manages through its next wave of overcapacity. US-Israeli 
attacks on Iran and theregional retaliation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/trump-pledges-whatever-it-takes-on-iran-as-war-widens?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 that started over the weekend quickly changed the annual conference’s script.

“The enormity of the impact of that conflict on the container shipping 
networks, on freight rates, on transit times, on supply chains, really came 
home at the event today,” Peter Tirschwell, vice president for maritime and 
trade at S&P Global Market Intelligence, said in aLinkedIn post 
<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peter-tirschwell-127a9b4_tpm26-activity-7434421119090135040-W7gb?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAAAQhrDgB0bYwZGt1gaWdmMK9EIR1HhRFMEU>
 after day one Monday.

The world’s leading carriers are canceling bookings 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/world-s-top-container-carriers-revise-sailings-to-avoid-mideast?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
, skipping port calls, warning of delays and implementing surcharges to 
transport containers through the conflict zone.

Read More: The Gradual Return of Ships to the Red Sea Hits a Key Milestone 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-16/the-gradual-return-of-ships-to-the-red-sea-hits-a-key-milestone?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>

“This is going to be huge and it’s going to define 2026,” Tirschwell said. “So 
the idea that this was going to be a calmer year, that freight rates were going 
to settle down, that supply chains might begin to return to normal, that ships 
might return through the Suez — all that is totally off the table now.”

TPM gatherings have served as crisis counseling sessions over the past several 
years for logistics managers, carriers and shippers struggling with Covid 
disruptions, Russia’s war in Ukraine and US President Donald Trump’s tariff 
policies.

“We’re back in the soup,” Tirschwell said.

Read More: Yellen Sees Fed ‘Even More on Hold’ Given Iran Conflict Risk 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/yellen-sees-fed-even-more-on-hold-given-iran-conflict-risks?cmpid=BBD030326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>

Speaking to attendees via video conference, former Treasury Secretary Janet 
Yellen said, depending on how long the Iran conflict affects the oil market, 
there will be both a hit to US economic growth and an increase in inflationary 
pressures, complicating the job for the Federal Reserve.

“The recent Iran situation puts the Fed even more on hold, more reluctant to 
cut rates than they were before this happened,” she said.
