If the NATO summit in The Hague was all about making sure Donald Trump didn’t 
quit the military alliance, this week’sgathering in Ankara 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-05/trump-heads-to-nato-summit-to-meet-zelenskyy-face-wary-allies?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 is about showing why it’s in the US interest to stay.

In preparation, NATO chief Mark Rutte headed to the Oval Office and pulled up 
easy-to-read bar charts entitled the Trump Effect and the Trump Trillion. No 
doubtmilitary spending 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/nato-says-europe-and-canada-spent-20-more-on-defense-in-2025?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 is up and Trump’s threats and hectoring of Europe have demonstrably worked.

And yet, in that room, the US president looked a bit bored and unimpressed. 
NATO allies will need to find new ways beyond flattery to keep himengaged and 
interested 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-02/top-us-general-for-europe-resigns-as-trump-squeezes-nato-allies?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
.

Spain, a chronically low military spender, was headed to the summit pointing 
the finger at countries with even lower outlays. Trump’sfallout with 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-24/trump-meloni-spat-hasn-t-derailed-alliance-us-ambassador-says?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 Italy’s Giorgia Meloni may flare up again after he goaded her about a picture 
on Truth Social.

It helps that the host is Turkey’s Recep Tayyip Erdoğan, a strongman-like 
figure Trump admires. He leads a country not only with NATO’s second biggest 
military but whose strategic geographic position gives it sway in the two 
conflicts thatwill dominate 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-03/nato-allies-spar-over-pipelines-ukraine-support-in-summit-talks?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 this year’s summit.

Russia’s war in Ukraine 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-06/russian-strikes-kill-11-in-kyiv-on-the-eve-of-nato-summit?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 has now outlasted World War I and the onus on US allies will be to convince

, and that Ukraine is on a muchstronger footing 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-05/ukraine-says-it-hit-power-substations-in-russia-annexed-crimea?cmpid=BBD070626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260706&utm_content=6245>
 than a year ago.

Trump is far more interested in Iran. He’s still fuming at Europeans he 
believes should’ve lent their airspace and use of military bases when the US 
and Israel attacked Iran in February.

Arguments that they weren’t consulted or that the Middle East isn’t NATO’s 
business didn’t cut it.

And there’s the rub.
