China's economic data for April presented a sharply bifurcated picture. Manufacturing remained in expansion territory, supported by resilient export demand even as the Iran war drives up input costs. Services and construction, however, slipped back into contraction, with building activity registering its worst slump since the early stages of the pandemic. The divergence underscores a familiar structural tension: external demand is sustaining factory output while weak domestic consumption and a deepening property downturn weigh on the broader economy. How long export strength can carry overall growth, particularly if the energy shock worsens and global demand softens, remains an open question.

Beijing's intervention in the Meta-Manus transaction represents a significant escalation in China's approach to technology governance. Chinese authorities have ordered the two companies to unwind a completed $2 billion acquisition within weeks and restore Manus's assets to their original state — an instruction whose practical feasibility is genuinely unclear. Manus co-founders have been barred from leaving China. The stated rationale centers on preventing the outflow of strategically important AI capabilities and talent to the United States. The broader signal, however, appears directed at domestic entrepreneurs: Chinese startups that restructure offshore to attract US capital and circumvent domestic regulatory review will face severe consequences. Several private firms have reportedly already been instructed by the NDRC to reject US-origin capital unless explicitly approved. The episode risks reinforcing perceptions of elevated political risk in China's technology sector, potentially complicating Beijing's simultaneous effort to attract international capital back to the private economy.

China's role in global LNG markets is providing an inadvertent buffer for European buyers. Beijing's LNG imports are tracking roughly 1.3 million tons below year-earlier levels in April, enabled by years of investment in pipeline imports and domestic production capacity. However, domestic trucked LNG prices are rising in key industrial provinces, and the 30-day moving average of Chinese imports has begun to edge higher, suggesting demand destruction may be approaching its limits. The yen weakened toward levels that prompted intervention in 2024, with Japan's top currency officials issuing a "final" warning to speculators as the Bank of Japan maintains its wait-and-see posture amid the energy shock.
