Canada, Mexico Meet to Boost Trade Amid USMCA Uncertainty
As Trump muses about exiting UMSCA, Mexico and Canada discuss ways to boost 
bilateral trade

While the future of North America’s USMCA trade deal remains uncertain, with 
Donald Trump evenweighing 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-11/trump-privately-weighs-quitting-usmca-trade-pact-he-negotiated?cmpid=BBD021926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260219>
 whether to withdraw the US from the treaty, Canada insists on strengthening 
its trade relationship with Mexico as part of its diversification strategy — at 
the risk of further inflaming its tense relations with Washington.

Canada’s minister responsible for US trade, Dominic LeBlanc, led a group of 
around 370 business delegates to a visit in Mexico City is week to meet with 
local firms and government officials, the third high-level visit by Canadian 
policymakers since Prime Minister Mark Carney met with President Claudia 
Sheinbaum inSeptember 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-19/canada-mexico-pledge-coordination-on-trade-pact-review-with-us?cmpid=BBD021926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260219>
.

The goal of the visit, which registered more than 1,900 meetings between 
companies, was to “strengthen economic ties and diversify trade,” according to 
LeBlanc, who added that US officials have not expressed concerns about Canada’s 
trade diversification efforts with other countries.
Dominic LeBlanc, Canada’s US trade minister, left, and, Marcelo Ebrard, 
Mexico’s economy minister, in Mexico City on Monday.Source: Bloomberg
The visit comes at an opportune moment for Sheinbaum, who is growing concerned 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-18/claudia-sheinbaum-is-uneasy-as-mexico-investment-plan-stumbles?cmpid=BBD021926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260219>
 that her flagship program to promote investment in the country — touted as the 
economic antidote to searing US trade tensions — is struggling to get off the 
ground amid slow economic growth.

However, like in the previous visits, no major investment announcements were 
made, with Canada looking more interested in selling its products to Mexico 
than financing big projects.

Read More: Trump’s Latest Canada Threat Previews Rocky USMCA Talks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-25/trump-s-latest-canada-threat-previews-rocky-usmca-negotiations?cmpid=BBD021926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260219>

“I can safely say this mission is not going to move the number on 
Canadian-Mexican trade in the next nine months of this year. But over time this 
will build slowly and will help increase ties, and that’s the objective,” 
according to Michael McAdoo, who co-leads Boston Consulting Group’s worldwide 
trade and investment practice based out of Montreal, and traveled to Mexico 
with the Canadian delegation.

“The objective is to create the foundations for a deeper and broader 
relationship in the years and decades to come and not languish the way we have 
since 1994,” he added.

Sheinbaum said that the goal of the visit is not only to strengthen trade, but 
also investment.

Broader Export Reach

“They want to know what kind of investments Mexico is interested in 
receiving,” she said Tuesday during her daily press conference. “It’s not a 
question of replacing what is produced in Mexico, but rather of enabling 
Mexican companies to export to other parts of the world.”

At the end of his trip, LeBlanc reiterated that both Canada and Mexico want 
the USMCA to remain a trilateral treaty, trying to dispel any doubts that they 
might be prioritizing their bilateral relationship over the one they have with 
the US, which is the largest trading partner of both countries.

“There are no divisions in the interest of Mexico and Canada” with regards to 
a commitment to cross-continental trade, he said.

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-05/canada-to-scrap-electric-vehicle-rules-in-win-for-automakers?cmpid=BBD021926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260219>

Since NAFTA took effect in 1995, McAdoo said, Canada and Mexico have grown 
substantially their trade each with the US and very little with each other. But 
their existing links, which also include the transpacific deal known as CPTPP, 
make them a “natural” fit.

“That side of the triangle has had very, very slow growth compared to the 
others,” he said. “And so it’s critical as every country looks to secure 
reliable trading partners that every opportunity is explored.”

 | US industrial production rose in January by the most in nearly a year,

utility output. The 0.7% increase in production at factories, mines and 
utilities followed a downwardly revised 0.2% gain a month earlier, Federal 
Reserve data showed. Manufacturing output, which accounts for three-fourths of 
total industrial production, rose 0.6% — also the most since February 2025. The

“demonstrating once again how President Trump’s trade agenda is strengthening 
critical industries at home.”
