# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — February 13, 2026

**Markets: AI-driven selloff reaches Asia.** Regional equities slipped roughly 1% from a record after Wall Street's tech-led decline, with the Hang Seng down 1.7% as concern spread that newly capable AI tools from Google, Anthropic and others threaten incumbents across software, logistics, insurance and commercial real estate. Asia has so far weathered the rotation better than US markets, but the MSCI All Country World Index is on track for back-to-back weekly losses. Separately, OpenAI told US lawmakers that DeepSeek has used increasingly sophisticated distillation techniques to extract output from leading American models — a claim that, if it gains traction in Washington, could harden the policy backdrop for Chinese AI champions just as Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance and Baidu are collectively spending roughly $720 million on Lunar New Year red-packet promotions to seed their new AI shopping tools.

**China–Japan tensions deepen in consumer behavior.** Following PM Takaichi's comments that a Taiwan contingency could trigger a Japanese military response, Beijing's travel advisory is translating into measurable economic effects: bookings to Sapporo are projected to fall 62% in January–February, while domestic resorts such as Jilin's Beidahu have seen reservations climb up to 70%. With Takaichi's mandate secure, a near-term détente appears unlikely.

**Capital flows and outbound M&A.** Chinese regulators have instructed banks with concentrated US Treasury exposure to pare positions, framing the move as risk diversification rather than a geopolitical signal. The market response has been muted — Treasury volatility sits at a five-year low — and the guidance extends a multi-year trend rather than marking a rupture. Concurrently, Greater China outbound M&A approached $12 billion in January, the strongest start since 2017, with targets spanning Puma and Allied Gold. The pattern is consistent with Beijing's tacit blessing for strategic acquisitions amid soft domestic demand.

**Other developments.** The US and Taiwan finalized a trade pact including $44 billion in LNG and crude purchases, $15 billion in civil aircraft, and roughly $25 billion in US power-generation equipment by 2029. Hong Kong is weighing expanded confidential IPO filings beyond tech and biotech. Sanrio surged 15% on raised guidance. A hawkish BOJ member signaled conditions for the next hike could align by spring.
