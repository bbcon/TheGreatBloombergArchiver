Asian equity markets edged lower in early trading, with the Hang Seng declining 1.1% as the broader risk environment was weighed by rising geopolitical tensions and uncertainty around Iran. Against that backdrop, a notable bifurcation emerged within Chinese equities: shares of generative AI startups Zhipu and MiniMax Group soared as Hong Kong markets reopened after the Lunar New Year holiday, with investors rotating into pure AI plays and out of traditional internet giants. Robotics firms also attracted buying interest following their prominent showcase during China's Spring Festival gala, though the broader market remained under pressure.

Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi used her parliamentary address to set out a broad economic agenda, pledging to "hit the button" on growth through strategic investment, active fiscal policy, and assertive diplomacy. Her comments reflect the difficult balance she must strike: stimulating growth without triggering the bond market volatility that has historically accompanied aggressive Japanese fiscal expansion. Separately, Japan's manufacturing PMI remained in expansionary territory for a third consecutive month, suggesting firmer corporate sentiment. Japan's cultural economy also drew attention, with Bloomberg identifying how intellectual property rights tied to Hello Kitty, Demon Slayer, and other franchises have minted a new class of billionaires — with pop culture now the country's second most valuable export category.

India presented several constructive signals. HSBC's flash survey showed economic activity accelerating in February, partly attributed to improved sentiment following US tariff reductions on Indian goods. Prime Minister Modi's hosting of OpenAI's Sam Altman and Anthropic's Dario Amodei at the India AI Impact Summit catalyzed a roughly $4 billion combined rally in data center-related stocks. India also formally joined a US-led critical minerals supply chain initiative. Elsewhere in the region, Malaysian exports rose at their fastest pace in over three years in January, driven by electronics shipments. The US also finalized a trade agreement with Indonesia, with Jakarta committing to facilitate an estimated $33 billion in American goods purchases in exchange for lower US tariffs — a significant development for Southeast Asian trade architecture.
