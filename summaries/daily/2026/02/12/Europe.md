**European Morning Briefing — February 12, 2026**

**Europe's competitiveness debate sharpens ahead of EU summit.** French President Emmanuel Macron revived his "Made in Europe" agenda ahead of a key EU meeting, pressing for local-content priority in strategic sectors. Chancellor Friedrich Merz pushed back, warning against excessive protectionism, exposing a familiar Franco-German split over how to lift European productivity. UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves argued for the inclusion of partners such as the UK, Norway and Canada in any industrial framework. Separately, ECB Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel called for accelerated structural reforms, underscoring official concern that monetary policy alone cannot close the growth gap with the US.

**UK macro remains soft.** Fourth-quarter GDP barely expanded, with services stagnating — adding to political pressure on Prime Minister Keir Starmer. A RICS survey nonetheless pointed to the first projected rise in London house prices in more than a year, though overall transaction activity remains subdued following tax uncertainty. The political tone hardened as Jim Ratcliffe told Sky News the UK has been "colonized" by immigrants, sharpening an already contentious debate over benefits and migration.

**European energy security back in focus.** At the E-world conference in Essen, traders flagged that the continent will exit winter with gas inventories more depleted than in 2025, with German storage only roughly a quarter full. Summer-winter price spreads offer little incentive to refill, and Uniper and Bayernugs have already sought to close loss-making facilities. EU carbon prices fell sharply after Merz suggested the bloc should be open to revising or delaying its emissions market — the steepest drop since 2022.

**Corporate.** Nuveen agreed to acquire Schroders for $13.5 billion in cash at a roughly 29% premium, ending more than two centuries of independence for the UK's largest standalone asset manager while preserving the brand and London headquarters. Sanofi fell 6% in Paris after abruptly replacing CEO Paul Hudson with Merck KGaA's Belén Garijo. Adyen sank 20% in Amsterdam on a softer revenue outlook, and Magnum Ice Cream dropped 16% on weak operating profit. The Stoxx Europe 600 nonetheless rose 0.4%, supported by a broadly constructive earnings tape.
