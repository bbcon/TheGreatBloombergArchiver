Asia Daily Briefing — February 17, 2026

Trading across much of Asia remained subdued with several markets closed for the Lunar New Year holiday, leaving regional benchmarks thinly traded. The Nikkei 225 declined 0.42% to 56,566, and Nasdaq futures pointed lower, signaling continued pressure on technology names as the AI-related rotation that has gripped US markets bleeds into Asian sentiment. Indian IT stocks have been a notable casualty: an index of names including Tata Consultancy and Infosys has shed roughly $56 billion in market value since Anthropic released a tool perceived as encroaching on the sector's outsourcing business model.

In Australia, BHP delivered earnings at the upper end of expectations, with copper contributing more than half of group profit for the first time, offsetting weakness in iron ore. Shares closed at a record in Sydney. Separately, an Australian court dismissed the long-running greenwashing case brought against Santos by the ACCR, removing — at least at first instance — a significant legal overhang for the energy producer, though written reasons are not due until February 23. The Reserve Bank of Australia's February minutes confirmed that this month's rate hike reflected a downgraded view of productivity and potential output rather than excess demand, making the RBA the first major central bank to tighten in 2026 and a potential outlier — or forerunner — among DM peers.

In China, AI startup Moonshot is targeting a $10 billion valuation in a new funding round, more than doubling the $4.3 billion mark set only weeks ago in a round backed by Alibaba and Tencent — an indication that domestic AI capital formation remains robust despite the US tech selloff. On Taiwan, President Trump indicated future US weapons sales are under discussion with Xi Jinping, characterizing his recent call with the Chinese leader as "excellent"; Beijing's readout urged "prudence." Karishma Vaswani argues Belt and Road is not retrenching but evolving into a longer-horizon development partnership model, which may prove harder for Western initiatives to counter.

In India, Modi used the AI summit, alongside Macron's fourth state visit, to press global ambitions; Adani announced a $100 billion data-center buildout through 2035. January's trade deficit widened ahead of an interim tariff pact with Washington. In Japan, JGBs extended their rally through a smooth auction, while Mizuho fell 3.8% in Tokyo after disclosing a regulatory probe into suspected insider trading at its brokerage unit.
