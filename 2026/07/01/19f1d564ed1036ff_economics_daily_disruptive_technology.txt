Need-to-Know Research

A Bank of Korea experiment with a unified ledger proved that the two-tier 
structure of money тАФ with the central-bank kind that serves as an anchor and 
commercial deposits as principal means of payment тАФ carries over into the 
tokenized world.

Project Hangang, named after the river Han that flows through Seoul, also 
demonstrated that this can be done at scale, without forcing a choice between 
public trust and private-sector dynamism, economists including Governor Hyun 
Song Shin wrotein a paper 
<https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/sintra/ecb.forumcentbankpub2026_Shin_paper.en.pdf>
 prepared for the ECBтАЩs Sintra conference.

The trial тАФ featuring live transactions using tokenized wholesale central bank 
money and commercial bank deposits тАФ ran for three months from April to June 
2025. It involved some 80,000 users and 12,000 selected merchants and was among 
the first to take place in a real-world operating environment.

According to the authors, it offered useful lessons on design choices. 
Interoperability between existing large-value payment systems and the ledger 
must be secured, they highlight, and the legal nature of the wholesale CBDC 
issued on it defined.

Ensuring an adequate supply of intraday liquidity is also critical as the 
tokenization of government bonds advances and settlement volumes on the ledger 
grow, Shin and colleagues wrote.
