**Colombia: Political Realignment**

The Colombian election result is the region's most consequential political development of the period. De la Espriella — a celebrity attorney running on a pro-Trump, law-and-order platform — demolished the traditional Uribista conservative movement while positioning himself against the left's Iván Cepeda. Investors consider Cepeda more radical than outgoing President Gustavo Petro, meaning a de la Espriella victory would likely be market-positive at the margin. The result extends a regional rightward pattern seen previously in Brazil, Argentina, and Chile. Immediate post-election rhetoric was inflammatory on both sides, underscoring the political fragility. A de la Espriella win on June 21 would strengthen US hemispheric alignment and may ease some of the investor concern around the Andean left's policy trajectory.

**Mexico-US Tensions**

President Sheinbaum's hardening stance toward Washington introduces a new variable for Mexican asset markets. Her framing of US intelligence activities as destabilization — since the April CIA deaths — carries political risk for near-shoring investment sentiment, even if the immediate macro impact is limited. The World Cup in Mexico City provides a brief moment of economic stimulus context, but the underlying bilateral relationship appears to be deteriorating at the margins.
