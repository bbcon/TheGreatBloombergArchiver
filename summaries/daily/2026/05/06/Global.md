The dominant cross-cutting theme remains the US-Iran conflict and the tentative signals pointing toward a negotiated resolution. President Trump suspended "Project Freedom" — the US-led naval operation to escort stranded vessels through the Strait of Hormuz — citing a request from mediator Pakistan and what he described as "great progress" toward a comprehensive agreement. Axios reported the two sides are working toward a one-page memorandum of understanding to frame subsequent nuclear negotiations. The operation had produced the opposite of its intended effect: while two ships were escorted out on Monday, US forces repelled multiple Iranian drone, missile, and fast-attack boat strikes, and Iran struck an oil terminal in Fujairah, UAE. The path to a durable settlement remains uncertain.

Oil markets reacted sharply. WTI crude fell nearly 10% on the day, dropping below $100 a barrel from a peak near $112 a month ago, though December 2026 WTI futures remained around $77 — approximately 8% above early April levels — suggesting markets do not yet price a near-term, sustained reopening of the Strait. US shale producers are nonetheless responding: Diamondback Energy's CEO signaled an "inflection point," with the Permian Basin expected to add as many as 30 rigs. Exxon plans a 12% production increase. US gasoline prices topped $4.50 per gallon for the first time since July 2022.

Chinese aluminum and metal exports are rising materially, benefiting from the Middle East supply disruption and clean-tech demand. Microsoft is reportedly weighing abandonment of its 2030 hourly clean-energy matching target — a significant retreat from commitments made before the AI era — as data center power demand and natural gas's appeal grow. This dynamic is not isolated to Microsoft: across major hyperscalers, reported carbon emissions have risen 23-64% versus pre-ChatGPT baselines, underscoring a widening tension between AI infrastructure expansion and corporate climate commitments that has yet to find a credible resolution framework.
