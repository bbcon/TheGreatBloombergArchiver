**ASML and Industrial Outlook**
ASML's guidance upgrade — approximately 12% above the prior analyst consensus midpoint — provided the clearest market signal yet that AI-driven semiconductor capital expenditure is running well ahead of earlier projections. CEO Christophe Fouquet noted customers were not only increasing capex but accelerating all their plans, a distinction that implies demand durability rather than a pull-forward effect. Intel's adoption of ASML's most advanced lithography equipment for volume production was cited as evidence of broader commercial readiness. Shares rose 4% in Amsterdam, with Applied Materials and Lam Research also gaining in early US trading.

**UK Political Transition**
Burnham's advisers are split on whether Energy Secretary Ed Miliband should be appointed Chancellor, with left-wing supporters favouring the appointment and others strongly opposed. The OECD and Bank of England Governor both called for fiscal discipline on the same day, framing the Chancellor question as consequential for sovereign credibility. Germany's Rhine River water levels have fallen to their lowest for the time of year since 1990, threatening to disrupt manufacturing supply chains in southern Germany and Switzerland at a moment when Europe's largest economy remains fragile.
