AI has momentum

One of the most popular equity trading strategies — buying stocks that are 
already going up — isproducing its best returns in decades 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/ai-rally-drives-best-run-in-global-momentum-stocks-on-record?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>
.

It’s down to the AI mania as investors keep pouring cash into the next digital 
revolution. (Even thetrainers who teach Wall Street bankers 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-05-25/the-ai-trainers-charging-25-000-a-day-to-push-wall-street-s-agentic-shift?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>
 how to use AI tools are now charging $25,000 a day.)

MSCI’s global gauge of stocks with upward momentum has beaten the MSCI All 
Country World Index by 17 percentage points since the end of March, on track 
for its strongest two-month outperformance on record, in data compiled by 
Bloomberg back to 1991.

Bulls say AI mania can continue luring money into the market’s biggest winners.
The risk, though, is that the longer investors crowd into the same stocks, the 
more exposed markets are to a sudden reversal if hot inflation causes the Fed 
to tighten monetary policy or if earnings weaken.

“The momentum story will persist for another few months, with significant 
volatility in between, till it finally sees a climax,” said Hao Hong at hedge 
fund Lotus Asset Management. “If inflation expectations continue to run higher, 
then the Fed would have to make a bigger-than-expected move, and that will 
stall the momentum trade.” —Abhishek Vishnoi

The Warsh era

As the first full week of the Kevin Warsh era 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-23/warsh-s-fed-regime-change-may-require-patience-consensus?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>
 kicks off at the Fed, investors are betting he’ll prioritizethe central bank’s 
inflation-fighting credibility 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-24/treasury-market-ushers-in-warsh-era-with-bets-on-2026-rate-hike?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>
 over Donald Trump’s push for lower interest rates.

With the Iran war unleashing an inflation surge, traders think the Fed is 
certain to start raising rates by March next year. Just three months ago, they 
were betting on lower rates.

And a key gap in Treasuries — the spread between the yields on five-year and 
30-year securities —has shrunk to its tightest level in a year 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/treasury-curve-flashes-higher-for-longer-warning-under-warsh?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>
. The gap is narrowing because traders are pushing up the yield on the 
shorter-dated notes because they see rates as higher for longer.

Even Trump, who badgered Jerome Powell to lower rates, seems to be giving 
Warsh some leeway.Swearing the central bank chief into office 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/warsh-to-be-sworn-in-as-fed-chief-during-white-house-ceremony?cmpid=052626_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260526&utm_content=4817>

“I want him to be independent and just do a great job. Don’t look at me, don’t 
look at anybody, just do your own thing and do a great job.”
