Day four and there’s no sign of let up in the Middle Eastern war, with Iran 
continuing to retaliate against US and Israeli attacks with missile and drone 
strikesacross the region

As the death toll rises, the economic cost grows and the stress in financial 
marketsdeepens 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-3?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
, the White House is under increasing pressure to specify its aims more clearly.

If President Donald Trump is insistent on overthrowing the Islamic Republic’s 
government — as he implied when the bombing began on Saturday — that would 
suggest alonger conflict 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/trump-s-iran-war-adds-new-potential-shock-for-us-economy-voters?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 and a slim chance of Iran reining in its response.

The US State Department added to confusion among Americans in the region when 
an official posted a message on X saying that those in countries including 
Egypt, Israel, Qatar, the United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia should “DEPART 
NOW 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/state-department-tells-americans-to-leave-middle-east-for-safety?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
.” Yet, the State Department hasn’t changed its guidance on its website or 
posted that announcement itself.

There are also growing questions over what exactly led to the US and Israel’s 
decision to start their military campaign over the weekend.

Marco Rubio, Trump’s secretary of state, said late yesterday that Israel was 
poised to attack Tehran and Washington felt it would have to join in toprevent 
counter strikes 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/rubio-says-us-military-s-hardest-hits-on-iran-are-yet-to-come?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 against American forces and bases.

“We knew that there was going to be an Israeli action, we knew that that would 
precipitate an attack against American forces, and we knew that if we didn’t 
preemptively go after them before they launched those attacks, we would suffer 
higher casualties,” Rubio told reporters.

Iran’s foreign minister responded by saying the US had “entered a war of 
choice on behalf of Israel.”

Washington’s allies also questioned the justification for military action, 
with UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer implying thewar is unlawful 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/starmer-implies-trump-s-iran-war-is-unlawful-as-uk-us-ties-erode?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
, while Spain’s foreign minister urged Europe tospeak out 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/spain-presses-europe-to-denounce-us-strikes-on-iran-as-illegal?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 against the ongoing airstrikes.

Whatever the case, the US and Israel are striking Iran hard. And Iran, for 
now, is proving it’s more than capable of firing back.— Paul Wallace 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ASAuUIp54ZQ/paul-wallace>

Chart of the Day

Tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, through which a fifth of oil 
production flows, hasall but ground to a halt 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-03/why-oil-price-surge-is-limited-after-trump-s-iran-strikes?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 as a result of the Iran war. So far, traders are betting that the conflict 
will be relatively brief. Yet the scale of the disruption means that an energy 
crisis is looming closer the longer it drags on — more than a few days, and 
traders see chaos across markets, upending supply chains and stoking inflation.

And Finally

The Iran war has become attritional just days into the conflict, with waves of 
drone attacks by the Islamic Republicputting pressure 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/iran-strikes-missile-math-20-000-iranian-drones-take-on-4-million-patriots?cmpid=BBD030326_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260303>
 on the defenses of the US and its partners from Bahrain to the UAE. US-made 
Patriot air-defense missiles have been largely successful in stopping Iranian 
Shaheds and ballistic missiles. But using $4 million rockets to destroy $20,000 
drones illustrates a problem that has haunted Western military planners since 
early in Russia’s war on Ukraine: The cheap weapons can chew up resources meant 
for much more complex threats.
