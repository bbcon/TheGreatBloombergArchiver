China’s Outlook

If the increasingly popular prediction-market 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-29/wall-street-pros-see-potential-obstacles-in-prediction-markets?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
 universe offers a bet on China’s real GDP growth rate for this year, a 
contract on the 4.5%-to-5% range surely attracted buyers in recent weeks.

China’s leadership will unveil its 2026 target officially at next month’s 
National People’s Congress, an annual gathering of the legislature. In the 
run-up, a number of Chinese provinces have cut their own official targets — one 
sign that the central government will take similar action. By the end of 
January, more than a dozen of the mainland’s 31 regions hadreduced their goals 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-29/chinese-regions-cut-gdp-goals-in-sign-nationwide-target-may-drop?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
.

That in part reflects the fact that only half of China’s provinces actually 
achieved or surpassed their targets for last year, Nomura’s China economists, 
led by Ting Lu, wrote this week. The largest, the southern export powerhouse of 
Guangdong, recorded just 3.9% against its around-5% objective, they said — hurt 
in part by the continuing property slump.

The Nomura team is among many expecting the 2026 target to come in at 4.5% to 
5%, a range the South China Morning Post also recentlyreported 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-22/china-will-set-lower-growth-target-as-economy-slows-scmp-says?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
 would be what’s announced next month. It would mark the first downward move 
after three years of “about 5%.”

Once set, it’s practically a sure bet that’s what China’s GDP figures will 
indeed show. Larry Hu, head of China economics at Macquarie Group, argues that 
once the target is unveiled the question becomeshow it will be achieved, not 
whether.

That framework means that if there’s some external shock — such as the Xi-Trump
relationship 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-02-05/china-s-xi-has-a-warning-for-trump-on-taiwan-arms-sales?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
 running off the rails and tariffs and export controls getting ramped up all 
over again — then policymakers would use domestic stimulus to offset the 
damage. If trade continues to run fine, then “they may continue to tolerate 
weak domestic demand,” Hu and his colleague Yuxiao Zhang wrote in a note last 
month.

Economists at Citigroup, who are also now seeing a 4.5%-to-5% target, said 
last week that in light of the signs, they’ve tweaked their thinking about 
stimulus in 2026. Xiangrong Yu and his team had penciled in around 1 trillion 
yuan ($144 billion) infiscal measures 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-04/top-china-official-vows-to-boost-consumption-to-overhaul-economy?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
, a 20 basis point interest-rate cut and a half percentage point reduction in 
banks’ required reserve ratio.

“This policy package now represents a more binding ceiling rather than a 
baseline expectation,” the Citi economists wrote last week.

Need-to-Know Research

Work by researchers at the American Enterprise Institute and Brookings 
Institution suggests the US is already experiencing net negative migration.

Diving into available data, they calculated  
<https://www.brookings.edu/articles/macroeconomic-implications-of-immigration-flows-in-2025-and-2026-january-2026-update/>
that the US had a net decline in the immigrant population of 10,000 to 295,000 
in all of 2025. That would still imply a tiny net increase in the overall 
population.

For 2026, though, they predict the US will have net immigration somewhere 
between a gain of 185,000 and a decline of 925,000 — a prediction made before 
the US announcedyet more new restrictions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-14/us-freezes-visa-processing-for-dozens-of-countries-fox-reports?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
 on legal migration at the start of the year.

It seems increasingly possible that in 2026 “we could be at around zero or 
negative on population,” says Tara Watson, who directs the Brookings Center for 
Economic Security & Opportunity and is one of the study’s co-authors.

For more on US demographics, read Shawn Donnan’s weekend essay 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-30/trump-immigration-crackdown-could-shrink-us-population-for-first-time?cmpid=BBD020526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260205>
