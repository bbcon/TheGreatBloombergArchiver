**China's Mineral Leverage and AI Rivalry**
China's deployment of export controls on yttrium and tungsten against Japan — with flows at precisely zero since January — represents a calibrated but consequential escalation in the bilateral dispute over Taiwan-related statements by Prime Minister Takaichi. Sumitomo Electric's chief executive acknowledged supply disruptions while suggesting diplomatic engagement may eventually be necessary. Japan's 15-year stockpiling program and Lynas Rare Earths' heavy rare earth shipments offer partial mitigation, but a broadening or prolonged squeeze would expose manufacturing vulnerabilities. Separately, Anthropic's formal accusation that Alibaba's Qwen lab orchestrated systematic access to its Claude model signals an intensifying regulatory dimension to US-China AI competition; Alibaba shares extended declines.

**Markets and Corporate Developments**
South Korean equities suffered a second circuit-breaker halt this week, reflecting concentrated exposure to memory semiconductors and sensitivity to downstream demand signals from Apple. Samsung and SK Hynix are reportedly preparing a combined investment announcement Monday, which may provide some offset to near-term sentiment. Tokyo's CPI acceleration reinforces the Bank of Japan's gradual tightening trajectory. Alibaba and Tencent shares remain in bear market territory in Hong Kong, weighed by AI-progress skepticism and weak domestic consumption.
