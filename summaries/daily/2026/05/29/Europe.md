**Inflation and Growth Divergence**
The most significant European data of the period presents a genuine policy tension: CPI accelerated in France, Italy, and Spain to the fastest rates since 2024, providing clear support for ECB rate tightening, while France's economy simultaneously contracted in Q1. This combination — inflation overshoot alongside weak growth — is the precise scenario that most complicates the ECB's task. The inflation data appears to reflect energy price pass-through from the Middle East conflict, which means any ceasefire extension that reduces oil prices could ease pressure more quickly than the underlying demand picture would suggest. Sweden's contraction, in line with expectations, adds to the picture of uneven European growth momentum; Riksbank officials may interpret it as justification to pause before moving rates higher.

**EU Industrial Policy and Security**
The European Commission's rebooted Chips Act, targeting €120 billion in investment by 2035, reflects a strategic response to the semiconductor race being driven by US and Chinese capital — and by the Huawei LogicFolding disclosure specifically. Separately, EU officials are seeking access to Anthropic's Mythos AI model over cybersecurity concerns, placing Brussels at the frontier of AI governance debates. The Romanian drone incident underscores that NATO's eastern flank remains under direct kinetic pressure, with Bucharest calling the intrusion a "serious and irresponsible escalation." Russia-Armenia tensions were on display at the Eurasian Economic Union summit, where Armenian Prime Minister Pashinyan declined to attend as Yerevan pursues EU membership aspirations — a geopolitical fracture within Moscow's own institutional architecture.
