Trump Makes Economic Fallout His New Iran Target
The Iran war enters week four under what is at least Trump’s fourth advertised 
goal for the conflict

We are entering the fourth week of the Iran war and now encountering what is 
at least President Donald Trump’s fourth advertised goal for the conflict. 
After offering regime change, ending Iran’s nuclear ambitions, and degrading 
the Islamic republic’s arsenal of missiles and drones, Trump has in the last 
few days focused on another: Bringing downoil prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-22/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-23?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
.

Which is to say that Trump has within a month shifted the stated objective of 
the war from foreign policy and strategic targets to an economic one. Or that 
he has been forced quickly to find a way to address the economic consequences 
of the war he chose to begin.

Trump’s play over the weekend was to demand 
<https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116269822349947644> that Iran 
“fully reopen” the Strait of Hormuz or face an attack on its power plants — 
“starting with the biggest one first!” — by Monday evening Washington time.
Iran’s response 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/iran-strikes-gulf-targets-as-trump-s-hormuz-deadline-approaches?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 was to counter that if the US attacked its power plants, Tehran would target 
data centers, desalination plants and other infrastructure vital to the 
economies of America’s Gulf allies.

But as markets slumped across Asia and Europe on Monday out of fear of an 
escalating war, Trumpbacked off <bbg://news/stories/TCCMXPKK3NY9> his 
ultimatum, saying the two sides have had good talks in recent days and 
extending by five days his deadline to open the strait. Markets reversed their 
losses.

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/over-40-middle-east-energy-assets-severely-damaged-iea-says?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>

Earlier, he also responded to other countries’ refusal of his requests for 
help guarding the strait by indicating the US plans to walk away from any 
responsibility to do so. The strait, Trumpwrote 
<https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116263563453969628> on social 
media over the weekend, “will have to be guarded and policed, as necessary, by 
other Nations who use it — The United States does not!”

Though the US does not consume Gulf oil exports in the way it once did, the 
reality isAmerican consumers 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/rising-gas-prices-from-iran-war-push-us-consumers-to-find-ways-to-save?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 and companies won’t escape the hit to the global economy. Retail gasoline 
prices in the US are up by a third from a month ago as a result of the war.

Hormuz Tracker: China-Bound LPG Carrier Joins Sparse Transits 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-22/hormuz-tracker-china-bound-lpg-carrier-joins-sparse-transits?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>

Also suffering from higher global prices is anyone using natural gas, 
fertilizer or helium. The last item, it turns out is, is a key input for making 
semiconductors, which as we learned during the pandemic are in just about 
everything and a crucial component for the AI investment boom now underway. Pair
higher energy costs 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/goldman-sachs-raises-oil-forecasts-on-largest-ever-supply-shock?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 for power-hungry data centers with more expensive chips and you can make a 
compelling case the future we’ve been promised byAI evangelists 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-17/openai-anthropic-deals-power-abu-dhabi-s-100-billion-ai-bet?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 is also at stake if this conflict drags on.

It’s no wonder then that Trump is now so focused on the economics of this war.

With its launch Feb. 28 Trump called the end 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-03-02/trump-and-economic-statecraft?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 of an era of using economic tools like sanctions and tariffs as the main 
instruments of American power. Iran responded by making the consequences global 
and economic. Which means Trump is now left threatening yet more military 
action to limit the economic damage of a conflict he began because he lost 
faith in his economic tools in the first place. All of which risks escalation 
and moreeconomic carnage 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-15/fuel-crisis-iran-s-grip-on-strait-of-hormuz-hits-consumers-worldwide?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
.

Global Fallout

Three weeks ago the assurances were that the US would avoid being drawn into a 
quagmire and deploying soldiers into a land war. Discussions in Washington, 
however, have turned to invading Kharg Island, the outlet for the vast majority 
of Iran’s oil exports as a way to increase leverage on Tehran.

Maintaining an open Strait of Hormuz may be hard. How long would naval convoys 
run for? Months? Years? Would the strait join the list of the world’s 
treacherous waterways for a prolonged time and require a shipping premium 
permanently? And, as mentioned above, it’s not just oil that’s in play here.

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-22/strait-of-hormuz-trump-threatens-iran-but-saudi-arabia-finds-pipeline-bypass?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>

It’s also not just Iran we should be mindful of. This is now a global economic 
war.

Governments in places like India 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/two-indian-lpg-carriers-are-in-transit-through-strait-of-hormuz?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
,Pakistan 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-23/pakistan-tells-cricket-fans-to-stay-home-as-fuel-shortages-bite?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 and Thailand are already facing domestic consequences that are likely to rise. 
Especially if the US starts blocking the small number of ships carrying Iranian 
crude that have navigated the strait in recent days.

For a few days last week it looked like a repeat of the Cuban Missile Crisis 
was unfolding. Then a tanker carrying Russian oil headed for Cuba changed 
course over the weekend after the US Treasury added the Caribbean island to 
what is now a short list of banned destinations for Russian oil. Though it 
remains in the area and may still test a US naval blockade.

Read More: Russian Fuel Cargo Veers Away From Cuba as US Tweaks Rules 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-20/tanker-of-russian-fuel-veers-away-from-cuba-as-us-tweaks-rules?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>

In the meantime the Trump administration is arguing that consumers’ short-term 
pain at the pump is worth it for the long-term benefits of eliminating the 
strategic threat of a rogue Iran.

“Let’s just pick 50 days of temporary elevated prices,” Treasury Secretary 
Scott Bessenttold NBC  
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-22/bessent-says-us-to-press-ahead-in-iran-war-with-hormuz-in-sights?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
on Sunday, foreshadowing another month of war and soaring crude. “Prices will 
come off on the other side for 50 years of not having an Iranian regime with a 
nuclear weapon.”

But that’s not necessarily the message coming from Trump. He is making it 
clear his priority now is reopening the Strait of Hormuz and lowering oil 
prices. That seems to be a more durable goal than some of his others. It’s also 
the one that matters most to the global economy. And that may be the hardest to 
deliver.

Charted Territory

Diverted cargo 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/iran-s-hormuz-blockade-is-helping-a-little-used-kenyan-port?cmpid=BBD032326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260323>
 | The closure of the Strait of Hormuz is turning Kenya’s Lamu port into a 
surprise refuge, as vessels bound for the Middle East divert and discharge 
cargo at the rarely used facility. Lamu, about 340 kilometers (210 miles) north 
of the much busier Mombasa Port, has received 74 vessels since the start of the 
year, the Kenya Ports Authority said in apost on X 
<https://x.com/Kenya_Ports/status/2034355158746857610> last week. That’s about 
a third of all ships it serviced since it opened in 2021. The port received 
just two container ships in the first quarter of last year.
