Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter Will Wade digs into a milestone achieved by an American nuclear 
startup.

The Trump administration has been hailing the “rebirth of America’s nuclear 
industry” after a reactor startup reached akey milestone 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-04/us-says-antares-small-nuclear-reactor-reaches-criticality?cmpid=BBBXT061126_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5420>
.

Antares Nuclear Inc. is developing the new and unproven technology for small 
nuclear power plants, which could be used much more widely than traditional 
large-scale facilities. They are of particular interest to power-hungry AI data 
centers.

Last week, Antares achieved a self-sustaining fission reaction known as “
zero-power fueled criticality 
<https://www.energy.gov/articles/department-energy-celebrates-first-advanced-reactor-criticality?utm_source=chatgpt.com>
.” It shows the company’s design can produce a steady release of energy — 
enough for a single LED light bulb for now.

But it will be quite some time before this tiny flow of energy can be scaled 
up to fulfill the US Department of Energy’s boast of a “nuclear renaissance.”

“It’s different from proving commercial viability,” says Chris Gadomski, chief 
nuclear analyst at BloombergNEF. “They’re not going to be creating power 
tomorrow.”

The US nuclear industry has been stagnating for decades. The country has added 
just three traditional reactors this century. No new commercial-scale projects 
are under construction today despite great interest from President Donald Trump 
and several states.

The US hasn’t managed to commercialize the small modular reactors, or SMRs, 
that have tantalized hyperscalers with their potential to provide clean 
round-the-clock power for AI data centers. No such power plants are installed 
in the country today.

In this context, Antares has taken an unequivocally important step for US 
efforts to revive its nuclear industry.

It is the first company to achieve criticality under an Energy Department 
program initiated last year after Trump returned to the White House. The goal 
is to get two more firms to achieve the same thing with their SMR designs by 
July 4.

Oklo Inc., a next-gen nuclear company that includes OpenAI’s Chief Executive 
Officer Sam Altman as an investor, said it’s targeting criticality by that 
date. Valar Atomics Inc., another startup, is pushing to produce electricity at 
a low level by next month.

For Antares, the next steps include ramping up reactors to full power and then 
connecting turbines to convert the energy into electricity.

It’s aiming to deliver very small reactors for military bases in 2028, but 
next-gen nuclear technologies for commercial use are unlikely within Trump’s 
term.

“People don’t realize how challenging it is,” Gadomski said.

—Will Wade, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

The European Union has proposed new legislation to triple data center capacity 
in the bloc and shift away from foreign companies. Yet the gap between 
intentions and delivery remains substantial, with most early-stage plans 
speculative, or facing delays in grid connections or permits, according to 
BloombergNEF. The initiative would require total live capacity to reach 
approximately 25.7GW by the early 2030s, up from 8.6GW currently.
