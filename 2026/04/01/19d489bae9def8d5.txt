Tariffs, a year later

A year since Donald Trump tripped up global markets with his so-called 
Liberation Day tariffs, the investment playbook inspired by his move has been
firmly put on the shelf 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-31/iran-war-unwinds-a-year-of-investor-bets-on-trump-s-tariffs?cmpid=040126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260401>
.

ThatтАЩs because the Iran warтАЩs unprecedented oil shock forced a swift unwinding 
of popular, crowded trades that had come to define markets since last April.

Global stocks, which had rallied on the back of the AI boom and falling 
interest rates, have lost about $14 trillion in value since the conflict began. 
Bonds have swung sharply as traders have repriced the outlook for interest 
rates. Emerging markets are seeing a rush for exits due to their reliance on 
oil imports.
