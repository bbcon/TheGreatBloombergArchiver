 Deep Dive: Primark’s Wobbly Crown 


 <https://bloom.bg/4qMixPS> A Primark store in Oxford Street, London. 
Photographer: Jason Alden/Bloomberg 
Primark’s fashion dominance is being eroded as prices climb and cheap online 
rivals like Shein lure shoppers away, and thehigh street discount darling is 
starting to feel the heat <https://bloom.bg/4qMixPS>.

 * AB Foods is weighing a spin-off to test Primark’s strength as a standalone 
business—but it comes at a tricky moment. Sales flatlined at £9.5 billion last 
year, a subdued holiday season triggered a profit warning, and the retailer is 
still without a permanent CEO following Paul Marchant’s exit last March. 
 * To be fair, it’s not just one retailer’s problem. Higher taxes and stubborn 
inflation are squeezing shoppers across the UK and Europe—especially those with 
the least disposable income, the very customers Primark relies on. 
 * Analysts say any breakup will only work if performance improves first.
