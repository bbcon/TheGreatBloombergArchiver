Once again, hopes for an agreement to end the war in the Middle East have come 
to naught. Donald Trump hasdoubled down 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/trump-s-blockade-of-iran-ports-risks-expanding-war-to-high-seas?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260413>
 by threatening to blockade the Strait of Hormuz — a move which will likely 
further restrict supplies of crude and inflict deeper pain on the global 
economy — after the US and Iranfailed to reach 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-12/how-the-us-iran-talks-in-islamabad-ended-without-a-deal?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260413>
 a deal in talks in Pakistan.

Speaking to reporters on Sunday night, Trump said he didn’t care if Iran 
returned to the negotiating table, arguing the US had already achieved its 
military objectives. “If they don’t come back, I’m fine,” he said.

The risk of escalation is rising. The US military said it would impose a 
blockade 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-12/trump-promises-hormuz-blockade-after-us-iran-peace-talks-fail?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260413>
 of all maritime traffic entering and exiting Iranian ports at 10 a.m. on 
Monday Washington time, adding that it would allow other vessels to transit the 
strait if they’re not stopping in the Islamic Republic.

Iran issued a threat to neighboring ports if its own facilities were targeted.

“If the security of Iran’s ports in the Persian Gulf and the Sea of Oman is 
threatened, no port in the Persian Gulf and the Sea of Oman will be safe,” 
Iran’s armed forces said in a statement, according to state-run IRIB News.

They added that US restrictions on the movement of vessels in international 
waters are “illegal and constitute an act of piracy.”

Earlier on Sunday, Trump said the US was “LOCKED AND LOADED” and ready to 
“finish up the little that is left of Iran.” The US leader threatened to 
retaliate in the event of Iranian resistance to the blockade, posting on social 
media that “Any Iranian who fires at us, or at peaceful vessels, will be BLOWN 
TO HELL!”
A large billboard at Vanak Square in Tehran stating that the Strait of Hormuz 
will remain under Iran’s control and that Donald Trump failed to achieve 
results.Photographer: Fatemeh Bahrami/Anadolu
Trump has suggested that other countries would also be participating in the 
blockade, but has yet to name any specific nations that will be sending ships 
or other assets to the region to assist.

While the US is capable of enforcing a blockade, it would entail substantial 
costs and risks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/trump-s-hormuz-blockade-risks-piling-pain-on-asia-allies-china?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260413>
, Bloomberg Economics’ analysts including Jennifer Welch wrote in a note. US 
warships would be closer to Iranian drone and missile threats, leading to a 
dangerous escalation cycle if any of them are hit.

“It’s particularly risky with Iran to seek de-escalation through escalation,” 
said John Bradford, executive director at Yokosuka Council on Asia Pacific 
Studies and former US naval officer. “The tactic works better with some actors 
than others, and Iran has shown it’s resilient to that tactic.”—Richard Frost
