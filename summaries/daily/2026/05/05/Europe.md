European markets showed modest resilience on Tuesday, with the Stoxx Europe 600 gaining 0.6%, though the region faces a complex macro backdrop that warrants careful monitoring. The European Commission's Dombrovskis described the continent as facing a stagflationary shock — a characterization that captures the uncomfortable combination of higher energy costs and sluggish growth, even if European electricity markets have remained calmer than oil markets might imply. ECB President Lagarde, speaking at a climate conference, reinforced the message that Europe's dependency on imported fossil fuels is "clearly unsustainable," adding institutional weight to the energy transition narrative.

HSBC reported first-quarter profits that missed consensus estimates, weighed by an unexpected UK fraud-related charge and provisions related to Middle East conflict risks. Shares fell 5.9% in London. The result partially obscures a more constructive picture in wealth management and Hong Kong, and the bank upgraded its net interest income outlook. By contrast, UniCredit delivered a record quarterly profit of €3.22 billion, up 16% year-on-year and well ahead of the €2.68 billion consensus — illustrating the continued divergence in European banking fortunes.

UK political dynamics are evolving in ways that may complicate the government's policy agenda. Labour's perceived failure to deliver on election commitments has revitalized Scottish nationalist sentiment, adding a domestic pressure point for Prime Minister Starmer. Meanwhile, the EU-US trade relationship remains tense: EU finance ministers reiterated that the bloc retains retaliatory tools should the US follow through on proposed 25% auto tariffs, while French officials emphasized European options against threats to strategic industries. Romanian politics added to continental uncertainty, with a no-confidence vote against Prime Minister Bolojan backed by both the far-right and the Social Democrats — a coalition that, if successful, could deepen a fiscal crisis around the EU's widest budget deficit.
