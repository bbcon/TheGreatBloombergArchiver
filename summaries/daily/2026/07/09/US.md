**Monetary Policy**
The minutes of the June FOMC meeting — the first chaired by Kevin Warsh — delivered a genuinely hawkish signal. "Most participants" identified scenarios in which inflation would remain elevated due to AI-driven demand, the Middle East conflict, or tariff effects, and indicated that "some policy firming would likely be warranted." A "few" members went further, seeing a case for an immediate hike. Bond markets responded: yields moved higher alongside energy prices this week, and the trajectory of rate expectations has shifted decisively toward the next move being up rather than down.

**Inflation and Energy**
The critical transmission mechanism is refined products. The gasoline crack spread — the margin between crude and refined gasoline — sits near $57 per barrel, close to the highest since June 2022, while the diesel crack spread remains materially elevated from pre-war levels. This stickiness in retail fuel costs carries direct implications for CPI and reinforces the Fed's hawkish posture. Separately, the Trump administration advanced a new tranche of "forced labor" tariffs covering 60 trading partners under Section 301, with Senator Ron Wyden calling the proposal a pretext to reconstruct a tariff regime invalidated by the Supreme Court. Total extra tariffs paid by US businesses and consumers since March 2025 have reached $317 billion.
