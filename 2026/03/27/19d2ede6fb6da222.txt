Dollar surprise

While almost all financial assets have fallen since the war broke out, the 
dollar stands out: It’s on track for itsbiggest monthly gain since July 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/dollar-s-best-month-since-july-roils-wall-street-s-fx-roadmap?cmpid=032726_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
, buoyed by haven flows and diminished expectations for Fed interest-rate cuts.

The Bloomberg Dollar Spot Index’s 2% gain in March has scrambled Wall Street’s 
playbook for the world’s dominant reserve currency.It also risks adding to 
inflationary pressures around the world by making oil and food crops — 
denominated in dollars — even more expensive for importers.

The gain marks a sharp reversal for the greenback. The Bloomberg dollar gauge 
sank roughly 8% in 2025, the most since 2017, and most forecasters came into 
this year predicting further losses.

The war has changed the equation. JPMorgan Chase strategists, for example, 
have turned bullish for the first time in a year. In the futures market, 
speculators flipped to betting on dollar gains, whereas in mid-February they 
were the most bearish in about five years.

“The short dollar positions of early 2026 were caught offsides,” said Steven 
Englander at Standard Chartered Bank. —Miles J. Herszenhorn 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AXr97Yf5k54/miles-j-herszenhorn>, Anya 
Andrianova <https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ARyaBUHvMI8/anya-andrianova> and

EV stock revival

One secondary effect from the war in Iran: Sales of electric vehicles are 
surging again, especially in Asia, which depends heavily on oil imported from 
the Middle East. That in turn is lifting shares of the region’s EV makers.

The Hong Kong-listed shares of China’s BYD are up 12% in March 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/byd-stock-rebound-gathers-pace-as-oil-shock-drives-ev-sales-boom?cmpid=032726_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260327>
, on track for their biggest monthly gain in more than a year. Rivals Nio and 
Zhejiang Leapmotor have risen even more. The sector had slumped in recent 
months on worries over sluggish demand and tough price competition in China.

But rising energy prices are reversing the trend. In March, customer traffic 
has been brisk at BYD dealerships across Asia as gas prices climb.

“We’ve seen stories out of the Philippines and Indonesia where locals are 
queuing up to buy an electric vehicle,” said Leonid Mironov at Gavekal Capital. 
“Longer term, this will help re-establish the EV narrative and consumer 
mindshare, especially in developed markets.” —Charlotte Yang 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AVYbVNaKlaM/charlotte-yang>
