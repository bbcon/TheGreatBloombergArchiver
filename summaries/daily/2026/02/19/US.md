Federal Reserve officials were notably reluctant to ease monetary policy when they convened last month, with several members suggesting rates may need to rise if inflation remains persistently elevated, according to FOMC minutes released Wednesday. The hawkish tone came as a surprise relative to market expectations — traders had priced in a more neutral posture — and prompted a reassessment of the near-term rate path. Separately, National Economic Council Director Kevin Hassett drew scrutiny after publicly attacking a New York Fed study concluding that US companies bear the primary burden of existing tariffs, calling it "an embarrassment" and suggesting its authors should be "disciplined." The episode highlights ongoing friction between the administration and independent economic research institutions.

On the capital flows front, Treasury Department data showed overseas investors purchased a net $1.55 trillion of long-term US financial assets in 2025, up from $1.18 trillion the prior year. Equity allocations accounted for $658.5 billion of the total, with Treasuries absorbing another $442.7 billion — a result that appears to undercut the narrative of a sustained "Sell America" rotation, though dollar weakness may have amplified the attractiveness of US assets to foreign buyers rather than reflecting unambiguous confidence in US policy.

US industrial production rose 0.7% in January, the largest monthly gain in nearly a year, with manufacturing output up 0.6%. The White House cited the figures as evidence that its trade agenda is strengthening domestic industry, though the data predate the full effect of tariff escalations implemented in subsequent weeks.

In corporate news, AI-adjacent names were mixed: Figma jumped 17% after revenue guidance exceeded estimates, and DoorDash gained 14% on a strong order forecast, while Carvana fell sharply on margin pressure. Bitcoin continued its retreat, declining more than 40% from its October peak as US institutional flows — which had become the marginal price-setter via spot ETFs and CME futures — remain in withdrawal.
