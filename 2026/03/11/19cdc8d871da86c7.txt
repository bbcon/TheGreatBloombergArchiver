Iran War Has Pakistan Walking on Eggshells
The war on Iran is piling pressure on Islamabad’s network of global alliances.

Pakistan is walking a fine line as the US-Iran conflict reverberates across 
the region, upending oil supplies along with Islamabad’s delicate web of 
alliances.

The government has publicly called for restraint, dialog and de-escalation, 
but behind the cautious language lies a more complicated reality.

Pakistan’s new defense agreement 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-18/saudi-arabia-and-pakistan-deepen-ties-with-mutual-defense-pact?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 with Saudi Arabia formalizing long-standing cooperation, its relations with 
Iran, and its reliance on the US leave little room for taking sides.

The pact with Saudi Arabia states that aggression against one would be treated 
as a threat to both — placing Pakistan ina tricky position 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/pakistan-says-will-be-there-before-it-is-needed-for-saudi-arabia?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
.

Its top military leader, Field Marshal Asim Munir, was in Saudi Arabia at the 
weekend as Iran attacked Al Kharj and Riyadh. The Saudi defense minister said 
they discussed the strikes and “measures needed to halt them.”
Asim Munir.Source: Iranian Presidency/Anadolu Agency/Getty Images
Relations run deep for the two Muslim nations. Riyadh has already granted 
Islamabad a loan of $5 billion, as talks proceed on an economic cooperation 
agreement.

Yet it’s no easy decision for Pakistan to stand shoulder-to-shoulder with 
Saudi Arabia.

It would turn Pakistan against a neighbor at a time when sympathies for Iran 
run deep in a significant portion of the Shiite population, which shares strong 
religious and ideological ties with the Islamic Republic.

Pakistan is meanwhile enjoying warm US relations: Donald Trump hosted Munir 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-18/pakistan-army-chief-discusses-israel-iran-conflict-with-trump?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 at the White House last year. So Pakistan hascondemned Iran’s attacks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-23/pakistan-condemns-us-strikes-in-iran-while-embracing-trump?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 while seeking peace negotiations.

Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto has offered to mediate, possibly in a 
joint visit to Iran with Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif.

While Pakistan is not a direct participant, it’s among those suffering 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/pakistan-plans-fuel-saving-steps-after-oil-shock-sparks-panic?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 the war’s impact. With gas from Qatar suspended, the government halted 
supplies to fertilizer companies, raised fuel prices by a record 21% and 
introduced austerity measures to cut consumption.

The result is a familiar but increasingly fraught geopolitical balancing act: 
neutrality in public, caution in private, and constant diplomatic engagement 
behind the scenes. — Bilal Hussain

Global Must Reads

Energy markets whipsawed again as investors raced to interpret rapidly 
shifting comments from the Trump administration on the war on Iran. Oilprices 
plummeted 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/us-says-it-s-escalating-war-on-iran-dampening-chance-for-talks?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 after Energy Secretary Chris Wright posted — and then deleted — a message that 
said the US Navy had escorted an oil tanker through the Strait of Hormuz, and 
White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt later conceded no such operation 
had occurred.

The International Energy Agency is proposing the largest release 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/iea-proposes-massive-release-of-emergency-oil-stockpiles?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 of oil reserves to date, of about 300 million to 400 million barrels, with a 
decision possible later today, a source says. That’s as governments seek to 
contain a spike in energy prices driven by the war and the near total closure 
of the Strait of Hormuz, with Gulf producers so far shaving about 6% from 
global oil output.

The US war effort is showing unexpected signs of strain 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-10/iran-s-attack-drones-and-missiles-put-us-military-under-unexpected-strain?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 against Iran’s arsenal of missiles and drones, which has forced the Defense 
Department to dig deep into inventories of expensive interceptors. The US and 
Israel havedrawn lessons 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/iran-war-us-israel-steer-conflict-using-lessons-learned-from-2025-clash?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 from the 12-day war last year, including the importance of destroying the 
Islamic Republic’s command nodes and launchers, and have been using tactics 
such as moving personnel and equipment out of range to avoid retaliatory 
strikes.

Israel is quietly planning for a potential base 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-11/israel-eyes-red-sea-base-in-somaliland-to-fight-iran-backed-houthis?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 at the mouth of the Red Sea from which to attack one of Tehran’s last proxies 
still operating at full strength: the Houthis of Yemen. Prime Minister Benjamin 
Netanyahu’s government recognized Somaliland in December and will follow up 
with a strategic security partnership with the breakaway Somali territory. 
Meanwhile, Israel is set toexpand its defense budget 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/netanyahu-s-cabinet-to-expand-budget-by-13-billion-to-fund-war?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 by almost 40 billion shekels ($13 billion) to fund the war with Iran.

Chinese authorities moved to restrict state-run enterprises and government 
agencies from running OpenClaw AI apps on office computers, acting swiftly to 
defusepotential security risks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/china-moves-to-limit-use-of-openclaw-ai-at-banks-government-agencies?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 after local companies and consumers began experimenting with the agentic AI 
phenomenon. The warning underscores Beijing’s growing concern about the 
platform that requires unusually broad access to private data and can 
communicate externally, potentially exposing computers to hacking.

Ukraine’s air defenses are being stretched as the US-led war with Iran burns 
through global stockpiles 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/ukraine-faces-squeeze-on-air-defenses-as-iran-war-burns-supplies?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 of Patriot missiles, the most valuable weapon Kyiv has to defend against 
Russian attacks.

North Korea test-fired cruise missiles from its latest warship, the second 
time in a week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/n-korea-tests-missiles-as-us-reportedly-moves-arms-to-mideast?cmpid=BBD031126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260311>
 it has launched its new sea-to-surface weapons as reports grow that the US is 
shifting air-defense assets out of South Korea to help it fight Iran.

 the ruling party and stoking renewed uncertainty as the nation contends with

And Finally

When Bodo Glimt hosts Sporting Lisbon today in the last 16 of Europe’s elite 
football competition, it’ll be more than just the latest lucrative chapter in 
the club’s David-against-Goliath tale. The team from the Norwegian Arctic 
fishing port, which has beaten giants Inter Milan, Atletico Madrid and 
Manchester City already in the Champions League this season, also has another 
chance to showcase a transformation underpinned by a fighter pilot turned
