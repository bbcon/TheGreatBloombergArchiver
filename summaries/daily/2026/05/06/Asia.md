Asian equities delivered one of the more striking single-session moves in recent memory. A gauge of Asian shares rose 2.6% to a record high, led by South Korea's Kospi, which surged more than 6% as Samsung Electronics rallied 14-16% to cross a $1 trillion market capitalization — only the second Asian company to achieve that threshold. The re-rating reflects a market judgment that memory's role in the AI infrastructure stack is structural rather than cyclical, with Samsung sitting at the intersection of chipmaking and data infrastructure expansion. AI-related names across the region followed suit, with the liquidity and momentum factors particularly pronounced.

The yen rallied as much as 1.8% against the dollar, reigniting speculation of Japanese official intervention — the second potential episode since April 30. Goldman Sachs analysts estimate Japan retains sufficient reserves for roughly 30 intervention episodes at last week's scale, though officials are expected to conserve firepower for more strategically effective moments. The yen remains approximately 9% weaker against the dollar over the past twelve months.

Geopolitically, Japan's deployment of 1,400 combat troops to the Philippines for joint Balikatan drills — including a ship-sinking exercise in the South China Sea — marks the first such deployment since World War II and signals a meaningful shift in Tokyo's willingness to project military power proximate to China. In China, Foreign Minister Wang Yi hosted Iran's Abbas Araghchi and urged Tehran to continue negotiations, ahead of the Trump-Xi summit expected next week where the Iran war will be a central agenda item. Indonesia tightened foreign currency purchase limits to $25,000 from $50,000 to support a beleaguered rupiah. In India, a five-week administrative halt on gold and silver imports has pushed domestic bullion prices higher and raised shortage concerns in the world's second-largest gold market.
