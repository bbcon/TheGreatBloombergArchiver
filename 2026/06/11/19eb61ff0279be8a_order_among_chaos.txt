As Peru continues a nail-biting vote count to find out who will become its 
ninth president in a decade, at least one institution is projecting calm.

The central bank is expected to leave interest rates unchanged today. No fuss. 
No recounts. Just another steady-handed move from Julio Velarde.

Velarde has run the central bank for nearly two decades and kept the country 
from veering off the rails through a revolving door of presidents, ministers 
and upheaval.

In a country that sets the gold standard for ungovernability 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-06-05/peru-presidential-runoff-2026-pits-roberto-sanchez-against-keiko-fujimori?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
, Velarde has become Peru’s closest thing to stability.
Velarde during a summit in Lima in November 2024.Photographer: Manuel 
Orbegozo/Bloomberg
The presidential race is the latest drama.

Conservative Keiko Fujimori led out the door after Sunday’s runoff. Then her 
leftist rivalRoberto Sánchez pulled ahead 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-07/peru-on-edge-as-exit-polls-show-no-clear-presidential-winner?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
 as ballots from rural areas trickled in.

Now attention has shifted abroad, where overseas votes may yet break the 
deadlock 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-08/peru-s-diaspora-emerges-as-tie-breaker-in-razor-edge-presidential-race?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
 and hand Fujimori the office she has pursued since 2011.

This may be her best shot so far 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/fujimori-takes-thin-lead-in-final-stretch-of-peru-s-runoff-vote?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
. It would be quite the prize — or quite a risk in a nation where nearly every 
president this century has ended up in court or behind bars, her father among 
them.

Yet all this suspense may ultimately prove futile.

Whoever wins, Peru’s status quo is unlikely to change. Nor the clashes with a 
fragmented yet influential Congress. Sánchez would struggle to push through the 
kind ofreforms that worry investors 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-07/peru-presidential-election-2026-voters-decide-between-fujimori-sanchez?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
 as he lacks the legislative support.

Fujimori would also need to build bridges, but she’d have a stronger chance of 
making it through a full five-year term since her party commands the largest 
bloc in Congress and should have enough seats in the newly restored Senate to 
help shield her.

Impeachment remains a constant of Peru’s fractious political system.

Either way, the next holder of the presidency may matter less for Peruvians 
than whether Velarde will still be there. — Carla Samon Ros 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AYGOPszB-_4/carla-samon-ros?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>

Chart of the Day

The FIFA World Cup is often dubbed the biggest event on the planet. But that’s 
not the case in the US 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/world-cup-2026-poll-shows-most-americans-don-t-care-about-tournament-or-messi?cmpid=BBD061126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5396>
, even with America hosting matches for the first time in three decades. In a 
recent survey, more than half of US adults said they’re unlikely to watch any 
of the 104 games on TV at home and only 13% said they were absolutely certain 
to. In sharp contrast to other parts of the world, soccer features low on the 
US fans list and that means many won’t be spending any money on the sport’s 
showpiece tournament.
