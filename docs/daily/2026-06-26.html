US

Monetary Policy and Inflation Outlook

Goldman Sachs estimates the Fed's preferred core PCE gauge will reach 3.2% YoY by December — well above the 2% target — leaving rate cuts a distant prospect. One unnamed Fed official described borrowing costs as "well positioned," reinforcing the Committee's patient stance. Market pricing has shifted rate-cut expectations into 2027, and JPMorgan, Bank of America, and Goldman strategists have coalesced around a renewed dollar-bullish view, citing Fed Chair Warsh's explicit commitment to restoring price stability. JPMorgan's Meera Chandan noted the Fed has effectively "activated" the dollar, with the rate differential versus other major central banks unlikely to narrow materially near term.

Markets: AI Trade Fractures

The tech sector sold off sharply on Friday following Apple's price hikes and a report that OpenAI may delay its IPO. Nasdaq 100 futures declined more than 1%. The episode crystallized a broader fragmentation within the AI trade: memory names are up over 200% year-to-date, data center infrastructure over 100%, while the Magnificent Seven cohort is negative for 2026 and AI-exposed software has shed roughly a third of its value. Rising component costs — chipmakers exercising pricing power — appear to be compressing margins downstream, and investors are increasingly differentiating within the space rather than treating AI as a single thematic position. ---

China's Mineral Leverage and AI Rivalry

China's deployment of export controls on yttrium and tungsten against Japan — with flows at precisely zero since January — represents a calibrated but consequential escalation in the bilateral dispute over Taiwan-related statements by Prime Minister Takaichi. Sumitomo Electric's chief executive acknowledged supply disruptions while suggesting diplomatic engagement may eventually be necessary. Japan's 15-year stockpiling program and Lynas Rare Earths' heavy rare earth shipments offer partial mitigation, but a broadening or prolonged squeeze would expose manufacturing vulnerabilities. Separately, Anthropic's formal accusation that Alibaba's Qwen lab orchestrated systematic access to its Claude model signals an intensifying regulatory dimension to US-China AI competition; Alibaba shares extended declines.

Markets and Corporate Developments

Samsung and SK Hynix are reportedly preparing a combined investment announcement Monday, which may provide some offset to near-term sentiment. Tokyo's CPI acceleration reinforces the Bank of Japan's gradual tightening trajectory. Alibaba and Tencent shares remain in bear market territory in Hong Kong, weighed by AI-progress skepticism and weak domestic consumption. ---

Macro: Southern Italy Rebalancing

Campania, the region encompassing Naples, grew 0.9% in 2025, outpacing Lombardy's 0.7% — a notable reversal of the long-standing north-south divergence. The turnaround reflects a decade of EU Recovery Fund investment alongside private-sector commitments from Apple, Cisco, and Accenture. Morningstar DBRS notes that a more balanced Italian economy would strengthen fiscal resilience and reduce euro-area fragmentation risk. Separately, French Finance Minister Roland Lescure reaffirmed his commitment to deficit reduction, a signal that fiscal consolidation remains on the agenda despite political headwinds.

Venezuela Earthquake and Fiscal Stress

The twin earthquakes that struck Venezuela have imposed a material humanitarian and fiscal burden on an economy already operating under severe strain. The decision to deploy IMF Special Drawing Rights for reconstruction underscores the government's limited conventional fiscal capacity. Acting President Delcy Rodríguez's mobilization of VenApp — previously associated with political surveillance — as a disaster-response tool reflects the improvised nature of the emergency response. The scale of structural damage and the trajectory of the death toll remain uncertain.

Brazil Political Dynamics

Strait of Hormuz: Fragile Normalization

The attack on the container vessel Ever Lovely — the first since the US-Iran interim peace deal — tested the durability of the nascent ceasefire. Iran subsequently stated that Hormuz navigation would be managed "in the spirit" of the peace agreement, and ship-tracking data confirmed two-directional traffic resumed Friday. Brent's decline following the incident suggests the market interpreted the episode as an isolated provocation rather than a structural breakdown, though the confidence of shipowners and crews remains fragile. Qatar is accelerating LNG production in parallel, reflecting producers' urgency to capitalize on the reopening window. A deterioration in the security environment remains the primary upside risk to oil prices and global inflation.

Energy Transition and AI Infrastructure

Texas, the US's largest oil and gas producer, is on a trajectory where solar and battery storage could overtake natural gas in new capacity additions within the next few years. BloombergNEF projects Texas solar capacity more than tripling to 82 gigawatts by 2035 against a bull-case gas addition of only 11 gigawatts. The economics of utility-scale storage are increasingly undermining gas's intermittency advantage. Meanwhile, global EV sales reached 1.65 million in May, with battery-electric vehicles growing 11% year-on-year — the third consecutive month of growth — and China's BEV sales turning positive for the first time in 2026.
