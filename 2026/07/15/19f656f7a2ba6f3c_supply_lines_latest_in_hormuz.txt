President Donald Trump backpedaled 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/trump-backs-off-20-fee-for-strait-of-hormuz-shipments?cmpid=BBD071526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6484>
 from his plan to impose a 20% charge on cargo shipments through the Strait of 
Hormuz.

Instead of collecting the fee, he said in a Truth Social post 
<https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/116918941071241802> that he’s going 
to seek “Trade and Investment Deals” with Gulf States.

Around the same time of Trump’s reversal, US Central Command said in a post on 
X <https://x.com/CENTCOM/status/2077214603713359959> that it completed a 
seven-hour wave of strikes against dozens of targets near the strait and along 
Iran’s coast aimed at degrading Tehran’s ability to threaten commercial 
shipping.

Big Take: 1,000 Days of War and Upheaval That Reshaped the Middle East 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-us-iran-war-middle-east-impact/?cmpid=BBD071526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6484>

Effective at 4 p.m. Washington time yesterday, the US announced it had resumed 
its blockade on Iranian shipping to and from its ports and coastal areas.

On Wednesday, a handful of vessels braved transits 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-15/handful-of-vessels-crossing-hormuz-as-us-blockade-takes-effect?cmpid=BBD071526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6484>
 through the contested passage, Bloomberg’s Weilun Soon reported.
