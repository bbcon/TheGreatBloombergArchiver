Tech buy signal?

Tech stocks are the cheapest they’ve been 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-30/rout-in-big-tech-stocks-sends-a-signal-that-has-preceded-gains?cmpid=033126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260331>
 relative to the broader US stock market in eight years.

The Nasdaq 100 Index, down 12% from its record high in October, is now priced 
below 21 times estimated earnings for the coming year, about 1.7 points above 
the S&P 500’s valuation.The last time the Nasdaq 100’s premium to the broader 
market was this narrow, it heralded a stretch of outperformance for the tech 
benchmark.

Of course, the upheaval of the Iran war threatens to confound many market 
signals of the past, and only time will tell if that’s the case with this one. 
Still, Big Tech’s time-honored pattern of leading the market has many Wall 
Street strategists recommending the sector as the best place to be.

“We are buyers of Big Tech,” said Julian Emanuel at Evercore ISI. “All the 
more so since there are a number of tech stocks whose valuation is below the 
pandemic trough.” —Alexandra Semenova 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AT8YbvmLmbw/alexandra-semenova>
