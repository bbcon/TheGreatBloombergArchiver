Deep Dive: China’s Medical Tourism Boom
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-06-10/china-s-medical-tourism-boom-draws-foreign-patients-seeking-cutting-edge-care?cmpid=061126_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5413>
The main reception at the Shanghai SinoUnited Hospital in June.Photographer: 
Qilai Shen/Bloomberg
Stuart Lye, 58, was living in New Zealand and on borrowed time — doctors had 
told him he had just three months to live in 2018, when he was diagnosed with 
high-risk myeloma. While years of treatment, including chemotherapy, stem cell 
transplants and drugs, had extended his life, his health was worsening andhe’d 
run out of options at home 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-06-10/china-s-medical-tourism-boom-draws-foreign-patients-seeking-cutting-edge-care?cmpid=061126_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5413>
.

 * He’d heard about CAR-T, a form of immunotherapy. But it’s not yet available 
commercially in New Zealand, and the clinical trials that are underway aren’t 
for his specific cancer.
 * Fortuitously, a fellow New Zealand patient who had received the treatment 
in China introduced Lye to a hospital in Shanghai and last year, after just 10 
days of communication with the center, he and his wife decided to head abroad. 
Following seven weeks undergoing a clinical trial, his cancer was brought under 
control. The entire process, including hospital care and airfare, cost Lye 
about $65,000.
 * Lye is one of a growing number of foreigners traveling to China for 
life-saving treatments as the easier availability of cutting-edge procedures, 
offered far cheaper than patients can access at home, propels the country intoa 
rising hub for medical tourism 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-04/china-s-first-foreign-owned-hospital-seeks-more-medical-tourists?cmpid=061126_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5413>
.
 * While traditional hotspots in the region such as Thailand, South Korea and 
Malaysia focus on servicessuch as cosmetic surgery 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2025-01-15/the-hidden-cost-of-korea-s-beauty-industry-video?cmpid=061126_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5413>
, IVF or physicals, China is trying to differentiate itself by providing some 
of the world’s most advanced procedures.
