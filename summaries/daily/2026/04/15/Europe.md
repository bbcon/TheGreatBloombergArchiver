The ECB's policy path for the April 30 decision remains genuinely unclear after ECB President Christine Lagarde declined to signal either a hike or a hold at the IMF Spring Meetings in Washington. Her comments placed the eurozone between the ECB's March baseline scenario — which itself incorporated two rate increases in 2026 — and the adverse scenario projecting 3.5% inflation this year. She explicitly stated the ECB has no tightening bias, yet simultaneously acknowledged that simply "looking through" the energy shock risks undermining the bank's credibility on price stability. Those positions are in tension, and Lagarde did not resolve them, leaving the market to price a range of outcomes ahead of the meeting.

The real economy signals from Europe are deteriorating. European chemical producers are facing acute input cost pressures, with prices of fossil-fuel-derived feedstocks — essential for everything from food packaging to pharmaceuticals — surging as supply remains trapped in the Persian Gulf. SKW Stickstoffwerke Piesteritz's COO warned that the loss of basic chemical supply chains could trigger an economic collapse, language that may be directionally correct even if the extreme outcome is not the central case. The Port of Antwerp-Bruges, home to major operations for TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil, and BASF, is managing additional disruption from a fuel spill.

French luxury goods suffered a notable earnings shock. Hermès shares fell as much as 14% — its largest single-day decline on record — after first-quarter sales growth disappointed, with the company attributing weakness to the confidence effects of the war. Kering also reported disappointing figures. ASML provided the counterpoint: Europe's most valuable company raised its full-year sales forecast to €36-40 billion from €34-39 billion, driven by AI-related semiconductor demand, with its CEO noting that supply cannot meet demand "for the foreseeable future."

Politically, German Chancellor Friedrich Merz is pressing for rapid disbursement of a €90 billion EU loan for Ukraine following Hungarian Prime Minister Orbán's electoral defeat. Merz met with Ukrainian President Zelensky in Berlin and reinforced support for Ukraine's EU membership. Italy's Giorgia Meloni has used Pope Leo XIV's moral critique of the Iran war as a pretext to distance herself from Trump, a shift that reflects broader European recalibration as the US president's behavior becomes increasingly difficult for allied governments to manage domestically.
