**UK Political Economy**
The UK's political transition appears to be accelerating. Burnham's allies are reportedly exploring a land tax, public utility renationalization, and deeper devolution — a materially more interventionist fiscal posture than Reeves pursued. Mansion House this week may function less as a policy announcement and more as a symbolic close to a chapter. The death of Senator Graham, meanwhile, removes a key interlocutor between US allies and the Trump administration at a sensitive moment in Ukraine diplomacy, with potential downstream implications for European defense coordination.

**Corporate and Industrial**
Akzo Nobel's rejection of Nippon Paint's €7.5 billion approach — made without board engagement — keeps its Axalta merger on track but raises questions about whether Nippon Paint will return with a higher bid or pursue other avenues. Volkswagen's CEO failed to secure supervisory board backing for a structural overhaul, leaving Europe's largest automaker in strategic limbo. The EU is separately developing a solidarity instrument to support supply-chain diversification away from China, reflecting the broader European push for industrial sovereignty that also underpins Germany's €35 billion space defense program announced by Defense Minister Pistorius.
