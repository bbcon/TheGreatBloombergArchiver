**European Briefing — May 21, 2026**

**Eurozone activity contracts again under energy shock.** The composite PMI fell to 47.5 in May from 48.8, holding below the 50-threshold for a second consecutive month and marking the sharpest contraction in two and a half years. The data confirm that the Iran war and accompanying surge in energy costs are weighing materially on activity. The divergence within the bloc is notable: Germany's composite reading was broadly stable, while France's plunged to its lowest level since 2020. The European Commission separately projected the fastest euro-area inflation since 2023, leaving the ECB in an increasingly uncomfortable position as it weighs further rate increases against deteriorating growth. French political risk eased somewhat with parliament's confirmation of Emmanuel Moulin as Bank of France governor, removing one source of uncertainty around the ECB's policy debate.

**UK fiscal response and Foreign Office retrenchment.** Chancellor Rachel Reeves is preparing a package to ease cost-of-living pressures stemming from the Iran conflict, including free summer bus travel for children and potential cuts to food tariffs. The UK also concluded a long-awaited free-trade agreement with the Gulf Cooperation Council, deepening commercial ties with oil-rich states at a sensitive moment. Less constructively, the Foreign Office is undertaking job cuts that will roughly halve the team handling Iran-related fallout — a curious resource decision given the conflict's persistence. UK May PMI data also showed waning momentum and mounting price pressures.

**Corporate and political notes.** Spanish construction group ACS, chaired by Florentino Pérez, has emerged as a significant beneficiary of the AI capex cycle through data-center contracts with Meta and a $2 billion partnership with BlackRock. Ubisoft fell 14% in Paris after guiding to declining sales on a thinner release slate. In Hungary, the ruling Tisza party plans to use its two-thirds majority to impose an eight-year prime ministerial term limit, effectively barring Viktor Orbán's return. Chancellor Merz proposed associate EU membership for Ukraine, though Kyiv has previously been cool to the formulation.
