Embracing risk again

With the ceasefire in the Middle East appearing to hold, confidence is 
creeping back into financial markets.

US stocks have climbed for seven successive days, the longest winning run 
since October 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-08/stock-market-today-dow-s-p-live-updates?cmpid=041026_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260410>
. Asian equities are wrapping up their first weekly gain since the war began, 
while, even more impressively, the MSCI Emerging Markets Index ison course for 
its biggest rally since mid-2020 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-10/em-stocks-set-for-best-weekly-gain-since-2020-before-iran-talks?cmpid=041026_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260410>
. Every major currency apart from the Indonesian rupiah and Turkish lira is up 
on the week versus the dollar.

Meanwhile, measures of implied volatility across stocks, bonds and currencies 
are back to where they were at the start of the conflict. US junk bond spreads 
are already at pre-war levels, with deals from the likes ofCoreWeave and Chobani
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-09/coreweave-tapping-junk-debt-market-after-meta-ai-computing-deal?cmpid=041026_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260410>
 going through successfully this week. In fact, the Bloomberg US Corporate High 
Yield Index closed yesterday at a record high. As a result, financial 
conditions are easing, adding to the more optimistic mood.

With WTI crude oil holding around $100 a barrel, Israel and Lebanon agreeing 
to talks and the shaky truce between the US and Iran mostly holding, this 
appears to be the rare Friday when investors are preparing more for good news 
than negative headlines over the weekend.
