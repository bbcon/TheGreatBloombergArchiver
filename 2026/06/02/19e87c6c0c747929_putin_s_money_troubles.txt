The economics of Russia’s war in Ukraine are increasingly tilting against 
Vladimir Putin.

That doesn’t mean he’ll stop.

As we report, top finance officials are telling the Russian president that his 
invasion isgrowing too costly 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-01/russia-finance-officials-tell-putin-war-spending-is-unaffordable?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
. The budget deficit is ballooning and growth is grinding almost to a halt as 
defense chiefs seek billions of dollars more for the military.

Putin appears unmoved, telling aides to seek savings elsewhere. He remains 
determined to seize Ukraine’s eastern Donetsk region, even as battlefield 
reports show Russian troops are struggling to make any headway.

Ukraine is growing more confident 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/ukraine-and-allies-grow-confident-russia-s-invasion-losing-steam?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 that it may have turned a corner after a brutal winter of Russian attacks.

Tens of billions in European Union funding are about to start flowing to Kyiv, 
easing a financial crunch. Ukrainian drones are striking Russian logistics 
supplies deep behind the front line and targeting oil refineries to limit the 
Kremlin’s ability to fund its aggression. The war is beingbrought home 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-17/record-drone-attack-on-moscow-kills-three-targets-refinery?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 to Russian citizens as never before.
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-06-02/officials-warn-putin-on-war-spending-budget-crisis-risk-video?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
Bloomberg’s Tony Halpin reports on top officials telling Vladimir Putin that 
spending on the war in Ukraine is on an unaffordable path.
Still, Putin unleashed another massive missile attack 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-02/at-least-nine-killed-in-major-russian-missile-attack-on-ukraine?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 on Ukraine overnight, the second since Russia threatened “systematic”heavy 
strikes 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-26/putin-steps-up-kyiv-missile-strikes-seeking-new-momentum-in-war?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 on Kyiv last month.

The US appears largely to have abandoned diplomacy aimed at ending the war 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/lavrov-tells-rubio-to-evacuate-us-diplomats-citizens-from-kyiv-mplnvqzc?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 as President Donald Trump focuses onthe conflict 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/ukraine-says-peace-talks-with-russia-on-hold-due-to-iran-war?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 with Iran. Europe’s leaders haven’t yet figured out who could represent them in
any outreach 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-28/eu-warns-of-russian-trap-over-reopening-talks-with-the-kremlin?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 to Putin.

His generals keep telling Putin he’s winning despite massive casualties in a 
fifth year of war.

Nobody in his inner circle dares contradict that message or suggest they cut 
their losses and reach a deal, even as Ukraine says it’s willing tocall a halt 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-10/zelenskiy-s-top-aide-sees-ukraine-nearing-a-deal-with-putin?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
 on the existing front lines.

Putin acts with impunity, confident he’ll prevail and apparently indifferent 
to theconsequences at home 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-08/russians-weary-of-war-and-repression-start-to-blame-the-kremlin?cmpid=BBD060226_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260602&utm_content=5110>
.

For Kyiv and its allies, the challenge is to raise the costs still further 
until Putin’s calculation finally changes.
