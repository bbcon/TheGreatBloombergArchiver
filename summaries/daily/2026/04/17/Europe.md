UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer faces a deepening political crisis after it emerged that Peter Mandelson, his ambassador to Washington, was granted security clearance despite objections from vetting officials — a decision complicated by Mandelson's past associations with Jeffrey Epstein. Starmer responded by dismissing the Foreign Office's top civil servant, Olly Robbins, but the episode has rekindled questions about his judgment and grip on power, with calls for his resignation growing. The episode arrives at a moment when Starmer was attempting to position the UK as a constructive international actor, co-hosting with France a summit on establishing a naval force to ensure freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz.

European divergences over the Hormuz mission are becoming apparent, with France and the UK convening the discussion but member states approaching the operational question from differing strategic perspectives. The London Metals Index reached a record high, driven largely by aluminum, as the Iran war disrupts supply chains and industrial metals respond to both genuine scarcity and safe-haven demand. Alstom fell 36% in Paris after withdrawing its free cash flow guidance for the full year, citing slow progress on key projects — the sharpest single-day decline for the French rail manufacturer since 2023.

On monetary policy, a Bloomberg survey points to an ECB rate hike in June as 2026 inflation runs above earlier projections. ECB policymaker Madis Müller called for vigilance on inflation risks, and President Christine Lagarde used the IMF-World Bank meetings in Washington to warn of the risks posed by Anthropic's new Mythos AI model — a somewhat unusual intervention that nonetheless reflects genuine institutional anxiety about cyber vulnerabilities at a moment of elevated geopolitical stress. Bloomberg Opinion editors cautioned that European central bankers should be careful not to amplify the energy shock's economic damage through premature tightening.

In France, three major mobile operators entered exclusive talks to acquire Patrick Drahi's SFR in a transaction valued at approximately $24 billion, a deal that would reshape the country's telecoms landscape if completed.
