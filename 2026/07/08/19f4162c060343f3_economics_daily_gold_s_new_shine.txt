Bullish on Bullion

Sometime during the crash in the price of gold in the mid-2010s, one veteran 
financial-market journalist declared that “this is the moment when the world 
realized gold isn’t a financial asset.” Famous last words, as they say.

After gold’s surge over the past several years, and continuous debate about 
the long-term durability of the US dollar’s dominance as the core of the global 
financial system, gold has become increasingly coveted as a store of value. Its 
appeal is arguably the highest now since then-President Richard Nixon in 1971
ended the dollar’s convertibility 
<https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/gold-convertibility-ends> to gold.

Central banks around the world have been steadily building their stockpiles, 
perhaps at least in part thanks to the idea that gold bars are beyond the reach 
of the US Treasury Department. The Treasury by contrast has the capacity to 
restrict the use of cash dollars or Treasuries.

When the price of gold dropped earlier this year, China was among those taking 
the opportunity toadd to its holdings 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-07/china-s-pboc-keeps-up-gold-buying-spree-as-iran-war-dents-prices?cmpid=BBD070826_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6321>
. Goldman Sachs analysts last month reaffirmed their call that central bank 
diversification into gold is a “structural 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/central-banks-scoop-up-a-load-of-gold-in-bumpy-first-quarter?cmpid=BBD070826_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6321>
” trend.

The volumes of international gold shipments are now such that swings from 
month to month can have big impacts on merchandise trade figures. That was on 
display Tuesday, when data showed the monthly US trade deficit widened to the 
biggest inmore than a year 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-07/us-trade-deficit-widens-to-biggest-in-more-than-a-year?cmpid=BBD070826_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6321>
. That was thanks in part to a volatile drop in exports of gold, shipments of 
which to Switzerland had surged earlier in 2026.

As for the financial trading of gold, International hubs are looking to build 
alternatives to New York and London.

step in the (increasingly

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-27/hong-kong-to-get-edge-in-asia-gold-hub-push-with-clearing-system?cmpid=BBD070826_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6321>

beneficiary.

Need-to-Know Research

After yesterday’s newsletter 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-07-07/reflections-from-pierre-yared-a-departing-member-of-brainiac-trump-team?cmpid=BBD070826_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260708&utm_content=6321>
 showcased research suggesting less population growth doesn’t hold back gross 
domestic product, today we look at analysis saying that, at least when it comes 
to specific groups of people, diminished ranks have a negative economic impact.

A new Peterson Institute for International Economics paper 
<https://www.piie.com/publications/policy-briefs/2026/class-dismissed-effect-international-student-exclusion-us-stem?utm_source=piie-updates&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=class-dismissed>
 aims to quantify the impact of restrictions on foreign student numbers — part 
of Trump’s immigration crackdown. “Latest available data indicate that student 
visa issuance is about one-third below normal,” they said. That’s an issue 
because foreign students are a key part of the STEM workforce, and PIIE 
researchers don’t see that being made up by native-born graduates.
Source, Michael A. Clemens, Jeremy Neufeld, and Amy M. Nice calculations based 
on US State Department data
A sustained one-third drop in annual flows of foreign STEM graduates into the 
US labor force would shrink the high-skill STEM workforce 6.2% overall and 
11.5% at the PhD level, the PIIE study said. After a decade, real US GDP would 
be as much as 1.6% smaller than it would otherwise have been, the researchers 
led by Michael Clemens calculated.

The annual loss to the US economy after 10 years would be comparable to wiping 
out the contribution of a mid-sized state such as South Carolina or Wisconsin, 
they said.
