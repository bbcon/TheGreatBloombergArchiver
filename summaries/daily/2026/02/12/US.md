# US Daily Briefing — February 12, 2026

**Labor Market Surprises, Complicating the Easing Path**

January nonfarm payrolls expanded by 130,000 — the strongest monthly gain in over a year — while the unemployment rate ticked lower. The print upended expectations of continued labor market softening and prompted futures markets to push the next anticipated Fed cut to July from June. Treasury yields rose in response, though pricing still embeds two cuts for the full year. That conviction may prove optimistic: with prime-age employment ratios suggesting the economy is operating near full capacity and the available labor supply revised lower, additional stimulus risks feeding through to inflation rather than activity. The January CPI release, expected to show core inflation easing to a post-pandemic low of 2.5%, will be the next critical data point. Markets appear to be assuming Kevin Warsh, the president's nominee to chair the Fed beginning in May, will deliver the priced easing — an assumption that may face tests later in the year if inflation proves sticky.

**House Republicans Break with Trump on Canada Tariffs**

In the most significant legislative pushback to the administration's trade agenda to date, the Republican-led House passed a bill terminating tariffs on Canadian imports, with six Republicans crossing the aisle. The measure is unlikely to survive a presidential veto, but the vote exposed GOP fissures heading into midterm primaries and reflects growing concern among swing-district members about affordability pressures. Trump's economic approval ratings have weakened as Democrats have outperformed in recent special elections.

**Equity Underperformance and Sector Rotation**

The S&P 500 is up just 1.4% year-to-date, ranking 69th among 92 global indices tracked by Bloomberg and trailing world ex-US equities by roughly 7.7 points. Capital is rotating both abroad — EM portfolio inflows reached nearly $100 billion in January, the second-highest on record — and within US markets toward economically-sensitive names. The Dow Jones Transportation Average has outpaced the S&P 500 by 13 percentage points over the past six weeks. In single-stock news, Cisco fell roughly 8% premarket on a soft outlook tied to rising memory costs, while Nuveen agreed to acquire Schroders for $13.5 billion.

**EPA Rescinds Endangerment Finding**

The administration moved to rescind the 2009 endangerment finding underpinning federal greenhouse gas regulation, setting up litigation likely to reach the Supreme Court and potentially constraining future climate rulemaking regardless of political control.
