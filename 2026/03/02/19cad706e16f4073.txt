 Deep Dive: UK’s Cash Boost 


Reeves. Photographer: Jason Alden/Bloomberg 
Chancellor of the Exchequer Rachel Reeves has been given  a £22 billion 
windfall <https://bloom.bg/4cSu1hd> ahead of her spring update this week, an 
unexpected boost as she seeks to show her efforts to stabilize the public 
finances are working.

 * Cash tax receipts have come in far stronger than the budget watchdog had 
forecast with billions extra generated from self-assessed income tax and the UK 
levy on sales, according to a Bloomberg analysis of official data. 
 * The outperformance raises the prospect of a sharp reduction in bond sales 
this year, easing the pressure on gilt yields that are the highest in the Group 
of Seven. 
 * The spring statement, due on March 3, is expected to be a muted affair, but 
closely scrutinized will be the amount of headroom Reeves has against her 
fiscal rule that taxes cover day-to-day spending by the end of the decade.

Robert Redford’s portrayal in The Candidate of a superficially attractive 
politician adrift without principles has proved prophetic, as Keir Starmer rose 
as the respectable front man for Labour Together only for the alliance to
unravel in scandal and strife <https://bloom.bg/4aYoyD7>, Martin Ivens writes. 
Now, with key allies disgraced and the party divided over migration and 
direction, Starmer appears caught in his most serious crisis, drifting without 
a clear plan.
