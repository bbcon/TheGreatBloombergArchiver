# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — February 10, 2026

**China sentiment inflection deepens, with caveats.** The pivot in foreign positioning toward Chinese equities continued to gather pace. Eight Roads, the venture vehicle backed by Fidelity's Johnson family, has reportedly shelved plans to offload stakes in roughly 40 Chinese tech companies for approximately $400 million — a sale conceived last year at a steep discount to a peak combined valuation near $1 billion. The reversal reflects improved valuations and the lower temperature in US-China relations following the Trump-Xi meeting in October. Goldman Sachs and BNP Paribas surveys point to accelerating allocations, and Qube's China long-only fund has grown more than tenfold to $2 billion year-on-year. Set against this, Bloomberg reports Chinese regulators are urging domestic institutions to pare US Treasury purchases — a development that bears monitoring for cross-border flow implications, though its operational scope remains unclear. Separately, BoE's Catherine Mann noted Chinese exporters have raised prices to the UK to offset US tariffs, a modest data point consistent with tariff pass-through rather than deflationary dumping.

**Japan's Takaichi trade extends.** The Nikkei 225 gained 2.8% Tuesday after Monday's 3.9% advance, with JGB yields edging lower — the rare equity-up, bond-stable combination the new prime minister needs. Markets are positioning for fiscal expansion paired with industrial policy directed at semiconductors, AI, defense, and shipbuilding. The constraint is external: a more assertive Tokyo and a weaker yen complicate the China relationship, and Xi faces a genuine dilemma over whether to engage Japan's most popular postwar leader or maintain distance given Tokyo's alignment with Washington.

**Regional and corporate notes.** Asian indices set fresh records overnight on easing AI capex concerns. Memory chip tightness continues to bifurcate Asian tech, pressuring Nintendo and Apple suppliers while lifting Korean and Japanese memory producers; Korean equities have added roughly $1.7 trillion in market cap since the start of 2025 but still trade at a governance discount. Singapore upgraded its 2026 growth outlook on AI-infrastructure demand. Adani Enterprises disclosed it received an OFAC information request on February 4 regarding alleged Iranian LPG imports through Mundra. Qatar's LNG expansion is creating absorption challenges, with stalled India talks a notable Asia data point. Hong Kong single-family offices rose 25% to 3,384 by end-2025, with 19% planning reduced US exposure — among the highest reallocation intentions surveyed.
