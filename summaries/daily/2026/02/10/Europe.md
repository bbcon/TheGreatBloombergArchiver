**European Macro & Markets Daily — February 10, 2026**

**UK politics: Starmer under siege, gilts wobble.** The Epstein scandal has metastasized into the most acute political crisis of Keir Starmer's premiership, despite no evidence the Prime Minister ever met the late financier. The catalyst remains the 2024 appointment of Peter Mandelson as ambassador to Washington — subsequently dismissed after correspondence with Epstein emerged, and now compounded by disclosures that Mandelson shared market-sensitive policy decisions with Epstein while serving as deputy PM. Starmer's chief of staff and communications head have resigned in the past 48 hours, and Scottish Labour leader Anas Sarwar has publicly called for him to stand down. Gilt yields moved higher following Sarwar's intervention, though the reaction remains an order of magnitude below the 2022 Truss episode — sterling has held up and the curve has not dislocated. Prediction markets now price Starmer's departure by end-June. The succession question is the more durable concern for fixed income: the most plausible candidates (Rayner, Burnham) sit to Starmer's left and are perceived as more fiscally expansive. As Mizuho's Jordan Rochester puts it, the question of "who's next" is a Damocles sword over gilt traders. Separately, BoE's Catherine Mann flagged that Chinese exporters raising prices to the UK to offset US tariffs is adding to domestic inflation.

**France: Villeroy's surprise exit hands Macron a succession card.** Bank of France Governor François Villeroy de Galhau unexpectedly announced his June departure, roughly 16 months ahead of schedule, citing personal reasons. The timing transfers the appointment from a potential post-2027 Le Pen or Bardella administration to Macron, allowing him to insulate the institution with a six-year term ahead of presidential elections. Given Bardella's stated preference for ECB QE to address French debt, the political stakes around the choice are material.

**Corporates.** BP shares fell 5% after suspending its $750m quarterly buyback and withdrawing capital return guidance, capping a turbulent year that saw CEO Auchincloss ousted. Barclays committed to returning at least £15bn through 2028. Standard Chartered CFO Diego De Giorgi unexpectedly resigned to join Apollo as EMEA head, removing him from CEO succession contention. Kering rose 10% in Paris on a smaller-than-expected Gucci sales decline and a special dividend. Alphabet is tapping sterling and Swiss franc markets, including a rare 100-year bond — the first century note from a tech issuer since Motorola in 1997. The Stoxx 600 was flat into the European afternoon.
