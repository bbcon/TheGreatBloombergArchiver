Brazil Goes From Big Loser to Winner in Trade Showdown With Trump
Brazil has gone from one of the biggest losers in Trump’s trade showdowns to a 
winner
Read in browser 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-02-26/brazil-and-trump-s-new-tariffs>
 <https://www.bloomberg.com/economics>

 
<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17056564&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=02262026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
 
<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17056564&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=02262026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
Brazil turned the other cheek when Donald Trump slapped 50% tariffs on its 
goods last year, refusing to retaliate even as it faced some of the most 
painful levies in the US president’s arsenal.

It’s a strategy that paid off last week, when the Supreme Court invalidated 
Trump’s sweeping global tariffs, turning Brazil and President Luiz Inacio Lula 
da Silva into perhaps the world’sbiggest winners 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-23/china-india-among-winners-after-us-court-blocked-trump-tariffs?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
, for now.

Even with Trump’s replacement levies 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/trump-s-10-global-levy-takes-effect-as-us-rebuilds-tariff-wall?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 in place, Brazil’s average tariff rate plunged by as much as 21 percentage 
points — to 10% from about 31%, according to estimates from Bloomberg 
Economics. That’s the largest drop among major US trading partners.

It’s another victory for Latin America’s largest economy — and for Lula, who’d 
already managed tosteer his country 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-21/lula-stares-down-trump-and-scores-tariff-victory-for-brazil?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 through the worst of Trump’s trade storm.

The leftist leader stood firm 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-31/lula-s-defiance-pays-off-as-trump-blinks-on-brazil-tariffs?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 last July when Trump hiked tariffs in a bid to stop the coup-attempt trial of 
former President Jair Bolsonaro, criticizing the US leader for interfering in 
Brazilian affairs. His team, meanwhile,banked on dialogue 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-03/brazil-s-lula-says-open-to-us-trade-talks-if-treated-as-an-equal?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 and a favorable outcome in the US legal system, even as relations with 
Washington soured.

Lula won Trump over 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-23/-i-saw-him-he-saw-me-trump-and-lula-get-their-new-york-minute?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 in a brief encounter at the United Nations, and by November, most of Brazil’s 
biggest exportshad been exempted 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-20/trump-deepens-tariff-cut-on-brazil-coffee-beef-as-prices-mount?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 from the heightened levies.

In Washington, the Supreme Court ruling lowered those that remained at a 
fortuitous time — just as Lula wasvisiting 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-21/brazil-india-seal-rare-earth-deal-amid-global-supply-strains?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 India’s Narendra Modi, as part of his ongoingpush to diversify 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-08/lula-pushes-to-turn-brics-buddy-modi-into-major-trade-partner?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 Brazil’s commercial and investment tiesbeyond the US and China 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-05-05/brazil-makes-trade-pitch-to-southeast-asia-as-us-and-china-fight?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
, its two biggest trade partners.

Lula-Trump Meeting

He’s expected to head to Washington in March for a White House visit. Among 
the topics Lula hopes to discuss, according to an official familiar with his 
plans: Trump’s steel tariffs, and a potentialreturn to the quota system 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-02-12/brazil-sees-quotas-as-good-solution-to-trump-s-steel-tariffs?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 that previously applied to Brazil, the third-largest supplier of steel to the 
US.

The next steps on tariffs from Washington, though, aren’t clear for Brazil or 
many other trading partners because different statutes will be used to justify 
levies going forward.

Speaking with Bloomberg TV on Wednesday, US Trade Representative Jamieson 
Greer indicated that Trump will sign a directive in coming days raising his 
global,150-day tariff 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-23/all-about-section-122-trump-s-new-go-to-tariff-provision?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 to 15% “where appropriate” and is seeking “continuity” with nations that 
struck trade deals. (Watch the full interviewhere 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-02-25/us-trade-rep-greer-on-15-tariff-usmca-eu-trade-deal-video?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
.)

Recall that USTR launched 
<https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2025/july/ustr-announces-initiation-section-301-investigation-brazils-unfair-trading-practices>
 a Section 301 investigation against Brazil last year, though officialsrejected 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-19/brazil-pushes-back-against-us-probe-on-its-trade-practices?cmpid=BBD022626_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 Washington’s allegations of unfair trade practices. Greer’s office offered a 
reminder last Friday that 301 probes involving Brazil and China are ongoing.

“If these investigations conclude that there are unfair trading practices and 
that responsive action is warranted, tariffs are one tool that may be imposed,” 
Greer said inhis statement 
<https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/february/ambassador-greer-issues-statement-supreme-court-ieepa-decision>
.
