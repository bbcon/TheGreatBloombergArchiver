**Manufacturing and Trade Policy**
The gap between the tariff revival narrative and the underlying data widened further last week. Factory employment stands 38,000 below year-ago levels, a deterioration that contradicts the White House's central economic promise. The administration now faces a structurally complex transition: the 150-day temporary tariff authority expires July 24th, requiring replacement policy. A forced-labor investigation targeting 60 economies has yielded recommendations of 10%–12.5% duties, with a Washington hearing scheduled Tuesday. A parallel investigation covering 16 economies — including China, India, and the EU — on industrial overcapacity has moved more slowly, raising the possibility that associated tariffs may be deferred until after November midterm elections, given polling showing broad public skepticism toward import taxes.

**Markets**
Equities opened firmer after the long weekend, with chipmakers and AI-adjacent hardware names — Sandisk, Western Digital, Applied Materials — rebounding 4%–6% in premarket trading following Friday's session. Morgan Stanley cautioned that equities will struggle to reach new highs as rotation away from semiconductor leaders toward laggards constrains index-level gains. The 10-year Treasury yield eased modestly to 4.46%.
