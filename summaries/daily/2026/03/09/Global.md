The defining development of March 9 is Iran's selection of Mojtaba Khamenei — the 56-year-old son of the assassinated Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, with deep ties to the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps — as the new supreme leader. The appointment, ratified by Iran's Assembly of Experts, signals continuity of hardline governance rather than any movement toward negotiated resolution. Prediction markets place a 48% probability on no ceasefire occurring before the end of April. President Trump described himself as "not happy" with the selection and is reportedly weighing deployment of special forces to seize Iran's near-bomb-grade uranium, amid US official concern that enriched stockpiles may have been relocated since UN inspectors last verified their position nine months ago. Tehran has stated it can sustain high-intensity conflict for at least six months.

Brent crude surged intraday to $119.50 — the largest intraday move in percentage terms since April 2020 — before retreating to near $104-105 after the Financial Times reported G7 finance ministers would convene to discuss a coordinated strategic petroleum reserve release. OECD nations collectively hold over 1.2 billion barrels in emergency reserves; the announcement provided meaningful short-term relief, though Citigroup estimates the Hormuz closure is removing approximately 16 million barrels per day of crude and refined products from global markets — roughly 16% of world supply — a disruption that dwarfs the 2022 Russian shock. Saudi Arabia has begun reducing output as Hormuz closure fills storage tanks; Iraq, the UAE, and Kuwait have similarly curtailed production. QatarEnergy has delayed a major LNG expansion project to at least 2027 following drone attacks on the Ras Laffan facility.

Beyond energy, the war is disrupting approximately one-fifth of global air freight, compressing Asia-Europe container traffic, and creating acute stress in fertilizer markets. Iran accounts for approximately 5% and 11% of global urea and ammonia trade respectively; urea prices have risen 25% since hostilities began, reaching levels last seen during Russia's Ukraine invasion. Crop prices are rising in parallel, threatening a global food inflation impulse that will materialize with a lag. The IMF has urged policymakers to prepare for a prolonged conflict scenario. European natural gas futures jumped as much as 30% on Monday. Aluminum briefly hit a near four-year high, and coal prices reached their highest level in over a year as gas substitution effects propagated through power markets.
