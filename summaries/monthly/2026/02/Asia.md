**Japan: Political Tailwinds and Structural Tension**

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's snap election victory early in the month delivered the LDP's largest postwar single-party mandate and set the dominant regional narrative for February. The Nikkei 225 surged nearly 4% on the Monday following the result and added a further 2.8% the following session, with the yen strengthening to ¥153 from near ¥160 in January and 30-year JGB yields falling roughly 40 basis points from their January highs by mid-month — a rare equity-up, bond-stable combination the new prime minister requires to validate her fiscal expansion agenda. Takaichi's formal re-election in parliament came with pledges of strategic investment and active fiscal policy, and Tokyo announced it would invest up to $36 billion in US oil, gas, and critical minerals projects as the first concrete tranche of the $550 billion framework agreed with the Trump administration. The structural tension, however, is unresolved: planned fiscal expansion against a 230% debt-to-GDP backdrop, combined with implicit pressure on the Bank of Japan to delay further rate normalization, draws uncomfortable comparisons to the UK's 2022 Truss episode in some analyst framing. A hawkish BOJ board member nonetheless signaled by late month that conditions for the next hike could align by spring, and Takaichi's forthcoming nominations of two BOJ board members — filling the seats of dovish members — will be closely parsed by bond and currency markets.

**China: Cautious Sentiment Inflection**

Foreign positioning toward Chinese equities gathered pace through February. Eight Roads shelved plans to offload stakes in approximately 40 Chinese tech companies, Goldman Sachs and BNP Paribas surveys pointed to accelerating allocations, and MSCI Asia Pacific January portfolio inflows approached $100 billion — the second-strongest reading in two decades per IIF data. At the same time, more than a dozen provinces lowered their 2026 growth targets, with consensus coalescing around a national figure of 4.5%–5% ahead of March's National People's Congress — the first downward revision in three years. Citi moved its previously baseline stimulus package to a ceiling rather than a base case. Xi Jinping's call to anchor growth around domestic demand as the "main driver" reinforced Beijing's consumption-led rebalancing rhetoric, though the IMF's Article IV assessment noted that Chinese household consumption at roughly 40% of GDP is essentially unchanged since 2005. On geopolitics, a Panama court decision voiding a CK Hutchison port contract drew a sharp response from Beijing, which instructed state firms to halt new project discussions in Panama, while Trump and Xi spoke by phone on trade and Taiwan with tone described as constructive but substantive commitments remaining opaque. A Pentagon episode in which Alibaba, BYD, and Baidu were briefly added to a military-adjacency list before the designation was withdrawn triggered a more than 3% slide in Alibaba in Hong Kong before partial recovery — illustrating the hair-trigger sensitivity of Chinese large-caps to US regulatory action.

**Regional Divergence: Indonesia, India, and Southeast Asia**

Indonesia presented the starkest cautionary signal in the region. MSCI placed the country's emerging-market classification under review and FTSE Russell followed suit, citing governance deterioration under President Prabowo. The rupiah weakened to levels last seen nearly three decades ago, Moody's cut the credit outlook to negative, and market capitalization fell nearly $80 billion from peak levels following the dismissal of financial regulatory heads and the appointment of a family member as Bank Indonesia deputy governor. A subsequently finalized US-Indonesia trade agreement provided some diplomatic offset but did not materially alter the institutional concerns. India's trajectory was considerably more constructive: the AI Impact Summit in New Delhi concluded with AI infrastructure pledges reaching at least $270 billion across the week, anchored by Ambani's commitment of up to 10 trillion rupees through Reliance and Jio and a $100 billion data-center buildout through 2035 from Adani. The RBI held rates at 5.25%, citing improved visibility from the US trade framework. South Korea's KOSPI led regional gains by mid-month, up nearly 30% year-to-date, supported by AI hardware exposure and shareholder-return reforms.

**The Australia Outlier and Regional Policy Divergence**

The Reserve Bank of Australia delivered the first rate hike among major central banks in 2026, with services inflation proving more persistent than anticipated and the RBA's deputy governor reiterating late in the month that Australian inflation remains "too high." The decision stands in marked contrast to the ECB's disinflation undershoot and the Fed's unresolved succession dynamics, underscoring how post-Covid synchronization is fading across the region. Japan's manufacturing PMI remained in expansionary territory for a third consecutive month, though Q4 GDP had expanded just 0.2% annualized, complicating the BOJ's normalization path. Mainland Chinese retail flows into gold, silver, copper, and tin — driven by limited domestic investment alternatives — meaningfully amplified the metals cycle's volatility through the month; China's top four gold ETFs recorded nearly $1 billion of outflows on a single Tuesday early in the month, and Shanghai copper futures registered record January volumes.
