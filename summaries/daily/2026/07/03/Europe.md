**ECB and Bank of England Policy**
The ECB's policy stance is evolving against a backdrop of leadership uncertainty. Lagarde's comments introduce a non-trivial possibility of a transition at the top of the institution before her term concludes, which could affect the pace and sequencing of any further policy adjustments. Separately, ECB policymaker Emmanuel Moulin described the bank as in a "good position" following recent rate hikes. At the Bank of England, the internal debate between Mann's hawkish activism and Bailey's caution reflects genuine uncertainty about UK inflation's trajectory — UK companies, per Bloomberg Economics data, plan to raise prices by 4% over the coming year, double the 2% CPI target.

**German Growth Outlook**
The Stoxx 600 rose 0.2% to approach back-to-back record highs, with mining and utility stocks leading. Germany's reform package, encompassing tax relief and labor market measures, offers a meaningful upside scenario for an economy that had previously halved its own growth forecast for 2026. Whether the legislative and implementation timeline delivers on Merz's projections for 2027 remains to be seen, but the direction of fiscal policy in Europe's largest economy has shifted meaningfully toward stimulus.
