Pre-war playbook

After SpaceX and its bankers pulled off the largest-ever IPO without a hitch 
last week, investors areturning their focus back to Earth 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-14/stock-market-moves-on-from-spacex-with-iran-deal-fed-in-focus?cmpid=061526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260615&utm_content=5524>
.

With a new week of trading kicking off, SpaceX buzz is giving way to optimism 
about a peace agreement between the US and Iran and the potential end to the 
economic damage associated with the war.

Fast-money investors are turning back to their pre-war playbooks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/hedge-funds-reopen-pre-war-playbooks-as-iran-risks-recede?cmpid=061526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260615&utm_content=5524>
, snapping up shorter-maturity Treasuries, Asian currencies and US consumer 
stocks.

“With inflation expectations subsiding on the back of the deal, the play is to 
go ‘back to the future’ with what worked in January or February — before the 
war,” said Thomas Hayes of hedge fund Great Hill Capital in New York.

The next major event that could upset the narrative comes on Wednesday, when 
the Federal Reserve votes on interest rates.(More on that below).

Of course, SpaceX lingers as an important read for investor sentiment, given 
how closely linked Elon Musk’s company is to the AI theme that has driven 
indexes to records this year. It’s also viewed as a referendum on the IPOs of 
Anthropic PBC and OpenAI, which could go public as soon as this year with 
valuations around $1 trillion each. —Carmen Reinicke
