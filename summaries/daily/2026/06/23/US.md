**Monetary Policy**
The Federal Reserve remained at the center of market attention on June 23, with Chicago Fed President Austan Goolsbee adding to the hawkish tone established at last week's FOMC meeting. Goolsbee expressed concern that above-target inflation appears to be moving in the wrong direction, language that reinforces the signal from the June FOMC, where half of the committee penciled in at least one rate hike in 2026. Treasury Secretary Bessent, separately, invoked the legacy of the late Alan Greenspan — who died Monday at age 100 — to argue for Fed forbearance, drawing parallels between the mid-1990s technology productivity boom and today's AI cycle. Current Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has similarly cited that episode, though the analogy remains contested.

**Equity and Credit Markets**
The AI-driven rally that has defined 2026 faced its sharpest test yet. Nasdaq 100 futures slumped 2.5%, with chipmakers including Micron, Intel, and Sandisk down 6–10% in premarket trading. SpaceX, which lost approximately $600 billion in market value over three sessions, pressed ahead with a roughly $20 billion investment-grade bond offering rated Baa1 by Moody's despite sustained negative free cash flow — an unusual extension of credit market trust. IBM bucked the trend, gaining 3.9% on a JPMorgan upgrade.
