Asian equity markets extended their rally on March 18, with the MSCI Asia-Pacific index rising approximately 2%, led by memory-chip names perceived as relatively insulated from the Middle East conflict. The standout performance came from South Korea, where the Kospi surged 5% after authorities moved to restrict publicly listed companies from listing certain subsidiaries — a practice long associated with shareholder value dilution and the so-called "Korea discount." Samsung Electronics rose 7% after signaling it is exploring a shift toward multi-year memory chip contracts, a structural change that could help stabilize supply dynamics. The Kospi now trades above 5,900, and the governance reform agenda under President Lee Jae Myung appears to be regaining momentum following a brief interruption caused by the Iran conflict.

In Japan, Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi characterized her upcoming White House meeting with President Trump as "extremely difficult." Japan's pacifist constitution constrains overseas military deployment, making Trump's earlier request for warship contributions to Strait of Hormuz operations untenable. Though Trump has since withdrawn the demand, the encounter carries considerable stakes: Japan is seeking US support in its standoff with China, and was hoping to position Takaichi's visit ahead of a planned Trump-Xi summit in Beijing — a trip now postponed by five to six weeks. Japan's February export data added to the complex backdrop, with shipments to the US falling 8% year-on-year and car exports by value down 14.8%, though overall exports beat expectations at +4.2%. Takaichi is expected to offer pledges of additional US investment, potentially in nuclear energy, as a diplomatic offset.

China faces mounting energy pressure, with industry consultant FGE NexantECA indicating Beijing is close to drawing on its estimated 1.4 billion barrels of commercial oil reserves as Strait of Hormuz disruptions persist. Alibaba raised AI computing and storage prices by as much as 34%, reflecting surging demand. The AI theme also drove significant market moves in Hong Kong, where LLM providers MiniMax and Zhipu gained approximately 20% and 19% respectively following Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang's endorsement of OpenClaw, an agentic AI platform. Goldman Sachs projected the Indian rupee could slide to 95 against the dollar over the next year due to Iran conflict fallout, with pressure on the Reserve Bank of India to respond if inflation accelerates. Separately, Pakistan's airstrikes on Kabul prompted global alarm from the EU and China, raising concerns about a second concurrent conflict igniting alongside the Gulf war.
