The heat wave searing much of Europe is officially the most severe ever 
recorded in the region

Researchers at World Weather Attribution, who looked at heat and humidity 
levels during both the day and night during three consecutive days in the month 
of June, found that temperatures were between 5C and 12C above the seasonal 
averages across France, Germany, Italy, Spain and southern England.

Theodore Keeping, a research associate at Imperial College London and a 
co-author of the study, said the onset of such high levels of heat this early 
in the season was striking.

тАЬThis was the most severe heat wave for any time of the year, not just June,тАЭ

Climate change drove temperatures higher than they would otherwise have been,

By Eric Roston 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AQdgGkZhizQ/eric-roston?cmpid=BBD062626_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260626&utm_content=5981>

In eight high-income countries тАФ several of which are currently experiencing 
extreme heat worsened by global warming тАФ the share of people reportingтАЬvery

The finding comes from the fourth World Risk Poll report, sponsored by the 
LloydтАЩs Register Foundation and conducted together with Gallup Inc., released

The eight countries are Spain, the UK, Ireland, Canada, Denmark, Croatia, 
Kuwait and New Zealand. All high-income countries together averaged a 
five-point drop in a high level of concern, to 49%.

тАЬThis is a notable finding, as high-income countries contribute most to

authors write.

In low- and middle-income countries, by contrast, the percentage of 
respondents saying they were very concerned about climate risk either stayed 
the same as in 2019 or slipped up or down a couple of points.

Other wealthy countries тАФ the US in particular тАФ stand out on a metric that 
the report authors say may be an impediment to public support for action to 
curb warming. People who consider climate change a тАЬvery serious threatтАЭ vastly 
underestimate the percentage of their compatriots who believe the same thing.

Fifty-one percent of Americans think climate change is тАЬvery seriousтАЭ but 
believe only 10% of others do as well. The gap is second only to that in 
Portugal, where 66% of people are тАЬvery concernedтАЭ but believe just 24% of 
others are.
