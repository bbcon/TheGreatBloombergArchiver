Asian equity markets advanced approximately 1.2% on Monday following an Axios report indicating Iran had transmitted a new proposal to Pakistan — the primary mediator — suggesting willingness to reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for a lifting of the US port blockade, with nuclear negotiations deferred to a later phase. MSCI's emerging markets index reached a record level, and the regional technology gauge surged 3.3% to an all-time high, reflecting the same AI-driven semiconductor enthusiasm driving US markets. Taiwanese and South Korean indices, both heavily weighted toward chipmakers, have materially outperformed the US market over the eighteen months since the 2024 election.

China featured prominently across several distinct developments. Beijing blocked Meta's $2 billion acquisition of agentic AI startup Manus, citing concerns over technology leakage to the US. The decision comes as Beijing tightens oversight of domestic AI firms and signals continued wariness about foreign acquisition of strategic technology assets. Separately, China's tax authorities launched a sweeping crackdown on circular invoicing practices in the metals trading sector, rattling copper, aluminum, and silver traders in Shanghai who rely on such invoices for bank financing. The State Taxation Administration framed the investigation as targeting fraudulent activity that has artificially inflated economic output measures. Economists have also sharply upgraded forecasts for China's import growth, now expecting it to surpass export expansion for the first time since 2021 — a development that, if sustained, could moderate political pressure over the bilateral trade balance.

The US sanctioning of Hengli Petrochemical's Dalian refinery, one of China's largest private "teapot" operators, marks an escalation in Washington's effort to interdict Iranian oil revenues. Beijing's likely response — condemning the sanctions while maintaining de facto tolerance of private-sector flows — represents a calibrated posture that avoids a direct confrontation while protecting China's energy security interests. How Beijing responds formally will be watched ahead of an anticipated Trump-Xi meeting next month. In India, Sun Pharmaceutical agreed to acquire Organon for approximately $11.75 billion on an enterprise basis, marking one of the largest outbound acquisitions in Indian corporate history.
