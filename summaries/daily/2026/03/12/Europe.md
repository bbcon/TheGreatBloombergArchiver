European bond markets are absorbing overlapping pressures. Yields have risen across the UK, Germany, and Australia as investors price in both the fiscal cost of military spending increases and the probable need to shield households from an energy shock. The European Union has warned that inflation could exceed 3% this year if oil remains near $100 per barrel and gas prices stay elevated — a scenario that would substantially complicate ECB rate decisions in an environment where the central bank had been cautiously easing. The EU's inflation warning is particularly resonant given that Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG complex, which produces roughly 20% of global LNG supply, has been affected by the regional conflict, driving European and Asian gas prices to their highest levels since early 2023.

The Iran war is creating political fractures within the bloc. EU officials have acknowledged fears that economic pressure from elevated fuel prices may deepen disagreements between member states, potentially giving Moscow leverage to erode European solidarity over Ukraine. Kremlin envoy Kirill Dmitriev met senior Trump officials in Florida to discuss the energy crisis and prospective US-Russia economic cooperation — a development that adds to European anxiety about the durability of Washington's NATO commitments.

In France, Marine Le Pen's National Rally is contesting municipal elections in Marseille, testing whether the party's appeal can extend beyond its provincial base into a cosmopolitan urban setting. Polls indicate crime ranks among voters' top concerns in the port city. Meanwhile, the Swiss franc has continued appreciating as a safe-haven destination, placing the SNB in a difficult position: intervention risks US accusations of currency manipulation, yet inaction compounds pressure on Swiss exporters. Analysts suggest the SNB may be forced to revisit negative interest rates despite significant domestic opposition.
