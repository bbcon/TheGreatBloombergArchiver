Imports soared more than 27% in May, leaving a trade surplus of $105.4 
billion, the biggest since January, with companies loading up on foreign chips 
and equipment.

Eric Zhu of Bloomberg Economics said the gains partly reflected higher prices 
and a low base of comparison tied to last yearтАЩs drop in China-US trade ahead 
of a tariff truce.

тАЬEven so, the report allays concerns that the Iran war is taking a toll on 
ChinaтАЩs export engine, and highlights increasing linkages between the global 
tech cycle and trade flows,тАЭ he wrote in aresearch note <>.

The latest numbers align with a World Trade Organization assessment 
<https://www.wto.org/english/news_e/news26_e/wtoi_05jun26_405_e.htm> last week 
that showed merchandise trade growth globally has been growing above trend 
since the start of 2025. The biggest driver is electronic components, helping 
offset weakness in automotive products and agriculture, according to the WTO.
WTO breakdown of merchandise tradeSource: World Trade Organization
ChinaтАЩs strong performance is driven by the global AI infrastructure buildout, 
with outbound shipments of computers and parts soaring 66% in May from a year 
ago, and sales of integrated circuits abroad surging 111%.

Exports to the US rose almost 36% тАФ the most since 2021 тАФ extending their 
recovery after a long streak of double-digit declines during the tariff war 
started by President Donald Trump.

тАЬA stabilizing China-US trade relationship also boded well for direct exports 
to the US,тАЭ HSBC economist Taylor Wang wrote in aresearch note 
<https://www.research.hsbc.com/R/55/kz6zMLf>.
