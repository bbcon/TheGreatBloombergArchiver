Space X loses its IPO glow

After all the Wall Street fawning over SpaceX’s IPO last month, the stock 
hasn’t yet lived up to the hype. 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/spacex-fizzles-to-close-1-above-ipo-price-weeks-after-debut?cmpid=071526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6566>

SpaceX shares have fallen steadily this month and are now just $1 away from 
the $135 price tag buyers paid last month in the biggest first-time share sale 
ever. It’s worse for anyone that bought shares back in mid-June, when they 
popped above $200.

It’s a poor showing for a company that’s been given a resoundingly bullish 
reception. More than a dozen banks including Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan Chase and 
Goldman Sachs started coverage withbuy-equivalent ratings 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-07/spacex-shares-win-early-bullish-calls-from-wall-street-brokers?cmpid=071526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6566>
.

Skeptics note that the stock trades at more than 30 times estimated sales, 
among the highest in the Nasdaq-100. SpaceX is also facing an extended lock-up 
that will see insiders periodically releasing shares into the market over the 
coming months.

“We still don’t think SpaceX has found its low,” said Ken Mahoney at Mahoney 
Asset Management.

The counterargument: It’s normal for newly public companies to experience 
stock volatility. A Truist Wealth analysis of 30 major technology IPOs over the 
past 15 years found that they averaged a maximum decline of 55% in the first 
year of trading.

“I might actually consider, if this downdraft continues much more, picking up 
some shares of the individual company because I like the inspirational message 
and goal of the company,” said Talley Leger, chief market strategist at the 
Wealth Consulting Group. —Carmen Reinicke

SK Hynix’s ballooning premium

The latest example of how South Korea’s market swings are leading to some 
weird distortions — SK Hynix’s US-listedADRs are trading at a 51% premium 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/sk-hynix-adr-premium-balloons-to-nearly-50-over-korean-shares?cmpid=071526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6566>
 to its South Korean shares.

The extent to which the Seoul-listed shares can be converted into ADRs has 
been a key uncertainty in the market, inhibiting flows that typically keep 
prices closer, according to traders.

“Sourcing shares to borrow in order to short the ADR is nearly impossible,” 
said Sanghyun Park, founder of Clepsydra Capital.

The premium also reflects strong demand for SK Hynix itself. The company 
trades at about 5.1 times forward earnings, versus roughly 6.9 times for 
Micron, despite holding a leading position in the high-bandwidth memory market 
powering AI servers. That valuation gap has drawn investors willing to pay a 
premium for easier access to the stock through the US listing. —Carmen Reinicke 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AT8Yc28TTKE/carmen-reinicke?cmpid=071526_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260715&utm_content=6566>
