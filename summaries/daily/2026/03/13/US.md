US equity futures were modestly higher on Friday, with S&P 500 futures up 0.13% to 6,686, though the index has declined roughly 3% in March as the Iran conflict has reframed the macro outlook. The Magnificent Seven gauge is nearing correction territory, down nearly 10% from its peak, with the group trading mixed. The session's most notable corporate story was the departure of Adobe CEO Shantanu Narayen after 18 years, with shares falling approximately 8.5% in pre-market trading following a lackluster revenue outlook and deepening investor concern about Adobe's competitive positioning in the AI era.

The macro backdrop for the Federal Reserve has grown meaningfully more complex. Economists have pushed back expectations for the next rate cut to June, and Goldman Sachs revised its forecast further to September. With oil above $100 per barrel, the inflation-growth tension has sharpened: elevated energy prices are simultaneously stoking price pressures and eroding household purchasing power, limiting the policy space typically available in a slowdown. The ICE BofA MOVE Index, a measure of Treasury market volatility, climbed to its highest level since June, as traders dialed back 2026 rate-cut expectations materially. Goldman also raised its 12-month US recession probability by 5 percentage points, to 25%.

TD Securities quantified the threshold at which demand destruction might outweigh inflation concerns for the Fed at roughly $150 per barrel of Brent, a scenario under which it expects the Fed to ease by more than a percentage point to address recession risk. A more severe scenario involving material damage to Middle East energy infrastructure, with oil reaching $200, could see GDP contract and rates fall below 2%.

On the fiscal front, American officials disclosed that the first six days of the Iran war cost over $11.3 billion, with the Financial Times reporting the US has burned through "years" of critical munitions. The financial and strategic costs of the campaign are accumulating without a discernible endgame, a dynamic that is beginning to register in market pricing and economic forecasts.
