Deep Dive: London’s Young Workers Shut Out
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-30/london-s-youth-unemployment-crisis-looms-over-local-elections>
Photographer: Jose Sarmento Matos/Bloomberg
Once a global hub for entry-level jobs, London is now the epicenter of a 
growing youth joblessness crisis that isthreatening to blight the futures 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-30/london-s-youth-unemployment-crisis-looms-over-local-elections?cmpid=033026_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260330>
 of hundreds of thousands of people and fuel a backlash against Keir Starmer’s 
Labour government in key municipal elections in just over a month.

 * Young people drawn to the capital for its rich cultural life and 
opportunities are finding the junior roles that traditionally launched careers, 
from marketing to HR traineeships, increasingly hard to find in a job market 
upended by AI and global turmoil.
 * The number of graduate roles in London fell from around 13,000 in 2019 to 
just over 2,000 at the start of 2026, according to Adzuna, a job-search website.
 * Around one in four 16-24 year-olds in the capital are looking for work, the 
highest proportion in the UK. Young people in other European capitals like 
Madrid or Paris are faring better.  
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-30/london-s-youth-unemployment-crisis-looms-over-local-elections>

Opinion

Unless the Iran conflict ends very soon, oil consumption needs to adjust to 
lower supply, Javier Blas writes. One way to do this is through politicians 
forcingsome reduction in oil use 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2026-03-30/oil-prices-iran-war-is-pushing-energy-market-into-demand-destruction-mode?cmpid=033026_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260330>
. Examples include lower speed limits on highways, and less use of heating and 
air conditioning. Mandatory work-from-home is another option, though 
politically and economically more fraught.
