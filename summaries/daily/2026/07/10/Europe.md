• Andy Burnham secured nominations from 322 of 403 Labour MPs on the first day of the party leadership contest, making his path to UK prime minister appear highly probable.
• EasyJet shares surged 14% in London after Apollo Global Management submitted a takeover offer topping a rival Castlelake proposal, establishing a potential bidding war.
• Vodafone rose 11% after French billionaire Xavier Niel agreed to acquire a stake from Emirates Telecommunications Group for approximately $6 billion.

**UK Political Transition**

The Labour leadership contest to replace Keir Starmer has effectively resolved after a single day of nominations. Andy Burnham's 322-of-403 MP vote share leaves his rivals with little viable path to the membership stage, and markets appear to be pricing a smooth transition. Burnham indicated he sees his mandate as representing "a new approach to politics," though the policy contours of that approach remain to be elaborated. The scale of his parliamentary backing — 80% of the caucus — suggests the party is prioritizing unity over ideological differentiation at a moment of likely electoral pressure from Reform UK.

**European Corporate Developments**

EasyJet's 14% single-session gain reflects the premium Apollo's offer carries over the existing Castlelake proposal, though neither bid price has been formally disclosed. The situation sets up a competitive auction dynamic and raises broader questions about the valuation gap between European and US-listed aviation assets. Volkswagen's separate announcement that it intends to cut its model lineup by as much as half underscores the depth of restructuring pressure facing European automakers — the company's China profits have deteriorated materially, removing a historically significant earnings cushion. The full scale of the restructuring, described internally as the most significant in decades, is still being finalized.
