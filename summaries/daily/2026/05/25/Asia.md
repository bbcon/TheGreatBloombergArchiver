**China Capital Controls Rattle Markets**
Beijing's most forceful crackdown yet on illicit cross-border equity trading sent shockwaves through Chinese financial markets. Futu Holdings founder Leaf Li saw his net worth fall $1.7 billion in a single session, with his fortune now less than half its late-October 2026 level. The move is part of a broader effort to stem capital outflows estimated at roughly $1 trillion in 2025 alone — the largest annual figure since data began in 2006. Chinese retail investors are reportedly scrambling to identify alternative channels, underscoring the depth of demand for offshore exposure even as authorities tighten controls.

**Huawei Semiconductor Push**
Huawei's semiconductor chief He Tingbo used a rare public appearance at an industry conference to outline a new pathway to close the gap with TSMC. The company and manufacturing partner SMIC currently lag TSMC by approximately five years in process capability; the announced approach suggests a potential acceleration, though the timeline and technical specifics remain unverified independently. The development will be closely watched by governments and chipmakers monitoring China's progress toward greater self-sufficiency in advanced semiconductors.

**India: Rupee Pressure and Energy Costs**
Reserve Bank of India Governor Sanjay Malhotra made the unusual step of publicly suggesting the rupee may now be undervalued following its recent depreciation — a signal analysts at ANZ interpret as an effort to arrest the currency's slide amid significant balance of payments pressures. The comments come ahead of the RBI's June 5 rate decision. Separately, India raised fuel prices for the fourth time in May, reflecting the direct pass-through of elevated global energy costs from the Hormuz disruption.

**Singapore Flags Macro Risks**
Singapore revised down its 2026 economic outlook despite a stronger-than-expected first quarter, citing Middle East energy disruption, US tariff risks, and potential pullback in AI capital spending as material downside risks to the outlook.
