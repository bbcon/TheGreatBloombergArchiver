Asian markets displayed tentative resilience on Tuesday, with the MSCI Asia index gaining close to 1% overnight as investors assigned some probability to a diplomatic outcome before Trump's deadline, though conditions remained fragile given Iran's continued strikes across the Gulf in the hours preceding the cutoff. Samsung Electronics reported a striking eightfold increase in quarterly profit, driven by robust AI memory chip demand that has thus far defied the market disruption generated by the war; shares rose as much as 4.9% in Seoul. The result underscores the degree to which AI-related semiconductor demand has become a stabilizing force for the Korean technology complex even as broader market sentiment deteriorates.

India's macro position warrants close attention. The Reserve Bank of India is expected to hold rates at its Wednesday meeting, caught between the need to support slowing growth and the currency pressure generated by India's acute dependence on Middle Eastern energy—the country sources roughly half its crude and most of its cooking gas from that region. The rupee has declined more than 3% year-to-date, prompting what the RBI describes as some of its most aggressive intervention steps in over a decade. State Bank of India separately disclosed approximately $5 billion in positions against the rupee that were caught by the regulator's crackdown on currency speculators, with estimated losses of around $32 million from forced unwinds.

China's housing stress deepened in focus, with lenders increasingly confronting underwater mortgages as property values continue to fall, raising the risk of borrower defaults and complicating banks' efforts to preserve asset quality without triggering a broader credit deterioration. Separately, China's central bank purchased the most gold in more than a year in March, a signal of continued reserve diversification. In Taiwan, the Kuomintang's chair began a six-day visit to Beijing—the first by a sitting KMT leader in a decade—raising cross-strait engagement questions at a sensitive geopolitical moment.
