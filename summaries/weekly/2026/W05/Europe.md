**ECB and the Cross-Atlantic Rate Dynamic**
The week's most consequential development for European rate markets was the Warsh nomination and its potential to sustain dollar strength into mid-2026. The ECB has been navigating an easing cycle calibrated against declining eurozone inflation and a growth backdrop that remains uneven across member states. A Fed chair perceived as more orthodox on inflation would likely support the dollar against the euro, introducing a complicating factor for the Governing Council: euro weakness, if pronounced, risks reversing a portion of the imported disinflation that has aided the ECB's task and could force a more cautious pace of cuts than the growth backdrop alone would warrant. The tension is not yet acute — Warsh would inherit a committee he cannot immediately redirect, and the confirmation-to-policy transmission is slow — but Bund-Treasury spreads bear watching in the sessions ahead as markets price the implications of a potential shift in the Fed's reaction function. European duration investors are effectively pricing two sources of uncertainty simultaneously: the ECB's terminal rate and the dollar-denominated discount rate against which European assets are benchmarked.
