President Donald Trump’s summit with China’s Xi Jinping last week was billed 
as the mostpivotal moment 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/audio/2026-05-18/big-take-what-trump-xi-summit-means-for-trade-podcast?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 of the year so far for global commerce. But there’s another major trading 
power soon facing its own showdowns with the world’s two largest economies.

On Tuesday, European Union officials will meet to try to finalize 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-19/eu-races-to-finalize-us-trade-deal-to-avoid-more-trump-tariffs?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 legislation for the bloc’s trade deal with the US. Failure to do so means the 
EU risks missing Trump’s July 4 deadline to have the accord in place.

Video: EU Officials Rush to Finalize US Trade Deal Amid Trump Tariff Threat 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-05-19/eu-officials-rush-to-finalize-us-trade-deal-video?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>

Trump previously threatened to increase tariffs on European auto imports to 
25% from 15% because Brussels wasn’t moving quickly enough to implement a pact 
signed nearly a year ago.

That would bring badly timed economic pain to industrial Europe, which is 
already reeling from theenergy shock 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/eu-seeks-to-shield-farmers-from-war-induced-fertilizer-shock?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 reverberating from the Iran war.

And the ripple effects won’t stop at factory gates: Ryanair CEO Michael 
O’Leary said there will becorporate casualties 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/ryanair-ceo-sees-european-airline-casualties-on-jet-fuel-spike?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 among European airlines if the conflict continues and the Strait of Hormuz 
remains restricted.

It’s not only the transatlantic trade relationship that’s strained. The EU’s 
ties with Beijing has also hit a rough patch.

EU officials are exploring whether the bloc needs new measures to protect its 
economy against an influx of Chinese goods, according toreporting this week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/eu-plans-tougher-trade-measures-to-rebuff-chinese-export-surge?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 from Bloomberg’s Alberto Nardelli and Alexander Weber.

Hormuz Latest: Trump Says Holding Off on New Iran Strikes After Gulf Appeal 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/us-and-iran-still-at-odds-despite-renewed-diplomatic-efforts?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>

These discussions revolve around which instruments EU member states are 
willing to deploy against overcapacity in China’s manufacturing sector, and 
whether new measures are needed. The threshold and terms to deploy the EU’s 
most potent trade tool — the anti-coercion instrument — will also be considered.

June Summit

Later this month EU officials will hold an orientation debate on China and how 
to handle its economic practices. That discussion will pave the way for a 
debate among the bloc’s leaders at a summit in June where they will look to 
forge a common approach to Beijing.

The urgency for Brussels to act on the Chinese import wave has rarely been 
greater as both sides try to protect theirfragile economies 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/china-economy-slows-sharply-as-investment-returns-to-contraction?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
.

Over the past month, China has announced new regulations 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/how-china-s-new-trade-rules-counter-push-to-rewire-global-supply-chains?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 aimed at retaliating against foreign nations and companies “implementing or 
assisting in actions that harm the security of China’s industrial and supply 
chains.”

New Economy: China Pushes Back on the West’s De-Risking Strategy 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-05-16/china-pushes-back-on-the-west-s-de-risking-strategy-new-economy?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>

Beijing’s latest in a series of moves serves as a warning especially toward 
the EU, which has been debating the so-called “Made in Europe 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/china-criticizes-eu-s-made-in-europe-plan-as-protectionist?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
” proposal. It mandates that EU nations favor domestic companies in public 
procurement contracts. The goal is to ensure that a fifth of the bloc’s 
economic output comes from manufacturing by 2035.

On Friday, another threat landed squarely at Europe. China warned the EU that 
it wouldtake countermeasures 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-15/china-criticizes-eu-over-improper-jurisdiction-in-nuctech-probe?cmpid=BBD051926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260519&utm_content=4548>
 if the EU proceeds with a probe of Chinese security-devices maker Nuctech. 
Brussels is investigating whether Beijing’s subsidies for the company give it 
an unfair market advantage over European firms.
