**Chile: Growth Reality Check**
Chileans who voted for a market-oriented administration to accelerate economic recovery are confronting a more difficult reality. Growth has stalled sufficiently to raise recession concerns in one of Latin America's historically most stable economies, while the broader regional shift toward pro-market governments has increased competition for foreign direct investment. The dynamic illustrates a recurring tension in the region: structural reform agendas that are credible in design can still be undermined by adverse external conditions, including elevated global rates, softer commodity demand, and geopolitical uncertainty constraining capital flows.

**Brazil-US Trade**
Brazil's government held its fifth high-level meeting with US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer, reflecting sustained diplomatic engagement as both sides navigate the tariff environment. No specific outcomes were disclosed. The frequency of contact suggests Brazil is actively seeking to differentiate its trade relationship with Washington, though the broader US tariff architecture continues to create uncertainty for Brazilian exporters.
