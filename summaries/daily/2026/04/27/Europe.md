Germany's economic outlook continues to deteriorate, with the government last week halving its 2026 growth forecast to 0.5% — a significant downward revision from projections made when Chancellor Merz took office promising a "year of growth." The Iran war's disruption to energy markets is the proximate cause, though Germany entered 2026 already burdened by structural competitiveness challenges. The Ifo Institute's business expectations index fell to its lowest level since 2023, while eurozone PMI data slipped into contraction territory with delivery times and input price inflation rising at their fastest pace since 2022 — consistent with mounting stagflationary pressure. Thursday's first-quarter GDP release will offer an early quantitative read on the damage, though economists expect the full impact to emerge in subsequent quarters given that hostilities began mid-period. Germany's €500 billion infrastructure stimulus fund may be the primary buffer preventing outright contraction, per Ifo President Clemens Fuest.

King Charles III arrived in Washington Monday for a four-day state visit, including bilateral meetings with President Trump, a White House banquet, and an address to a joint session of Congress. The visit comes as UK-US relations are under strain, against the backdrop of a weekend shooting at the White House Correspondents' Dinner. Prime Minister Starmer faces his own domestic pressures: Labour appears set to lose control of the Welsh Parliament for the first time since its formation in 1999, with May 7 local election results expected to be broadly unfavorable for the government.

In European corporate and financial developments, Germany was reported to have sounded out European banks earlier this year to serve as potential white knights in defense of Commerzbank against UniCredit's ongoing takeover approach. Bayer is seeking a favorable Supreme Court ruling today that could limit the scope of Roundup weedkiller litigation that has cost the company more than $10 billion. S&P cut credit ratings for Belgium and Slovakia and placed Finland on negative watch, reflecting fiscal deterioration partly linked to elevated energy costs. Lufthansa has cancelled approximately 20,000 flights over the next six months in response to the surge in jet fuel prices — an early and visible sign of the war's operational consequences for European aviation.
