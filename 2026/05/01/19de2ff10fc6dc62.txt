As Latin America shifts to the right, the movement’s intellectual lodestar is 
looking wobbly.

Javier Milei’s approval is at the lowest since he took office 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/argentina-bond-investors-increasingly-fear-the-day-after-milei?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
 in Argentina in 2023, with campaigns against corruption and inflation — the 
two main tenets of his presidency — faltering.
Milei at the National Congress in Buenos Aires on March 1.Photographer: Anita 
Pouchard Serra/Bloomberg
Milei’s cabinet chief and the public face of his austerity drive, Manuel 
Adorni, is under investigation over allegations of illicit enrichment.Inflation 
accelerated 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-14/argentina-inflation-picked-up-more-than-expected-on-energy-shock?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
 in March after nine months without slowing, and wages aren’t keeping up.

Still, Milei is sticking to his Trumpian script.

He turned a routine Adorni appearance before congress this week into a media 
spectacle 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/milei-stands-by-cabinet-chief-adorni-amid-growing-corruption-scandal?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
, showing up with all his ministers and his sister, cheering and hurling 
insults at left-wing opponents.

Adorni, who once boasted the government fired an official for buying a coffee 
machine, offered few details on an all-cash trip to Aruba with his family, a 
private jet getaway to a posh beach resort in Uruguay, and two properties 
purchased while earning $2,500 a month.

Then there’s the Libra scandal, a meme coin Milei promoted last year that 
quickly crashed and cost investors $250 million. Faced with lurid new details, 
the government’s explanations remain sparse.

The allegations cut deeper as the economy shows signs of strain 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-22/argentina-s-economic-activity-posts-biggest-slump-since-2023?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
: Argentines now rank corruption as their top concern.
Milei during a campaign rally ahead of the Buenos Aires provincial elections 
on Sept. 3.Photographer: Anita Pouchard Serra/Bloomberg
To be sure, sectors like energy and agriculture are booming. Milei reaffirmed 
his support with a midterm landslide in October, and there’s a long ways to go 
before he’s up for reelection late next year.

Donald Trump helped secure Milei’s midterm victory with a $20 billion lifeline 
because he considered him a “beacon” of the right in South America. But Milei 
could soon be just one of manyas conservatives gain ground 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-22/kast-urges-investors-to-bet-on-chile-as-his-reform-push-begins?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
 across the region.

Elections this year in Peru, Colombia and Brazil might lock in that rightward 
turn, just as Milei’s grip begins to slip.— Manuela Tobias 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AWYP0NeWwZo/manuela-tobias>

India is likely to endure a blistering summer until monsoon rains arrive in 
June, with higher-than-average heat levels already straining power grids as the 
world’s most populous nationstruggles to cope 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-01/india-s-heat-exposes-a-fragile-grid-as-energy-crunch-deepens?cmpid=BBD050126_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260501&utm_content=4032>
. Temperatures have surged beyond 40C (104F) — punishing levels, given the 
humidity — and nights offer only mild relief, forcing residents to run cooling 
appliances around the clock, pushing up electricity demand to unprecedented 
levels. Data from air-quality monitoring platform AQI this week showed that 
every one of the 50 hottest cities in the world was in India.
