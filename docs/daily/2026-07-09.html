Thursday, July 09, 2026

US

  • FOMC Minutes hawkish: a "few" members saw a case for rate hikes at the June meeting chaired by Kevin Warsh, with "almost all" participants indicating policy firming would likely be warranted if inflation remained elevated.
  • PepsiCo Q2 revenue growth narrowly missed estimates as lower prices weighed on North American snacks.
  • US equity futures edged higher (S&P 500 futures +0.2%) even as the Iran conflict escalated; the 10-year Treasury yield stood at 4.58%.

Monetary Policy

The minutes of the June FOMC meeting — the first chaired by Kevin Warsh — delivered a genuinely hawkish signal. "Most participants" identified scenarios in which inflation would remain elevated due to AI-driven demand, the Middle East conflict, or tariff effects, and indicated that "some policy firming would likely be warranted." A "few" members went further, seeing a case for an immediate hike. Bond markets responded: yields moved higher alongside energy prices this week, and the trajectory of rate expectations has shifted decisively toward the next move being up rather than down.

Inflation and Energy

The critical transmission mechanism is refined products. The gasoline crack spread — the margin between crude and refined gasoline — sits near $57 per barrel, close to the highest since June 2022, while the diesel crack spread remains materially elevated from pre-war levels. This stickiness in retail fuel costs carries direct implications for CPI and reinforces the Fed's hawkish posture. Separately, the Trump administration advanced a new tranche of "forced labor" tariffs covering 60 trading partners under Section 301, with Senator Ron Wyden calling the proposal a pretext to reconstruct a tariff regime invalidated by the Supreme Court. Total extra tariffs paid by US businesses and consumers since March 2025 have reached $317 billion. ---

Asia

  • China CPI core slipped to 1.0% in June, while PPI fell 0.3% month-on-month — its first monthly decline since July 2025 — suggesting domestic demand remains soft even as export activity stays firm.
  • SK Hynix's US listing is more than seven times oversubscribed ahead of pricing Thursday, with the deal expected to raise approximately $24.5 billion, among the largest-ever US debuts by a foreign company.
  • The Taiwan dollar fell as much as 0.6% to 32.21 per US dollar, erasing gains from last year's historic rally, as dollar strength and record dividend payouts weighed on sentiment.

China: Stalling Reflation

June inflation data present a nuanced picture. While the base effect from weak 2025 kept the year-on-year PPI in positive territory, the month-on-month decline in factory prices signals that China's reflation momentum is fading. Core CPI at 1.0% points to softening domestic demand even as the trade surplus widens. A growing number of analysts estimate Q2 GDP growth may have slipped below the lower bound of the official 4.5%–5.0% target. The People's Bank of China's monetary policy committee flagged growing concern over divergence between external and domestic growth. The next meaningful policy window is the Politburo meeting in late July.

Chipmaker Capital Markets

A wave of semiconductor share issuance reflects both strong investor appetite and insider urgency to monetize gains before the cycle turns. Alongside SK Hynix's landmark US offering, China's CXMT — a memory chipmaker that Apple is reportedly seeking to source from — plans to raise at least 29.5 billion yuan via a domestic IPO. Bain Capital sold its entire Kioxia stake, logging returns exceeding 4,500% from the Japanese chipmaker's debut. Short interest is building: Michael Burry disclosed a short position in US chipmaker Micron, describing it as defining "cyclical like no other." ---

Europe

  • Volkswagen CEO Oliver Blume brought a sweeping restructuring plan — potentially eliminating up to 100,000 jobs and closing four German factories — before the supervisory board Thursday.
  • German exports unexpectedly rose in May for a fourth consecutive month, driven by a sharp rebound in US-bound shipments, providing a degree of macro relief.
  • AstraZeneca fell 8.4% in London after a late-stage heart drug trial with Ionis Pharmaceuticals suffered a rare setback; Levi Strauss dropped 6% after a weak revenue forecast.

Industrial Restructuring

Volkswagen's restructuring proposal represents one of the most consequential corporate decisions in European industry this year. The scale — up to 100,000 positions and four factory closures — reflects structural pressure from the EV transition, softening Chinese demand, and a cost base that has become increasingly uncompetitive. The supervisory board meeting pits management against labor representatives in a company where workers hold half the board seats. The outcome will signal how far European industrial incumbents are prepared to go to defend margins in a deteriorating competitive environment.

Defense and Macro Backdrop

The NATO summit in Ankara produced a functionally successful outcome: Trump departed expressing satisfaction with allies' increased defense contributions, averting the disruption markets had feared. European defense contractors have been among the primary beneficiaries of Trump's pressure, with Rheinmetall's gains remaining substantial despite a pullback exceeding 40% from its June peak. Meanwhile, the UK electricity grid issued a third tight-supply warning this summer as a heat wave stretching across Europe strained power systems, adding near-term upward pressure on energy prices. ---

LatAm

  • The JPMorgan EM FX index resumed its decline as renewed US-Iran hostilities and dollar strength reversed gains accumulated since the June ceasefire; the Indian rupee fell to a month low on the ceasefire collapse.
  • Oxford Economics estimates the war's long-term impact on median Latin American import prices at approximately 0.1%, materially smaller than the 2022 Russia-Ukraine shock.

Colombia Political Transition

De la Espriella's razor-thin electoral mandate complicates governance of one of Latin America's most challenged economies. The incoming administration faces a near-term fiscal crisis, structural electricity shortages, and an intensifying security threat from narco-trafficking groups. His alignment with the Trump administration may deliver bilateral support, but that relationship carries conditionality risks in a country where anti-US sentiment has historically constrained foreign-policy maneuverability.

Regional EM Dynamics

The broader Latin American macro picture remains hostage to the oil-price and dollar dynamic. While the region is a modest net beneficiary of lower oil — Oxford Economics models suggest inflationary passthrough will be limited — currency weakness driven by dollar strength and risk-off sentiment represents a tightening of financial conditions. Mexico's central bank is scheduled to release meeting minutes Thursday, and the region will be watching closely for signals on the rate path given that renewed energy-price pressure complicates the case for further easing. ---

Global

  • Brent crude jumped approximately 6% Wednesday — the largest single-day gain since late April — after Trump declared the US-Iran ceasefire "over"; Brent traded near $78 Thursday, up roughly 11.6% on the week.
  • Strait of Hormuz traffic fell to a near standstill Thursday, with observable crossings among larger vessels at the lowest since before the June interim agreement.
  • FOMC minutes and rising energy prices are jointly shifting global rate expectations toward further tightening, complicating monetary policy across both developed and emerging markets.

Hormuz: Renewed Escalation

US forces struck approximately 90 Iranian military targets over two consecutive days, targeting Tehran's capacity to threaten commercial shipping. Iran retaliated with missiles and drones against US bases in Kuwait and Bahrain. The binary framework traders have applied since March remains operative: the risk of a ground conflict with permanent damage to production infrastructure appears contained — Trump explicitly called such escalation a "waste of time" — but the Strait's near-closure reintroduces meaningful supply uncertainty. Creative workarounds have emerged, including dark shuttle transits developed by Abu Dhabi producers and convoy crossings using Iranian-approved northern routes, but these are partial mitigants rather than structural solutions.

Global Inflation and Monetary Policy Divergence

The energy re-escalation arrives at a moment when inflation risks had appeared to be moderating. Bond market breakevens, while still below pre-war levels, are moving higher again. The combination of sticky refined product crack spreads, Ukrainian drone strikes turning Russia into a net diesel importer, and an intensifying Hormuz crisis creates a genuinely adverse inflation backdrop. A former Bank of Japan official suggested the BoJ may accelerate rate hikes later in 2026 and push its benchmark above 2%. Thailand's Constitutional Court cleared a $12 billion emergency borrowing decree, removing a major obstacle to fiscal stimulus. A new Super El Niño phase has begun, described as potentially among the strongest on record, adding climate risk to an already complex global macro environment.
