Spain is the EUтАЩs second-largest manufacturer of cars and therefore is coming 
to grips with the tensions of a changing industry, and seeking ways to adapt to 
protect its economy.

So far the strategy is paying off: There are already at least two Chinese 
companies either building or assembling cars in Spain, and another three have 
plans to start doing so. Battery-maker Contemporary Amperex Technology is also 
building a тВм4.2 billion plant in partnership with Stellantis.

In Tarragona, two Cosco units will own 51% of the concession while an 
Argentine partner, PTP Group, will own the rest, with the venture committing to 
invest тВм116 million.

Operations are expected to start in 2026, with rebuilding works complete by 
late 2028. The concession includes a rail terminal with links to two major 
logistics hubs elsewhere in Spain and to France.
