Deep Dive: ‘Temu Range Rover’
A Jaecoo 7 displaying a “Sold” sticker at a dealership in Abingdon, England, 
on June 28, 2026.Photographer: Murray Ballard for Bloomberg Businessweek
Land Rovers are as British as the king, crumpets and cricket. And in the 
Cotswolds — the quintessentially English weekend destination about 70 miles 
west of London — they’re both a status symbol and (occasionally) a necessity, 
to navigate the rough roads that wind through the rolling hills. So why are 
those country lanes suddenly teeming with aChinese imitator 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/chery-s-temu-range-rover-highlights-china-s-ascent-in-uk-car-market?cmpid=071626_morningemea&utm_campaign=morningemea&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6615>
 called the Jaecoo 7?

 * A bit more than a year after entering the UK market, the import is 
outselling Land Rover’s comparable Range Rover Evoque on its home turf, earning 
the nickname “Temu Range Rover,” a nod to the online discounter (which doesn’t 
sell them … yet).
 * China’s carmakers have pushed hard into Europe in recent years, accounting 
for 10% of the market in May, the first time they’ve hit that milestone — and 
far ahead of their share in the US, where they’re effectively banned.
 * “Established manufacturers recognize they’re going to have to work with 
some of the emerging brands, because they’re pioneering more efficient ways of 
manufacturing,” says Ben Nelmes, chief executive officer of research group New 
AutoMotive CIC.
