Google, ex-rock star

Alphabet has had, by every measure, a very good 12 months:

 * The stock price has more than doubled over the past year
 * The Google parent is now worth $4.3 trillion, trailing only Nvidia
 * Its shares were just added to the Dow Jones Industrial Average
 * Gemini is a success and the companyтАЩs chips are popular
And yet, suddenly, the stock is struggling. The shares lost 6% in June and 
have been in the red for four of the last five months, including drops of more 
than 7% in February and March.

тАЬThis really shows the fickleness of the AI trade,тАЭ said Alec Young of 
research firm MoneyFlows. тАЬJust a month or two ago Alphabet was the rock star.тАЭ

The stock has been caught in a market rotation away from the biggest AI 
spenders 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-01/alphabet-s-2-trillion-gain-turns-rock-star-into-question-mark?cmpid=070126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260701&utm_content=6073>
 and toward the chipmakers that are receiving much of that cash.

In early June, Alphabet announced plans to raise about $85 billion from equity 
sales to fund its capital expenditures. And it has seen a series of 
high-profile departures, with key AI researchers leaving for rivals.

тАЬThe market has shifted to companies seeing immediate growth from AI, which is 
infrastructure and chips, while companies with huge capex are in the penalty 
box,тАЭ said Young.

The penalty box is crowded. The Magnificent 7 Index is down 1.9% this year, 
falling well short of the S&P 500тАЩs 9.6% gain. Alphabet is actually the best 
performer among that cohort in 2026, while competing AI spenders Microsoft and

Down goes gold

Gold is picking up right where it left off.

The metal is kicking off the third quarter with a decline 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/gold-steadies-as-us-iran-peace-talks-frame-inflation-outlook?cmpid=070126_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260701&utm_content=6073>
, briefly dropping below $3,980 an ounce, on signals the Fed may move to 
tighten monetary policy. It sank 14% in the second quarter, capping the worst 
performance since 2013.

WhatтАЩs happening: After setting a record in January, gold has been hurt by 
speculation that the Fed may hike rates this year to tackle sticky inflation. 
Higher borrowing costs are a headwind for non-yielding metals.

On the technical front, gold is also contending with signs of weakness after 
the 200-day moving average recently dropped below its 50-day counterpart. The 
pattern тАФ called a death cross тАФ can signal a long-term downtrend is forming.

Mike McGlone of Bloomberg Intelligence notes that gold is locked into its 
highest 60-day correlation with the S&P 500 since 1975. A modest drawdown in US 
stocks could, he says, push gold to $3,400 an ounce. тАФYihui Xie
