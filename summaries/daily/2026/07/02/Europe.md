**ECB Policy Divergence**
Three weeks after the ECB unanimously raised rates in response to the Iran conflict's energy price shock, no consensus has emerged on the next move. Lane's camp emphasizes that second-round inflation effects from the energy spike may not yet be visible in the data and that policymakers should not pre-commit to a path. The opposing view holds that retreating oil prices diminish the case for further tightening. The debate may prove consequential at the ECB's July decision meeting. Warsh's appearance at Sintra provided an unexpected moment of alignment: ECB President Lagarde expressed her own regret at having felt "bound by forward guidance," a sentiment Warsh quickly endorsed.

**Germany and Corporate News**
Chancellor Merz's CDU-SPD coalition reached agreement on a reform package encompassing pension system adjustments, labor market measures, and €10 billion in annual income tax relief — a meaningful fiscal stimulus signal for an economy that has struggled with stagnant growth. On the corporate side, KNDS's IPO postponement, citing volatility in the defense sector, removes one of the few anticipated highlights in an otherwise subdued European equity issuance calendar.
