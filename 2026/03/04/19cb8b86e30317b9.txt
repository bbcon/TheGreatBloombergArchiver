Trump’s Lashes Out at Spain, Threatening Trade Embargo
Trump lashed out a Spain, threatening to sever trade ties with the EU country

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17137063&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=03042026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
President Donald Trump said the US is going to “cut off all trade with Spain.”

On Tuesday he lashed out 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/trump-threatens-to-cut-off-trade-after-spain-denies-air-base-use?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
 at the 23rd-largest source of American imports after the country denied access 
to its military bases for his bombing campaign against Iran. In response, 
Spain’s government issued a sharp rebuke saying he must respect international 
trade agreements.

Trump didn’t explain how he planned to follow through, which could prove 
particularly difficult since the US has a trading relationship with thebroader 
European Union 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/europe-tests-trump-s-short-fuse-with-delays-on-us-trade-deal?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
. Later, he suggested he had the power to impose a full embargo on goods from 
the country.

Video: Trump Threatens Trade With Spain Over Air Base Access 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-03-03/trump-threatens-trade-with-spain-over-air-base-access-video?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>

The threat sent Spanish equities to the lowest in almost three months, the 
worst-performing country index among major European markets today.

Deborah Elms, head of trade policy at the Hinrich Foundation in Singapore, 
said that even though the Supreme Courtstruck down 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/trump-administration-loses-push-to-delay-tariff-refund-fight?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
 Trump’s use of IEEPA authority to impose tariffs, “other trade restrictions 
are possible.”

“A court defeat is not going to stop Trump from using trade and trade threats 
as a weapon for a dizzying array of issues,” she wrote in aLinkedIn post 
<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/deborah-elms_trump-threatens-to-halt-trade-with-spain-activity-7434769671842689024-tsOd?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAAAQhrDgB0bYwZGt1gaWdmMK9EIR1HhRFMEU>
.

Meanwhile, Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez said he won’t yield to threats 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/spanish-leader-says-he-won-t-back-down-to-trump-s-trade-threats?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
 over his objections to the US and Israeli war in Iran.

“We are not going to be complicit in something that is harmful to the world 
and contrary to our values and interests simply out of fear of someone’s 
retaliation,” Sánchez said in a speech Wednesday.

In an interview 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-03-03/santander-s-botin-on-us-spain-ties-m-a-and-hiring-video?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
 with Bloomberg’s Dani Burger, Banco Santander Executive Chair Ana Botin played 
down the latest transatlantic rift.

“Spain and the United States have had an amazing relationship forever, 
centuries, and I’m sure that’s going to happen again very soon,”Botin said 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/us-spain-to-resume-amazing-ties-soon-santander-s-botin-says?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
. “Santander, on the other side, is a big investor in the United States and we 
see ourselves as a bridge between the United States and Latin America and 
Europe and the United States.”

 with the US to be implemented quickly but the bloc can’t accept a deal with 
worse conditions.

Speaking at the same meeting, US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer said the 
administration plans to completeseveral investigations 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/us-trade-probes-will-conclude-within-five-months-greer-says?cmpid=BBD030426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260304>
 that will allow Trump to impose new tariffs within five months to replace the 
levies deemed illegal by the high court.
