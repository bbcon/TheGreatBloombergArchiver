become a “skunk at the party <https://bloom.bg/4cSOxyk>” for the US economy.

 Deep Dive: Wagering on War 


 <https://bloom.bg/4cSOz9q> The Polysights analytics platform website for 
Polymarket traders.Photographer: Gabby Jones/Bloomberg 
Conflict-related betting on prediction markets hit a record last week as 
traders piled intowagers on the US-Israeli strikes <https://bloom.bg/4cSOz9q> 
on Iran, while blockchain analysts flagged suspicious activity and lawmakers 
called for a crackdown.

 * Bettors placed $425.4 million in wagers on geopolitics questions on 
Polymarket in the week ending March 1, up from $163.9 million the week before. 
 * Six accounts on Polymarket made around $1 million in profit by betting on 
the US to strike Iran by Feb. 28. The accounts were all freshly created in 
February <https://bloom.bg/4037Tt5> and had only ever placed bets on when US 
strikes might occur. 
 * US regulations are broadly understood to prohibit financial contracts tied 
to war, but Polymarket’s main exchange operates offshore and outside the 
oversight of US regulators.  <https://bloom.bg/4cSOz9q>

 Opinion 



By diverting Tomahawk missiles and critical intelligence assets to a war with 
Iran, Trump risks draining US military stockpiles and driving up global energy 
prices in ways thatdirectly benefit Moscow <https://bloom.bg/3NbppZe>, Marc 
Champion writes. The longer the conflict continues, the more it eases pressure 
on Russia—weakening Ukraine’s position while strengthening the Kremlin’s war 
effort.
