**Colombia Political Transition**
De la Espriella's razor-thin electoral mandate complicates governance of one of Latin America's most challenged economies. The incoming administration faces a near-term fiscal crisis, structural electricity shortages, and an intensifying security threat from narco-trafficking groups. His alignment with the Trump administration may deliver bilateral support, but that relationship carries conditionality risks in a country where anti-US sentiment has historically constrained foreign-policy maneuverability.

**Regional EM Dynamics**
The broader Latin American macro picture remains hostage to the oil-price and dollar dynamic. While the region is a modest net beneficiary of lower oil — Oxford Economics models suggest inflationary passthrough will be limited — currency weakness driven by dollar strength and risk-off sentiment represents a tightening of financial conditions. Mexico's central bank is scheduled to release meeting minutes Thursday, and the region will be watching closely for signals on the rate path given that renewed energy-price pressure complicates the case for further easing.
