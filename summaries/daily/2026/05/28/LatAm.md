**Cuba Energy Crisis**
The diversion of the Russian diesel tanker is a material setback for Cuba, which is navigating one of its most severe fuel shortages in years. The cargo was intended to provide meaningful relief to an energy system under sustained stress, and its failure to arrive tightens an already constrained supply picture. The episode illustrates the degree to which smaller, sanctions-adjacent economies remain exposed to the secondary effects of geopolitical disruption in global energy markets — including shipping route uncertainty amplified by the US-Iran conflict. The broader regional implication is that countries with limited access to spot energy markets face disproportionate vulnerability when supply chains are disrupted.
