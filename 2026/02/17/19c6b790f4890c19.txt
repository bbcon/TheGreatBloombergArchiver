Obesity Drug Boom Buoys Ireland’s Pharma Production for Export
Pharma giant Novo Nordisk is investing more in Ireland hoping to capitalize on 
the obesity-drug boom

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=16929886&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=02172026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
A nondescript industrial estate in Ireland’s Midlands region is about to 
become a flashpoint in the race to dominate the weight-loss drug market.

Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk is planning to boost its investment 
in Athlone, an ancient town on the banks of the River Shannon, where it will 
make its hit Wegovy weight-loss pill for markets outside the US, Bloomberg News
reported last week 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-12/novo-set-to-make-wegovy-pill-in-ireland-for-markets-outside-us?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
.

US rival Eli Lilly has invested in two plants in Limerick and Cork where it 
makes the active ingredients for its own obesity drugs. Novo is hoping its pill 
will form a major part of its fight to reclaim market share it once dominated 
and make up lost ground to Lilly.

Read More: Trump’s Pharma Threats Take Tariffs Into Uncharted Territory 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-04-01/trump-tariffs-pharmaceutical-industry-levies-threaten-major-disruption?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>

“If we were about to throw in the towel, we would not be investing in 
factories in Ireland,” Chief Executive Officer Mike Doustdar said, without 
giving detail on the scale of the new Athlone plan.

The battle, fueled by global demand to trim waistlines 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-01-14/novo-nordisk-braces-as-generic-weight-loss-drugs-come-to-upend-market?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
, is set to underpin Irish exports for years to come. In the first half of 
2025, a specific hormone derivative accounted for 56%, by value, of all Irish 
pharmaceutical export to the US, likely originating from Lilly’s new production 
line, AIB Group said.

It’s unlikely to go unnoticed by President Donald Trump, who has singled out 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-08/ireland-s-whiskey-boom-hit-by-trump-s-tariffs-deal?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
 Ireland several times for its drugs-driven $112 billion goods imbalance with 
the US from January through November last year. Several pharmaceutical giants 
committed to expand in the US to avoid tariff pain.

What’s interesting is that Irish exports of polypeptide hormones began to 
increase from mid-2024, before pharma firmsfront-loaded 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-05-06/us-ireland-trade-deficit-at-record-on-pre-tariff-pharma-imports?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
 to the US to avoid potential “Liberation Day” tariffs last year, according to 
the Central Bank of Ireland.

Read More: Irish Economy to Slow on Falling Exports, Bank of Ireland Says 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-27/irish-economy-to-slow-on-falling-exports-bank-of-ireland-says?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>

This, combined with the recent investments by Lilly and Novo, suggests 
underlying demand for pharmaceutical manufacturing in Ireland persists, despite 
Trump’svows to re-shore 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-23/pharma-tariffs-could-cost-ireland-75-000-jobs-donohoe-warns?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
.

While exports to the US have decreased slightly 
<https://www.cso.ie/en/releasesandpublications/ep/p-gei/goodsexportsandimportsdecember2025/>
 since front-loading ended, they are set to buoy the Irish economy, as Bank of 
Ireland Group sees new Irish production facilities leading to a permanent 
upward shift in GDP. This may put to bed Irish policymakers’ fears about 
drug-makersabandoning the country 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-19/nine-drugmakers-strike-pricing-deals-with-trump-to-avoid-tariffs?cmpid=BBD021726_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260217>
, at least for now.
