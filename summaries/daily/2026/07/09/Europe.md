**Industrial Restructuring**
Volkswagen's restructuring proposal represents one of the most consequential corporate decisions in European industry this year. The scale — up to 100,000 positions and four factory closures — reflects structural pressure from the EV transition, softening Chinese demand, and a cost base that has become increasingly uncompetitive. The supervisory board meeting pits management against labor representatives in a company where workers hold half the board seats. The outcome will signal how far European industrial incumbents are prepared to go to defend margins in a deteriorating competitive environment.

**Defense and Macro Backdrop**
The NATO summit in Ankara produced a functionally successful outcome: Trump departed expressing satisfaction with allies' increased defense contributions, averting the disruption markets had feared. European defense contractors have been among the primary beneficiaries of Trump's pressure, with Rheinmetall's gains remaining substantial despite a pullback exceeding 40% from its June peak. Meanwhile, the UK electricity grid issued a third tight-supply warning this summer as a heat wave stretching across Europe strained power systems, adding near-term upward pressure on energy prices.
