AI giveth, and AI taketh away from some. Recent days have seen shares of 
wealth-management stocks <https://bloom.bg/4rDPU8c>, software makers and 
insurance brokerages hit by fears that artificial intelligence will eat their 
business. BecauseWall Street’s new trade <https://bloom.bg/4qvtk0u> is dumping 
any stock in AI’s crosshairs.

The latest selloff erupted on Tuesday when a tax-strategy tool rolled out by a 
little-known startup, Altruist, sent shares of Charles Schwab, Raymond James 
Financial and LPL Financial Holdings down by 7% or more. It was the most recent 
example of a sell-first, ask-questions-later mentality that has rapidly taken 
hold as new products emerge from the hundreds of billions of dollars poured 
into AI, sowing anxiety about how the technology may upend entire industries.

And the rush of spending into AI shows little sign of slowing — just look at 
Alphabet’sultra rare 100-year bond <https://bloom.bg/401WdXh>, a landmark 
transaction in the debt-fueled race for AI supremacy. Here in Asia, Jack 
Ma-backed Ant Group is betting itsnext phase of growth 
<https://bloom.bg/4rJh7q6> on health care powered by, you guessed it, AI. What 
began as a digital payments platform has become one of China’sbiggest investors 
in medical AI <https://bloom.bg/4rMZB4s>, backing software that fields patient 
questions and connects them with doctors, pharmacies and insurers. Which 
businesses will that threaten, or boost?—Stephanie Phang 
<https://bloom.bg/4gs6Wkp>



 What You Need to Know Today 



In the Asian market day, equities climbed to a record and the dollar declined 
<https://bloom.bg/4qqe9ph> ahead of Wednesday’s US jobs report after weak 
retail salesreinforced bets <https://bloom.bg/4qtqpp2> that the Federal Reserve 
will cut interest rates later this year. The MSCI Asia Pacific Index rose 1% to 
an all-time high, widening its year-to-dateoutperformance 
<https://bloom.bg/4rbcjdh> versus European and US equities. A gauge of emerging 
markets <https://bloom.bg/3OjviDM> also climbed to a record.
One kilogram silver bars and a US dollar banknote at a Goldenmark bullion 
dealer.Photographer: Damian Lemanski/Bloomberg 



Bitcoin has just drawn fresh support from some of its largest holders, though 
the return of demand remains narrow enough to raise doubts about whether it 
marks a recovery ormere damage control <https://bloom.bg/4r5aYEN>. So-called 
whale wallets accumulated about 53,000 coins in the past week, their biggest 
buying spree since November, after weeks of heavy selling. Those kinds of 
purchases helped steady prices after a steepdrawdown <https://bloom.bg/46ta8cE>
, even as most other investors stayed on the sidelines.




The White House revised its fact sheet on the US-India trade agreement to 
adjust language around agricultural goods,adding to confusion 
<https://bloom.bg/4qsAJh6> about the deal already raised by farmer groups. In a 
revised statement on the trade deal, the US removed a reference to pulses — a 
staple food in India that includes lentils and chickpeas — and adjusted 
language around India’s offer to buy more American goods.
Bloomberg 



Goldman’s India push is paying off. The Wall Street giant vaulted to fourth in 
Indian equity offerings last year — after never ranking in the top five over 
the past decade — and finished fifth among banks in mergers, according to data 
compiled by Bloomberg. The firm overtook arch-rival Morgan Stanley in stock 
sales for the first time in a decade. Readhow the company shifted gears 
<https://bloom.bg/4rL2zX3> after playing on the fringes of Indian dealmaking 
for years.




The slump in Treasuries after China’s latest call to curb its holdings was 
fleeting, but it put a spotlight on Beijing’sdecade-long shift 
<https://bloom.bg/3ZYZSFo> from US debt and rekindled fears about a broader, 
global retreat. Data on China’s Treasury holdings suggests why traders were so 
quick to move on from the report that Beijing hadurged 
<https://bloom.bg/4rMK7gP> Chinese banks to limit their Treasury purchases. 
Once the largest foreign lender to the US government, China has quietly halved 
its holdings of Treasuries since 2013 — and investors appear to have decided 
the latest headlines fit that trend.




China’s factory deflation eased more than expected in January thanks mostly to 
aglobal metals rally <https://bloom.bg/4qsAKSc>, even as weak demand at home 
restrains a more sustainable turnaround in prices. “There’s no decisive 
reflation yet,” Robin Xing, economist at Morgan Stanley, said in aninterview 
with Bloomberg Television <https://bloom.bg/4qsALpe>. 




The world’s biggest nickel mine is set to have its permitted output slashed by 
the Indonesian government, as authorities step up efforts to reduce supply in a 
bid toboost global prices <https://bloom.bg/4qqe4Sv>. Weda Bay Nickel has been 
told it will receive 12 million tons of ore production quota this year, down 
from 42 million tons in 2025, according to people familiar with the matter. The 
mine on the island of Halmahera, North Maluku province, is owned by Tsingshan, 
France’s Eramet and Aneka Tambang.

 For Your Commute 



One of China’s premium Shanghainese dining chains abruptly shut 10 outlets in 
the city, becoming the latest casualty of the country’s consumption slump and 
sparking an outcry from diners <https://bloom.bg/3MCxsxV> just days before the 
Lunar New Year holidays. Shanghai XNG, the operator of Xiaonanguo restaurants 
best known for dishes like red-braised pork and smoked fish, suspended 
operations across all its outlets in China’s largest city. Its shares plunged.
