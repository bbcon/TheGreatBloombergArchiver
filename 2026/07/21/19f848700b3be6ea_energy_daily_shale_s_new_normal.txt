Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporters David Wethe and Kevin Crowley look at how the US shale patch is 
responding to higher oil prices.

Gone are the go-go days of the US shale patch, when producers came out guns 
blazing,eager to boost output 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2013-06-24/frack-music-attracts-halliburton-to-submarine-spy-tool?cmpid=BBBXT072126_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260721&utm_content=6786>
 through fracking.

Drillers are now so efficient they can expand production marginally without

Producers are simply letting spending budgets drift to the higher end of

oil output will expand by roughly 250,000 barrels in 2026.

If that holds, it would be the smallest expansion since the US oil industry 
rebounded from the pandemic.

тАЬWhat weтАЩre really going to see is just production creep,тАЭ said Reed Olmstead 
<https://www.spglobal.com/en/research-insights/experts/olmstead-reed>,

everybody trending towards the high end of their guidance, and thatтАЩll create

The Trump administration has repeatedly called on US companies to increase

In fact, the shale patch entered this year with analyst expectations for 
output to actually drop for the first time since the pandemic.

But now that oil prices have remained sufficiently above the range 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-06/us-shale-drillers-to-increase-oil-production-as-iran-war-lifts-crude-prices?cmpid=BBBXT072126_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260721&utm_content=6786>

Diamondback Energy Inc. is a key example. The biggest pure-play producer in

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-05/diamondback-fang-sees-10-more-permian-oil-rigs-by-year-end-as-war-lifts-price?cmpid=BBBXT072126_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260721&utm_content=6786>

Other companiesтАЩ spending budgets will undoubtedly be watched closely in the
