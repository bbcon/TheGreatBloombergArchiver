**G7 Diplomatic Friction**
European governments expressed skepticism at the Evian summit that the Strait of Hormuz can operationally reopen by Friday, as Trump has asserted. G7 officials cited unresolved questions around de-mining missions, patrol commitments, and the absence of a publicly released MoU text. The divergence is more than procedural: European diplomatic sources indicated serious difficulty forging a common G7 position on Iran, complicating post-deal burden-sharing on security guarantees. French President Macron separately refused to withdraw France's digital services tax despite Trump's threat of retaliatory wine tariffs, setting up a bilateral confrontation at the summit.

**UK and Sanctions**
The UK announced fresh sanctions on Russia and extended energy support to Ukraine, coordinating with European allies to pressure Moscow toward peace talks. Separately, a British jury convicted two men of conducting arson attacks against Prime Minister Starmer's properties, with investigators linking the operation to a Russian-speaking Telegram account. Rathbones shares fell 16% in London after the wealth manager disclosed £60 million in additional remediation costs following a regulatory customer-treatment review.
