**Oil Markets and the Iran Interim Deal**
The decline in Brent to sub-$75 represents a significant reversal from the late-April peak near $125, and the signal from shipping markets — more tankers transiting with AIS transponders active — suggests confidence is returning incrementally. However, the thorniest structural issues remain unresolved. Iran and the US contradict each other on nuclear inspection scope, uranium enrichment limits, ballistic missile programs, and the administration of Hormuz itself. Lebanon and Gaza remain active flashpoints that Iran has flagged as potential deal-breakers. The market appears to be pricing a degree of normalization that the diplomatic reality does not yet fully support, creating asymmetric downside risk if talks deteriorate.

**AI Capital Cycle: Infrastructure Phase**
The AI investment cycle is visibly shifting from software and model development toward physical infrastructure — power, data centers, and memory. ByteDance is weighing a $20 billion offshore loan, with up to $70 billion in AI-related capital expenditure planned for the year. The SpaceX bond deal illustrates how AI's financial architecture is becoming increasingly complex, with profitable infrastructure businesses cross-subsidizing cash-consuming AI ventures. The simultaneous crowding of financial capital into semiconductor and data-center plays, against a backdrop of leveraged positioning and stretched valuations, may signal that the risk-reward calculus across the AI trade is deteriorating even as the fundamental demand story remains intact.
