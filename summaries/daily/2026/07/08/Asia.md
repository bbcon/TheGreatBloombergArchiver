• New Zealand's Reserve Bank raised its Official Cash Rate 25 basis points to 2.5%—the first hike in three years—and signaled further tightening may be required.

**Equity Rotation**
A sharp intra-regional divergence is the dominant market story in Asia. The Kospi's 20% drawdown from June highs reflects investor rotation away from Korean and Taiwanese chipmakers following Samsung's 19-fold surge in quarterly profit—a print that paradoxically triggered a sell-the-news response, compressing Samsung's P/E to its largest discount to MSCI World on record. Capital has migrated toward Chinese internet names: Alibaba surged 13% after a pre-earnings analyst briefing, and the MSCI China Index, which had touched its lowest level relative to global equities since the aftermath of September 11, 2001, has gained 7% in July. Reports that DeepSeek and Zhipu are developing proprietary AI chips provided additional catalyst for Chinese tech.

**New Zealand Policy and Regional Risks**
The Reserve Bank of New Zealand's 25-basis-point hike to 2.5%—its first tightening in three years—reflected above-target inflation and expectations of strengthening economic activity. The NZD and government bond yields both advanced on the decision. Separately, the Philippines' impeachment trial of Vice President Sara Duterte continues to weigh on investor sentiment, with the case threatening to distract policymakers from addressing one of Southeast Asia's slowest-growing economies at a moment when the US-Iran oil shock has already dented consumer confidence.
