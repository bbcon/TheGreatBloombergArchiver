Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporters Julian Hast and Nathan Risser take a look at the past, present and 
future of AmericaтАЩs shifting energy mix.

The transformation of the US and its economy over the last 250 years can be 
tracked through its energy use: An early dependency on burning wood gradually 
gave way to coal, and then to oil.

That latest phase appears to be drawing to a close 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-02/america-runs-on-natural-gas-fuel-set-to-surpass-oil-as-us-top-energy-source?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
.

Natural gas has been narrowing the gap for years. It comprised 36% of US 
energy consumption in 2025, compared with37% for petroleum 
<https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=67824&utm_medium=email>, 
according to data published this week by the Energy Information Administration. 
Gas is likely to take the lead by the end of the decade, based on the current 
trajectory.

The shift highlights several important trends in the US.

First, the advent of fracking and horizontal drilling in the 2000s unlocked 
huge volumes of previously uneconomic gas reserves. The country is now the 
worldтАЩsbiggest producer 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-03-09/how-natural-gas-became-america-s-most-important-export-under-trump?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 and supply continues to soar to new heights.

That burgeoning output pushed gas past coal 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-11-19/a-massive-coal-plant-that-asked-for-trump-s-help-has-gone-dark?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 as the nationтАЩs largest power-plant fuel. From 2011 to 2020, more than 100 
coal-fed plants were replaced by тАФ or converted to тАФ gas-fired generators, 
according to the EIA.

Third, US power consumption is growing for the first time in decades as the 
economy electrifies, led by electric vehicle use, a massivedata-center buildout 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-01-16/trump-s-interior-pick-calls-energy-a-tool-for-peace-prosperity?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 and manufacturing. Gas, which is readily available, relatively cheap and has
strong support 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-17/trump-is-pushing-gas-to-power-ai-boom-but-building-plants-take-years?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 from the Trump administration, is increasingly meeting that need.

EVs have also helped erode demand for gasoline 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-20/driving-an-electric-car-saves-you-the-most-money-in-these-9-us-states?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
, which is unlikely to return to highs reached prior to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Still, if the US is entering an age of electrification, thereтАЩs no assurance 
over how long gas will lead the way.

While renewables accounted for less than 9% of US energy consumption by source 
last year, according to the EIA, theyтАЩre growing fast.Wind and solar 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-16/solar-surpasses-gas-in-california-after-panel-installs-surge?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 power tripled from 2015 to 2025, outpacing gas. A revival of interest in 
nuclear energy isshowing momentum 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-23/us-to-award-17-5-billion-in-loans-for-large-nuclear-reactors?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
, too.

New gas-export capacity 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2025-12-15/us-lng-export-boom-means-goodbye-to-cheap-domestic-natural-gas?cmpid=BBBXT070326_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260703&utm_content=6168>
 under construction will also allow the country to ship more of the commodity 
overseas. Greater exposure to global gas markets is likely to raise domestic 
prices over time and undermine the fuelтАЩs competitive advantage.

AmericaтАЩs energy mix, in flux for a quarter of a millennium, will shift and 
adapt again.
