• China's May retail sales fell 0.6% YoY, the first contraction outside the pandemic since 1990, missing the trajectory implied by a successfully developing economy and deepening concerns about structural consumer stagnation.

**China: Consumer Stagnation**
The May retail sales contraction — 0.6% YoY — is difficult to attribute solely to the expiration of government trade-in subsidy programs; underlying growth averaged 8% annually in 2019 and has run at roughly 4% over the past three years, barely matching the vastly wealthier United States. Macquarie analysts highlight structural parallels with 1990s Japan: a burst asset bubble, subdued consumption, and an aging population, with home prices down approximately 30% from their 2021 peak. The comparison has limits — China's manufacturing sector continues gaining global market share, supported by deliberate currency weakness — but the combination of industrial strength alongside consumer stagnation creates an internally contradictory growth picture with significant implications for commodity-exporting economies.

**Korean Semiconductors: Leverage and Volatility**
Tuesday's 10% Kospi decline was amplified materially by leveraged single-stock ETFs tracking Samsung and SK Hynix — instruments Korean regulators approved and publicly expressed regret over during the week. An SK Hynix ETF listed in Hong Kong had swelled to $17 billion in assets, translating a 13% single-name decline into roughly a 26% loss for double-leveraged holders. SK Hynix's concurrent announcement of a $29 billion US depositary receipt offering — which would rank among the five largest share sales on record — was absorbed constructively following Micron's demand signal. Alibaba fell to a 16-month low in Hong Kong after Anthropic accused it of orchestrating the largest known illicit access attempt on a US AI model, with Baidu and Xiaomi falling more than 3% in sympathy.
