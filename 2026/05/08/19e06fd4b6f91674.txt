It’s been some week. Investors sent MSCI’s Asian stock gauge up 5.6%, the 
second-biggest gain since September 2024, as moves toward ending the conflict 
in the Middle Easteased concern 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-07/us-stock-futures-fall-oil-climbs-on-iran-tensions-markets-wrap?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260508&utm_content=4278>
 over energy prices and allowed attention to shift back to betting big on AI.

Standout markets were Korea’s Kospi 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-07/world-s-best-performing-market-to-get-lift-from-us-retail-money?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260508&utm_content=4278>
, which jumped 14%, the most globally. Taiwan’s benchmark surged about 7%, 
while Japan’s Nikkei 225 came in third with a 5.8% gain. Among notable stock 
gainers were SK Hynix, up 30%, and Samsung, up 18%.

Underscoring the positive momentum 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-06/up-75-already-in-2026-korea-s-stock-market-is-hotter-than-ever?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260508&utm_content=4278>
, Goldman Sachs boosted its target for the Kospi for the fourth time this year, 
projecting gains of a further 20%.

“Korea is our highest conviction view,” strategist Timothy Moe wrote in a 
note. “Our forecast of earnings growth this year at 300% is the strongest for 
any market in Asia ever, with the exception of the 1999 recovery from the Asian 
Financial Crisis.”

Asian currencies have also done well as traders left the haven of the dollar 
and investors poured money into the region’s equities. The Philippine peso, 
Malaysian ringgit and Thai baht all surged more than 1% this week.

There are risks, of course. Equities slipped on Friday after the US and Iran 
clashed 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-08/us-iran-clash-near-hormuz-as-response-on-proposed-deal-awaited?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260508&utm_content=4278>
 near the Strait of Hormuz. American forces targeted missile and drone launch 
sites and other military assets in Iran that they said were responsible for 
attacking three US warships transiting the strait. And gains are looking frothy.

But with Donald Trump saying a month-long ceasefire remains in effect, the 
outlook remains positive. That means Asia’s outsized gains stand a good chance 
of continuing.

—Richard Frost

trillion, as well as ¥3.8 trillion posted in the prior period.

Singapore will add new curbs to discourage the flipping of some homes 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-08/singapore-cracks-down-on-home-flipping-after-sharp-price-jump?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260508&utm_content=4278>
 as the government seeks to ease speculation and boost affordability in the

Authorities will introduce new measures targeting so-called Executive 
Condominiums, Minister for National Development Chee Hong Tat said. The period 
for which owners must reside in their apartments will be doubled to 10 years as 
part of efforts to help first-time home buyers secure a unit, Chee said.
