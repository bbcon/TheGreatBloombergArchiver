European markets traded higher Friday, with the Stoxx 600 gaining 0.6%, following Asian equities upward as the Iran ceasefire appeared to hold ahead of weekend talks in Islamabad. The macro backdrop, however, carries growing tightening implications. Bloomberg Economics now sees the European Central Bank shifting to rate hikes rather than cuts, reflecting the persistence of energy-driven inflationary pressure from the Middle East conflict. UK gilt markets remain under particular strain: the Bank of England, previously expected to cut, is now seen holding, with one fixed-income specialist noting that "for about a year gilts have been looking standout cheap" but that the war has upended the prior consensus.

Hungarian politics commanded significant attention as Prime Minister Viktor Orbán faces his most serious electoral test in Sunday's vote. The outcome may hinge on older voters who consume primarily state-controlled media — outlets Orbán has shaped into government mouthpieces over 16 years in power — and who remain largely unaware of corruption allegations or reports of Russian influence. Should Orbán fall, it would mark the end of a political era in Central Europe, though the result remains genuinely uncertain.

In Italy, Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni ousted the CEO of state-backed aerospace and defense contractor Leonardo while retaining the heads of Eni and Enel, a recalibration of executive leadership at state firms that follows a referendum defeat last month. The opposition is energized: Silvia Salis, a 40-year-old former Olympic hammer thrower and progressive mayor of Genoa, is emerging as a potential challenger, with some commentators already labeling her the "anti-Meloni." In corporate news, Sodexo plunged 20% in Paris after issuing weaker-than-expected revenue and margin guidance, and UBS confirmed it will not hand over privileged documents to the Credit Suisse Nazi-linked accounts investigation, citing legal exposure risks following its court loss earlier in the week.
