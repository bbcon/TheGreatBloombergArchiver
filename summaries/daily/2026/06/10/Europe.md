**Germany's Diminished Standing**
The UN Security Council rebuke is a meaningful diplomatic signal. Germany's continued deepening of defense ties with Israel, including after Netanyahu's ICC indictment, appears to have carried a measurable reputational cost in the Global South. Domestically, Chancellor Merz faces record disapproval ratings and rising support for far-right parties, leaving the government with limited political capital.

**UK Defense and Domestic Politics**
The UK's 10-year defense investment plan is imminent, but internal Treasury resistance to committing to a firm timeline for reaching 3% of GDP illustrates the fiscal constraints facing the Starmer government. Separately, Belfast's unrest — triggered by a knife attack attributed to a Sudanese national — threatens to become a broader political liability, testing the government's capacity to manage simultaneous security, fiscal, and geopolitical pressures.
