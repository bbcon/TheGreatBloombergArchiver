**Hormuz and Oil Markets**
The Strait of Hormuz has not been fully closed, but shipping flows have declined markedly from levels seen before US-Iran hostilities intensified. Dark transits — tankers moving without tracking signals — have become the primary mechanism for continuing Iranian oil exports, and these are increasingly being targeted. Crude timespreads are in heavy backwardation and refined product premiums are at historic highs, signaling acute near-term supply anxiety. Insurance premiums for Hormuz transits have surged and some crews are refusing passage. The market's central question is how long the US will sustain military pressure if oil prices continue to rise — a political constraint Trump's administration has not yet resolved publicly. An unusual options strategy involving $1-wide put spreads on roughly 400 million barrels has emerged as the preferred hedge against abrupt US policy reversals.

**AI Governance Fracture**
Xi's World AI Conference appearance and the formation of the AI Cooperation Organization with 29 member nations crystallizes a bifurcating global AI order, with China promoting open-source, low-cost models as an alternative to US proprietary platforms. The simultaneous selloff in AI-linked equities globally — triggered partly by Moonshot's competitive model release — illustrates that the commercial and geopolitical dimensions of AI competition are now directly interacting with capital markets. The "DeepSeek moment" parallel cited by multiple market participants suggests investors may be re-rating AI capex return assumptions more fundamentally than a single-session selloff implies.
