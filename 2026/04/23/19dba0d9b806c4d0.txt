Transshipping Rises

Another potential source of tension in USMCA talks is the issue of illegal 
transshipment — especially through Mexico — as a way to avoid Trump’s tariffs.

About $300 billion worth of goods subject to Trump administration tariffs are 
avoiding the levies annually and reaching the US from Southeast Asia and 
Mexico, exposing enforcement vulnerabilities, according to new estimates from 
AI-driven supply chain platformAltana.

US imports from China plummeted last year as Trump ramped up tariffs.

But shipment-level records analyzed by Altana show how businesses often route 
goods through Asian countries with lower tariff rates, and onto Mexico, where 
treatment under the US-Mexico-Canada Agreement offers another opportunity for 
savings. (Click here 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-23/rerouted-us-imports-avoiding-trump-s-tariffs-top-300-billion?cmpid=BBD042326_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260423>
 to read the full story.)
Altana’s says its transshipment-detection capabilities show trade is being 
rerouted to avoid tariffs through established supplier networks — not new 
supply-chain infrastructure.Source: Altana
New York-based Altana, whose technology is used by the US Customs and Border 
Protection agency for trade enforcement, estimates that suspect transactions in 
the first 10 months of 2025 surged 76% compared with the same period in 2024, 
from just over 100 million transactions to 188.5 million.

Shipments of some high-tariffed goods imported directly from China into the US 
last year dropped by 35%, while Mexican imports of the same categories into the 
US climbed. Electronics and semiconductors, automotive, pharmaceuticals and 
metals categories are particularly exposed, the data show.
