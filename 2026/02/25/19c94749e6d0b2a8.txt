Mexico Cartel Takedown Is a Risk for President Sheinbaum
The killing of a cartel boss shows President Sheinbaum has opted to confront 
the drug gangs head-on.

As news broke of the killing of notorious drug lord “El Mencho,” Mexican and 
US authorities rushed to thank each other for their support in pulling offthe 
biggest security coup 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-22/leader-of-mexico-s-jalisco-cartel-reported-slain-in-federal-raid?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 of President Claudia Sheinbaum’s term.

In addition to a major departure 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/mexico-s-big-jalisco-cartel-win-also-risks-more-violence-ahead?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 from the strategy of her predecessor and mentor, Andrés Manuel López Obrador, 
the operation showcased a level of information-sharing not seen since at least 
2008. That’s when then-President Felipe Calderón signed the so-called Merida 
Initiative on US security cooperation to fight drug trafficking.

Sheinbaum and her supporters regularly revile Calderón, yet in reality she’s 
now opted toconfront organized crime head-on 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-08-18/battle-tested-cop-is-mexico-s-hope-to-tame-cartels-and-placate-trump?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
, pressured by Washington to obtain quick results — and a domestic public that 
sees security as the top concern.

Mexico has understood its relationship with President Donald Trump as “trophy 
diplomacy,” extraditing nearly 130 cartel membersto US prisons 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-20/mexico-sends-third-group-of-inmates-with-drug-cartel-ties-to-us?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 since January 2025. Ryan Wedding, a Canadian former Olympic snowboarder on the 
FBI’s most-wanted list, was detained in Mexico last month after several years 
at large.

US military personnel have been granted entry to conduct training and 
exercises on several occasions this past year.
Sheinbaum at her daily news conference in Mexico City last month.Photographer: 
Alfredo Estrella/AFP/Getty Images
Sheinbaum’s government says it’s a strategy that’s been in place since she 
took office almost 18 months back, including addressing the root causes ofdrug 
crime 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-14/mexico-state-invests-millions-in-tequila-after-extortion-scandal?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
, strengthening the national guard and bolstering intelligence.

If so, Sunday’s dramatic shoot-out 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-23/mexico-details-how-it-took-out-its-most-wanted-drug-cartel-chief?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 is the most visible evidence to date.

It’s also controversial.

Several members of her party reject the idea of open combat 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-12/mexico-s-claudia-sheinbaum-touts-very-good-talk-with-trump?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 with the cartels. As recently as November, Sheinbaum branded the war on drugs 
“outside the law,” and amounting to a license to kill.

The risk is she ends up satisfying neither her electorate nor the 
tariff-wielding US president.

Mere hours after the weekend operation, Trump called on the Mexican government 
to do more to combatorganized crime 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-03/mexico-banks-struggle-as-us-sanctions-spark-cartel-money-crackdown?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
. — Gonzalo Soto

what Customs said actually arrived last year suggests that as much as a quarter 
of shipments to American shores may haveslipped under the tariff radar 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-02-24/china-s-112-billion-cargo-gap-shows-record-us-tariff-evasion?cmpid=BBD022526_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>

disproportionately as they go after them for infractions instead of shell 
companies that evaporate overnight.

And Finally

Around 1,000 military personnel from Spain, Norway, Poland, the Netherlands, 
the UK and Germany joined a collection of frigates, helicopters and patrol 
aircraft this month to scour the Norwegian Sea in pursuit of two submarines 
posing as adversaries. The annual exercise takes place at the gateway to the 
Arctic — a region of growing strategic importance even before Trump renewed his 
claim for US control of Greenland while questioning NATO’s relevance. So this 
year’s Alliance drill had an additional audience: the US president. Read what
