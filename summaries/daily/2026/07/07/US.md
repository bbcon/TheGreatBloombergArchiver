**AI and Semiconductor Sentiment**
The core development in US equity markets Tuesday was not a domestic data release but a sentiment inflection in the AI trade. Samsung's 19-fold surge in quarterly operating income — beating consensus by roughly 6% — nonetheless failed to satisfy investors conditioned to exceptional upside from AI-adjacent chipmakers. The selloff in Seoul cascaded into US premarket trading, with Micron down 5.1%, Seagate off 4.4%, and Western Digital declining 5.6%. The episode illustrates a growing tension in the AI trade: order backlogs for data-center infrastructure reportedly extend nearly three years, yet market participants are beginning to rotate out of semiconductors and reassess whether capital expenditure intensity at hyperscalers — Microsoft, Amazon, Meta — leaves adequate room for equity multiple expansion. Morgan Stanley's Michael Wilson argued the divergence between chipmakers and hyperscalers is unsustainable and that a semiconductor correction represents a healthy rebalancing rather than a structural break.

**Tariff Policy**
The US Trade Representative opened a three-day public hearing Tuesday on forced-labor tariff investigations covering 60 countries, with a 10% or 12.5% additional duty already flagged. White House advisers have signaled intent to use these proceedings to restore tariff levels toward the emergency tariff regime. The existing 10% global baseline tariff expires later this month, adding near-term policy uncertainty for importers.
