The battle of wits and words between China and the US is on display again, and 
it’s both indirect and in your face.

On the one hand, President Xi Jinping sought to position China as a champion 
of AI for all 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/xi-vows-to-make-ai-for-all-in-debut-at-china-s-top-tech-summit?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
, calling for efforts to help countries in the Global South expand their AI 
capacity and for prudence in sharing the benefits. He hailed China’s progress 
in developing low-cost artificial intelligence, and used hisfirst appearance at 
the World AI Conference in Shanghai 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-17/xi-jinping-poses-for-family-photo-at-ai-conference-video?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 on Friday to urge the world to adopt an inclusive approach, encouraging 
collaboration without rivalries.

“AI development should not be a solo performance by a single country, but a 
symphony of international cooperation,” he said, adding thatsafety risks must 
be contained 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-17/china-s-xi-says-ai-needs-human-oversight-video?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
. His presence at the gathering, attended by scores of tech and government 
leaders, conveys a potent signal of China’s ambitions to dominate a 
technological sphere with the potential to revolutionize industry and 
economies. Chinese models are winning over companies worldwide, and Chinese AI 
pioneerMoonshot touted a model that performs on par 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/china-s-powerful-new-moonshot-ai-model-closes-gap-with-us-rivals?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 with some of the top-tier platforms from OpenAI and Anthropic.

Across the Pacific, President Donald Trump accused China of interfering with 
US elections 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/trump-accuses-china-of-election-meddling-putting-ties-at-risk?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 in 2020, threatening to upend ties as he focuses on securing a victory in the 
midterms in November. Speaking in aprime-time address from the White House 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/audio/2026-07-17/trump-s-china-election-claims-us-iran-strikes-more?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 on Thursday night, Trump said the Chinese government stole 220 million voter 
files, including names, addresses and other sensitive data in what is “believed 
to be the largest compromise of election data in history” — claims that US 
intelligence agencies have previously debunked.

Is it a politically motivated misdirection 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-17/trump-s-china-rhetoric-a-political-misdirection-puri-video?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
, as King’s College London visiting lecturer, Samir Puri, suggests? “
Scapegoating 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2026-07-17/trump-shouldn-t-be-scapegoating-china-ccg-s-wang-video?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
” as Center for China and Globalization President Henry Huiyao Wang says? 
There’s still some time before a planned visit by Xi to the US in September, 
part of broader efforts by Trump’s administration to stabilize ties and 
maintain a trade truce struck last year. Lots of time for the rhetoric to shift.

unveiling of US tariffs in April last year as investors rotated out of 
technology shares,deepening a selloff in chipmakers

 after their blistering rally this year. Japan’s Nikkei 225 Stock Average and 
Taiwan’s Taiex Index tumbled, and Asia’s main benchmark slid to its weakest 
level since May. Chip bellwethers tumbled across the region with TSMC selling 
off even after its earnings beat estimates, whileJapan’s Kioxia sank as much as 
16% 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/chipmaker-kioxia-s-market-value-halves-from-peak-on-ai-selloff?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
. A measure of investor sentiment on China technology hardware is flashing its
most bearish reading ever 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/china-chip-stock-pullback-drives-investor-fear-to-four-year-high?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
.

Michael Burry, the investor made famous in The Big Short, recommends bargain 
hunting in Hong Kong’s stock market 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/michael-burry-says-good-time-to-chase-hong-kong-stock-bargains?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 after it lagged peers during the global AI rally this year. “It is a 
particularly good time to look to Hong Kong for cheap stocks that should do 
well as the shine comes off Korea, Japan & the Soxx,” Burry, founder of Scion 
Asset Management, wrote in a post on X on Friday. Burry said earlier this month 
that he had bought more shares of Chinese e-commerce firm JD.com.

US Launches New Strikes on Iran as Hormuz Traffic Slumps

The US launched fresh strikes against Iran as hostilities across the Middle 
East continued to escalate and shipping traffic slumped in the critical Strait 
of Hormuz.
Get the latest 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/us-launches-new-strikes-on-iran-as-hormuz-shipping-traffic-falls?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
Two US-sanctioned tankers carrying cooking fuel are U-turning and zig-zagging 
in the Gulf of Oman and Arabian Sea, part of aflotilla of vessels laden with 
Iranian energy exports 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/iran-linked-tankers-u-turn-and-zig-zag-as-us-enforces-blockade?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 that are facing an aggressive US blockade of the country’s shipping. The 
unusual movements come as the US enforces its new action against Iranian 
shipping that began on Tuesday. Since then, American forces have redirected 
three merchant ships, boarded a vessel for verification purposes, and disabled 
a tanker that failed to comply with instructions, US Central Command said in a 
social-media post late on Thursday.

SpaceX said it will aim to launch its Starship rocket again in a few days 
after aborting Thursday’s mission when some of its engines didn’t fire up, 
sending shares lower. Starship was slated to take off on its 13th major mission 
from SpaceX’s South Texas launch facility, called Starbase, at 5:45 p.m. local 
time. But right at the planned liftoff time,plumes of smoke erupted 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/spacex-to-launch-giant-starship-rocket-in-first-flight-since-ipo?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260717&utm_content=6676>
 from the launch tower, and the rocket remained in place.
