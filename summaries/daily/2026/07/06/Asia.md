**Semiconductors and Capital Markets**
South Korea's AI-driven semiconductor windfall is prompting a policy response: President Lee Jae Myung's chief of staff indicated excess corporate tax revenue from Samsung and SK Hynix should be channeled into a dedicated long-term investment fund. The proposal reflects both the scale of the earnings cycle — analyst estimates point to an 18-fold year-on-year jump in Samsung's operating profit — and broader concern about economic concentration risk. Regulators are separately examining leveraged ETFs tracking the two chipmakers, with one opposition lawmaker calling for their delisting given their capacity to amplify volatility.

**Currency and Monetary Policy**
Goldman's revised yen target of 165 per dollar places it among the most bearish institutional forecasts surveyed by Bloomberg. The revision incorporates fiscal deterioration in Japan, the persistence of US rate differentials, and a Bank of Japan tightening path too gradual to meaningfully compress the rate gap. Separately, Bloomberg Economics projects New Zealand's Reserve Bank will hike its cash rate to 2.5% on Wednesday, a move consistent with the broader post-Iran-war upward shift in global rate trajectories.
