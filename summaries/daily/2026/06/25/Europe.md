**Energy and Grid Stress**
Europe's power markets are under acute strain. The combination of record heat driving air-conditioning demand, low wind output from the same high-pressure system causing the heat dome, and thermally constrained nuclear generation along hot French and Swiss rivers has created a textbook supply-demand squeeze. French gas-fired generation rose to nearly 5 gigawatts on Wednesday — the highest since April — as system operators drew on dispatchable fossil capacity to balance the grid. The UK grid operator was briefly concerned about insufficient margin. The episode illustrates the structural vulnerability of the energy transition: renewable build-out has not yet been matched by the storage and interconnection needed to manage low-wind, high-demand extremes.

**Politics**
Andy Burnham appears set to become the UK's next prime minister following the fall of the current government — a transition whose timing coincides, as noted, with the tenth anniversary of Brexit. His foreign policy positions remain largely undefined, though allies have signaled flexibility on fiscal rules. In Brussels, E5 leaders — Germany, France, the UK, Italy, and Poland — met in Berlin ahead of the NATO summit to discuss burden-sharing, framing European defense integration explicitly as delivering what the Trump administration has demanded.
