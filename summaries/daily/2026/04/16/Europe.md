The European Central Bank appears likely to hold rates unchanged at its upcoming meeting, according to people familiar with internal deliberations. Policymakers are choosing to defer judgment on whether the Iran conflict's economic fallout warrants a monetary policy response, with tighter financing conditions credited for keeping inflation expectations anchored in the near term. Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey struck a similar note, signaling no urgency to adjust rates given the difficulty of assessing the war's full economic impact. Eurozone inflation in March came in slightly above the preliminary estimate, but this appears insufficient on its own to shift the ECB toward tightening.

Elsewhere in European markets, easyJet shares fell sharply — their steepest single-session decline since 2022 — after the airline warned of a first-half loss attributable to travel disruptions stemming from the conflict. Pimco indicated it sees value in European bonds following the war-driven selloff, suggesting some institutional buyers view current yield levels as attractive entry points. Hungary's Orbán government suffered a meaningful electoral defeat, triggering what is being described in markets as a "euro convergence trade" — investors positioning for a potential shift in Budapest's economic policy orientation toward greater EU alignment.

At Monte dei Paschi di Siena, shareholders voted to reinstate ousted CEO Luigi Lovaglio, with nearly 50% of attending shareholders supporting the candidate list that included his reappointment, against roughly 39% for the board-backed alternative proposing Fabrizio Palermo. The outcome represents a significant defeat for the outgoing board. The BBC announced plans to cut up to 2,000 positions, approximately 10% of its workforce, as part of a structural transition led by incoming Director General Matt Brittin.
