 Value beats growth 



Yesterday’s beating on software stocks adds credence to the idea that this 
could be the momentvalue investors have been waiting for. 
<https://bloom.bg/49X2glR>

Value stocks (think consumer staples, energy producers and miners) have been 
zooming higher as investors seek out companies that will benefit from an uptick 
in economic growth. On the flipside, tech has struggled this year on worries 
that AI will make a lot of business models obsolete.

The Russell 1000 Value Index has advanced 8.6% since the beginning of 
November, beating its growth counterpart by 14 percentage points.

The group has consistently underperformed for over a decade as tech, in 
particular, powered bull market runs. That’s changing, and the trend may have 
staying power.

“Value has gained a lot of ground versus growth recently, but if you look 
further back – even just to the start of the Fed’s last hike cycle – there is 
plenty of room for outperformance,” said Andrew Greenebaum of Jefferies.

A word of warning: The last two times the value index outperformed its growth 
counterpart by this much, over a similar time span, was during a bear market 
rout in 2022 and the early days of the dot-com bust in 2001. — Joel Leon 
<https://bloom.bg/3H6WMZZ>

 ‘Sickening scenarios’ for Bitcoin 



Michael Burry, who rose to prominence for his wager against the US housing 
market ahead of the 2008 financial crisis, iswarning that Bitcoin’s plunge 
could deepen <https://bloom.bg/3OkXQwC> into a self-reinforcing “death spiral.”

Burry argued in a Substack post that the original cryptocurrency, which has 
fallen 40% since peaking in October, has been exposed as a purely speculative 
asset, failing to take off as gold benefits from investor unease over US 
deficits and policymaking.

Further losses, he said, could rapidly strain the balance sheets of major 
holders, force selling across the crypto ecosystem and trigger widespread value 
destruction.

“Sickening scenarios have now come within reach,” Burry wrote.

While Burry warns of fallout, crypto’s footprint is probably too small to 
trigger broad contagion. Bitcoin’s sub-$1.5 trillion market value, limited 
household exposure and narrow corporate uptake suggest any wealth effect is 
likely to stay contained. —Emily Mason <https://bloom.bg/40J0Lm9>
