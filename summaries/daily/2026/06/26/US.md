**Monetary Policy and Inflation Outlook**
Goldman Sachs estimates the Fed's preferred core PCE gauge will reach 3.2% YoY by December — well above the 2% target — leaving rate cuts a distant prospect. One unnamed Fed official described borrowing costs as "well positioned," reinforcing the Committee's patient stance. Market pricing has shifted rate-cut expectations into 2027, and JPMorgan, Bank of America, and Goldman strategists have coalesced around a renewed dollar-bullish view, citing Fed Chair Warsh's explicit commitment to restoring price stability. JPMorgan's Meera Chandan noted the Fed has effectively "activated" the dollar, with the rate differential versus other major central banks unlikely to narrow materially near term.

**Markets: AI Trade Fractures**
The tech sector sold off sharply on Friday following Apple's price hikes and a report that OpenAI may delay its IPO. Nasdaq 100 futures declined more than 1%. The episode crystallized a broader fragmentation within the AI trade: memory names are up over 200% year-to-date, data center infrastructure over 100%, while the Magnificent Seven cohort is negative for 2026 and AI-exposed software has shed roughly a third of its value. Rising component costs — chipmakers exercising pricing power — appear to be compressing margins downstream, and investors are increasingly differentiating within the space rather than treating AI as a single thematic position.
