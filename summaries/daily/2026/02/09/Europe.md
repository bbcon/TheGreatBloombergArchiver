**Europe Daily Briefing — February 9, 2026**

**UK political crisis intensifies.** Prime Minister Keir Starmer's position has come under serious strain following the resignation of Morgan McSweeney, his chief of staff, amid the fallout from the appointment of Peter Mandelson as ambassador to Washington. People familiar with the matter indicate a group of cabinet ministers may privately urge Starmer to step down, though allies expect him to survive the week. Markets are positioning for further weakness: hedge funds have built short positions in sterling against the euro, citing the combination of political turmoil, expectations of further BOE rate cuts, and elevated options demand. Gilts declined as the political pressure intensified, though separate UK labour market data suggested the jobs sector is beginning to stabilise as firms emerge from a period dominated by tax speculation. UK Q4 GDP and industrial production are due Thursday.

**Iberian political shifts.** Socialist António José Seguro won Portugal's presidency in the first runoff in four decades, defeating far-right candidate André Ventura after a turbulent campaign. In Spain, the Socialists suffered another setback in a snap regional vote, underscoring waning support for Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez.

**Corporate developments.** Swatch Group's defence against activist Steven Wood strengthened materially after last month's upbeat outlook drove the most-shorted stock in Europe to its best day on record; shares are up 15% year-to-date. The company has nominated its own new board member ahead of Wood's second-year campaign for a seat. Novo Nordisk shares rebounded in pre-market trade after Hims & Hers Health withdrew a copy of its Wegovy weight-loss pill. STMicroelectronics gained 6.5% in Paris on a deepening partnership with AWS. InPost jumped 14% in Amsterdam after an Advent/FedEx-led group disclosed buyout plans. Greggs fell 6% in London following a Jefferies downgrade citing GLP-1 headwinds. BP reports Tuesday, with Bloomberg Opinion's Javier Blas arguing the $750mn quarterly buyback is unaffordable given softer commodity prices.

**ECB calendar.** Lagarde, Lane, Simkus and Nagel speak today, with Schnabel, Cipollone and others later in the week alongside euro-area Q4 GDP on Friday.
