Trump Starts Rebuilding Tariff Protections Targeting China, Europe
The White House took the first official steps to impose more tariffs since its 
Supreme Court loss

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17249075&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=03122026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer kicked off the first of several 
sweeping trade investigations required to impose more tariffs and replace 
levies struck down by the nation’s Supreme Court.

In a Federal Register filing 
<https://ustr.gov/sites/default/files/files/Press/Releases/2026/USTR%20301%20FRN%20Industrial%20Excess%20Capacity%203-11-26.pdf>
, Greer’s office levied allegations of industrial overcapacity for several 
economies, saying China maintains atrade surplus 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-09/china-s-tariff-defying-export-boom-leaves-its-factory-workers-behind?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 across several sectors. (Click here 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-11/trump-tariffs-revival-trade-investigations-launched-in-china-eu?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 to read the full story.)

The move marks the formal step by the administration to rebuild Trump’s tariff 
wall after the landmark high-court ruling last month that the Trump 
administration illegally collected an estimated $170 billion in import taxes 
from American companies. The process forobtaining refunds 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/us-tells-tariff-refund-judge-it-can-t-comply-with-court-order?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 is currently a hotly contested issue between the government and importers.

Wednesday’s move, initiated under USTR’s Section 301 authority, also singled 
out theEuropean Union 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/eu-expects-to-be-exempt-from-us-universal-tariff-boost-to-15?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 — particularly Germany and Ireland — over its chemicals, machinery and 
vehicles, and Taiwan over semiconductor chips and electronics.

May 5th Hearing

The process will take at least a few months, with an open hearing set for May 
5 in Washington. Comments from the public can be submitted to USTRhere 
<https://comments.ustr.gov/s/>.

Launching a new trade investigation risks stirring up tensions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/china-frustrated-by-last-minute-scramble-to-plan-xi-trump-summit?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 with Beijing just weeks before a planned summit between US President Donald 
Trump and Chinese leader Xi Jinping.

Targeting Mexico, too, could further damage the already strained efforts 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/mexico-trade-consulations-favor-tweaking-usmca-over-major-revamp?cmpid=BBD031226_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260312>
 to renegotiate the US-Mexico-Canada Agreement that Trump signed during his 
first term. Canada was not among the initial batch of targeted countries in 
Wednesday’s filing.
