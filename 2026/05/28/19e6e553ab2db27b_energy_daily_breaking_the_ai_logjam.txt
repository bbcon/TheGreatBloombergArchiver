Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter John Ainger explores how the biggest-ever US power deal could affect 
AI development.

NextEra Energy Inc.’s $67 billion acquisition of Dominion Energy Inc. is a bet 
that it can swiftly deliver the infrastructure needed to power the AI boom.

Northern Virginia has the highest concentration of data centers globally. But 
connecting new facilities quickly has eluded Dominion and PJM Interconnection, 
the operator of the biggest US electric grid.

Wait times for Virginia-based Dominion to make those connections have grown to 
as long as seven years 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-08-29/data-centers-face-seven-year-wait-for-power-hookups-in-virginia?cmpid=BBBXT052826_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260528&utm_content=4935>
 and the company’s shares have lagged behind peers amid the boom. PJM, 
meanwhile, has struggled to both meet surging electric demand and tame soaring 
household utility bills. A federal regulator this month suggested that PJM may 
have “grown too big to function 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-12/biggest-us-grid-may-be-too-large-to-function-regulator-says?cmpid=BBBXT052826_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260528&utm_content=4935>
.”

If the NextEra-Dominion deal is completed — there’s no guarantee given the 
many regulatory approvals needed — the combined company will control about 15% 
of the installed generation on PJM’s grid. That may not be enough to force 
wholesale system changes, but it could help toward boosting capacity and 
resilience.

This deal will remove a “reliance” on PJM, said John Ketchum, NextEra’s chief 
executive officer, on an investor call last week. “It’s going to be up to the 
incumbent utilities to really help drive and solve the problem.”

PJM and Dominion declined to comment. NextEra representatives didn’t respond 
to requests for comment.

The potential creation of a behemoth at the heart of Virginia’s Data Center 
Alley underscores utilities’ rush to capture their greatest opportunity in 
decades — a building boom to satisfy surging AI-driven demand.

NextEra would be able to move quickly, said Ben Hertz-Shargel, an analyst at 
Wood Mackenzie Ltd. But the merger poses an underlying risk that regulators 
scrutinizing the deal will surely consider: whether speed may come at the cost 
of higher bills.

“You now have the largest developer paired up with the incumbent utility,” he 
said. “There is significant affordability uncertainty here.”

Now, there’s speculation about other potential utility tie-ups. But 
replicating the clout of NextEra, a rare powerhouse project developer, won’t be 
easy.

—John Ainger, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

Europe’s gas storage will be only about 75% full by the end of September, 
below the 80% target, in BloombergNEF’s base case, which assumes Qatar’s 
exports of liquefied natural gas resume in early July. A longer disruption to 
shipments could leave inventories near 70%, increasing the risk of entering 
winter with a limited buffer.
