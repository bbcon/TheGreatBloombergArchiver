**Equity Market Structure**
US equities remain technically resilient — the S&P 500 staged its second-largest intraday reversal since April 2025 on Tuesday — but the underlying composition raises legitimate concerns. The Nasdaq 100 is still down 4.25% for June despite two sharp rebounds. BofA's analysis shows index performance is entirely driven by sectors with the highest earnings-estimate revisions, while the equal-weighted S&P 500 is lagging the cap-weighted version by a margin not seen since the dot-com era. StoneX's Vincent Deluard flags that roughly two-thirds of Magnificent Seven free cash flow in the most recent quarter derived from unrealized gains on pre-IPO stakes in Anthropic, OpenAI, and SpaceX — gains unlikely to recur.

**Monetary Policy and Rates**
The May CPI release is the session's primary macro catalyst. Bond traders are actively building positions targeting multiple Fed rate hikes, with some pricing a move as early as September. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has maintained a higher-for-longer posture, and the combination of strong jobs data and renewed geopolitical inflation risk — via the Iran war — has effectively shifted market pricing away from cuts. Super Micro fell 9.3% premarket after announcing $7 billion in equity financing.
