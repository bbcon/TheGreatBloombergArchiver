Apple delivered a stronger-than-expected revenue forecast for the third quarter, sending shares up roughly 2.8% in early trading. The company acknowledged headwinds — higher memory-chip costs and persistent Mac shortages — but the guidance nonetheless represented the standout positive earnings event of the week. Alphabet's blowout results the prior session added $421 billion to its market capitalization in a single day, the second-largest one-day market cap gain on record for any stock. Reddit climbed 16% on a sales beat, while Atlassian rallied 24% after results eased concerns about AI's disruptive impact on enterprise software. On the other side, Roblox tumbled 22% after safety features for children constrained user growth, and Sandisk and Western Digital fell sharply despite a strong run year-to-date.

Equity futures were broadly steady, suggesting a natural pause following what appears to have been the best monthly performance for US stocks since late 2020. In credit markets, investors are showing early signs of fatigue after a $300 billion AI-related debt binge, with demands emerging for better pricing and stronger covenant protections.

On monetary policy, the departure of Stephen Miran — who cast the lone public vote for a rate cut across the entire G-7 this week — highlights the unusual position the US may soon occupy. With Kevin Warsh expected to assume the Fed chair role, the prospect of continued dovish pressure from the White House sits in direct tension with inflation dynamics being stoked by the Iran conflict and elevated energy prices. Fitch separately warned that the US debt burden remains "far above" other AA-rated sovereigns, a reminder that fiscal vulnerabilities persist even as near-term growth holds. The S&P Dow Jones Indices consultation on accelerating index inclusion for mega-cap IPOs drew attention, with SpaceX cited as a potential beneficiary that could join the S&P 500 in six months rather than twelve.
