When it comes to AI-related shares, investors are only seeing stars 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/asian-stocks-to-rise-as-nvidia-whipsaws-on-results-markets-wrap?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
. Tech stocks jumped in Asia after Nvidia’s earnings and SpaceX’s IPO filing 
inflamed enthusiasm toward the sector.

LG Electronics and Hyundai Mobis both surged more than 10% in Seoul as Nvidia 
CEO Jensen Huang touted new opportunities in theform of robots 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/nvidia-s-huang-spurs-asia-tech-rally-with-ai-robots-hype?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 and automated vehicles. Samsung rallied 8% after averting a strike 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/samsung-union-shelves-strike-plan-on-tentative-deal-yonhap-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
. SoftBank rose 20% in Tokyo on news OpenAI is preparing tofile an IPO 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/openai-preparing-for-ipo-filing-in-days-or-weeks-wsj-reports?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
.

A gauge of tech shares on MSCI’s Asian index has climbed 64% this year, 
compared to a 17% gain for the broader regional benchmark, as investors piled 
into what they perceive to be the picks and shovels of the AI gold rush. In so 
doing they are creating tech giants, with Samsung and Taiwan’s TSMC together 
adding about $1.2 trillion in market value in 2026.

The apex of AI ambitions is Elon Musk’s SpaceX, which filed for what stands to 
be thelargest-ever IPO 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/musk-s-spacex-files-publicly-for-nasdaq-ipo-under-symbol-spcx?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
, and his aspirations to build data centers in space (and a human settlement on 
Mars).

Back on Earth, the new technology is resulting in the more prosaic outcome of 
fewer bankers on Wall Street. JPMorgan’s Jamie Dimon became the latest CEO to 
talk about the impact, saying the bank will likely hire more AI specialists and
fewer traditional bankers 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/dimon-says-jpmorgan-will-hire-more-ai-people-fewer-bankers?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 as the adoption of the technology accelerates.

Earlier this week, Standard Chartered CEO Bill Winters sparked criticism 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/stanchart-ceo-reassures-staff-after-lower-value-human-backlash?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 after saying the lender will replace “lower-value human capital” with 
technology. That followed remarks from Goldman Sachs President John Waldron, 
who described back-office operations as a “human assembly line” ripe for 
automation. HSBC CEO is Georges Elhedery isbetting on AI 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/hsbc-mulls-deep-job-cuts-from-multiyear-ai-fueled-overhaul?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 to shrink its middle and back offices.

Perhaps Mars is the future 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-20/spacex-to-reward-musk-for-mars-colony-with-1-million-people?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
.

India’s central bank is said to be considering all options to stabilize its

with the matter.

Iran is assessing the latest peace proposal from the US, as the countries seek 
to avoid a resumption of hostilities.

“We have received the American side’s points of view and are reviewing them,” 
Foreign Ministry Spokesman Esmail Baghaei said, according to the semi-official 
Iranian Students’ News Agency.

The exchange of messages 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/iran-reviewing-trump-s-latest-offer-as-clock-ticks-on-ceasefire?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 is based on Iran’s 14-point text from several weeks ago, Baghaei said. That 
plan essentially suggests a short-term deal that would see Iran reopen the 
Strait of Hormuz and the US lift a blockade of Iranian ports, with the warring 
sides then going into deeper negotiations over Tehran’s nuclear program.

Global stockpiles of crude oil and products are being drawn down at a record 
pace 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/goldman-says-global-oil-stockpiles-falling-at-record-pace-on-war?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 this month as the war in the Middle East drags on, curtailing supplies, 
according to Goldman Sachs.

Visible inventories contracted by a record 8.7 million barrels a day in May, 
almost double the average pace since the conflict began, analysts wrote in a 
note. International Energy Agency Executive Director Fatih Birol warned last 
week that commercial oil inventorieswere shrinking 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/iea-chief-warns-commercial-oil-inventories-are-falling-very-fast?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 at an accelerated speed.

China’s Hormuz Workaround Is Making India Jealous

The closure of the Strait of Hormuz has sent the vast Asian chemicals industry 
into a tailspin. Deprived of the likes of Qatari natural gas and Saudi Arabian 
oil, the region’s fertilizer and plastics plants are slowing production or even 
shutting down. Everywhere but China, that is.
Read more 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2026-05-21/china-s-coal-to-chemicals-hormuz-workaround-is-making-india-jealous?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
Business activity in the euro area shrank at the quickest pace in 2 1/2 years, 
adding to fears that the Iran war and accompanying surge in energy costs are 
dealinga severe blow 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/eurozone-pmi?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260521&utm_content=4717>
 to the economy.

The Composite Purchasing Managers’ Index compiled by S&P Global fell to 47.5 
in May from 48.8 in April, holding below the 50 threshold separating growth 
from contraction for a second month. Analysts in a Bloomberg survey had 
anticipated an unchanged reading.

Among the region’s top two economies, Germany’s composite reading was more or 
less stable, while France’s plungedto the lowest level

 since 2020.
