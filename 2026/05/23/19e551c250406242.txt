Berkeley raises the bar for green homes

By Todd Woody 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AVl1IPrK6vs/todd-woody?cmpid=BBD052326_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260523&utm_content=4796>

The real estate listing for the $1.25 million Mediterranean Revival home in a 
leafy Berkeley, California, neighborhood, touts the property’selectric heat 
pumps, 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2022-06-04/yes-a-heat-pump-can-replace-your-air-conditioner?cmpid=BBD052326_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260523&utm_content=4796>
solar panels 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-29/to-lower-electricity-costs-consumers-quietly-install-diy-solar?cmpid=BBD052326_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260523&utm_content=4796>
, induction range and an updatedelectrical panel 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2022-08-17/how-to-lower-the-cost-of-a-home-electrical-upgrade?cmpid=BBD052326_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260523&utm_content=4796>
 that’s “EV ready.” The sellers aren’t just green-bragging, they’re showing 
buyers they’ve complied with a new city ordinance requiring such 
climate-friendly features.

The Bay Area university town of 120,000 is the first in the US to require 
sellers and buyers of single-family homes to replace fossil-fuel appliances or 
make other green upgrades as a condition of sale. It’s one of several cities 
leveraging real estate transactions to achieve carbon-emissions reduction 
targets as the Trump administrationeliminates 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-05-13/alabama-georgia-home-energy-efficiency-grant-canceled-by-trump-administration?cmpid=BBD052326_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260523&utm_content=4796>
 climate action.Austin 
<https://austinenergy.com/energy-efficiency/ecad-ordinance>, Minneapolis 
<https://www.minneapolismn.gov/media/-www-content-assets/documents/Energy-Disclosure-Policy-frequently-asked-questions---wcmsp-222012.pdf>
 andPortland 
<https://www.portland.gov/bps/climate-action/pdxhes/what-home-energy-score/home-sellers>
, Oregon, are among municipalities that require home sellers to obtain and 
disclose the results of energy audits to encourage voluntary efficiency 
improvements.
Homes in Berkeley, California.Bloomberg via David Paul Morris
Berkeley, though, is mandating that such upgrades happen. “What Berkeley is 
doing holds a lot of promise in terms of being a very effective way to increase 
the rate of retrofits as homes are sold and turn over to new owners,” saidDavid 
Ribeiro <https://www.aceee.org/about/aceee-staff/david-riberio>, director of 
local policy for the American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy (ACEEE), 
a nonprofit research organization.

The city has long pioneered environmental policies that have later been 
adopted across the country, from curbside recycling in the 1970s to a ban on 
polystyrene-foam takeout food containers in the 1980s. Berkeley voters in 2006 
set atarget 
<https://berkeleyca.gov/sites/default/files/2022-01/Berkeley-Climate-Action-Plan.pdf>
 of reducing greenhouse gas emissions 80% by 2050 and buildings are the city’s 
second-largest source of carbon pollution after transportation, according to 
Ammon Reagan, the sustainability program coordinator for the city’s energy 
office.

“Natural gas is the biggest source of building emissions and the biggest 
emission savings are from heat pumps,” he said.

Berkeley’s Building Emissions Saving Ordinance 
<https://berkeleyca.gov/construction-development/green-building/building-emissions-saving-ordinance-beso/time-sale-energy>
 (BESO) took effect in January and gives credits for a home’s low-carbon 
systems with a minimum score of six credits needed. Home sellers must disclose 
their score and make any needed changes before a sale or the buyer must do so 
within two years.

Brokers said the emissions mandate is starting to change the dynamics of the 
market as BESO scores appear on listings.

“Homes that are already electrified will be perceived as more move-in ready,” 
Berkeley brokerMegan Micco <https://www.meganmicco.com/> said in an email. 
“Homes that defer everything effectively carry a hidden cost that informed 
buyers will factor into their offers.”

Micco listed the $1.25 million Mediterranean Revival 
<https://www.meganmicco.com/properties/1788-sanlorenzo-berkeley/> whose 
upgrades let her highlight the house as “fully BESO compliant.” A mile away, 
agents recently held an open house at another1930s Mediterranean bungalow 
<https://www.2370marin.com/>, a smaller dwelling with a low BESO score priced 
at $898,000.

Bike lane, rain garden or both?

By Laura Laker
Bike lanes were installed in conjunction with new rain gardens in the 
Grangetown neighborhood of Cardiff.GreenBlue Urban
Cardiff’s growing network of bike lanes offers more than just places to cycle. 
Lined with greenery, benches and green-roofed bus shelters, the lanes around 
the city center seem almost like miniature parks. In a sense, they are.

The Welsh capital city’s new cycle paths are designed to also function as rain 
gardens — pockets of greenery that, by acting as sponges soaking up heavy 
rainfall, help prevent flooding. That greenery also provides habitats for 
wildlife, helps to filter pollutants and offers cooling shade during heat peaks.

This multi-purpose design is a pragmatic response to the current pressures on 
UK cities and regions to deliver more for less. With the UK economy going 
through a long period of sluggish growth and the climate-skeptic Reform UK 
Party overtaking the Conservatives as the main British party of the Right, 
green policies risk being sidelined if they cannot deliver on multiple goals. 
While such policies will not convince climate skeptics, their potential 
efficiency helps counter the narrative that environmental measures cause
economic harm 
<https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/europpblog/2025/11/07/the-far-rights-push-for-climate-realism-is-winning-political-ground/>
.

By making these interventions multi-purpose, authorities can also access a 
wider range of different funding pots. If there is flood mitigation and extra 
tree cover alongside bike lanes, there’s potentially environmental and health 
funding 
<https://www.gov.uk/government/news/almost-300-million-to-gear-up-new-walking-wheeling-and-cycling-schemes>
 available. Followinga winter of record rainfall 
<https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/about-us/news-and-media/media-centre/weather-and-climate-news/2026/dull-and-mild-february-brings-a-wet-winter-to-a-close>
, andthe wettest January on record 
<https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/blog/2026/january-2026-regional-weather-stats>, 
these decisions have been vindicated.

What’s more, wildlife is moving into this new habitat. In January 2024, an 
otter appeared in a newly “daylighted” section of canal in the city center, 
until recently buried beneath slabs of concrete.The resulting footage 
<https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/av/uk-wales-68011437> of the animal eating a fish 
on a patch of greenery 
<https://x.com/SEWBReC/status/1745757066369634550/photo/1> sent a ripple of 
excitement across the city.
