With SpaceX’s mega IPO coming Friday, the sustainability of the AI boom — and 
the staggering amounts of capital expenditure committed — is in sharp focus. 
For the dreamers, buying into AI-related companies such as SpaceX is buyinginto 
a future 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/musk-stock-fans-say-the-more-the-better-in-spacex-ipo-frenzy?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 of infinite promise (data centers orbiting space!) anda future of 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/wall-street-braces-for-spacex-with-stress-test-watch-parties?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 promised profits.

For the cynics, we’ve been here before. Famed short seller James Chanos says 
it’snot financial fundamentals 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/chanos-bearish-on-spacex-valuation-fueled-by-hopes-and-dreams?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 driving demand for SpaceX’spublic debut 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/spacex-leaves-some-banks-peeved-at-junior-roles-in-ipo-lineup?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 but investor enthusiasm for Elon Musk and AI.

“We’re going to be doing a $75 billion IPO for a valuation of close to $2 
trillion for a company with revenues of $19 billion and negative free cash 
flow,” Chanos said at the iConnections Global Alts conference in New York. 
“This is really a hopes-and-dreams IPO.”

Chanos said the valuation reflects expectations for future businesses that 
remain largely theoretical. “The total addressable market for space is 
infinite,” he said. “You can build whatever stories you want — colonies on 
Mars, factories on the moon, data centers in space — to justifythe valuation 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/how-the-spacex-ipo-will-affect-portfolios-and-everyday-investors?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
.”

Another promised dream is a submissive Iran, an outcome which continues to 
elude US President Donald Trump despite his repeated claims a peace deal is 
imminent.

The US launched strikes 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/us-launches-more-strikes-against-iran-further-straining-truce?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 against targets for the second straight day, with more possible. Trump said in 
a Fox News interview that he had spoken with top Iranian officials Wednesday 
and they had asked him to halt the bombing. He said the US would hit Iran again 
Thursday unless it accepts an accord that’s meant to extend their increasingly 
tenuous ceasefire by two months and reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

The attacks offer fresh evidence that a ceasefire agreed upon in April has all 
but collapsed.

Beijing is not happy with Alibaba, JD.com and other top e-commerce players. 
The marketregulator blasted 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/alibaba-jd-com-slide-after-beijing-slams-price-cut-promotions?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 some of the companies for seemingly promising tens of billions of yuan in 
subsidies during the annual “618” midyear online shopping fest, accusing them 
of false advertising, according to a report by state television CCTV.

The comments come as Beijing seeks to curb a race-to-the-bottom competition 
that’s embroiled some of the country’s biggest companies in price wars that are 
eroding profits. “The move signifies further tightening of regulatory 
scrutiny,” said Bloomberg Intelligence analyst Robert Lea.

China is shifting tactics to press its claims in the waters off Taiwan by 
moving toward a version ofmaritime quarantine 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/china-presses-taiwan-by-querying-foreign-ships-for-first-time?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
.

Chinese vessels radioed three passing merchant ships flagged in Singapore, 
Liberia and Benin, asking their ports of departure and destination and the 
number of crew aboard, Taiwan’s Coast Guard said. It was part of what China 
calls a “special maritime trafficlaw enforcement 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-06/china-escalates-patrols-near-taiwan-over-japan-philippines-talks?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
” since Saturday.

“By radioing passing vessels, China is seeking to create the impression that 
the waters around Taiwan fall under Chinese jurisdiction,” said Lin Ying-yu, an 
associate professor at Tamkang University in Taipei. He said the patrol 
appeared to rehearse tactics China could deploy as part of a broader “maritime 
quarantine” of the island.

China’s rapid adoption of AI in the workplace has prompted an unusually blunt 
call from a state-run newspaper to protect labor rights.

In an editorial published on Thursday, the Workers’ Daily — the official 
mouthpiece of China’s umbrella trade union organization —urged government 
agencies 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/ai-wave-sparks-alarm-in-china-with-call-to-protect-worker-rights?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260611&utm_content=5414>
 to mount an active response as new threats emerge to the rights of employees. 
It called on regulators to improve labor standards and strengthen oversight of 
AI algorithms, including by giving a greater say to trade unions and workers’ 
representatives.
