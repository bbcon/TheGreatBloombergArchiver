**China: Technology and Domestic Economy**
China's Moonshot AI startup launched the Kimi K3 model, which drew immediate comparisons to leading US systems in quality benchmarks while pricing significantly below American rivals. A White House official accused Moonshot of improperly using US AI models and Nvidia chips in its development, adding a geopolitical dimension to what is increasingly a parallel technology ecosystem. Domestically, the economy remains under pressure: the Iran conflict has reduced Chinese oil imports to 2016 levels, and 60% of the Q2 slowdown in industrial production was concentrated in the oil supply chain. Beijing simultaneously closed a "1+N" offshore refinancing loophole used by distressed property developers, tightening an already constrained sector.

**Southeast Asia**
Indonesian equities approached bull-market territory, rallying 20% from their early-June low following S&P's reaffirmation of the country's credit rating and supportive Bank Indonesia policy — though the index remains down approximately 26% year-to-date and foreign investors are net sellers. The Philippine peso continued to test its record low of 61.75 per dollar, with the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas intervening to limit volatility amid shrinking reserves and rising oil import costs.
