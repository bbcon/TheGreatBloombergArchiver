Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
reporter Alex Longley looks at the latest surge in oil tanker rates as a result 
of the Iran war.

There’s never been a more lucrative time to be an oil tanker owner. 

All of the world’s three main categories of crude-carrying vessels are earning 
north of $200,000 a day, whether you want to book one to carry 2 million 
barrels or 700,000.

At the start of the war, gains in rates were dominated by the biggest ships, 
supertankers. In that segment, shifting global oil flows,stranded vessels 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-strait-hormuz-iran-blockade-ships-stranded-gps-jamming/?cmpid=BBBXT033026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260330>
 and abuying spree 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-16/niche-corner-of-the-oil-market-is-rocked-by-huge-bet-on-tankers?cmpid=BBBXT033026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260330>
 from a South Korean shipping magnate all helped push earnings to stratospheric 
levels.

The oil market rewiring has gathered pace since then. With the world short of 
crude, the market is turning to its marginal seller in times of crisis: the US.

Some market participants are expecting to see a record of around 5 million 
barrels a day exported next month as buyers overseas seek to compensate for 
Middle Eastern shortfalls.

According to Clarksons Securities AS, weekly vessel bookings over the last two 
weeks amounted to 6 million barrels a day — a level that may not be reached but 
points to the strength of exports ahead.

Right now, Oil Brokerage Ltd. estimates that about 5% of mainstream tankers 
are trapped inside the Persian Gulf. That’s limiting the ability of the global 
fleet to reroute at a time when there’s been a sudden pull on barrels from 
wherever they can be found.

“The fleet is out of position relative to this amount of cargo,” Clarksons 
analysts including Frode Morkedal wrote in a note.

The high earnings may not last forever. Usually, losing millions of barrels a 
day of supply means fewer cargoes to transport and a hit to rates for 
shipowners.

Earnings for some specific routes have retreated slightly from their recent 
highs, including forvessels headed to the Red Sea port of Yanbu 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-24/fees-to-ship-saudi-crude-from-yanbu-ease-as-more-tankers-arrive?cmpid=BBBXT033026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260330>
 to pick up shipments for Saudi Arabia.

Clarksons say rates there are down to $175,000 a day from $300,000 a day 
earlier in March.

But if you offered oil tanker owners those fees for their ships 12 months ago, 
they’d have been delighted. In this market, even rates that seem low are 
actually enormous.

—Alex Longley, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

Mainland China overtook Singapore to become Asia’s largest renewable fuels 
producer in 2025, according to BloombergNEF. Most of its new capacity is 
focused on producing sustainable aviation fuel, or SAF, and growth is set to 
continue this year, with 1 billion gallons of additional capacity slated to 
come online. That would make it the second largest producer in the world, after 
the US.
