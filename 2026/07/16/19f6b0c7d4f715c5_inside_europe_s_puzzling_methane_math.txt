Counting methane emissions

By Petra Sorge 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/AV7cfwcPiCo/petra-sorge?cmpid=BBD071626_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6619>

Physicist Jaroslaw Necki vividly recalls his trips to the тАЬSahara,тАЭ the vast 
open-pit mines of central Poland rich with soft lignite coal. Working in these 
treeless landscapes, where sand and clay stretch into the distance, heтАЩd often 
forget he was in the middle of Europe. Over the years, these excursions have 
made the associate professor at KrakowтАЩs Mining and Metallurgical Academy a 
leading global expert in methane emissions тАФ a powerful greenhouse gas known to 
have a significant impact on global warming.

Last year, Necki saw the results of the European UnionтАЩs first large-scale 
coal mine emissions test. The report, which was seen by Bloomberg, showed some
remarkable results 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-07-16/europe-s-coal-mines-claim-their-methane-emissions-have-plummeted-but-have-they?cmpid=BBD071626_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260716&utm_content=6619>
. PolandтАЩs two biggest lignite surface mines, Belchatow and Turow, recorded 
methane emission levels in 2024 that were roughly one-thousandth of those shown 
by most independent tests in the last two decades.
The Belchatow coal mine and power plant near Belchatow, Poland.Photographer: 
Dawid Zieli┼Дski/Bloomberg
In the Czech Republic, methane emissions from the same kind of open-pit coal 
mines dropped 91% between 2022 and 2024. And in Germany, which still depends on 
dirty fossil fuels for about one-fifth of its power production, nine out of ten 
lignite mines documented an overall emissions decline of 64% in 2024 from what 
was reported a year earlier. In all cases, the reductions were so large that 
they couldnтАЩt simply be explained by production cuts.

тАЬI donтАЩt believe these numbers at all,тАЭ said Necki.
Physicist Jaroslaw Necki with methane measuring equipment.Photographer: Dawid 
Zieli┼Дski/Bloomberg
He, as well as other experts who spoke to Bloomberg, said that the dramatic 
falls in reported methane emissions are the result of a small but fundamental 
change in the methodology for measuring them тАФ one that was introduced as part 
of a plan by the EU to track and contain methane. That tweak has allowed 
open-pit mine operators to essentially grade their own homework, enabling them 
to undercount their emissions. This could allow governments and companies to 
overstate their progress on reaching climate goals while underestimating the 
scale of action still needed.

тАЬWrong techniques are being intentionally applied to benefit coal mines тАФ not 
the environment,тАЭ Necki said.

All the mining companies contacted by Bloomberg described their methane 
testing as aligned with EU regulations and scientific standards.

Prior to last year, most operators of open-pit mines, which make up 
three-quarters of EU coal mines, used emission factors set under national 
formulas to measure pollution. Now, mine operators are being asked to establish 
their own emissions factors based on site-specific tests. But there are not yet 
any guidelines for conducting these tests.

In Germany, open-pit mine operators all took a common tack, according to 
reports seen by Bloomberg and submitted to the countryтАЩs environmental agency. 
Soil samples were collected тАЬfrom the freshly exposed coal embankmentтАЭ and then 
crushed into small particles, while the methane emitted during this process was 
captured and quantified.

That makes no sense, said Necki, who compared measuring ground-level coal 
emissions to shaking and opening a soda bottle, then trying to measure the 
remaining carbon dioxide inside. тАЬYou will not get a valid result,тАЭ he said, 
тАЬbecause the gas has already disappeared into the atmosphere.тАЭ
