**ECB Policy: Divergence and Risk**
The ECB's decision to raise rates stands as the most consequential tightening move among major central banks since the conflict in the Middle East began. The Bank of Canada held this week; the Fed and Bank of England are expected to do the same next week. ECB Governing Council member Joachim Nagel explicitly acknowledged the possibility of a second consecutive hike in July, while ECB President Lagarde defended the move by arguing that the eurozone economy retains sufficient resilience to absorb tighter financial conditions. Critics, including Berenberg economist Holger Schmieding, contend the ECB is overreacting — invoking its record of policy mis-steps in 2008, 2011, and 2022.

**UK: Growth and Political Deterioration**
April GDP data showed UK economic momentum fading after a solid start to 2026 — a result that arrived alongside a politically damaging cabinet resignation. Healey's departure over defence spending credibility leaves Starmer vulnerable to leadership challenges and diminishes the UK's standing in NATO discussions ahead of next week's G7 summit. Spain, by contrast, is emerging as the eurozone's fastest-growing major economy, supported by strong immigration-driven labour supply.
