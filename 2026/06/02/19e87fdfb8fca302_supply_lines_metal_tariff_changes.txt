Some US steel and aluminum tariffs are headed lower, under a makeover designed 
to encourage construction, manufacturing and investment.

The White House late Monday announced itέΑβs reducing rates next week on imports 
of HVAC equipment and machinery like forklifts, while clarifying the duties on 
goods from countries that signed trade deals with President Donald Trump.

Here are the main changes, according to the proclamation 
<https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/further-adjusting-the-tariff-regimes-for-imports-of-aluminum-steel-and-copper-into-the-united-states/>
:

 * a drop in the levies on some agricultural and industrial goods to 15% from 
25%
 * an expanded category of industrial equipment eligible for a rate with 
countries that have trade agreement with the US
 * an incentive for foreign companies to use more US steel and aluminum so 
they qualify for a 10% duty
Deborah Elms, head of trade policy at the Hinrich Foundation in Singapore, 
called it a positive step έΑΦ provided you can navigate the pile of 
HS-code-filled annexes spelling out all the inclusions and exclusions.
