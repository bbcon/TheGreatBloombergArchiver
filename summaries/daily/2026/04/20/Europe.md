European equities fell approximately 1% on Monday as the weekend's events in the Strait of Hormuz rekindled concerns about the durability of any near-term ceasefire. European natural gas prices climbed as much as 11%, compounding the energy cost pressures that have already weighed heavily on the continent's industrial base. The EU is preparing measures to optimize jet fuel distribution among member states and source alternative supplies, reflecting the practical strain that seven weeks of Hormuz disruption is placing on aviation and broader energy infrastructure.

In Germany, Chancellor Friedrich Merz faces an increasingly difficult political environment. His approval rating has fallen to deeply negative territory, with 80% of respondents in a recent poll expressing disapproval of his performance. Measures announced last week to cushion the blow of rising energy prices — including a €1,000 worker payment intended to be voluntarily funded by employers — were widely criticized as inadequate, with the federal government itself declining to participate. Merz has called crisis talks to address energy price impacts on German industry, but his coalition appears beset by the same infighting that characterized its predecessor, providing oxygen to the AfD, which leads in polls ahead of state elections in eastern Germany in September. UniCredit CEO Andrea Orcel pressed his case for acquiring Commerzbank, arguing the German bank requires deep structural change.

In the UK, Prime Minister Keir Starmer faces a significant domestic political test, making a statement to the House of Commons on Monday over the controversy surrounding Peter Mandelson's security vetting clearance as US ambassador. Calls for Starmer to resign are growing ahead of local elections next month, which are predicted to be damaging for Labour. Separately, Bulgaria's presidential election produced a decisive victory for former President Rumen Radev, who opposes sanctions on Russia and military aid for Ukraine, adding a complicating element to European political cohesion on the war in the east.
