Iran and the US are inching closer to a possible deal, but every step forward 
is colliding with anew flashpoint 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/iran-war-attack-on-barakah-nuclear-plant-from-iraq-is-warning-shot?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260522&utm_content=4759>
. Tehran said the latest US proposal has narrowed differences, yet disputes 
over Iran’s uranium stockpile and potential tolls in the Strait of Hormuz 
quicklyreignited tensions 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/progress-in-iran-talks-undercut-by-divide-over-uranium-tolls?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260522&utm_content=4759>
, with President Donald Trump threatening renewed strikes if talks fail. Oil 
marketsswung wildly 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-may-22?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260522&utm_content=4759>
 as traders tried to price the odds of peace versus another escalation, a 
reminder that diplomacy in the Gulf remains hostage to mistrust, military 
threats and the world’s most critical energy chokepoint.

The economic stakes are becoming harder to ignore. Rapidan Energy Group warned 
that if the Strait of Hormuz remains disrupted through August, the fallout 
could rival the2008 Great Recession 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/hormuz-closure-threatens-recession-rivaling-2008-rapidan-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260522&utm_content=4759>
, with Brent crude potentially surging toward $130 a barrel and triggering a 
sharp hit to global demand. Even a faster reopening would leave oil inventories 
dangerously thin for months, amplifying fears that the world economy is heading 
into another painful mix of inflation, supply shocks and slowing growth.

Far from the Gulf, the consequences are hitting fishing communities across 
Southeast Asia. From Thailand to the Philippines, soaring diesel costs are 
forcing boatsto stay docked 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-05-21/soaring-fuel-costs-push-fishermen-in-southeast-asia-closer-to-the-brink?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260522&utm_content=4759>
, squeezing seafood supplies and pushing workers deeper into financial 
distress. Many fishermen say they are returning to sea not because the numbers 
make sense, but because staying ashore is even worse. What began as a conflict 
over Iran’s nuclear program is now rippling through coastal communities and 
dinner tables halfway across the world.
