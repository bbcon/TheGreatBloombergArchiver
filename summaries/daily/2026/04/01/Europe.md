European equity futures and the Stoxx Europe 600 rose approximately 2% Wednesday as risk appetite recovered on Trump's Iran comments, though underlying anxiety over the regional energy outlook persists. The EU is actively considering reactivating emergency energy measures last used after Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine, while the European Commission has proposed limited adjustments to the EU Emissions Trading System to cushion the impact of elevated energy costs on industry competitiveness — a modest concession that stops well short of structural reform.

Aviation is emerging as an acute pressure point. Lufthansa is preparing contingency plans to ground aircraft if demand deteriorates, with teams assigned to develop crisis scenarios of varying severity. Europe is expected to avoid jet fuel shortages in April, but market participants directly involved in aviation fuel supply warn the situation could deteriorate in May. UK factories are experiencing their most intense supply chain strain and sharpest input price pressures since the immediate aftermath of Russia's Ukraine invasion, according to the latest PMI survey.

On the corporate side, Meg O'Neill assumes the role of CEO at BP on Wednesday, becoming the first woman to lead a major oil company. She inherits a business already under strategic pressure, now operating in an environment of war-driven price elevation that provides near-term revenue support but deepens long-term questions about capital allocation and transition strategy. LVMH shares fell 28% in the first quarter — the worst start to a year in the company's history — with the luxury conglomerate increasingly serving as a proxy for global confidence. Germany's leading economic institutes cut their 2026 growth forecast to less than half their prior projection, and warned that potential growth may approach zero in the medium term due to demographic decline and falling hours worked, raising questions about whether the government's debt-financed stimulus program can prevent structural stagnation.

Greece received a notable upgrade from MSCI to developed-market status, effective during the May 2027 index review — a milestone in the country's recovery from its sovereign debt crisis, during which it was demoted in 2013.
