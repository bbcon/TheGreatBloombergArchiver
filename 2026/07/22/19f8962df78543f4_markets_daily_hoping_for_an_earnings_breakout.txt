`Robust and broad-basedтАЩ earnings

A week into the second-quarter reporting season, the tea leaves read well.

Investors who spent weeks watching US stocks go nowhere are pinning their 
hopes for a breakout on corporate earnings, a key pillar of the four-year bull 
run.

More S&P 500 companies are raising their outlooks than maintaining or cutting 
them

. The measure, known as profit guidance momentum, has climbed to a record in 
Bloomberg Intelligence data going back to 2011.

That gives some peace of mind to investors restless over war in the Middle 
East and the future of the AI trade at home. Add that to signs that investors 
had dialed back their stock exposure to levels where the slightest good news 
may force them back тАФ and some see a foundation for gains.

Profits for the S&P 500 companies are expected to rise 26% in the second 
quarter, among the best readings ever outside of recoveries from major 
recessions, BI data show. Companies in the MSCI Europe Index are on track to 
post a better-than-expected 12% jump in earnings.
