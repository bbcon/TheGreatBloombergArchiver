All About AI

This week’s National Association for Business Economics conference — one of 
the top annual US confabs for “the dismal science” — came on the heels of the 
Supreme Court’s tariff ruling, and in the midst of a leadership transition at 
theFederal Reserve 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/fed-s-bostic-says-doubts-over-independence-a-major-concern?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
.

But the big focus of discussion was all about the rapid rise of artificial 
intelligence 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-30/ai-s-takeover-of-entry-level-tasks-is-making-college-grads-job-hunt-harder?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
. After all, NABE President Gregory Daco said, “AI is the number one topic that 
every business leader is talking about today.”

“Whether you talk about trade or you talk about monetary policy, or you talk 
aboutproductivity growth 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-26/ai-coding-agents-like-claude-code-are-fueling-a-productivity-panic-in-tech?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
, or you talk about the fiscal landscape, you’re going to be including AI as 
part of the discussion — because it’s really going to be a key driver of 
economic activity,” said Daco, EY-Parthenon’s chief economist.

While many observers have been quick to play down 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/citrini-s-dystopian-ai-vision-draws-global-investor-criticism?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 last weekend’s alarmist — or at least alarming — Citrini Research hypothesis 
about AI walloping the job market, Fed Governor Lisa Cook presented one 
sobering scenario at NABE.

“We appear to be approaching the most significant reorganization of work in 
generations,” and for a time AI may displace more jobs than it creates, she 
said. During that transition, the Fedmay not be able to counter 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/cook-says-fed-may-not-be-able-to-counter-ai-driven-unemployment?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 rising unemployment throughrate 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/government-bonds-shrinking-appeal-has-cost-new-york-fed-says?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 cuts, Cook said. That’s because higher joblessness may not in that case be 
signaling “increased slack” in the economy, she said.
Lisa CookPhotographer: Graeme Sloan/Bloomberg
Speaking away from NABE, Richmond Fed President Tom Barkin said the benchmark 
rate is “not the answer to all these issues” raised by AI disruption to 
business and labor.

But all may not be lost for humanity. Nela Richardson, chief economist at ADP 
and NABE vice president, test drove, as it were, the term “Waymofication” of 
the job market.

True, Waymo, the robotaxi service 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-26/why-it-s-so-hard-to-make-a-reliable-self-driving-car?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
, involves the elimination of drivers’ jobs. But, new roles were created, with 
a team of professionals behind the scenes keeping the cars on the road, she 
said.

MIT Professor Daron Acemoglu offered a basketball analogy for how AI can 
complement the human. “The Chicago Bulls didn’t try to have Michael Jordan 
replace Scottie Pippen — they worked together. That’s why that was a 
championship team.”

Away from NABE, Kansas City Fed President Jeff Schmid on Wednesday observed 
that, as the US population ages and fewer people enter the job market, 
AI-driven productivity can be a handy supplement.

Lagarde, meanwhile, said on Thursday that the ECB is observing productivity 
gains — but will also be “extremely attentive 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-26/lagarde-vows-extremely-attentive-ecb-on-ai-driven-job-cuts?cmpid=BBD022626_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
” for any related job losses.

Need-to-Know Research

Kevin Warsh has been critical of the current communications practices of the 
Fed. So how might things change when — assuming he wins confirmation — he takes 
the helm? Deutsche Bank’s US economists, led by Matthew Luzzetti, took a stab 
at that on Wednesday.

A couple things that likely won’t happen: Cancelling press conferences by the 
chair after policy decisions, or muzzling reserve bank president speeches. The 
briefings offer “a powerful and frequent platform for him to put his stamp on 
the committee’s message” and it would be a missed opportunity to give them up, 
they wrote. And if Warsh were seen to be silencing colleagues, it could look 
like an attack on independence, making it harder to get a majority to back his 
views.

What could well change is the quarterly Summary of Economic Projections, 
including the anonymous “dot plot 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/explainers/the-dot-plot-explained-how-the-fed-forecasts-interest-rates>
” of each official’s anticipated rate setting for coming year-ends. Plausible 
revamps include abandoning individual projections in favor of just the “central 
tendency” of forecasts, and their range, Luzzetti and his colleagues wrote. 
Baseline projections could be complemented with alternative scenarios, 
“downgrading the importance” of median forecasts.

Rather than year-end projections, rolling 12- and 24-month horizons could be 
adopted, Deutsche Bank said. That would signal the “broad direction of travel” 
without seeming to offer explicit guidance, such as can happen in September for 
the December rate decision, they wrote.
