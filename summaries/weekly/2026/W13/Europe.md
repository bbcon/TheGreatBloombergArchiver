**Gilt Markets Under Sustained Pressure**
UK sovereign debt remained the most acute stress point among developed-economy bond markets. Ten-year gilt yields briefly breached 5% mid-week — levels not seen since before the Global Financial Crisis and exceeding even the Truss episode of autumn 2022 in duration if not initial velocity — before partially retracing following Trump's temporary delay of strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure. Two-year yields climbed to approximately 4.7% earlier in the week, their highest since February 2024. The driver this cycle is less directly fiscal and more about the Bank of England's constrained policy options: markets pivoted from pricing at least two 25-basis-point cuts by year-end to pricing at least two hikes, as inflation expectations surged in response to the oil shock. Bank of England Governor Bailey attended an emergency cabinet meeting convened by Prime Minister Starmer early in the week, underscoring the severity of the dual stress the UK faces.

**Growth Data Deteriorates**
The S&P Global Composite PMI for the eurozone fell to 50.5 in March from 51.9 in February — the slowest expansion since May 2025 — as energy-driven cost pressures and supply chain disruption weighed on both manufacturing and services. Input prices rose at the fastest pace since February 2023, and future output expectations posted their largest deterioration since Russia's invasion of Ukraine. Germany's composite reading declined more than expected; France recorded a third consecutive month of contraction in services. UK businesses reported near-stagnation, with factory input costs rising at the fastest pace in more than three decades. Italy is now preparing to cut its 2026 GDP forecast to as low as 0.5%; the OECD assessed the UK as facing the largest output hit among G7 nations this year.

**ECB's Measured Caution**
The ECB navigated the week more deliberately than the Bank of England. President Lagarde used the ECB Watchers Conference in Frankfurt to signal the bank will not act until it has "sufficient information on the size and persistence" of the energy shock, while making clear it will not be "paralyzed by hesitation" if a broader inflationary spiral materializes. Governing Council member Vujcic — set to become ECB vice president in June — warned that stagflation risk is increasing and that the bank must remain "very agile and vigilant," explicitly declining to provide rate guidance. Goldman Sachs Chief Economist Hatzius estimated that in a severely adverse oil scenario, euro area headline inflation could rise by nearly 3 percentage points over the next year — the largest projected impact among major economies — while cautioning that nonlinear gas supply shock risks may not be fully captured in central forecasts.

**Political Landscape Shifts**
European political developments were notable across several countries. In France, Socialist Emmanuel Grégoire won the Paris mayoral race, extending the left's hold on the capital, while Marine Le Pen's National Rally fell short in major cities — a result that may temper RN momentum ahead of the presidential election. In Germany, Chancellor Merz's CDU dislodged the Social Democrats from power in Rhineland-Palatinate after 35 years, complicating the SPD's position within the governing coalition. Italy's Prime Minister Meloni suffered a referendum defeat on judicial reform. Denmark held a general election shaped heavily by concerns over Trump's Greenland threats, polling toward a vote for stability. UK prediction markets assigned a 68% probability that Starmer exits office before year-end, with likeliest successors positioned to his left — a configuration unlikely to reassure gilt investors.

**Corporate and Energy Dynamics**
Italy's Poste Italiane made a €12.5 billion offer for full control of Telecom Italia, with Poste shares falling 7% on Monday and Telecom Italia rising 4.2%. Pernod Ricard entered preliminary discussions to acquire Brown-Forman, with Brown-Forman's Class B shares rising as much as 21% while Pernod fell 5.7% in Paris. Henkel agreed to acquire hair-care brand Olaplex for $1.4 billion, with Olaplex surging nearly 50%. Airfare disruption added a further consumer cost, with ticket prices on major Asia-Europe routes rising as much as 560% this month. Trump's envoy to the EU, Andrew Puzder, warned that American LNG could be redirected to other buyers if Brussels fails to accelerate trade deal progress — a statement that made explicit the energy leverage Washington now holds over a continent that had only recently diversified away from Russian gas.
