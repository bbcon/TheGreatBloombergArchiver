The war in Iran is increasingly turning into a fight over the price of oil 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-17?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
.

Iran is hitting infrastructure 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/iran-hits-uae-gas-field-as-trump-pushes-allies-to-help-on-hormuz?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 in the Persian Gulf, including setting a massive gas field ablaze, as the 
government continues with its strategy of restricting the output of oil to 
pressure the US. Donald Trump, meanwhile, continues tobadger other countries 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/trump-appeals-for-help-with-hormuz-threatens-more-kharg-strikes?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 to help force the Strait of Hormuz open as his administration seeks to limit 
the impact on energy prices — and inflation.

Brent crude rose to $104 a barrel on Tuesday, after retreating on Monday. With 
little clear solution to the ongoing conflict, including the throttling of 
energy supplies, attention will shift to how central banks react to this 
complex situation. Eighteen meet this week, including the Federal Reserve and 
European Central Bank. As Bloomberg Opinion’s John Authersdiscusses 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/newsletters/2026-03-17/iran-war-can-the-fed-and-other-central-banks-unpoke-the-bear?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 in his latest column, can they look through this war, and if not, what will 
they do about it?

The drone attack on the Shah field, close to the United Arab Emirates’ border 
with Saudi Arabia, marks the first time Iran has damaged an oil or gas upstream 
facility in the country during the war. Overnight, the UAEshut its airspace 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/uae-closes-airspace-as-precautionary-measure-wam-reports?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 as it faced several incoming drones and missiles. Authorities said air traffic 
operations had returned to normal by early Tuesday.

With Iran saying it’s not interested in ceasefire talks, and no sign that 
Trump will back down, pressure is likely to continue on its neighbors.

For the Gulf states, as long as Iran’s strategy remains “inflicting maximum 
economic disruption, then by virtue of being some of the biggest exporters of 
oil and gas in the world, they are going to find themselves in the firing 
line,” Torbjorn Soltvedt, lead Middle East analyst at Verisk Maplecroft, a 
political risk firm, told Bloomberg TV.

Trump meanwhile reiterated his appeals 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/trump-gets-chilly-response-from-allies-over-plea-for-hormuz-help?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 late Monday for other nations, includingthose in Europe 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/finland-s-stubb-cautions-nato-allies-to-heed-trump-s-hormuz-call?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
, to help reopen the chokepoint as pressure grows on his administration to 
bring down surging US gasoline prices and calm global bond markets. US diesel 
on Mondayrose above 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/us-diesel-tops-5-a-gallon-as-war-disrupts-fuel-supply-chains?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 $5 a gallon for the first time since December 2022. The question now is what 
will it take for this apparent stalemate to shift.—Richard Frost

What You Need to Know Today

The oil market shock is set to have a greater impact 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/goldman-says-oil-s-biggest-shock-to-hurt-refined-products-most?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 on products such as jet fuel and diesel than on crude, according to Goldman 
Sachs.

“Prices have rallied much more for many refined products than for crude,” 
Goldman analysts said. The severe disruptions seen in supplies of so-called 
medium-heavy crude pose the risk of lower production of diesel, jet fuel and 
fuel oil, they said.

While crude prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/stock-market-today-dow-s-p-live-updates-?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 have surged by more than 40% since the first attacks — with Brent topping $100 
a barrel — some products have rallied far more. In parts of Asia, fuel costs 
haveas much as doubled <bbg://news/stories/TBH2B9T96OSG>, with South Korea 
following China and Thailand in capping exports to protect local markets.

A Hong Kong-owned bulk carrier has just entered the Persian Gulf via the 
Strait of Hormuz, a rare occurrence at a time when only a handful of vessels 
are safely transiting the waterway, most of them with Iran’s approval.

The Jia Xiang Da, a small-sized bulk carrier, approached the chokepoint 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-17/hong-kong-vessel-makes-rare-hormuz-transit-into-persian-gulf?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 from the Gulf of Oman late Monday, and traversed westwards into the Persian 
Gulf, ship-tracking data compiled by Bloomberg News show. By Tuesday morning, 
the ship was seen near the Iranian coast, heading in the direction of Kish 
Island and signaling it is due to reach Iraq’s Umm Qasr port by Wednesday. 
Draft readings indicate that the vessel is empty.

Default rates in direct lending will climb to 8% as advances in artificial 
intelligencecontinue to disrupt 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/private-credit-default-rates-to-reach-8-morgan-stanley-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260317>
 the software industry, according to Morgan Stanley.

While AI disruption has yet to impact private credit fundamentals in a 
“material way,” elevated leverage and looming maturity walls within the 
software sector may push default rates near peak levels unseen since the 
pandemic, a team of analysts said in a note.

Global credit markets have come under pressure in recent months as investors 
assess how AI will dislocate the revenue streams of software companies. 
Alternative asset managers spent the last decade piling into software firms, 
drawn by predictable earnings and higher margins.

Beijing is said to be restricting Chinese companies incorporated overseas from 
seeking IPOs

 in Hong Kong. While stopping short of an outright ban, regulators have

registered outside China but which hold assets and businesses within it, said 
people familiar with the matter. Some companies have already been asked by the 
Chinese securities regulator to overhaul their structure before proceeding with 
Hong Kong listings.

Germany’s Savior Is Failing His ‘Last Shot’ Mission

Friedrich Merz is a remarkably unpopular chancellor of Germany. According to 
recent polling, less than a quarter of Germans have a positive opinion of him, 
and those numbers are dwindling fast. That shouldn’t surprise anyone, least of 
all Merz. He came to power less than a year ago on a manifesto promising 
“Political Change for Germany.” Since then, he’s avoided tackling crucial 
reforms. If he thinks that’s playing it safe, he’s mistaken. Germans voted for 
change and expect him to deliver.
