South Korea delivered a strong first-quarter GDP print of 1.7% quarter-on-quarter, driven by AI-related export demand that powered semiconductor revenues. SK Hynix reported a five-fold jump in quarterly profit and reaffirmed plans to increase capital spending, underscoring the degree to which global AI infrastructure investment is supporting Korean export volumes even as energy price pressures and US tariff uncertainty weigh on broader sentiment. Hyundai, by contrast, missed earnings estimates, citing US tariff disruption, Iran war-related supply chain strain, and cooling demand in key export markets.

Japan's cabinet this week approved legal changes allowing the country to export lethal military equipment — including missiles, fighter jets, and warships — for the first time since the post-war constitution enshrined pacifism. Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi is pursuing a strategic vision of greater defense self-sufficiency in response to rising threats from China and North Korea, with government funding earmarked for defense R&D and a deal to supply warships to Australia already concluded. China has issued warnings about revived Japanese militarism, while domestic polling shows 67% of Japanese respondents opposed to weapons exports — a political constraint that Takaichi appears willing to absorb given her current approval trajectory. Planned visits by Takaichi and her defense minister to Southeast Asian nations signal efforts to develop an early order pipeline.

In China, an unexpected deterioration in the labor market has extended to early-career workers, with AI adoption cited as a contributing factor. Tencent and Alibaba are in preliminary discussions to participate in a maiden financing round for DeepSeek, which is expanding into agentic AI applications. PwC agreed to pay HK$1.3 billion in fines and compensation to settle investigations into its auditing of China Evergrande, as the firm attempts to rebuild following client and staff departures triggered by an earlier record fine.

Malaysia's king confirmed the anti-corruption agency head will not have his term renewed, following Bloomberg reporting on alleged improprieties. In India, approximately 9 million names were removed from voter rolls in West Bengal two weeks before state elections began, with opposition parties alleging politically motivated targeting of Muslim and lower-income voters.
