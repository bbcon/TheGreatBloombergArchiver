Antidumping and countervailing duty investigations donтАЩt typically cause a 
political firestorm, but President Donald TrumpтАЩs trade war is pitting segments 
of American industry against each other in novel ways.

Back in January, Chicago-based Vantage Specialty Chemicals filed a petition 
<https://www.vantagegrp.com/en/vantage-specialty-chemicals-files-anti-dumping-countervailing-duties-petitions-seeking-relief-from-unfair-indonesian-and-malaysian-trade-practices/>
 proposing steep duties on imported vegetable-based fatty acids from Indonesia 
and Malaysia, arguing that those governments are providing unfair subsidies and 
harming US production.

тАЬWe have already seen plant closures and the loss of hundreds of American 
manufacturing jobs because of these deliberate and unfair actions,тАЭ Mike 
Waldron, senior vice president of corporate affairs, said in a press release.

A new group representing American farmers, manufacturers and downstream 
producers is pushing back on the proposal from Vantage, which produces 
chemicals derived from beef tallow and vegetable oils.

The Coalition for Affordable Ingredients argues 
<https://www.affordableingredients.org/> that duties on fatty acids derived 
from palm and other vegetable oils would increase consumer prices on soap, food 
and other goods.

тАЬOne small companyтАЩs petition could quietly raise costs on billions of dollars 
of everyday products тАФ soap, food, dairy nutrition тАФ and almost no one knows 
itтАЩs happening,тАЭ said John Gurley, a partner at ArentFox Schiff and counsel to 
the coalition, said in arelease 
<https://www.affordableingredients.org/post/american-manufacturers-and-farmers-form-coalition-to-oppose-sweeping-import-duties-on-essential-ingr>
.

The dispute sheds light on the tradeoffs the Trump administration faces when 
it picks tariff winners and losers. Expect more such flare-ups ahead, with the 
broader strategyset to change 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-03/us-proposes-broad-tariffs-of-at-least-10-citing-forced-labor?cmpid=BBD071426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260714&utm_content=6483>
 when TrumpтАЩs 10% global import taxes under Section 122 expire at the end of 
next week and he leans on other authorities to replace them.

Voters and Affordability

Preliminary decisions on the Commerce DepartmentтАЩs countervailing duty and 
antidumping investigations are expected from July to September. That means a 
determination may come just ahead of the November midterm elections, where the
cost-of-living crisis 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-11/warsh-and-us-inflation-will-set-tone-for-july-fed-decision?cmpid=BBD071426_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260714&utm_content=6483>
 has emerged as a top issue for voters.

It wouldnтАЩt be the first time an antidumping and countervailing duty case 
caught the attention of the administration. Just last month, Trump walked back 
duties on Moroccan fertilizer imports in an attempt to appease distressed 
farmers.

In a letter to Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, CEO of Progressive Dairy 
Solutions Matt Budine said that fatty acids from Indonesia and Malaysia are 
used in the diets of dairy cows in the US. Antidumping and countervailing 
duties would make it harder for herds to reach production and butterfat targets 
while maintaining their health, Budine argued, as there is no equivalent 
product made in the US.

тАЬThe dairy industry has not had an easy few years, buffeted by rising feed and 
input costs, herd consolidation, and thin margins that leave little room to 
absorb new shocks,тАЭ Budine said in the letter shared with Bloomberg News, 
urging the Commerce Department not to impose the duties.

Vantage Specialty Chemicals didnтАЩt respond to a Bloomberg request for comment 
on the letter.
