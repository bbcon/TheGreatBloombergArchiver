Moving targets

By Todd Woody <https://origin.www.bloomberg.com/authors/AVl1IPrK6vs/todd-woody>

As countries and consumers embrace renewable energy 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-26/war-oil-price-shock-sparks-new-interest-in-green-tech-around-the-world?cmpid=BBD040626_GREENDAILY&utm_campaign=greendaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260406>
 in the wake of the Iran war, skyrocketing gasoline prices in the US are 
fueling efforts to weaken state climate laws.

Renewable energy mandates to reduce greenhouse gas emissions generally have 
little impact on US gas prices, according to experts. But the growing pain at 
the pump has charged debates in California, Massachusetts, Michigan and New 
York over whether climate action is affordable as households face rising costs 
for electricity, groceries and housing.

тАЬThe war in Iran is driving up gas prices at the pump to the breaking point 
for too many New Yorkers,тАЭ New York Governor Kathy Hochul said in March as she 
proposed to delay regulations implementing the stateтАЩs 2019 climate law,

As Michigan lawmakers debate how to respond to gas prices exceeding $4 a 
gallon, Republicans have introduced legislation to repeal a 2023 law that 
requires utilities to obtain 100% of power from emission-free sources by 2040, 
a goal they blame for rising electricity bills.

Climate policy experts note that high electricity rates are in large part due
