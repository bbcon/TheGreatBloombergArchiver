increasing strain

The Russian leader’s best chance may now rest with Donald Trump.

The US president is pushing to end the war quickly, after 13 months of 
frustrating diplomacy that’sdrawn criticism

.

Trump and Volodymyr Zelenskiy spoke last night 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/zelenskiy-trump-discuss-next-steps-for-peace-talks-in-call?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
, before US and Ukrainian officials meet in Geneva today. The Ukrainian 
president said they agreed the next round of US-led negotiations with Russia in 
early March should “create an opportunity to move talks to the leaders’ level.”

A summit, in other words.

Putin has ruled out meeting with Zelenskiy until there’s a deal on the table 
to sign. Zelenskiy says it’s the only way to resolve outstanding disagreements.

While Russia seems incapable of a military breakthrough, Putin shows no sign 
of compromising on his demands for Ukrainian territory.

Yet he also can’t risk alienating Trump.

That gives Zelenskiy leverage to press his case for halting the war on 
existing positions, alongside complex arrangements for a demilitarized free 
economic zone in eastern Donetsk favored by the US.

The sides also still must resolve the future 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-17/russia-and-ukraine-start-talks-in-geneva-with-expectations-low?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 for Ukraine’s Russian-occupied Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant.

None of that is easy for Ukraine, the victim 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/eu-chief-says-90-billion-for-kyiv-to-come-one-way-or-another?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 in this war. And it doesn’t give Putin what he’s always demanded.

Trump is likely to be increasingly consumed by domestic issues as November’s 
midterm elections approach. Hebarely mentioned 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/state-of-the-union-2026-analysis?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 Ukraine in his State of the Union address.

That presents a dilemma for Putin.

Does he gamble that Russia’s army and economy can withstand 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/russian-offensive-hampered-by-war-losses-western-officials-say?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 another year or more of war, or opt toease the pressure 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-17/putin-s-war-in-ukraine-is-also-hitting-russians-harder-at-home?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
 and save face by cutting a deal now?— Anthony Halpin 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ASAcLGhGNAk/anthony-halpin>

And Finally

North Korean ties with the US have been in deep freeze since a high profile 
bout of diplomacy during Trump’s first term collapsed in 2019. Now, Kim Jong Un 
just wrapped up a seven-day meeting of the ruling party congress in Pyongyang 
with anoffer of sorts 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-26/north-korea-s-kim-vows-to-build-more-nukes-but-opens-door-to-us?cmpid=BBD022626_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260226>
: It may be possible to improve relations if Washington recognizes his country 
as a nuclear power. Mind you, Kim also outlined plans to bolster his weapons 
arsenal and vowed to build stronger intercontinental ballistic missiles, state 
media reported.
