Asian equity markets posted strong gains, with the Nikkei 225 rising 2.20% to 58,583 and the South Korean Kospi advancing 1.91% to 6,084. The session reflected a broader rotation dynamic that has defined 2026 year-to-date: investors are concentrating capital in Asian hardware manufacturers perceived as infrastructure beneficiaries of AI, while retreating from US-listed software and services names exposed to AI disruption risk. Taiwan Semiconductor is up 30% year-to-date, Samsung Electronics has surged 70%, and SK Hynix has gained 57%. Overseas investors purchased a net $2.77 billion of Taiwan equities on Tuesday, the largest single-day foreign inflow since December 2005, extending a six-day buying streak. South Korea's total equity market capitalization has risen to $3.76 trillion, surpassing France to rank ninth globally — having already overtaken Germany in late January.

German Chancellor Friedrich Merz arrived in Beijing on his first official visit since taking office last May, framing the trip around balanced engagement with a country that serves simultaneously as strategic rival and indispensable trading partner. "It would be a mistake for us to seek to decouple ourselves from China," Merz said before departure. The visit carries a note of economic asymmetry: Germany's trade dependence on China has deepened, German investment in China reached a four-year high in 2025, and Berlin is increasingly reliant on Chinese critical materials for industrial production. Merz will hold separate meetings with President Xi Jinping and Premier Li Qiang. Both sides signaled an appetite for improved relations, though trade frictions and mineral supply chains remain unresolved structural tensions.

Hong Kong's Financial Secretary Paul Chan delivered the city's annual budget, reporting a consolidated surplus of HK$2.9 billion — a material turnaround from the HK$67 billion deficit originally forecast — enabling pledges of technology spending and tax relief for over two million residents. Singapore banks drew $61 billion in new wealth inflows from Asian high-net-worth clients, underscoring the city-state's continued role as a regional wealth management hub.

China separately warned it will take "all necessary measures" if the US proceeds with fresh tariffs tied to an ongoing probe into a 2020 trade deal. Meanwhile, a record $112 billion discrepancy between Chinese reported export data and US Customs arrival figures for 2025 suggests that as much as a quarter of China-origin shipments may have bypassed US tariff controls — complicating both revenue collection and any analytical assessment of the bilateral trade relationship. South Korea's fertility rate rose for a second consecutive year, a rare positive signal for a country with the world's lowest birth rate.
