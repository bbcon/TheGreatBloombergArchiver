There’s no apparent end in sight to the US-Israeli war on Iran.

There’s not much for Donald Trump to cheer as regards the war on Iran, with 
growing signs that he underestimated the Islamic Republic’s resilience.

The two main developments of the past 24 hours will only add to US 
frustrations — and to the pressure on the president to end the carnage the 
conflict has unleashed across the Middle East and global markets.

First, Iran’s most senior clerics defied him 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-08/iran-elevates-khamenei-s-son-as-escalating-strikes-shake-region?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
 by picking Mojtaba Khamenei, the hardline son of the assassinated Ali 
Khamenei, asthe new supreme leader 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-08/iran-war-new-supreme-leader-mojtaba-khamenei-faces-trump-israel-dissent?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
.

His election suggests no shift 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/who-s-who-in-iran-guide-to-the-islamic-republic-s-power-players?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
 in Iran’s approach to the conflict, let alone any bending to Trump’s will. 
Trump told Fox News he is “not happy” with the selection.

Barely an hour after the announcement at the Asian open, there was chaos in 
the oil market 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-08/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-march-9?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
. Brent surged almost 30% to nearly $120 a barrel, with the effective closure 
of the Strait of Hormuz forcing Iraq, the United Arab Emirates and Kuwait to 
cut production.

Oil pared its gains after reports that the Group of Seven nations will look at 
releasing fuel reserves 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-09/g-7-to-discuss-joint-emergency-oil-reserves-release-ft-says?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
, but it remains above $100.

Trump insists oil’s rise won’t last long and is a “small price to pay.” But 
with his party facing tricky midterm elections and with gasoline pump costs in 
the US already jumping last week, it’s more bad news.

Overnight, the US announced the death of a seventh soldier since the conflict 
erupted with US-Israeli strikes on Iran on Feb. 28.

The toll in Iran is more than 1,300, officials there say. And Arab Gulf 
states, as well as Israel, are still being targeted daily by Iranian drones and 
missiles.

For now, the war is unrelenting. Whatever diplomatic efforts are under way to 
de-escalate seem to be going nowhere. — Paul Wallace 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/authors/ASAuUIp54ZQ/paul-wallace>

And Finally

Soaring energy prices and tighter margins are prompting plant closures in 
Europe’s chemical industry, with investments in the sector tumbling. The 
challenges are set to intensify due to the US and Israeli war with Iran, with 
chemical producers set to pay about €3 billion ($3.5 billion) more annually 
just for natural gas if the post-attack price surge persists, according to one 
estimate. Because of its history and structure, the Leuna chemical park in 
eastern Germany is particularly exposed, making ita test case 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2026-03-09/iran-war-is-the-latest-blow-for-europe-s-battered-chemical-industry?cmpid=BBD030926_politics&utm_campaign=bop&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260309>
 for an economy reliant on energy and raw-material imports.
