Five weeks into the war, the US stock market has settled into a predictable 
pattern. It starts the week on a strong note, drifts sideways toward the middle 
and then,like clockwork every Thursday and Friday, collapses 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-01/stocks-enter-danger-zone-late-week-trading-has-crushed-s-p-500?cmpid=040226_marketsdaily&utm_campaign=marketsdaily&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260402>

Since the conflict began, the S&P 500 has posted cumulative gains over the 
first three days of the week but cratered 9%, all told, on Thursdays and 
Fridays.

The rationale, experts say, is simple. Investors canтАЩt trade on the weekend, 
and a lot can happen in that time, especially given Donald TrumpтАЩs affinity for
