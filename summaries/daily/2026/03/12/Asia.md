China's footprint across the current crisis is expanding on multiple dimensions simultaneously. Beijing has adopted legislation cementing Xi Jinping's push to assimilate ethnic minorities, a further departure from Beijing's long-standing practice of affording such groups nominal autonomy. Separately, Chinese oil refiners have begun canceling agreed refined-fuel export cargoes — including gasoline and diesel — as Beijing tightens controls to manage the domestic impact of the Iran conflict. The People's Liberation Army resumed aircraft incursions into sensitive airspace near Taiwan following a brief unexplained pause, with Xi having told Trump that Beijing would never permit Taiwan's separation. The combination of economic self-insulation, military signaling, and diplomatic positioning reflects a posture of strategic opportunism.

Hong Kong regulators raided the offices of two leading Chinese brokerages and arrested eight individuals on allegations of insider dealing and corruption — a notable enforcement action in a market already navigating external capital-flow pressures. Japan's Nikkei 225 declined 1.04% as global risk sentiment deteriorated, and more than a third of surveyed economists now expect the Bank of Japan to raise rates in April, a view reinforced by the global bond selloff that is pushing yields higher across the Asia-Pacific. Australian officials are seen potentially hiking rates next week. Turkey's central bank paused its rate-cutting cycle. India is in direct negotiations with Iran to secure safe passage for more than twenty tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, an attempt to protect energy supply that also illustrates New Delhi's continued transactional engagement with Tehran.

South Korea's parliament approved the special legislation required to implement its $350 billion US investment pledge, even as Washington simultaneously launched a trade investigation targeting Seoul. The juxtaposition captures the difficulty of navigating the current US policy environment, where bilateral deal-making and adversarial trade actions can proceed on parallel tracks.
