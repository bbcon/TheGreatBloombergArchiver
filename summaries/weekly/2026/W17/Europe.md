**UK Political Crisis**

Prime Minister Keir Starmer faced an increasingly difficult political environment across the week, centered on the Peter Mandelson vetting controversy. Former senior official Olly Robbins testified before a public inquiry on Tuesday that Downing Street was "dismissive" of concerns raised during Mandelson's vetting process, with his documented ties to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein at the center of the controversy. Starmer's decision to dismiss the Foreign Office's permanent secretary triggered what amounts to an open revolt by senior civil servants. Calls for resignation are growing ahead of local elections next month, predicted to be damaging for Labour. London Mayor Sadiq Khan offered qualified support while arguing that political stability remains a UK competitive asset. The erosion appears incremental rather than acute, but the trajectory is concerning for a government that entered office promising institutional restoration. The UK also concluded a £660 million deal with France targeting cross-Channel migration flows.

**UK Macro and Fiscal**

UK inflation accelerated in March, with motor fuel prices rising 9% month-on-month — the largest surge since Russia's invasion of Ukraine — on the back of Hormuz-driven energy costs, lifting CPI to 3.3%. Research published this week suggests the Iran conflict could erase most of Chancellor Rachel Reeves's fiscal buffer, though government borrowing fell to a three-year low in the near term, providing some breathing room. UK businesses stepped up job cuts in March, signaling fresh caution in the labour market as the war weighs on corporate confidence. The ECB, for its part, noted the conflict's "double uncertainty" — simultaneously threatening energy supply and inflation — complicates monetary policy deliberations, with President Lagarde effectively arguing for continued data dependency that delays further rate action.

**Germany and the Eurozone**

Germany's political and economic environment deteriorated visibly during the week. Chancellor Friedrich Merz's approval rating has fallen to deeply negative territory, with 80% of respondents expressing disapproval. Measures to cushion energy price impacts — including a €1,000 worker payment intended to be voluntarily funded by employers, with the federal government itself declining to participate — were widely criticized as inadequate. The ZEW investor expectations index collapsed to -17.2 in April from -0.5 in March, its lowest level in more than three years and well below the consensus estimate of -5.8. The Bundesbank estimated slight first-quarter GDP expansion, but preliminary April PMI data showed the eurozone economy unexpectedly contracting for the first time since late 2024, driven by a steep services decline as war-driven energy costs and eroding consumer confidence fed through. European natural gas prices rose as much as 11% on Monday. Analysts at TS Lombard raised the risk of a "Trichet 2.0" scenario in which the ECB tightens into a weakening economy.

**European Corporate and Geopolitical Developments**

The UniCredit-Commerzbank takeover battle entered a decisive phase: UniCredit CEO Andrea Orcel and Commerzbank counterpart Bettina Orlopp failed to find common ground despite Orcel having accumulated just under 30% of Commerzbank and tabling a €35 billion offer. The German government continues to back resistance, though the sustainability of that defense is sharpening as rate tailwinds fade. Europe's airline sector faces an acute jet fuel crunch, with jet fuel equivalent at $196 per barrel prompting Lufthansa to cancel 20,000 short-haul flights, KLM to eliminate 80 return services, and EasyJet to warn of a potential first-half loss. SAP gained 7.3% in Frankfurt on strong cloud revenue, while Siemens Energy rose 4.9% after raising its annual outlook. The EU finalized its €90 billion loan to Ukraine after Hungary lifted its veto, and the Europe-wide ban on Russian LNG imports took effect Saturday — a poorly timed development given current energy market stress. Bulgaria's presidential election produced a victory for former President Rumen Radev, who opposes sanctions on Russia and military aid to Ukraine, adding a complicating element to European political cohesion.
