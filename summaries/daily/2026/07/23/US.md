**Corporate Earnings**
Tesla's second-quarter results delivered the session's starkest disappointment: net profit fell 37% below consensus as spending on AI and robotics initiatives surged to $5.8 billion, producing the company's first cash burn in two years. Shares fell 6.1% premarket. Alphabet presented a more ambiguous picture — the Google parent beat revenue expectations on the strength of Cloud, but the announcement of capital expenditures potentially exceeding $200 billion this year reignited concern about returns on AI investment. ServiceNow gained 7.8% on better-than-expected bookings, while Texas Instruments fell 5.2% on a disappointing sales outlook.

**Rates and Fiscal**
The 30-year Treasury yield at 5.16% reflects a structural shift in long-duration demand: over $500 billion in AI-linked corporate issuance is competing directly with Treasuries for the same pool of pension and insurance buyers. Separately, Trump administration officials are privately exploring whether a review of the SVB failure could provide legal grounds to remove Fed Governor Michael Barr, escalating tensions around Federal Reserve independence.
