US

  • ISM Manufacturing PMI rose to its highest level in four years in May, beating consensus and signaling a meaningful acceleration in factory activity, with new orders and production both firming.
  • Alphabet announced an $80 billion equity offering — including a $10 billion Berkshire Hathaway investment — to fund AI capital expenditure, representing one of the largest equity raises in history for a public company.

Manufacturing and Economic Momentum

May's ISM Manufacturing PMI printed above the expansion threshold at its strongest reading in four years, driven by firmer new orders and production. The White House cited this as validation for its industrial policy, simultaneously announcing targeted reductions in steel and aluminum tariffs — cutting rates on select agricultural and industrial goods to 15% from 25% — to incentivize domestic investment. However, the same ISM report showed manufacturing employment has remained in contraction since September 2023, illustrating a familiar tension: output is recovering while labor market gains in the sector remain elusive. Non-farm payrolls and CPI data for May, due imminently, will be pivotal in determining whether economic momentum is broad-based or concentrated.

AI Capital Markets

The AI fundraising cycle has reached a scale that is beginning to test conventional capital market assumptions. Alphabet's $80 billion equity offering is exceptional for an established public company; Goldman Sachs estimates corporate buybacks will reach $1.3 trillion this year, providing some offset to the estimated $675 billion in new equity supply from IPOs and follow-ons. Near-term dilution risk appears manageable, though history suggests lockup expiries in 2027–2028 could generate a more significant supply overhang. Marvell Technology surged 26% in premarket trading after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang's endorsement, and Hewlett Packard Enterprise rallied 24% on a bullish AI-driven sales outlook.

Federal Reserve

A Brookings Institution paper by Christina and David Romer offered a nuanced assessment of the Powell Fed, praising its institutional independence while warning that cutting rates since 2024 — with inflation persistently above 2% and unemployment near historic lows — risks losing sight of the broader inflation picture. With 30-year Treasury yields having recently topped 5% and a strong ISM print arriving, the risk of renewed upward pressure on rates is real. ---

Inflation and Capital Flows

South Korea's CPI accelerated to a two-year high, complicating the Bank of Korea's policy calculus at a moment when global rate paths remain divergent. The print appears to reflect both energy pass-through — a direct consequence of the Strait of Hormuz disruption — and underlying demand pressures. Separately, the scale of foreign equity outflows from India is notable: global funds have reversed a decade's worth of accumulated inflows, suggesting a combination of valuation concerns, currency risk, and competition from AI-linked US equities that are attracting capital globally.

China Technology and AI

Chinese defense-affiliated universities seeking Nvidia H200 chips — including Beihang University and Northwestern Polytechnical University, both Commerce Department blacklist entities — underscore the persistent tension between US export controls and China's military-civil fusion strategy. Tencent's rally on WeChat AI agent reports reflects investor optimism around China's domestic AI commercialization, even as the country remains constrained in accessing frontier US semiconductor technology. SK Hynix announced plans to double wafer capacity to ease memory chip supply tightness, a move that reflects the extraordinary demand being generated by AI infrastructure globally.

  • UniCredit secured commitments taking its Commerzbank stake above 30%, adding significant momentum to its €38.6 billion acquisition bid for the German lender.

Inflation and ECB Policy

Eurozone inflation breaching 3% year-on-year is the most consequential European data point of the period. The acceleration — driven in meaningful part by energy costs linked to the Strait of Hormuz disruption — moves the ECB into territory where it has limited flexibility to remain accommodative. Market pricing has shifted decisively toward a rate hike at next week's meeting. The European Commission is reportedly considering granting member states fiscal flexibility to absorb elevated energy costs, creating a potential tension between tighter monetary policy and looser fiscal conditions.

UniCredit-Commerzbank

UniCredit crossing the 30% ownership threshold in Commerzbank is a structural development in European banking consolidation. The move effectively forces a formal takeover offer process and increases pressure on Commerzbank's board and German regulators to engage substantively with the €38.6 billion bid. Cross-border European bank mergers have historically faced political resistance in Germany, and the path to completion remains uncertain, but the momentum shift is real.

Chile

  • Oil steadied near $94/bbl (Brent) after its largest single-day gain in a month, with price action oscillating on conflicting signals from Washington and Tehran about the status of peace negotiations.

Strait of Hormuz and Energy

The decision by QatarEnergy and ADNOC — together responsible for roughly one-fifth of global LNG exports in 2025 — to adopt shadow fleet navigation tactics marks a qualitative escalation in the global energy disruption stemming from the Iran conflict. While some shipments are moving again, the volume remains limited, and the practice introduces new counterparty, regulatory, and insurance risks into LNG markets that previously operated with near-complete transparency. HSBC's warning about commodity "tipping points" reflects genuine concern: the longer the strait remains effectively closed, the greater the risk that inventory drawdowns in oil, gas, and derivative commodities reach levels that are difficult to reverse quickly even after a diplomatic resolution.

Geopolitics

US-Iran peace talks remain in a fragile and uncertain state, with Tehran threatening to suspend negotiations over Israeli military activity in Lebanon while Washington asserted talks were continuing "at a rapid pace." The contradictory signals from all parties — including divergent accounts from Trump and Netanyahu of a bilateral call — suggest the diplomatic process is not close to resolution. Russia's war in Ukraine is simultaneously creating fiscal strain in Moscow, with senior Russian finance officials warning Putin that defense spending is on an unaffordable trajectory, though there is no current evidence this has altered Putin's strategic calculus.

AI Infrastructure and Energy

The intersection of AI capital expenditure and energy demand is emerging as a structural theme. Data centers are projected to require 2 million tons of cement through 2030 in the US alone, and approximately 30% of power input is currently lost to cooling and transmission inefficiency. Microsoft is reportedly reconsidering clean energy commitments under AI buildout pressure. The scale of AI-driven electricity demand — with next-generation chip racks approaching 1 megawatt each — is forcing a fundamental redesign of data center architecture and raising legitimate questions about the compatibility of AI expansion timelines with carbon commitments.
