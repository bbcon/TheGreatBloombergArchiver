Donald Trump is stepping up calls 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/trump-leaves-allies-and-foes-guessing-on-his-endgame-for-iran?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 for other nations, especially European allies, to aid the US in reopening the 
Strait of Hormuz. He’s also threatened to delay his summit with Xi Jinping if 
China resistssending help 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/trump-floats-delaying-xi-summit-if-no-help-for-hormuz-ft-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
.

“It’s only appropriate that people who are the beneficiaries of the Strait 
will help to make sure that nothing bad happens there,” Trump saidin an 
interview <https://www.ft.com/content/1ca6d121-760b-4ec5-b6ad-514fdaa94873> 
with the Financial Times. “If there’s no response or if it’s a negative 
response I think it will be very bad for the future of NATO.”

Trump stressed China’s dependence on oil from the Middle East, adding that his 
trip to Beijing slated for the end of this month would be too late.

He subsequently told reporters on Air Force One that it would be interesting 
“to see what country wouldn’t help with a very small endeavor” to get oil 
tankers flowing through the critical waterway. Trump also took direct aim at 
the UK prime minister, saying he told Keir Starmer that the US “will remember” 
if it gets support for the war or not.

His threats come as the war continues with no obvious end in sight. Israel has 
expanded operations in Lebanon, while Iraqi militias have signaled a new phase 
of attacks on US and other foreign targets. Oil prices areclimbing higher 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-15/oil-supplies-in-focus-ahead-of-stock-bond-open-markets-wrap?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
, with Brent crude trading above $105 a barrel. A top aide to Trump said the 
Pentagon estimates the Iran war would take between four and six weeks.

The incentives for other countries to assist the US in forcing open the Strait 
of Hormuz are low, especially after Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi 
said the coastal corridor was only shut to ships from “enemies.” Two tankers 
carrying liquefied petroleum gas to Indiasailed through 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/two-lpg-ships-sail-through-hormuz-on-way-to-shortage-hit-india?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 the strait — a route that normally handles about a fifth of the world’s oil 
supplies.

So far, signs are the US president will be rebuffed. Australia ruled out 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/australia-s-role-in-iran-war-to-remain-defensive-minister-says?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 deploying naval vessels, while Japan’s defense minister said the nation 
currentlyhas no plans 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/japan-defense-minister-says-no-plans-to-send-ships-to-hormuz?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 to send warships. Chinese officials have so far condemned Trump’s war on Iran 
and would be unlikely to send ships, in part because some carriers bound for 
China still appear to be getting through. European allies will be wondering 
about Trump’s demands just months after hethreatened to annex 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-01-22/what-trump-s-greenland-retreat-means-for-investors-allies?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 Greenland.

Iran denied Trump’s assertion that it wants ceasefire talks, launching fresh 
attacks 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-16/iran-denies-it-wants-truce-as-dubai-airport-and-oil-port-damaged?cmpid=eveapac&utm_campaign=eveapac&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260316>
 across the Persian Gulf and forcing a suspension of flights at Dubai’s main 
airport.

The United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia reported drone and missile attacks 
overnight into Monday. Dubai halted flights at its main airport following a 
fire at a fuel tank that it said was caused by an Iranian drone. It announced a 
gradual resumption a few hours later, though Emirates said some of its 
scheduled flights for the day would be canceled.

The key UAE oil-export port of Fujairah was hit again on Monday, following a 
strike on Saturday that forced it to suspend some shipments for about a day. In 
Abu Dhabi, a Palestinian civilian was killed because of a missile falling on a 
car, authorities in the UAE capital said.

Trump said over the weekend that Iran is ready to make a deal to end the war, 
but the US wants better terms, including a commitment by Tehran to abandon 
nuclear activities. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi denied seeking 
talks or a ceasefire with the US.
