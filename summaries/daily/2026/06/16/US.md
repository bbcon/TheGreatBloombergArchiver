**Industrial Production**
US factory output was little changed in May after an upwardly revised 0.7% advance in April, with total industrial production rising only 0.1%. The Federal Reserve data suggests supply chain disruptions linked to the Hormuz closure, combined with elevated input costs from tariffs, are beginning to restrain manufacturing activity — a meaningful deceleration after four consecutive months of gains.

**Monetary Policy**
Markets await Warsh's debut press conference Wednesday. Futures traders made no significant moves on rate expectations, with the Fed still priced for a hike by year-end. The more consequential question is structural: Warsh has signaled a preference for curtailing the dot plot and press conferences, reverting toward Greenspan-era opacity. Goldman Sachs research estimates that the post-2000 shift toward transparency reduced the 10-year term premium by roughly 5 basis points — gains that a communication rollback could partially reverse, introducing greater intraday volatility to rates markets.

**Equity and Corporate**
The S&P 500 paused Tuesday as traders assessed Hormuz deal durability. Nvidia received $85 billion in orders for its $25 billion investment-grade bond sale, underscoring sustained institutional demand for AI-adjacent credit exposure.
