• S&P 500 capped its best quarter since 2020, gaining 9.6% YTD, while the SOX semiconductor index outperformed dramatically; S&P 500 futures are down 0.3% to start Q3.
• JOLTS data showed job vacancies returning to pre-pandemic peaks, a data point that may reinforce the Fed's cautious posture on rate cuts.
• Bitcoin fell to a 21-month low, down 33.3% for H1, as Fed hawkishness eroded the "debasement trade"; gold dropped 14% in Q2, its worst quarterly performance since 2013.
• Trump disclosed at least $1.4 billion in 2025 crypto earnings, reigniting concerns over presidential conflicts of interest.

**Equity Markets**
Markets entered Q3 on a cautious footing, with attention focused on Fed Chair Kevin Warsh speaking at the ECB's Sintra conference. The broader S&P 500 finished H1 up 9.6%, but beneath the surface the Magnificent 7 declined 1.9% YTD as investors rotated from hyperscale AI spenders toward semiconductor hardware beneficiaries. Alphabet, despite a stock that has more than doubled over the past year and a $4.3 trillion market cap, shed 6% in June alone — a consequence of its $85 billion equity raise and a series of high-profile AI researcher departures. Nike fell 4.3% in premarket after management flagged elevated consumer anxiety and offered no near-term improvement in the demand outlook. Constellation Brands rose 3.2% on an earnings beat.

**Monetary Policy and Hard Assets**
Gold opened Q3 below $3,980, extending a 14% Q2 decline — its worst since 2013 — as speculation that Warsh's Fed may hike rates weighs on non-yielding metals. A technical "death cross" has formed on gold charts, adding downside momentum. Bitcoin stabilized near $58,700 after hitting a 21-month low, with concerns that Strategy Inc. may reduce purchases amplifying rate-driven selling pressure.
