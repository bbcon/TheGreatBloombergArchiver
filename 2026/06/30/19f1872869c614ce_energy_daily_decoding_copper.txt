Welcome to our guide to the commodities driving the global economy. Today, 
Europe Metals and Mining Team Leader Mark Burton takes a look at the copper 
market as traders again attempt to anticipate the Trump administration’s plans 
for tariffs.

More than a year after first floating the idea, US President Donald Trump 
could decide at any moment whether to impose import tariffs on copper.

Traders have learned to expect the unexpected.

Trump has frequently rocked the copper market since returning to power, 
starting with theshock announcement 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-01-27/trump-vows-near-future-tariffs-calls-deepseek-progress-good?cmpid=BBBXT063026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260630&utm_content=6032>
 in January last year that he was thinking about introducing tariffs on the 
metal.

Six months later, prices in the US 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-30/us-copper-tumbles-as-trump-s-tariff-excludes-refined-metal?cmpid=BBBXT063026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260630&utm_content=6032>
 crashed when he decided tolimit the levies 
<https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/07/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-takes-action-to-address-the-threat-to-national-security-from-imports-of-copper/>
 to semi-finished products, sparing the refined metal that’s traded on 
exchanges in New York, London and Shanghai.

Adding to the turmoil, that decision came with the caveat that he might expand 
tariffs in the future, once he’d reviewed a Commerce Department report on the 
market that’s due on his desk today.
US President Donald Trump at the White House on Feb. 25, 2025, when he signed 
an executive action directing the Commerce Department to examine possible 
copper tariffs.Photographer: Bloomberg
Trump’s shifting stance on tariffs has been a blessing and a curse for 
traders, with many of the top merchants makingmassive profits 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-07-11/trump-s-copper-tariffs-deadline-marks-end-of-once-in-a-generation-trade?cmpid=BBBXT063026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260630&utm_content=6032>
 shipping copper to the US in advance of potential levies.

Meanwhile, traders in futures markets were so badly caught out by last year’s 
decision not to impose blanket tariffs that Goldman Sachs Group Inc.’s sales 
desk sent out amea culpa 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-01/goldman-told-clients-to-go-long-copper-a-day-before-price-plunge?cmpid=BBBXT063026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260630&utm_content=6032>
 after advising clients to bet on a surge in prices.

Now, traders and investors are again confronting the hazardous futility of 
trying to anticipate the president’s next move. They’re scouring the market for 
intel on the Commerce Department’s recommendations, but they also know there’s 
no guarantee Trump will take the advice.

Many have been gaming out the likely outcomes, and some expect him to preserve 
the status quo bypledging to re-examine the issue again 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-06-01/trump-s-choice-on-copper-tariffs-may-keep-metal-traders-guessing?cmpid=BBBXT063026_ENERGY&utm_campaign=energy&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260630&utm_content=6032>
 in the future. But none are ruling out the possibility that he’ll finally make 
good on his pledge to put tariffs in place.

—Mark Burton, Bloomberg News

BNEF today

Latin America’s battery-storage market is entering a new phase of growth. 
Annual additions are forecast to rise to more than 5 gigawatt-hours by 2028 
from 817 megawatt-hours in 2024, according to BloombergNEF. Chile remains the 
region’s largest energy-storage market amid a rapid buildout of solar power. 
Growth is increasingly diversifying, however, as Brazil and Mexico expand 
capacity.
