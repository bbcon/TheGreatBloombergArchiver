**Strait of Hormuz: Unresolved**

The US-Iran ceasefire is holding, but the Strait of Hormuz remains closed pending a broader agreement. President Trump characterized talks as progressing while Secretary Rubio cautioned that closure of a comprehensive deal could take several additional days. The prolonged uncertainty continues to weigh on energy-importing economies, with elevated oil prices feeding inflationary pressures across emerging markets. The four largest MSCI EM constituents — China, South Korea, India, and Taiwan — are each among the economies most exposed to an extended Strait disruption, illustrating the systemic nature of the risk.

**Emerging Market Fragility Beneath the Surface**

Emerging market assets have broadly stabilized since the initial shock of the Iran conflict, and capital flows to EM bond markets have turned modestly positive. However, this resilience is heavily concentrated: Oxford Economics analysis suggests that stripping out the three largest AI proxies leaves the MSCI EM index essentially flat relative to its post-Liberation Day trough. Political risk has re-emerged as a concern, with Indonesia's Prabowo Subianto drawing scrutiny for authoritarian policy moves, Turkey's opposition leader facing a court-ordered ouster, and Colombia heading into a first-round presidential vote with a leftist candidate polling as frontrunner. Oxford Economics' quarterly EM survey recorded its sharpest deterioration in conditions since late 2022, with growth risks skewed to the downside and inflation expected to remain elevated.

**Russia-Ukraine and Global Energy**

Russia's escalation of ballistic missile attacks on Kyiv, combined with reports that Moscow is considering restricting diesel and jet fuel exports in response to Ukrainian drone strikes on refinery infrastructure, introduces an additional layer of complexity to global energy markets. Canada's agreement to supply Germany with LNG from a planned west-coast facility represents a meaningful step toward European energy diversification, though the facility's timeline leaves near-term supply balances unaffected.
