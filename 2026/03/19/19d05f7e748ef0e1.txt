Tens of Thousands of Seafarers Are Caught in Persian Gulf Crossfire
Tens of thousands of seafarers are stranded in the Persian Gulf amid ship 
attacks

<https://sli.bloomberg.com/click?s=1149074&li=17345093&m=11b724c487f61753eb1a7a4a5f3ef261&p=03192026&lctg=6689f0ec-2ca1-4687-9972-bdcb0c2dfbdf&stpe=default>
Iran’s blockade of the Strait of Hormuz has caught a fleet of stranded tankers 
and 40,000 sailors in the crossfire of drones and electronic warfare.

According to today’s Big Take 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-strait-hormuz-iran-blockade-ships-stranded-gps-jamming/?cmpid=BBD031926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
, ship officers who spoke to Bloomberg on condition of anonymity described the 
hardships seafarers face stuck on vessels on either side of the strait, which 
has been almost entirely shut since the US and Israel began bombing Iran in 
late February.

Half of them, according to the International Maritime Employers’ Council, are 
effectively trapped in the Gulf. They face the constant threat of attacks, and 
have to navigate in a fog of electronic warfare — which means they are often 
blind to the position of other vessels.

The dangers of crossing the strait were laid bare 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-12/oman-evacuates-vessels-from-oil-terminal-in-precautionary-move?cmpid=BBD031926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 last week, when a Thai-flagged vessel, the Mayuree Naree, was struck by a 
projectile. Three crew remain missing, and are believed to be trapped in the 
ship’s engine room.

Hazard Pay

Hormuz, the Gulf of Oman and Persian Gulf have been declared a “Warlike 
Operations Area” by the International Transport Workers’ Federation and the 
Joint Negotiating Group, a collection of unions and employers.

That means that seafarers operating there are granted additional benefits, 
including higher pay, and can decide to leave their ship rather than enter a 
danger zone — although not all vessels come under these agreements.
 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-strait-hormuz-iran-blockade-ships-stranded-gps-jamming/>
Meanwhile in Washington, Trump temporarily waived a century-old shipping 
mandate to lower the cost of transporting oil, gas and other commodities around 
the US.

The waiver of the Jones Act 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-18/trump-waives-us-shipping-law-for-oil-and-gas-amid-iran-war?cmpid=BBD031926_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260319>
 authorizes foreign-flagged vessels to transport a range of commodities between 
US ports for the next 60 days.

The waiver exempts requirements that cargo carried between US ports must be 
transported on US-flagged, -built and -owned ships, allowing foreign vessels to 
temporarily ship several products, including coal, crude oil, and natural gas.
