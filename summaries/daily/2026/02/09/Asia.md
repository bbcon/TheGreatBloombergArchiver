# Asia-Pacific Daily Briefing — February 9, 2026

**Japan: Takaichi's Landslide Reshapes the Outlook**

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's snap election gamble produced the Liberal Democratic Party's largest postwar single-party victory, delivering a two-thirds lower-house supermajority — an outcome prediction markets had priced at roughly even odds just two weeks ago. The Nikkei 225 surged nearly 4% to a record high, with investors pricing in fiscal stimulus, accelerated corporate governance reform, and the prospect of multi-year policy continuity. The yen and JGBs were notably calmer than the January rout had suggested possible, helped by Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama's assurance that a proposed two-year consumption tax cut on food would not require additional bond issuance. Still, the yen drifted to 157.76 against the dollar, with 160 widely viewed as the intervention threshold. The central tension remains unresolved: Takaichi's fiscal expansion sits awkwardly against Japan's ~230% debt-to-GDP burden, and her well-documented opposition to BOJ rate hikes complicates the inflation response. Evercore ISI frames the risk as a "fat right tail" — a Japanese analogue to the Truss episode — should political authority override fiscal restraint.

**China: Treasury Guidance and Lunar New Year Liquidity**

Chinese regulators have verbally instructed major banks to limit US Treasury purchases and trim concentrated positions, citing volatility and concentration risk. The directive excludes state holdings, and markets shrugged — 10-year yields rose only two basis points — reflecting that Chinese diversification has been gradual and well-absorbed by other foreign buyers. Separately, the PBOC injected $86.4 billion via 14-day repos, with Industrial Securities forecasting over $500 billion in liquidity provision ahead of Sunday's holiday. The PBOC also extended its gold-buying streak to 15 months. In Hong Kong, the 20-year sentence handed to Jimmy Lai under the national security law will test Western capitals' appetite to press Beijing while pursuing stabilization.

**India and Southeast Asia**

Trump formally rescinded the additional 25% tariff on Indian goods tied to Russian oil purchases, cementing terms of last week's trade deal; the Nifty 50 gained 3.5% on the week. The RBI held at 5.25%, citing reduced external uncertainty. Gavekal cautions the deal is necessary but not sufficient given MSCI India earnings growth of 8.3% trails regional peers materially. In Thailand, the Bhumjaithai Party's strong showing — tracking 191 seats — lifted the SET Index as much as 4% and strengthened the baht over 1%, on expectations of policy continuity under PM Anutin Charnvirakul.
