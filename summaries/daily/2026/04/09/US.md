The dominant US market story on April 9 remains the aftermath of the US-Iran ceasefire, though the initial euphoria is already giving way to more measured assessment. Equities rallied sharply on Wednesday — S&P 500 futures have since eased, trading around 6,800 — while the rates market has not followed suit. Fed funds futures remain priced for zero cuts in 2026, a stark reversal from the two reductions that had been fully priced before hostilities began. Swaps markets now imply inflation above 3% over the next twelve months, against sub-2.25% at the start of the year, reflecting the accumulated energy shock rather than any geopolitical resolution. A growing number of Fed officials have reportedly flagged the risk that the Iran war stokes prices further, and with February core PCE data due, the inflation picture is unlikely to clarify in the near term.

Earnings season arrives at an unusually fraught moment. FactSet projects S&P 500 earnings growth of roughly 13% year-on-year, which would mark a sixth consecutive quarter of double-digit gains, and Bloomberg Intelligence estimates full-year growth of approximately 16%. Energy and AI-linked technology are the principal contributors; the broader market's earnings trajectory has, if anything, deteriorated. Analysts at Morgan Stanley note the Magnificent Seven now trade at multiples comparable to consumer staples, a dislocation that has historically preceded multiple expansion, though the thesis requires confirmation that AI capital expenditure is translating into broad profitability — a question investors expect to dominate first-quarter conference calls.

Separately, a Democratic congressman has written to the SEC and CFTC requesting an investigation into unusual trading activity in oil and equity futures markets prior to the postponement of US military strikes against Iran last month. Morgan Stanley launched a Bitcoin-tracking ETF under the ticker MSBT — the first such product from a traditional Wall Street investment bank — underscoring the asset class's institutional entrenchment even as Bitcoin has fallen roughly 40% from its October peak, trading near $71,000. On the corporate front, Constellation Brands guided below expectations on beer volume growth, while Marvell Technology and Texas Instruments gained on analyst upgrades.
