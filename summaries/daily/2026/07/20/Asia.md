**Equity Rotation**
The rotation out of chipmakers and into Chinese internet names accelerated sharply. Samsung and SK Hynix have each declined at least 25% this month, while Alibaba, Xiaomi, and Meituan have surged more than 25%. The shift reflects two reinforcing dynamics: deteriorating investor confidence in the sustainability of AI hyperscaler capex, and fresh evidence from Moonshot's Kimi K3 that Chinese AI development is more competitive than previously assumed. Michael Burry disclosed short positions in Nvidia, Applied Materials, and the SOXX ETF while adding to long exposure in Hong Kong-listed names, characterizing valuations there as attractive relative to global peers.

**Policy and Diplomacy**
Secretary of State Marco Rubio confirmed that Xi Jinping's planned September visit to the US remains on schedule despite Trump's unsubstantiated claims of Chinese electoral interference — a signal that the bilateral truce, however fragile, is being preserved at the diplomatic level. India's student protests over alleged exam paper leaks represent an emerging domestic political challenge for Prime Minister Modi. Malaysia reported a surprise surge in Q2 GDP growth, with robust services and electronics exports cushioning the impact of Middle East disruptions.
