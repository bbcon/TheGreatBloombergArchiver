China’s Exports Stay Strong as AI Boom Offsets War Headwinds
Demand for AI gear helped buoy global goods trade last year, and the momentum 
looks solid this year

Booming demand for artificial intelligence gear is keeping China’s trade 
volumes on a path to exceed last year’s record levels, offsetting disruptions 
from higheroil prices 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/live-blog/2026-03-25/iran-war-trump-latest-news-updates-live-oil-markets?cmpid=BBD032526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260325>
 in the weeks after war broke out in Iran.

Nearly 20 million containers moved through Chinese ports in the first three 
weeks of March, an increase of more than 6% from the same period a year 
earlier, according to fresh data from the Ministry of Transport.

Read More: AI Demand Shields China’s Booming Trade From Iran War Shock

While moderating from the 12% gain seen in the first nine weeks of 2026, the 
recent pace indicates that aftershocks from the conflict in the Middle East 
have yet to become a serious drag on China’s export engine.

<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/global-trade-to-slow-amid-opposing-forces-of-energy-surge-and-ai?cmpid=BBD032526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260325>
 this year.

According to the Geneva-based trade body, AI-enabling goods rose to almost 17% 
of world trade last year from 13% in 2023. Trade in this merchandise jumped 22% 
last year from 2024.

Korea’s Exports

That momentum doesn’t appear to be abating yet. Economists point to a strong 
correlation between China’s outbound shipments and its imports from South Korea 
because of the deep integration between the two countries’ supply chains.

South Korea’s exports to China recorded a 69% jump in the first 20 days of 
March, with its overall semiconductor sales abroad surging 164%.

Still, South Korea is stepping up contingency planning for a worst-case Middle 
East scenario 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-25/south-korea-shifts-to-crisis-mode-as-iran-oil-shock-intensifies?cmpid=BBD032526_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260325>
, with Prime Minister Kim Min-seok warning that the government must strengthen 
its preemptive response systems as the conflict shows signs of persisting.
