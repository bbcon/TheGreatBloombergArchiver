Assembly Jobs

As the talks get under way, consider MexicoтАЩs exports of computer equipment. 
This category surged 144% last year, doubling its share of the countryтАЩs total 
exports to nearly 13%, fueled partly by demand from the AI-drivendata center 
boom 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/features/2026-meta-facebook-ai-data-center-louisiana/?cmpid=BBD052826_TRADE&utm_campaign=trade&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260528&utm_content=4847>
 in the US, as well as low tariffs in both Mexico and the US.

But computer assembly employs just 60,000 lower-skilled workers, and virtually 
none of the individual components are produced in Mexico. Economists argue that 
this points to a form of triangulation, in which the main beneficiaries are 
Asian manufacturers that use Mexico as a gateway to access the US market at 
lower tariff rates.
