US equity and bond markets came under significant pressure on Friday, as surging oil prices and renewed inflation concerns brought a sharp halt to the AI-driven equity rally that had carried stocks to successive record highs in recent weeks. S&P 500 futures fell 1.0% and Nasdaq 100 futures declined 1.5%, while the 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.54%, its highest level in approximately a year, extending a rise that has persisted across every session this week. The two-year yield climbed to 4.06%, a level last seen in March 2025. Fed futures markets now price roughly a two-thirds probability of a rate hike by December, a notable repricing that reflects Fed Governor Michael Barr's assessment earlier in the week that inflation remains "the overwhelming risk facing the economy." Stronger-than-expected US producer price data reinforced that concern.

On technology and corporate news, Nvidia's market capitalisation approached $6 trillion — a valuation no publicly traded company has previously reached — as AI-related demand for semiconductors continues to drive earnings upgrades that outpace share price gains. Cerebras Systems debuted in what is described as the year's largest IPO, surging 68% to a $67 billion market cap. Memory-chip names Sandisk and Micron also stand out: despite gains of 482% and 172% respectively this year, both trade at roughly nine times forward earnings as analyst estimates have risen faster than prices. Separately, Apple's relationship with OpenAI has deteriorated to the point where the startup is reportedly preparing potential legal action. Microsoft pared intraday losses after Bill Ackman disclosed a new stake in the company.

On the fiscal and political side, Trump's financial disclosures revealed personal equity purchases in the first quarter, including positions in Nvidia, Oracle, Microsoft, Boeing and Costco. With midterm elections less than six months away, Republicans have yet to coalesce around a credible response to voter concerns about the cost of living — a political vulnerability that the ongoing energy shock is amplifying.
