Status Anxiety

When Trump travels to Beijing next month, he’s expecting an even bigger 
welcome from his counterpart, Xi Jinping, than he got last time in 2017.

“I said, ‘You’ve got to top it.’ He said, ‘I’ll top it. We’re going to top 
it,’” the US presidentdeclared on Friday 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-20/trump-will-travel-to-china-in-late-march-for-high-stakes-xi-meet?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
.

Compare that to Friedrich Merz. The German chancellor, who is currently on his 
ownthree-day visit 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/merz-brings-big-business-group-to-china-in-bid-to-improve-ties?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 to the world’s No. 2 economy, has a far less assured tone.

“We want to engage in a dialog between equals,” he told reporters 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/germany-s-merz-reaches-out-to-xi-before-inaugural-trip-to-china?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
, rather hopefully, before setting off.
Friedrich Merz speaks in Germany before leaving for China
Germany can only faintly aspire to such a status now. While the country’s 
prowess as an exporter allowed it to stand as tall as China on the world stage 
earlier this century, the struggles faced by Europe’s biggest economy in recent 
years have left it a much diminished player.

That’s the harsh truth 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-21/merz-will-struggle-to-defy-economic-gravity-on-first-china-trip?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 Merz is confronting in Beijing. Aside from achieving next to no growth for 
years now, the Germans are realizing just how dependent their industry is on 
China for critical materials to produce everything from electric vehicle 
batteries to fighter jets.

Meanwhile the overall economic relationship between the two has shifted 
decisively in Beijing’s favor. German investment in China reached a four-year 
high in 2025. Exports to the European nation rose, while shipments back in the 
other direction fell.

Such a backdrop was reflected in German economic data 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/german-rebound-driven-by-domestic-demand-as-trade-damps-growth?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 out earlier on Wednesday. A much-awaited pickup in growth during the fourth 
quarter was driven by domestic spending, though held back by trade.

Merz can take heart. A year since his election victory, and after his 
governmentloosened debt rules 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-03-21/germany-s-landmark-spending-bill-wins-final-lawmaker-approval?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
 to spark a massive investment program in defense and infrastructure, the 
economy is finally starting to benefit.

Factory orders have begun to show a distinct improvement, and jumped in 
December by themost in two years 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-05/german-factory-orders-unexpectedly-rise-most-in-two-years?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
. This month, business confidence as measured by the Munich-based Ifo institute 
brightenedmore than anticipated 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-23/german-business-outlook-improves-amid-first-signs-of-recovery?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
.

But for all the hope augured by those numbers, Merz knows that the days when a 
German chancellor could visit Beijing with a strut are long gone.

“China has risen to the ranks of the major powers,” he said in Berlin before 
departing. “Our China policy has to take account of that.”

Need-to-Know Research

China’s Belt and Road Initiative, once dubbed the “project of the century 
<https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-10-16/xi-s-1-trillion-project-of-the-century-faces-uncertain-future?cmpid=BBD022526_NEF&utm_campaign=nef&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&utm_term=260225>
,” doesn’t get a whole lot of press these days, after Xi back in 2021 appeared 
to downsize his ambitions in prioritizing “small but beautiful 
<https://www.gov.cn/xinwen/2021-11/21/content_5652298.htm>” projects. Newly 
available data now suggests it’s roaring back.

That’s according to Gabriel Wildau at global advisory firm Teneo. The more 
cautious phase declared in 2021 “is now over,” he wrote in a note Tuesday. 
Wildau cited data showing “total BRI-related engagements reached an all-time 
high of $214 billion in 2025,” spanning investments and construction contracts 
for Chinese companies.
Zambian and Chinese flags outside of the Chinese centre for investment in 
Lusaka, Zambia, Sept. 21, 2023.Photographer: Zinyange Auntony/Bloomberg
“For both investment and construction deals, average deal size reached record 
levels last year, confirming that ‘small-but-beautiful’ was an artifact of the 
Covid era,” Wildau wrote. Energy, metals and mining, along with real estate and 
technology, are among top targets by sector. As for geography, central Asia has 
surged to account for one third of total investment last year, he said. The 
overall picture: “China’s foreign lending is high and rising.”
