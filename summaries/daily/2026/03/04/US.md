US equity markets displayed notable divergence from global peers on Wednesday, with the S&P 500 futures pointing to a modest gain of roughly 0.3% even as Asian markets collapsed and European indices remained choppy. The benchmark has declined less than 1% since hostilities in Iran began — a resilience that Goldman Sachs chairman David Solomon, speaking at a conference in Sydney, described as "benign" and somewhat surprising. The structural explanation is straightforward: the US is a net energy exporter, making it substantially less exposed to a sustained Strait of Hormuz disruption than European or Asian economies. As Barclays' Ajay Rajadhyaksha noted, the strait's closure is a far more acute problem for large Asian importers than for the American economy.

That insulation is not unlimited, however. Fed funds futures markets are pricing out rate cuts at an accelerating pace as traders weigh the inflationary implications of sustained high oil prices. Bloomberg Economics has modeled scenarios in which a prolonged Hormuz closure pushes Brent toward $108 per barrel — a level that would simultaneously push inflation higher and slow growth, placing the Federal Reserve in an awkward position between a president demanding lower rates and an economy facing a supply-side price shock. The 10-year Treasury yield edged up to 4.075%, a modest move that nonetheless reflects the repricing of the easing path.

In corporate news, Blackstone came under scrutiny as more than 25 senior executives collectively contributed approximately $150 million to support redemption payments from the firm's flagship private credit fund amid a record withdrawal wave. Separately, executives at the Bloomberg Invest conference, including Apollo's Marc Rowan and Soros's Dawn Fitzpatrick, warned of "a painful 18 to 24 months" ahead for private credit and private equity as default risks rise and asset sales remain difficult. Moderna gained 5.8% after settling Covid delivery-technology litigation for $950 million, while cryptocurrency-linked equities rallied as Bitcoin recovered ground lost earlier in the week.
